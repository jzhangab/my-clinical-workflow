**1.0 Background and Rationale**

Cervimab (CVM-101) is a humanized immunoglobulin G4 (IgG4)
anti-programmed cell death protein 1 (PD-1) monoclonal antibody approved
for the treatment of adult patients with unresectable or metastatic
colorectal cancer (mCRC) harboring deficient mismatch repair (dMMR) or
microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H) tumors. Approval was based on
randomized controlled trial (RCT) evidence demonstrating superiority
over chemotherapy with respect to progression-free survival (PFS) and
overall survival (OS).

Post-marketing real-world data (RWD) are essential to characterize the
comparative effectiveness and safety of Cervimab in populations
underrepresented in pivotal trials, including elderly patients (≥75
years), patients with Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group (ECOG)
performance status ≥2, patients with concurrent autoimmune disease
managed on immunosuppression, and those with KRAS/NRAS/BRAF
co-mutations. This analysis plan governs all post-launch RWD studies
conducted by Veridian Oncology, Inc. or its authorized research
partners.

This plan has been developed consistent with FDA guidance on Submitting
Documents Using Real-World Data and Real-World Evidence to the FDA for
Drugs and Biologics (2023), FDA Framework for FDA\'s Real-World Evidence
Program (2018), FDA guidance on Real-World Data: Assessing Electronic
Health Records and Medical Claims Data to Support Regulatory
Decision-Making (2021), and ICH E9(R1) Addendum on Estimands and
Sensitivity Analysis.

**2.0 Study Objectives and Research Questions**

**2.1 Primary Objectives**

- Assess the comparative effectiveness of Cervimab versus
  standard-of-care systemic therapies (pembrolizumab, FOLFOX-based
  regimens, FOLFIRI-based regimens, TAS-102, regorafenib) in patients
  with dMMR/MSI-H mCRC in routine clinical practice.

- Characterize the real-world safety profile of Cervimab with particular
  emphasis on immune-mediated adverse events (imAEs) of special
  interest, including immune-mediated colitis, pneumonitis, hepatitis,
  endocrinopathies, and severe cutaneous reactions.

- Estimate healthcare resource utilization (HCRU) and associated direct
  medical costs in commercially insured and Medicare populations
  receiving Cervimab versus comparators.

**2.2 Secondary Objectives**

- Evaluate overall survival (OS), real-world progression-free survival
  (rwPFS), time to next treatment (TTNT), and duration of response (DoR)
  in real-world settings.

- Characterize treatment patterns including treatment sequencing, dose
  modifications (delays, reductions), concurrent corticosteroid use for
  imAE management, and reasons for discontinuation.

- Assess patient-reported outcome (PRO) domains including health-related
  quality of life (HRQoL) using FACT-C and EQ-5D-5L in registry substudy
  populations.

- Examine outcomes in pre-specified subgroups: age ≥75, ECOG PS 2--3,
  concurrent autoimmune disease, Lynch syndrome versus sporadic MSI-H,
  right-sided versus left-sided primary, hepatic metastases, prior
  anti-VEGF/EGFR exposure.

**2.3 Exploratory Objectives**

- Explore potential predictive biomarkers of response and toxicity
  including tumor mutational burden (TMB), PD-L1 combined positive score
  (CPS), co-mutation status (BRAF V600E, PIK3CA), and circulating tumor
  DNA (ctDNA) dynamics where data are available.

- Examine adherence patterns and factors associated with early
  discontinuation (within 6 months) in real-world populations.

**4.0 Study Population**

**4.1 Eligible Population**

- Adult patients (≥18 years) with a confirmed diagnosis of unresectable
  or metastatic CRC documented by ICD-10-CM codes C18.0--C20, C26.0.

- Evidence of dMMR/MSI-H tumor status by IHC (loss of MLH1, MSH2, MSH6,
  or PMS2) or PCR/NGS-based MSI testing.

- Initiation of Cervimab (CVM-101) or specified comparator therapy on or
  after the Cervimab US approval date.

- Minimum 6-month enrollment/activity prior to index date (EHR-based
  studies) or 12-month continuous enrollment (claims-based studies).

**4.2 Exclusion Criteria**

- Concurrent enrollment in interventional clinical trials that could
  confound outcome ascertainment.

- Prior treatment with any PD-1/PD-L1/CTLA-4 checkpoint inhibitor within
  12 months before index date (for new-user analyses only; separate
  prevalent-user sensitivity analyses will be conducted).

- Missing index date, primary outcome data, or inability to ascertain
  dMMR/MSI-H status.

**4.3 Index Date and Time-Zero Definition**

The index date is defined as the date of first Cervimab infusion (or
first fill of comparator therapy) identified in the data source. An
active comparator, new-user design will be the primary analytical
approach to minimize immortal time bias and prevalent-user bias.

**6.0 Outcomes**

**6.1 Primary Outcomes**

- Overall Survival (OS): Time from index date to all-cause death,
  ascertained via Social Security Death Master File linkage, CMS
  mortality data, or EHR-recorded date of death. Patients alive at end
  of follow-up are censored at last activity date.

- Composite Grade ≥3 Immune-Mediated Adverse Events (imAEs): First
  occurrence of Grade ≥3 immune-related adverse event as identified by
  validated ICD-10-CM/CTCAE mapping algorithms, requiring systemic
  corticosteroids ≥20 mg/day prednisone equivalent or hospitalization
  within 30 days.

**6.2 Secondary Outcomes**

- Real-world Progression-Free Survival (rwPFS): Time to first evidence
  of radiographic or clinical progression (investigator-assessed in
  registry) or all-cause death, per Ronco et al. (2020) real-world
  progression algorithm for oncology claims data.

- Time to Next Treatment (TTNT): Duration from index therapy to
  initiation of a subsequent line of systemic anti-cancer therapy.

- Healthcare Resource Utilization (HCRU): All-cause and cancer-related
  inpatient admissions, ED visits, outpatient infusion visits, and total
  direct medical costs in 12-month post-index window.

- Treatment Duration and Dose Modifications: Therapy duration, dose
  delays (\>14 days from scheduled), dose reductions, and reasons for
  permanent discontinuation.

**7.0 Statistical Analysis Methods**

**7.1 Propensity Score Methods**

To mitigate confounding by indication, inverse probability of treatment
weighting (IPTW) using stabilized weights derived from logistic
regression propensity score models will be the primary analytical
approach. Propensity score overlap weighting (Li et al., 2018) will be
explored as a sensitivity analysis for improved covariate balance.
Covariate balance will be assessed using standardized mean differences
(SMD; target SMD \<0.10 post-weighting).

Covariates for PS models: age, sex, geographic region, insurance type,
year of index, ECOG PS, primary tumor location (right/left/rectal),
presence of hepatic/pulmonary/peritoneal metastases, number of prior
systemic therapy lines, KRAS/NRAS/BRAF mutation status (where
available), Charlson Comorbidity Index, prior corticosteroid use, prior
anti-VEGF/EGFR therapy.

**7.2 Time-to-Event Analysis**

- Kaplan-Meier method for unadjusted survival curves with log-rank
  tests; restricted mean survival time (RMST) as primary summary
  statistic per FDA guidance recommendation.

- Weighted Cox proportional hazards regression for confounder-adjusted
  hazard ratio (HR) estimates with 95% confidence intervals;
  proportional hazards assumption tested via Schoenfeld residuals.

- Competing risks analyses using Fine-Gray subdistribution hazard models
  where competing events (e.g., non-cancer death) are pre-specified.

**7.3 Subgroup Analyses**

Pre-specified subgroup analyses will be performed for: age (\<65 vs.
65--74 vs. ≥75), sex, ECOG PS (0--1 vs. 2--3), treatment line (1L vs.
2L+), Lynch syndrome status, BRAF V600E mutation, prior anti-VEGF
exposure, and hepatic metastases. Forest plots will summarize subgroup
HRs. Interaction p-values will be reported without adjustment for
multiplicity, and results interpreted as exploratory.

**Protocol Synopsis --- RWE-01**

**1. Objectives**

The primary objective is to compare overall survival between patients
receiving Cervimab and pembrolizumab (active comparator) in first-line
treatment of dMMR/MSI-H mCRC using propensity score-weighted analyses of
real-world EHR and claims data. Secondary objectives include comparison
of rwPFS, TTNT, safety (imAE rates), HCRU, and costs. Exploratory
objectives include treatment sequences and outcomes in elderly/PS ≥2
subgroups.

**2. Study Design**

A retrospective, multi-database, active comparator new-user cohort
design will be employed. Patients will be identified using validated
diagnosis and treatment identification algorithms applied to Flatiron
Health EHR data (primary), with parallel analyses conducted in Medicare
and SEER-Medicare databases for replication and generalizability. New
users of Cervimab or pembrolizumab initiated in the first-line
metastatic setting will be identified. The index date is the first
documented Cervimab or pembrolizumab administration.

**3. Eligibility Criteria**

- Inclusion: Adult patients (≥18 years); confirmed mCRC by ICD-10 codes;
  confirmed dMMR/MSI-H by IHC or molecular testing documented in the
  EHR; initiating first-line anti-PD-1 therapy (Cervimab or
  pembrolizumab) on or after the Cervimab US approval date; ≥6 months of
  EHR activity prior to index date.

- Exclusion: Prior checkpoint inhibitor therapy at any time; concurrent
  enrollment in blinded interventional trial; missing or unverifiable
  dMMR/MSI-H biomarker status; index date = date of death.

**4. Exposure and Comparator Definition**

Cervimab: identified by drug name and NDC/HCPCS code in EHR structured
data or claims. Pembrolizumab (active comparator): identified by drug
name and NDC code. Treatment duration is defined as the period from
index date to last documented administration plus one standard dosing
interval (3 weeks for pembrolizumab; dose-interval per approved labeling
for Cervimab) unless discontinuation is documented earlier.

**6. Sample Size and Power**

Based on published RWD for pembrolizumab in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC (OS HR ≈
0.60 vs. chemotherapy), assuming non-inferiority margins of HR ≤1.20
(OS) and HR ≤1.25 (rwPFS) for Cervimab vs. pembrolizumab, a minimum of
1,500 Cervimab patients and 1,500 pembrolizumab patients (1:1 ratio)
provides ≥80% power at one-sided α=0.025. Anticipated enrollment in the
Flatiron network and Medicare database over a 3-year accrual period and
1.5-year minimum follow-up is estimated at N≥3,500 eligible patients
across both cohorts.

**7. Statistical Analysis**

Primary analysis: IPTW-adjusted restricted mean survival time (RMST)
difference at 24 months with 95% CI. Logistic propensity score model
covariates as specified in Section 7.1 of the RWD-AP. Sensitivity
analyses include PS matching (1:1, nearest neighbor, caliper 0.02 SD),
overlap weighting, and analysis restricted to 2025--2027 to minimize
calendar-time confounding. Pre-specified subgroup analyses: age \<65 vs.
≥65, sex, Lynch syndrome status, BRAF V600E, hepatic metastases,
treatment line.

**Protocol Synopsis --- RWE-02**

**1. Background and Rationale**

The pivotal trials supporting Cervimab approval enrolled a median of 24
months of follow-up. Long-term immune-mediated toxicities --- including
delayed-onset colitis (\>12 months), hypophysitis, type 1 diabetes
mellitus, and rare Grade 4--5 events --- are not fully characterizable
within this window. CERV-REG is designed as a dedicated post-marketing
safety registry mandated as part of the Cervimab NDA approval to collect
prospective, granular safety data over 5 years with systematic medical
record verification.

**2. Registry Objectives**

- Primary: Estimate the 5-year cumulative incidence of Grade ≥3
  immune-mediated adverse events (imAEs) by organ system in real-world
  Cervimab-treated patients.

- Secondary: Characterize delayed-onset imAEs occurring \>6 months after
  last Cervimab dose; describe management patterns (high-dose steroids,
  biologics \[infliximab, mycophenolate\]); assess outcomes following
  imAE (re-challenge decisions, resolution rates, sequelae).

- Exploratory: Identify risk factors for severe imAEs (age, baseline
  autoimmune disease, concurrent medications, HLA alleles in biomarker
  substudy); evaluate outcomes in special populations (elderly ≥75, ECOG
  PS 2--3, organ transplant recipients).

**3. Registry Design**

CERV-REG is a prospective, longitudinal, non-interventional patient
registry. Consecutive Cervimab-treated patients meeting eligibility
criteria will be enrolled at participating sites. Patients will be
followed from registry enrollment through 5 years, last Cervimab dose +
24 months, or death, whichever occurs first. A matched comparator cohort
of pembrolizumab-treated patients (1:5 ratio, matched on age ±5 years,
sex, treatment line, ECOG PS) will be enrolled at a subset of sites to
enable comparative safety analyses.

**5. Safety Monitoring and DSMB**

A Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) consisting of three independent
oncologists, one immunologist, and one biostatistician will review
unblinded safety data semiannually. DSMB charter specifies stopping
rules for: cumulative incidence of Grade 5 imAE exceeding pre-specified
threshold (\>1.5% at 24 months), unexpected clustering of severe imAEs
(e.g., fatal myocarditis), or new safety signals identified through
pharmacovigilance.

All serious adverse events (SAEs) will be reported to the FDA via
MedWatch within required timeframes (15-day expedited reports for
unexpected serious suspected adverse reactions; 7-day reports for fatal
or life-threatening unexpected SUSARs). Annual safety reports (ASRs)
will be submitted per 21 CFR 312.32 and 21 CFR 314.81.

**6. Biomarker Substudy**

An optional biomarker substudy will enroll up to 500 consenting
patients. Blood samples (20 mL) will be collected at baseline and at 3,
12, and 24 months for: HLA typing (Luminex-based), T-cell receptor (TCR)
sequencing (Adaptive Biotechnologies), cytokine panel (Luminex 42-plex),
and anti-nuclear antibody (ANA)/anti-thyroid antibody screening. Tumor
tissue (archival FFPE or fresh biopsy) will be collected for TMB, PD-L1
CPS, and tumor-infiltrating lymphocyte (TIL) quantification. All samples
will be biobanked at a CLIA-certified central laboratory.

**7. Statistical Analysis --- RWE-02**

Cumulative incidence of each imAE category will be estimated by
Kaplan-Meier method (treating non-imAE death as competing risk via
Fine-Gray model). Rates will be reported per 100 person-years with exact
Poisson 95% CIs. Multivariable Cox regression will identify independent
predictors of Grade ≥3 imAE. All analyses will be stratified by
treatment line, age group, and ECOG PS. Comparative analyses against the
pembrolizumab cohort will employ PS-matched Kaplan-Meier and weighted
Cox models. Minimum registry size of 2,500 provides ≥90% power to detect
a 1.5-fold increase in Grade ≥3 imAE rate (assumed 15% base rate at 24
months) at α=0.05 two-sided.

**Protocol Synopsis --- RWE-03**

**1. Background and Rationale**

Post-launch health economics and outcomes research (HEOR) is essential
to characterize the real-world economic value of Cervimab, inform US
payer formulary and reimbursement decisions, and support value-based
contracting arrangements with health systems. Payers require
demonstration of cost-effectiveness and comparative HCRU in routine
clinical practice beyond controlled trial settings. Simultaneously,
capturing patient-reported outcomes (PROs) in the real-world setting
bridges the gap between investigator-assessed clinical endpoints and
patient-centered evidence requirements from the FDA and payers.

**2. Study Objectives**

- Primary HCRU Objective: Compare all-cause and cancer-related total
  direct medical costs per patient-year between Cervimab and
  chemotherapy-treated (FOLFOX ± bevacizumab) patients in the 12 months
  following treatment initiation, using administrative claims data.

- Primary PRO Objective: Assess change from baseline in FACT-C total
  score at 6, 12, and 24 months in Cervimab-treated patients enrolled in
  the CERV-REG PRO module.

- Secondary Objectives: Characterize rates of outpatient infusion
  visits, inpatient admissions (any cause, imAE-related,
  cancer-related), emergency department (ED) visits, specialist
  referrals, and costs of corticosteroid/biologic management of imAEs.

**3. HCRU Claims Analysis Design**

A retrospective, new-user active comparator cohort design will be
applied to IBM MarketScan Commercial Claims (commercially insured, age
18--64) and CMS Medicare FFS claims (age ≥65). Patients with continuous
enrollment for 12 months pre-index and at least 1 month post-index will
be included. HCRU outcomes will be assessed over 12 months post-index.
Generalized linear models (GLM) with gamma distribution and log link
will be used for total cost comparisons. IPTW as described in the RWD-AP
will address confounding. All costs will be inflation-adjusted to 2025
USD using the Consumer Price Index for medical care.

**4. PRO Substudy Design**

The PRO substudy will leverage the CERV-REG infrastructure (Study
RWE-02). All CERV-REG enrollees will be offered participation in the
electronic PRO (ePRO) module using validated instruments: FACT-C
(Functional Assessment of Cancer Therapy --- Colorectal), EQ-5D-5L
(generic HRQoL and health utility index), PROMIS Fatigue Short Form 8a,
and the CRC-specific Gastrointestinal Symptom Rating Scale (GSRS-IBD).
ePRO assessments will be administered via validated electronic platform
at baseline, and at 3, 6, 12, 18, and 24 months. Missing data will be
handled using multiple imputation (MI) with chained equations (MICE),
assuming missing at random (MAR). Sensitivity analyses under missing not
at random (MNAR) will be conducted using pattern-mixture models.

**6. Budget Impact Modeling**

A budget impact model (BIM) will be developed per ISPOR Good Practice
guidelines to estimate the 3-year payer budget impact of Cervimab
adoption from the perspective of a hypothetical US payer with 1 million
covered lives. Inputs will be derived from the RWE-03 HCRU results,
published epidemiology of dMMR/MSI-H mCRC incidence (estimated 4--5% of
mCRC), and drug acquisition costs per publicly available list prices.
Model transparency requirements (structured uncertainty analysis,
scenario analyses) will follow AMCP Format v4.0.

**Section B --- Human Subjects Protections**

**B.1 Informed Consent**

Studies RWD-AP and RWE-01 utilize pre-existing, de-identified or limited

subjects and are therefore eligible for a Waiver of Informed Consent
under 45 CFR 46.116(f) and 21 CFR 56.109(c). The Sponsor certifies that:
(1) the waiver will not adversely affect the rights and welfare of
subjects; (2) the research could not practicably be carried out without
the waiver; (3) the research involves no more than minimal risk; and (4)

pertinent information after participation.

Study RWE-02 (CERV-REG) involves prospective enrollment of identifiable
human subjects and collection of biospecimens. Informed consent will be
obtained from all CERV-REG participants prior to enrollment. The consent
process will include a full explanation of: (a) study purpose and
duration; (b) data elements collected and data sharing practices; (c)
voluntary participation and right to withdraw without penalty; (d)
potential risks (minimal --- no investigational intervention
administered); (e) potential benefits (contribution to knowledge; access
to study results); (f) confidentiality protections; (g) contact
information for the PI and for the IRB.

Consent forms will be provided in English and, where applicable at
multi-lingual sites, in Spanish, Mandarin Chinese, and other languages
per site demographics and IRB requirements. A short-form consent process
per 21 CFR 50.27(b)(2) may be utilized for eligible participants with
limited English proficiency.

**B.2 Risk-Benefit Assessment**

The risks associated with all four studies are classified as minimal
risk per 45 CFR 46.102(j), defined as risks that are no greater in and
of themselves than those ordinarily encountered in daily life or during
routine physical or psychological examinations or tests. Specifically:

- Retrospective data studies (RWD-AP, RWE-01, RWE-03 claims component):
  No direct contact with subjects; risk limited to potential privacy
  breach, mitigated by robust data security measures described in the
  Data Use Agreement and Section B.3 below.

- Prospective registry (RWE-02): No investigational therapy
  administered; risks limited to time burden of PRO questionnaire
  completion (estimated 15--20 minutes per assessment) and potential
  psychological discomfort from health-status questions. Blood draw
  risks (bruising, discomfort) for biospecimen substudy are standard
  phlebotomy risks. No physical examinations beyond standard of care
  will be performed.

Potential benefits include generation of knowledge applicable to the
treatment of future dMMR/MSI-H mCRC patients, refinement of toxicity
management algorithms, and PRO data that may directly inform clinical
practice improvements. The benefit-to-risk ratio is favorable.
