**1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

This Pediatric Study Report presents the findings of CERV-PED-001, a
Phase I/II, open-label, multicenter study evaluating the safety,
pharmacokinetics (PK), pharmacodynamics (PD), and preliminary efficacy
of cervimab in pediatric patients aged 2 to \<18 years with relapsed or
refractory dMMR/MSI-H solid tumors. This study was conducted pursuant to
the PREA deferral agreed with FDA (Pediatric Plan PP-CERV-2024-001) as a
post-marketing requirement under BLA 761XXX.

A total of 78 pediatric patients were enrolled across 22 pediatric
oncology centers. The study met its primary PK objective: cervimab
exposure (AUC and Cmax) in pediatric patients at the weight-adjusted
dose of 3 mg/kg Q3W was within 80-125% of the target exposure range
established in adults at 400 mg Q6W. The safety profile was consistent
with the known profile in adults, with no unexpected pediatric-specific
safety signals identified. Preliminary efficacy data demonstrated an ORR
of 36% (95% CI: 24-49%) in the evaluable population.

**2. BACKGROUND**

**2.1 Pediatric Relevance and PREA Basis**

While metastatic CRC is exceedingly rare in the pediatric population,
dMMR/MSI-H solid tumors in children --- including high-grade gliomas
with germline MMR mutations (Constitutional Mismatch Repair Deficiency,
CMMRD), colorectal tumors in Lynch syndrome, and other MSI-H solid
tumors --- present an unmet medical need. The FDA\'s Oncology Center of
Excellence (OCE) identified dMMR/MSI-H as a disease-relevant molecular
feature across multiple pediatric solid tumor types and required a PREA
study.

**2.2 Prior Nonclinical and Clinical Data Supporting Pediatric Study**

Nonclinical juvenile animal toxicity studies (6-month rat and 3-month
cynomolgus monkey, conducted per ICH S11) demonstrated no
pediatric-specific developmental or reproductive toxicities at exposures
up to 10-fold the clinical AUC. PD-1 expression profiles and T-cell
receptor repertoires in pediatric dMMR tumors mirror those in adult
tumors, supporting the biological plausibility of cervimab activity in
the pediatric setting.

**2.3 FDA-Agreed Pediatric Plan**

FDA agreed to a PREA deferral pending availability of adult safety and
PK data. The agreed Pediatric Plan specified: (1) a Phase I PK/safety
run-in in patients ≥12 years; (2) dose extrapolation to younger patients
using population PK modeling; (3) an expansion cohort (Phase II) across
all age groups at the recommended pediatric dose (RPeD).

**3. STUDY DESIGN AND METHODS**

**3.1 Study Design**

CERV-PED-001 was conducted in two sequential stages:

- Stage 1 (Dose-Finding, n=18): Open-label PK/PD run-in in adolescents
  (12 to \<18 years) using a modified 3+3 design starting at 2 mg/kg
  Q3W, escalating to 3 mg/kg Q3W and 5 mg/kg Q3W. Dose-limiting
  toxicities (DLTs) were assessed over Cycle 1 (21 days).

- Stage 2 (Expansion, n=60): Enrollment of patients across three age
  cohorts (Cohort 1: 12-\<18 years; Cohort 2: 6-\<12 years; Cohort 3:
  2-\<6 years) at the RPeD of 3 mg/kg Q3W, with PopPK-guided dose
  confirmation for younger cohorts.

**3.2 Eligibility Summary**

Key eligibility criteria included: age 2 to \<18 years; histologically
confirmed solid tumor with centrally confirmed dMMR/MSI-H; relapsed or
refractory disease with no adequate standard therapy; ECOG/Lansky
performance status ≥50; adequate organ function; and no prior
anti-PD-1/PD-L1 therapy.

**3.3 Pharmacokinetic Assessments**

Serial PK sampling was performed at Cycles 1, 3, and 6. Rich PK sampling
at Cycle 1 (Cohort 1 and Cohort 2) and sparse PK sampling at subsequent
cycles for all cohorts was used to support population PK model building.
PK parameters estimated included: AUC0-21d, Cmax, Ctrough, t½, CL/F, and
Vd.

**4. PHARMACOKINETIC RESULTS**

**4.1 Population PK Model**

A two-compartment model with first-order elimination, incorporating body
weight as an allometric covariate on clearance and volume, provided the
best fit to the pooled pediatric-adult dataset. Weight-normalized
clearance in pediatric patients was consistent with adults after
allometric scaling.

**5.2 Pediatric-Specific Safety Observations**

No pediatric-specific safety signals were identified. The irAE profile
was qualitatively similar to adults. Notably, no cases of
immune-mediated myocarditis were observed. Infusion reactions were
observed in 3 patients (4%), all Grade 1-2, managed with antihistamines.
Growth parameters (height, weight, Tanner staging) were monitored
throughout; no meaningful deviations from expected age-appropriate
trajectories were observed.

**6. EFFICACY RESULTS**

**6.1 Objective Response Rate (Primary Efficacy Endpoint)**

Among 72 response-evaluable patients, the ORR was 36.1% (95% CI:
24.9--48.7%), including 4 complete responses (5.6%) and 22 partial
responses (30.6%). Disease control rate (DCR) was 65.3%.

**7. CONCLUSIONS AND REGULATORY IMPLICATIONS**

CERV-PED-001 has fulfilled the terms of the FDA-agreed Pediatric Plan
(PP-CERV-2024-001). Key conclusions:

- The Recommended Pediatric Dose (RPeD) of 3 mg/kg Q3W achieves PK
  exposures comparable to the approved adult dose, satisfying the
  primary PK objective.

- The safety profile in pediatric patients is consistent with the known
  adult profile, with no identified pediatric-specific safety signals.

- Preliminary efficacy data support anti-tumor activity of cervimab
  across dMMR/MSI-H pediatric solid tumor types.

- These data support a pediatric labeling update for cervimab in
  patients ≥2 years with dMMR/MSI-H solid tumors and will be submitted
  as a supplemental BLA.

This report is submitted in fulfillment of PREA requirements per 21
U.S.C. 505B. A request for pediatric labeling changes will accompany
this report.
