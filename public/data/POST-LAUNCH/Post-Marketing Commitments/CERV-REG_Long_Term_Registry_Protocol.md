**1. INTRODUCTION AND RATIONALE**

**1.1 Purpose of the Registry**

CERV-REG is a long-term observational registry designed to
systematically capture the real-world safety, effectiveness, and
clinical outcomes of cervimab across the full spectrum of patients and
clinical settings in which it is used post-approval. The registry serves
multiple complementary objectives: pharmacovigilance, effectiveness
monitoring, health outcomes research, and generation of evidence to
support potential label expansions.

**1.2 Regulatory Context and Linkage**

CERV-REG addresses FDA\'s expectation, outlined in the REMS program for
cervimab and in the Risk Management Plan (RMP), for long-term
post-marketing follow-up of immune-related adverse events, secondary
malignancies, and late-onset toxicities. The registry data will inform
annual Periodic Benefit-Risk Evaluation Reports (PBRERs/PSURs) and will
provide observational data to supplement PMC/PMR interventional studies.

**1.3 Relationship to Other Post-Marketing Activities**

CERV-REG is designed to be synergistic and non-duplicative with
CERV-PMC-001 (PMC/PMR protocol). Patients enrolled in CERV-PMC-001 may
separately consent to enrollment in CERV-REG for long-term follow-up
beyond the PMC/PMR study window. The registry does not mandate any
study-specific assessments beyond routine clinical care.

**2. REGISTRY OBJECTIVES**

**2.1 Primary Registry Objectives**

- Characterize the long-term (5- and 10-year) safety profile of
  cervimab, with emphasis on: (a) late-onset irAEs occurring \>6 months
  after last dose; (b) secondary malignancies; (c) immune-mediated
  endocrinopathies requiring ongoing hormone replacement.

- Estimate real-world long-term overall survival (OS) and
  time-to-treatment-failure (TTF) in patients treated with cervimab.

- Evaluate the impact of duration of cervimab therapy, re-treatment
  after discontinuation, and subsequent therapies on long-term outcomes.

**2.2 Secondary Objectives**

- Document pregnancy outcomes in patients of childbearing potential who
  were exposed to cervimab within 6 months of conception.

- Characterize patterns of cervimab use outside the approved indication
  (off-label use monitoring).

- Evaluate HRQoL over time using validated patient-reported outcome
  instruments (FACT-C, EQ-5D-5L).

- Generate comparative effectiveness evidence versus historical controls
  and alternative systemic therapies.

**3. REGISTRY DESIGN**

**3.1 Study Type**

CERV-REG is a prospective, observational, non-interventional registry.
The registry does not prescribe, modify, or mandate any clinical
interventions. All treatment decisions are at the sole discretion of the
treating physician per routine clinical care.

**3.2 Registry Entry Points**

- Newly initiating cervimab for any approved or off-label indication
  (prospective enrollment)

- Patients who previously received cervimab and are transitioning from
  CERV-PMC-001 (long-term follow-up extension)

- Patients identified through electronic health record (EHR) data
  linkage partners (with consent)

**3.3 Sample Size and Enrollment Plan**

A target enrollment of 5,000 patients over 5 years is planned, with a
minimum follow-up of 10 years from date of last enrollment. This sample
size enables detection of secondary malignancy rates as low as 0.5% per
1,000 person-years and will support robust subgroup analyses by age,
sex, indication, and treatment duration. Enrollment will be opened at
approximately 200 sites in the United States in Year 1, with EU/Canada
expansion in Year 2.

**3.4 Registry Duration**

- Enrollment period: 5 years from registry activation

- Follow-up: Minimum 10 years from last patient enrolled (i.e., \~15
  years total operational period)

- Registry may be extended by mutual agreement with FDA based on interim
  analyses

**4. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA**

**4.1 Inclusion Criteria**

- Any patient receiving or who has received cervimab for any approved
  indication

- Age ≥ 2 years at time of first cervimab dose

- Signed informed consent (guardian consent + assent for pediatric
  patients)

- Willingness to complete annual follow-up contacts (minimum) for the
  duration of the registry

**4.2 Exclusion Criteria**

- Current enrollment in a blinded interventional clinical trial where
  registry participation could compromise blinding

- Inability or unwillingness to provide informed consent

> *► There are no treatment-based exclusions. Off-label use is captured
> and tracked but does not preclude enrollment.*

**5. DATA COLLECTION PLAN**

**5.1 Baseline Data Elements**

- Demographics: age, sex, race/ethnicity, geographic region

- Tumor characteristics: primary site, histology, dMMR/MSI-H testing
  method and result, PD-L1 CPS, TMB, Lynch syndrome status

- Prior treatment history: prior lines, regimens, radiation, surgery

- Cervimab details: start date, indication, dose, schedule, combination
  partners

- Comorbidities: autoimmune history, prior immunosuppressive therapy,
  smoking, performance status

**6. DATA MANAGEMENT AND GOVERNANCE**

**6.1 Electronic Data Capture**

All registry data will be entered into a validated, 21 CFR Part
11-compliant EDC platform (Veeva Vault Clinical). Sites will be trained
prior to activation. An electronic CRF (eCRF) completion guide will be
provided to all site staff. Patient identifiers will be replaced by
registry-assigned pseudonymous codes; a secure key management system
will be maintained per applicable privacy regulations.

**6.2 Data Quality Assurance**

Central monitoring will be performed by the registry data management
team. Source data verification (SDV) will be conducted for a risk-based
sample of critical data fields (vital status, SAEs, irAEs). Automated
range checks, consistency checks, and query workflows will be built into
the EDC platform.

**6.3 Data Linkage**

With patient consent, registry data will be linked to: (a) National
Death Index (NDI) for vital status; (b) SEER database for secondary
malignancy ascertainment; (c) CMS claims data for healthcare utilization
analyses. Linkage protocols will be documented in a separate Data
Linkage Agreement and Privacy Impact Assessment.

**6.4 Scientific Steering Committee**

A Scientific Steering Committee (SSC) comprising independent clinical
experts, an ethicist, a patient advocate, and sponsor representatives
will oversee the scientific integrity of the registry. The SSC will
convene annually to review data quality reports, emerging safety
signals, and publication plans.

**7. PHARMACOVIGILANCE AND SIGNAL MANAGEMENT**

CERV-REG data will be reviewed quarterly by NovImmune Pharmacovigilance
in the context of the overall Pharmacovigilance System.
Disproportionality analyses (Reporting Odds Ratio, PRR) will be applied
to registry AE data semi-annually. Any identified safety signal meeting
pre-defined thresholds will be escalated per the established Signal
Management SOP and communicated to FDA within 15 days per applicable
reporting requirements.

**7.1 Special Populations Surveillance**

Dedicated surveillance modules will be activated for: pregnant patients
(Cervimab Pregnancy Registry sub-module); pediatric patients; elderly
patients (age ≥75); patients with pre-existing autoimmune conditions;
and patients receiving corticosteroids at baseline. Separate aggregate
analyses of these subgroups will be included in each annual registry
safety report.

**8. PUBLICATION AND DATA SHARING POLICY**

Results from CERV-REG will be published in peer-reviewed journals on a
regular schedule, with primary safety and effectiveness analyses
published at Year 3, Year 5, and Year 10. The registry will be
registered at ClinicalTrials.gov prior to first enrollment.
Summary-level registry data will be made available through the
sponsor\'s clinical data sharing portal per ICH E17 and PhRMA/EFPIA data
sharing principles.

**9. ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS**

The registry protocol will be reviewed by a central IRB (WCG IRB) and
local IRBs where required. Patients will be approached for consent only
in the context of a clinical interaction with their treating physician.
Participation is entirely voluntary and will have no impact on access to
cervimab or any other treatment. The registry complies fully with the
Declaration of Helsinki, ICH GCP E6(R2), HIPAA, and applicable EU GDPR
provisions.
