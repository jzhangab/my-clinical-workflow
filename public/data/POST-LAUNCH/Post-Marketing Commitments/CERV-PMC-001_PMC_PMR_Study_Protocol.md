**1. BACKGROUND AND RATIONALE**

**1.1 Disease Overview**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) is the third most common cancer and second
leading cause of cancer-related mortality in the United States, with
approximately 153,000 new cases and 53,000 deaths annually. A clinically
and molecularly distinct subpopulation --- those with deficient mismatch
repair (dMMR) or high microsatellite instability (MSI-H) --- constitutes
approximately 4-5% of metastatic CRC cases and exhibits distinct
immunogenic characteristics rendering it susceptible to immune
checkpoint blockade.

**1.2 Cervimab and Approved Indication**

Cervimab (cerviximab-pgrc) is a humanized IgG4 monoclonal antibody
targeting the Programmed Death-1 (PD-1) receptor. On \[APPROVAL DATE\],
the U.S. FDA granted regular approval for cervimab for the first-line
treatment of adult patients with unresectable or metastatic dMMR/MSI-H
CRC, based on the CERV-301 pivotal trial demonstrating a statistically
significant and clinically meaningful improvement in progression-free
survival (PFS) (HR = 0.42; 95% CI: 0.31--0.57; p\<0.0001) and overall
survival (OS) (HR = 0.61; 95% CI: 0.46--0.81; p=0.0006) versus FOLFOX4.

**1.3 Basis for PMC/PMR**

As a condition of approval, FDA required the following post-marketing
studies under 21 CFR 505(o)(3) and FDA\'s PMC/PMR tracking:

- PMC-4231 (Confirmatory Efficacy): Evaluate real-world effectiveness,
  including overall survival and time-to-next-treatment, in an
  unselected metastatic CRC population treated with cervimab in routine
  clinical practice.

- PMR-4232 (Mandatory Safety): Characterize the incidence, severity,
  time-to-onset, and outcomes of rare but serious immune-related adverse
  events (irAEs), including Grade 3/4 colitis, pneumonitis, hepatitis,
  and endocrinopathies, in a larger, more diverse population than the
  pivotal trial.

- PMR-4233 (Biomarker): Prospectively validate the predictive utility of
  PD-L1 combined positive score (CPS), tumor mutational burden (TMB-H),
  and circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) dynamics as biomarkers of response
  and survival.

**2. STUDY OBJECTIVES AND ENDPOINTS**

**2.1 Primary Objectives**

- To estimate overall survival (OS) in patients with dMMR/MSI-H mCRC
  receiving cervimab as first-line therapy in routine clinical practice.

- To characterize the incidence and severity profile of serious
  immune-related adverse events (irAEs) (PMR-4232).

**2.2 Secondary Objectives**

- Progression-free survival (PFS) per investigator assessment using
  RECIST v1.1

- Objective response rate (ORR) and duration of response (DoR)

- Time to next treatment (TTNT)

- Health-related quality of life (HRQoL) via FACT-C and EQ-5D-5L

- Characterization of irAE management strategies and corticosteroid use

- Patient-reported outcomes related to treatment tolerability

**2.3 Exploratory Objectives**

- Correlation of PD-L1 CPS, TMB-H, and ctDNA kinetics with clinical
  outcomes (PMR-4233)

- Evaluation of outcomes in subgroups: BRAF V600E, Lynch syndrome, RAS
  mutation status

- Assessment of secondary resistance mechanisms via exploratory tumor
  genomic profiling

**3. STUDY DESIGN**

**3.1 Overview**

This is a Phase IV, open-label, multicenter, prospective observational
cohort study conducted at approximately 80-100 oncology centers in the
United States (primary) and select international sites (EU, Canada). The
study will enroll patients newly initiating cervimab for the approved
indication and will follow them longitudinally for a minimum of 5 years
from the date of last enrollment.

**3.2 Cohort Design**

Two cohorts will be enrolled:

- Cohort A (Prospective): Patients initiating cervimab at the time of or
  following protocol activation. Full prospective data collection per
  protocol schedule.

- Cohort B (Retrospective-Prospective): Patients who initiated cervimab
  within 12 months prior to site activation, for whom adequate medical
  records are available. Retrospective baseline and early follow-up data
  will be supplemented by prospective follow-up.

**3.3 Sample Size**

A total of 1,500 evaluable patients are planned (Cohort A: n=1,200;
Cohort B: n=300). This sample size provides \>90% power to estimate
36-month OS with a margin of error of ±3% (assuming \~45% 36-month OS)
and to detect a serious irAE rate of 8% with a 95% CI half-width of
±1.5%.

**3.4 Duration**

- Enrollment Period: 36 months from study activation

- Follow-Up Period: Minimum 5 years from last patient enrolled

- Total Study Duration: Approximately 8 years

- Final PMR/PMC Report Due: \[DATE per FDA agreement\]

**4. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA**

**4.1 Inclusion Criteria**

- Histologically or cytologically confirmed unresectable or metastatic
  CRC

- dMMR confirmed by IHC and/or MSI-H confirmed by PCR, NGS, or
  equivalent validated assay

- Initiating or recently initiated (Cohort B) cervimab as first-line
  systemic therapy per approved labeling

- Age ≥ 18 years

- Signed informed consent (or waiver per applicable IRB authorization)

- Available baseline tumor specimen (FFPE) for central biomarker
  assessment (PMR-4233 sub-study)

**4.2 Exclusion Criteria**

- Prior systemic anti-cancer therapy for metastatic disease (except
  Cohort B retrospective window)

- Receipt of any investigational agent within 4 weeks of enrollment

- Participation in interventional clinical trial concurrent with study
  enrollment

- Inability to comply with study procedures or provide adequate informed
  consent

**5. SAFETY MONITORING AND REPORTING**

**5.1 Adverse Event Definitions**

All adverse events will be graded per NCI CTCAE v5.0. Immune-related
adverse events (irAEs) are defined as adverse events with an
immune-mediated etiology requiring immunosuppressive therapy, occurring
during or within 90 days of last cervimab dose.

**5.2 Expedited Reporting**

All unexpected serious adverse drug reactions (SADRs) will be reported
to FDA within 15 calendar days per 21 CFR 312.32(c). Fatal or
life-threatening unexpected SADRs will be reported within 7 calendar
days. Annual IND Safety Reports will be submitted per 21 CFR 312.33.

**5.3 Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB)**

An independent DSMB will be constituted prior to study activation. The
DSMB will conduct formal safety reviews at 6-month intervals and ad hoc
as clinically indicated. The DSMB charter is provided as a separate
document (Appendix C).

**5.4 Stopping Rules**

The DSMB may recommend study modification or early termination if
observed serious irAE rates exceed 15% Grade 3-5 or if a previously
uncharacterized safety signal is identified at a frequency and severity
warranting sponsor notification to FDA.

**7. DATA MANAGEMENT AND STATISTICAL ANALYSIS**

**7.1 Data Collection**

Data will be collected via a validated electronic data capture (EDC)
system (Medidata Rave). Source documents include medical records,
electronic health records, radiology reports, pathology reports, and
patient-reported outcome instruments. Central laboratory will process
all biomarker specimens.

**7.2 Statistical Analysis Plan**

A full Statistical Analysis Plan (SAP) will be finalized before any
unblinded data review. Key statistical approaches:

- OS and PFS: Kaplan-Meier estimates; log-rank test; Cox proportional
  hazards model with stratification by ECOG PS, primary tumor location,
  and RAS/BRAF mutation status

- ORR: Exact binomial confidence intervals

- irAE rates: Point estimates with 95% Wilson score intervals;
  time-to-onset analyzed by Nelson-Aalen estimator

- Biomarker analyses: Pre-specified landmark analyses at Week 9 for
  ctDNA; Cox regression models for PD-L1 CPS and TMB-H as continuous
  predictors

**7.3 Analysis Populations**

- Full Analysis Set (FAS): All enrolled patients who received at least
  one dose of cervimab

- Safety Analysis Set: Identical to FAS

- Biomarker Evaluable Set (BES): FAS patients with evaluable baseline
  biomarker specimens

The study will be conducted in accordance with the Declaration of
