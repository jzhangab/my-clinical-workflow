**1. INTRODUCTION AND SCOPE**

This budget impact analysis (BIA) estimates the financial consequences
of adding cervimab to a US health plan formulary for the treatment of
adult patients with dMMR/MSI-H metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC) who
have received at least one prior line of systemic therapy. The analysis
was conducted following ISPOR Task Force guidelines for budget impact
analysis in healthcare (Sullivan et al., 2014) and AMCP Format for
Formulary Submissions (Version 4.1, 2019).

The analysis estimates the change in drug spending, total medical
spending, and net per-member-per-month (PMPM) cost for a representative
US health plan with 1,000,000 covered lives over a 5-year period.
Results are presented for the base case and stratified by commercial and
Medicare Advantage coverage. Scenario and sensitivity analyses explore
the impact of key assumptions.

**2.2 Patient Flow Model**

The patient flow model accounts for: (1) incidence of new mCRC
diagnoses; (2) proportion with dMMR/MSI-H status; (3) biomarker testing
rate in the ≥2L setting; (4) treatment eligibility (ECOG PS ≤2, adequate
organ function); (5) proportion receiving active treatment vs. best
supportive care. The resulting eligible patient pool of approximately
2.3 patients per 1,000,000 plan members annually reflects the small but
molecularly defined target population.

**3.2 Market Mix Rationale**

Uptake assumptions are informed by: (1) analogue analysis from
pembrolizumab adoption in first-line MSI-H mCRC (KEYNOTE-177); (2)
anticipated prior authorization requirements restricting use to
confirmed dMMR/MSI-H patients; (3) competitive landscape assumptions;
and (4) key opinion leader (KOL) survey data (n=42 medical oncologists,
conducted Q4 2025). KOL surveys indicated that 68% of respondents would
consider cervimab their preferred second-line therapy for confirmed
dMMR/MSI-H patients within 12 months of approval.

**3.3 Drug Displacement Assumptions**

Introduction of cervimab is assumed to displace existing second-line
chemotherapy regimens in the dMMR/MSI-H population. Market share is
primarily captured from FOLFOX ± bevacizumab (55%), FOLFIRI ±
bevacizumab (35%), and other regimens (10%). There is minimal off-label
PD-1 inhibitor use in this specific setting currently, so displacement
from other checkpoint inhibitors was modeled conservatively at 5%.

**6.2 Break-Even Rebate Analysis**

At WAC pricing with no rebate, the net PMPM impact is approximately
+\$0.07--\$0.09 in Years 3--5. To achieve budget neutrality (0 PMPM
impact including medical cost offsets), the required rebate on WAC is
approximately 18.4%. To achieve the favorable negative PMPM impact seen
in the base case, a rebate of approximately 23--25% is required. The
proposed 25% rebate therefore achieves budget neutrality plus modest
payer savings.

**6.3 Testing Rate Sensitivity**

Increasing the dMMR/MSI-H testing rate from 72% to 90% in the ≥2L
setting would increase the eligible patient pool by approximately 25%,
resulting in additional cervimab treated patients but also additional
patients identified who would have otherwise received chemotherapy
without the benefit of checkpoint inhibitor therapy. From a purely
budget perspective, higher testing rates improve the cost offset profile
at net price given the per-patient net savings.

**7.2 Value-Based Contracting**

Cervi Therapeutics is prepared to enter value-based contracting
arrangements with payers, including: (1) outcomes-based rebate tied to
real-world PFS achieving ≥12 months; (2) indication-specific pricing
with separate net price for post-pembrolizumab vs. chemo-naïve
populations; (3) pay-for-performance with rebate clawback if real-world
ORR falls below 35%; and (4) total cost of care arrangement
incorporating downstream medical cost savings. These contracting models
are aligned with PBMI and AMCP frameworks for specialty oncology
value-based contracting.

**8.2 Medical Policy Language (Suggested)**

Cervimab (Cervimab IV, Cervi Therapeutics) is covered under the medical
benefit as a specialty oncology product when ALL of the following
criteria are met: (1) diagnosis of metastatic colorectal adenocarcinoma;
(2) dMMR confirmed by immunohistochemistry (IHC; loss of at least one
MMR protein: MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2) OR MSI-H confirmed by PCR or
next-generation sequencing (NGS); (3) prior receipt of at least one
systemic therapy for metastatic disease (fluoropyrimidine-based regimen
with oxaliplatin or irinotecan, ± bevacizumab); (4) ECOG Performance
Status 0--2; (5) prescribed by a board-certified medical oncologist; (6)
documentation of adequate hepatic, renal, and hematologic function
within 30 days of initiation. Coverage is subject to annual review and
reauthorization upon documentation of continued clinical benefit.

**9. CONCLUSIONS**

The budget impact analysis demonstrates that formulary adoption of
cervimab at the proposed net price of approximately \$13,875 per
infusion will have a negligible-to-favorable impact on health plan
budgets. The small but molecularly defined eligible population
(approximately 2.3 patients per million plan members per year) limits
total drug cost exposure, while reductions in downstream medical
costs---primarily AE management, hospitalization, and supportive
care---offset incremental drug acquisition costs.

Under the base case scenario with 40% peak market penetration and 25%
net rebate, the 5-year cumulative PMPM impact is negative
(budget-saving) at −\$0.020 per member per month on average. Even under
optimistic uptake and WAC pricing scenarios, the absolute PMPM impact
remains well below the \$0.50 PMPM threshold commonly cited by payers as
warranting restrictive access management.

The combination of a small target population, favorable per-patient
economics at net price, and comprehensive patient access and value-based
contracting programs positions cervimab as a formulary-appropriate
specialty oncology product that delivers transformative clinical value
without imposing significant budget burden on health plans.
