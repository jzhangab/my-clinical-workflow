**1.2 Mechanism of Action**

Cervimab is a fully human IgG4 anti-PD-1 monoclonal antibody that blocks
the interaction between PD-1 and its ligands PD-L1 and PD-L2. PD-1 is an
inhibitory receptor expressed on activated T cells; PD-L1 and PD-L2 are
expressed on tumor cells and tumor-infiltrating immune cells in
dMMR/MSI-H colorectal cancer. By blocking this checkpoint, cervimab
restores anti-tumor T cell activity, enabling immune-mediated killing of
tumor cells. The S228P Fc mutation prevents Fab-arm exchange, ensuring
homogeneous antibody composition in vivo. Unlike IgG1 checkpoint
inhibitors, the IgG4 isotype avoids complement activation and minimizes
ADCC, reducing infusion-related reactions and off-target immune
activation.

**SECTION 2: DISEASE AND CURRENT TREATMENT**

**2.1 Disease Overview**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) is the third most common cancer and second
leading cause of cancer death in the United States. The dMMR/MSI-H
phenotype is identified in approximately 4--5% of metastatic CRC cases,
corresponding to \~4,500--5,000 US patients annually. This subtype is
characterized by deficient DNA mismatch repair leading to hypermutation
(tumor mutational burden typically 40--50 mut/Mb), dense immune
infiltration, and striking sensitivity to PD-1 checkpoint blockade---in
contrast to the intrinsic chemotherapy resistance observed in this
molecular subtype.

**5.2 Patient-Centered Value**

Structured patient interviews and focus groups (n=42 patients, n=18
caregivers) conducted in partnership with Colorectal Cancer Alliance and
Fight Colorectal Cancer identified the following patient-centered value
drivers: (1) time with family and ability to maintain daily activities;
(2) avoiding alopecia, nausea, and peripheral neuropathy; (3)
convenience of less-frequent clinic visits (Q6W vs. Q2W); (4) hope for
durable disease control (\'staying stable\'); and (5) transparency about
immune-related risks. Cervimab directly addresses items 1--4, which were
ranked as highest priority by patients. The incidence of immune-related
AEs (irAEs) was acknowledged but patients expressed acceptance of this
risk profile when clearly communicated by their oncologist.

**8.2 Testing Algorithm Recommendation**

Universal MMR/MSI testing at diagnosis of all CRC tumors is recommended
per NCCN Guidelines (Version 2.2025) and ASCO/CAP/AMP/ASC joint
consensus guidelines. Reflex testing from initial pathology block is the
preferred approach to minimize turnaround time and ensure timely
treatment planning. For patients who were not tested at initial
diagnosis, repeat testing from archived tumor tissue or fresh biopsy is
recommended at progression. ctDNA-based MSI testing may be considered in
cases where adequate tumor tissue is unavailable, though standard
tissue-based testing remains the regulatory-supported standard.

**SECTION 10: PRODUCT DIFFERENTIATION**

**10.1 Cervimab vs. Pembrolizumab**

Pembrolizumab (Keytruda, Merck) is approved for first-line dMMR/MSI-H
mCRC; it is NOT approved for ≥2nd line treatment. Cervimab is the first
anti-PD-1 agent with Phase 3 RCT data in the ≥2nd line setting. In the
absence of head-to-head data, the NMA suggests comparable efficacy in
patients with no prior checkpoint inhibitor exposure. Key
differentiating features of cervimab include: (1) convenient Q6W dosing
vs. pembrolizumab Q3W or Q6W (label does not permit Q6W in CRC); (2)
IgG4 isotype with S228P stabilization ensuring structural homogeneity;
(3) evidence in chemotherapy-pretreated population (no pembrolizumab
Phase 3 data in this setting).

**10.2 Cervimab vs. Nivolumab**

Nivolumab (Opdivo, BMS) has single-arm Phase 2 data (CheckMate 142)
supporting off-label use in dMMR mCRC but no Phase 3 RCT in this
setting. Cervimab is the first agent with a randomized controlled Phase
3 trial with a chemotherapy active comparator in this indication,
providing the highest-quality evidence for clinical decision-making and
formulary review. The CERVI-301 trial design and endpoint selection
align with FDA guidance for this indication.

**10.3 Q6W Dosing Schedule**

The 400 mg Q6W dosing schedule was selected based on pharmacokinetic
modeling demonstrating \>95% PD-1 receptor occupancy throughout the
6-week interval. This schedule offers important practical advantages:
(1) fewer clinic visits per year (\~8.7 vs. \~26 for chemotherapy and
\~17 for Q3W regimens); (2) reduced patient travel burden and time away
from work/family; (3) reduced infusion suite utilization and scheduling
burden for healthcare providers; (4) alignment with common chemotherapy
combination cycles, facilitating future combination studies.

**11.1 Recommended Place in Therapy**

Based on the totality of evidence, cervimab is positioned as the
preferred second-line treatment option for all adult patients with
confirmed dMMR/MSI-H mCRC who have received at least one prior systemic

improved tolerability vs. chemotherapy; and a manageable irAE profile
with established management guidelines.
