**TABLE OF CONTENTS**

**1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

Cervimab is an investigational fully human anti-PD-1 monoclonal antibody
(IgG4 isotype) developed by Cervi Therapeutics for the treatment of
adult patients with previously treated, mismatch repair deficient (dMMR)
or microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H) metastatic colorectal cancer
(mCRC). This Health Technology Assessment (HTA) dossier presents the
clinical, humanistic, and economic evidence package supporting the value
of cervimab relative to standard chemotherapy regimens.

**1.1 Disease and Unmet Need**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) is the third most commonly diagnosed cancer and
second leading cause of cancer-related mortality in the United States,
with approximately 153,020 new cases and 52,550 deaths estimated in 2023
(Siegel et al., 2023). The dMMR/MSI-H molecular subtype accounts for
approximately 4--5% of all metastatic CRC cases (\~4,000--5,000 patients
annually in the U.S.). These patients have historically derived limited
benefit from standard chemotherapy, with median overall survival rarely
exceeding 12--15 months in second-line settings. The discovery that
dMMR/MSI-H tumors harbor high mutational burden, rendering them
particularly susceptible to checkpoint inhibition, has transformed
treatment paradigms. Despite the approval of pembrolizumab in the
first-line setting, there remains a significant unmet need for patients
with disease progression following initial therapy, and no PD-1
inhibitor has demonstrated superiority over pembrolizumab in a
head-to-head trial.

**1.2 Cervimab: Mechanism and Development**

Cervimab is a fully human IgG4 anti-PD-1 monoclonal antibody engineered
with an S228P Fc stabilization mutation to minimize Fab-arm exchange.
Cervimab binds PD-1 with sub-nanomolar affinity (KD = 0.42 nM) and
demonstrates complete blockade of PD-L1 and PD-L2 interactions at
clinically relevant concentrations. The drug is administered
intravenously at 400 mg Q6W, enabling a convenient 6-weekly infusion
schedule aligned with outpatient oncology workflows. Phase 1/2
dose-finding studies established a favorable safety and pharmacokinetic
profile, with dose-proportional exposure across the 1--10 mg/kg dose
range and no dose-limiting toxicities observed at the recommended Phase
2 dose.

**1.3 Pivotal Trial Summary (CERVI-301)**

The CERVI-301 Phase 3 trial is a multicenter, international, open-label,
randomized controlled trial enrolling adult patients with confirmed
dMMR/MSI-H mCRC who progressed on or after first-line therapy. A total

investigator\'s choice of standard chemotherapy (FOLFOX ± bevacizumab or
FOLFIRI ± bevacizumab). The primary endpoint was progression-free
survival (PFS) per RECIST v1.1 by blinded independent central review
(BICR). Key secondary endpoints included overall survival (OS),
objective response rate (ORR), duration of response (DoR), and
patient-reported outcomes (PROs).

**2. DISEASE BACKGROUND AND EPIDEMIOLOGY**

**2.1 Epidemiology of Colorectal Cancer**

Colorectal cancer represents a major global health burden. In the United
States, the American Cancer Society estimates 106,590 new colon cancer
cases and 46,430 new rectal cancer cases for 2023, with 52,550 deaths.
Despite improvements in early detection via colonoscopy screening
programs, approximately 20--25% of patients present with stage IV
(metastatic) disease at initial diagnosis, and an additional 20--30% of
patients with localized disease will eventually develop metastatic
recurrence.

**2.2 The dMMR/MSI-H Subtype**

Mismatch repair (MMR) is a critical DNA repair pathway responsible for
correcting single-nucleotide mismatches and small insertion-deletion
loops arising during DNA replication. Deficiency in MMR (dMMR), caused
by loss of MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, or PMS2 protein expression or pathogenic
germline mutations (Lynch syndrome), results in failure to repair these
errors. The consequent accumulation of somatic mutations---particularly
at short tandem repeat loci---defines microsatellite instability-high
(MSI-H) tumors.

In mCRC, approximately 4--5% of cases harbor the dMMR/MSI-H phenotype.
This subtype is associated with a distinctive biology: high tumor
mutational burden (median \~40 mut/Mb vs. \~5 mut/Mb in MSS tumors),
dense CD8+ tumor-infiltrating lymphocyte infiltrate, high expression of
PD-L1 and other immune checkpoints, and intrinsic resistance to
fluorouracil-based chemotherapy. These features collectively render
dMMR/MSI-H tumors highly immunogenic and ideally suited for checkpoint
blockade.

The transition from first-line pembrolizumab to second-line treatment
represents the critical unmet need that cervimab addresses. Patients
progressing on pembrolizumab face a landscape with no approved
immunotherapy and chemotherapy regimens offering limited survival
benefit. Furthermore, patients who received first-line chemotherapy due
to ineligibility for immunotherapy (e.g., due to autoimmune
contraindications or early treatment decisions) represent an additional
population with high unmet need who may benefit from cervimab.

**3. CLINICAL EVIDENCE SUMMARY**

**3.1 Phase 1/2 Dose-Finding Study (CERVI-101)**

The CERVI-101 open-label, multicenter, dose-escalation study enrolled 87
patients with advanced solid tumors including dMMR/MSI-H CRC across dose
levels of 1, 3, 6, and 10 mg/kg Q3W and 400 mg Q6W. No dose-limiting
toxicities were observed through the highest dose level. The RP2D of 400
mg Q6W was selected based on pharmacokinetic modeling demonstrating
\>95% PD-1 receptor occupancy throughout the dosing interval, favorable
exposure-response relationships, and patient convenience.

**3.2.2 Patient Population**

At randomization, baseline characteristics were well balanced between
arms. Median age was 61 years (range 22--83); 54% male; 71% ECOG PS
0--1; 58% received prior bevacizumab; 22% had liver metastases only; 31%
had ≥3 metastatic sites. Lynch syndrome was confirmed in 34% of
patients. MMR/MSI status was confirmed by local and central laboratory
testing with \>99% concordance.

**3.3 Patient-Reported Outcomes**

PRO data from CERVI-301 demonstrate that cervimab preserves or improves
health-related quality of life (HRQoL) while chemotherapy is associated
with meaningful deterioration across multiple domains. The EORTC QLQ-C30
Global Health Status/QoL scale showed a mean change from baseline of
+8.4 points in the cervimab arm versus -4.1 points in the chemotherapy
arm at week 12 (clinically meaningful threshold: 10 points;
statistically significant difference of 12.5 points, p=0.0003). Time to
definitive deterioration in GHS/QoL (≥10-point decline) was
significantly prolonged with cervimab (NR vs. 5.9 months; HR 0.41,
p\<0.0001). The EQ-5D-5L utility value at week 12 was 0.74 for cervimab
versus 0.64 for chemotherapy (difference +0.10), consistent with the
cost-effectiveness model inputs detailed in the HEOR dossier.

**5. HUMANISTIC BURDEN AND PATIENT PERSPECTIVE**

**5.1 Impact of dMMR/MSI-H mCRC on Patients**

Patients with dMMR/MSI-H mCRC experience a substantial humanistic
burden. The diagnosis of metastatic cancer is profoundly distressing,
with significant anxiety, depression, and existential concerns reported.
Functional impairment from disease symptoms (abdominal pain, diarrhea,
fatigue, cachexia) and from the adverse effects of chemotherapy (nausea,
vomiting, peripheral neuropathy, alopecia, cytopenias) compound the
psychological burden.

A prospective substudy within CERVI-301 assessed caregiver burden using
the Zarit Burden Interview (ZBI) and demonstrated significantly lower
caregiver burden in the cervimab arm (mean ZBI score 18.4 vs. 27.6 at
week 24, p=0.002), consistent with the reduced toxicity profile and
preserved patient function.

**5.2 Patient Advocacy Input**

Structured qualitative interviews and focus groups with 42 patients with
dMMR/MSI-H mCRC and 18 caregivers were conducted by the sponsor in
partnership with the Colorectal Cancer Alliance (CCA) and Fight
Colorectal Cancer (FCC) to inform the evidence development program. Key
themes identified included: (1) overwhelming preference for oral or
less-frequent IV administration; (2) strong aversion to alopecia,
nausea, and peripheral neuropathy; (3) prioritization of time with
family and functional independence over maximal tumor response; and (4)
willingness to accept immune-related risks if clearly explained. These
patient-centered insights informed the Q6W dosing schedule design and
the PRO battery selection for CERVI-301.

Based on projected uptake scenarios across a 5-year period in a
hypothetical US health plan of 1 million lives, the estimated net annual
budget impact of cervimab introduction ranges from \$0.03 PMPM in Year 1
to \$0.14 PMPM by Year 5, assuming 40% market penetration and 25%
rebate. The eligible patient population is small (approximately 2.3
eligible patients per million plan members annually), limiting the
aggregate budget impact while providing substantial value per treated
patient.

**7. PROPOSED PLACE IN THERAPY**

**7.1 Target Population**

The proposed indication for cervimab is: adult patients with dMMR/MSI-H
metastatic colorectal cancer who have received at least one prior line
of systemic therapy for metastatic disease. This population aligns
precisely with the CERVI-301 enrollment criteria and represents the
highest-value target based on the mechanistic rationale and clinical
trial data.

**7.3 Testing and Biomarker Requirements**

Universal MMR/MSI testing by IHC and/or PCR/NGS is recommended for all
patients with newly diagnosed CRC. NCCN guidelines and ASCO/CAP/AMP/ASC
recommendations support reflex testing at diagnosis. Cervimab therapy
should be reserved for patients with confirmed dMMR or MSI-H status.
Both IHC and molecular testing are acceptable per CERVI-301 eligibility
criteria; central laboratory concordance in the trial was \>99%.

**10. CONCLUSIONS**

Cervimab represents a clinically meaningful treatment option for
patients with previously treated dMMR/MSI-H metastatic colorectal
cancer, a population with high unmet need and historically poor outcomes
on chemotherapy. The Phase 3 CERVI-301 trial demonstrated statistically
significant and clinically meaningful improvements across all
pre-specified endpoints including PFS, OS, ORR, and patient-reported
quality of life, with a markedly improved tolerability profile versus
standard chemotherapy.

The health economic analyses support a favorable value profile at a WAC
of \$18,500 per infusion, with a base-case ICER of approximately
\$142,300/QALY---within the range commonly considered cost-effective by
US payers and HTA bodies for oncology indications with significant unmet
need. The budget impact to health plans is modest given the small but
molecularly defined eligible population.

Cervi Therapeutics is committed to ensuring broad, timely access to
cervimab for eligible patients through value-based contracting
arrangements, outcomes-based agreements, and comprehensive patient
assistance programs. The sponsor welcomes the opportunity to discuss the
evidence package in detail with ICER, payers, and other HTA
stakeholders.
