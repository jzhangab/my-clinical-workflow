**1. Introduction**

This report documents the comparative characterization of the top 8
anti-PD-1 monoclonal antibody candidates nominated from the HTS campaign
(MOS-HTS-2020-001). Each candidate was produced as a full-length human
IgG4 antibody (with S228P Fc stabilization) in CHO-S cells, purified to
\>98% monomer by Protein A chromatography followed by size-exclusion
polishing, and characterized across a battery of biophysical,
biochemical, and functional assays.

Candidate MOS-PD1-047 (Cervimab) was selected as the single development
candidate based on superior overall profile across all categories. This
report provides the data package supporting that selection and the
downstream IND regulatory filing.

**5. Developability Assessment**

**5.1 Polyspecificity and Self-Association**

The baculoviral particle (BVP) polyspecificity reagent (PSR) assay was
used to assess non-specific binding tendency. MOS-PD1-047 (Cervimab)
scored 0.08 (PSR score; threshold for advancement: \<0.25), indicating
low polyspecificity and excellent developability. All 8 candidates
scored below the threshold; MOS-PD1-047 scored the lowest.

Self-association by AC-SINS (affinity-capture self-interaction
nanoparticle spectroscopy): MOS-PD1-047 showed 0.4 nm red-shift
(acceptably low; threshold \<2.0 nm), confirming no significant
self-aggregation tendency at therapeutic concentrations.

**7. Regulatory Basis for Lead Selection**

The lead selection framework and characterization data presented in this
report support the following regulatory positions:

- Species selection for toxicology studies: Cynomolgus monkey is the
  pharmacologically relevant species per ICH S6(R1), based on
  demonstrated binding cross-reactivity (KD cyno/human ratio 1.45) and
  confirmed functional activity in cyno PBMC MLR assay.

- Starting dose for FIH studies: Binding affinity and receptor occupancy
  data support MABEL-based starting dose calculation (reference:
  MOS-NOAEL-2022-001 toxicology report).

- Molecular characterization: Sequence, molecular weight, pI,
  glycosylation profile, and biophysical properties meet FDA/ICH Q6B
  specifications for biological characterization.

- Developability: The favorable stability, yield, and biophysical
  profile support commercial manufacturing feasibility per ICH Q8(R2)
  pharmaceutical development principles.
