**1. INTRODUCTION AND REGULATORY BASIS**

This Stability Studies Report (SSR) documents all stability studies
conducted on Cervimab drug substance (DS) and drug product (DP) in
accordance with the following regulatory guidance documents:

- ICH Q5C: Stability Testing of Biotechnological/Biological Products

- ICH Q1A(R2): Stability Testing of New Drug Substances and Products

- ICH Q1B: Photostability Testing of New Drug Substances and Products

- ICH Q1D: Bracketing and Matrixing Designs for Stability Testing

- ICH Q6B: Specifications: Test Procedures and Acceptance Criteria for
  Biotechnological/Biological Products

- FDA 21 CFR Part 211.137, 314.50, and 600.12 (stability requirements)

- FDA Guidance for Industry: Stability Testing of Drug Substances and
  Drug Products (Draft, 1998)

The stability program was designed to: (1) support the proposed shelf
life and storage conditions for IND-phase clinical use; (2) characterize
the degradation profile and identify stability-indicating quality
attributes; and (3) provide annual stability update data per IND
commitments. The program encompasses real-time/long-term, intermediate,
and accelerated conditions for both DS and DP, as well as photostability
(DP) and in-use studies.

(≤0.2% change), charge variants (≤0.5% main peak change), potency
(within 95--108%), or sub-visible particles (MFI counts within assay
variability) were observed through 10 cycles. The drug substance
demonstrates robust freeze-thaw stability consistent with the expected
manufacturing hold and shipping process.

**4.2 Arrhenius Modeling (DS --- Long-Term vs. Accelerated)**

Apparent Arrhenius activation energy (Ea) for the rate-limiting
aggregation reaction was estimated at \~80 kJ/mol based on the
temperature-dependent SEC-HPLC monomer degradation rates observed at
2--8°C, 25°C, and 40°C storage conditions. This Ea value is within the
typical range for antibody aggregation (60--100 kJ/mol) and predicts a
Q10 factor of approximately 3.2, meaning reaction rates triple for every
10°C temperature increase. This confirms the critical importance of
maintaining cold chain integrity throughout the supply chain.

**5. STABILITY COMMITMENT AND ONGOING PROGRAM**

**5.1 IND Stability Commitment**

The following stability commitments are provided to the FDA as part of
the IND application, in accordance with 21 CFR 312.23(a)(7)(i) and FDA
guidance on stability testing of IND materials:

- Real-time stability studies at 2--8°C (DP) and -80°C (DS) will
  continue through 36 months for the three initial GMP lots, with annual
  stability update reports submitted as IND amendments

- Stability testing of each new drug substance lot will be initiated at
  time of manufacture, including long-term (-80°C) and intermediate
  (2--8°C) storage conditions

- Each new drug product manufacturing lot released for clinical use will
  be placed on the 2--8°C real-time stability program from the date of
  fill-finish

- Any out-of-specification stability result will be reported to the FDA
  within 45 days of confirmation per 21 CFR 312.32 (IND safety reports)

- Shelf life extensions beyond the current 24-month proposed expiry will
  be requested via IND amendment supported by additional stability data
  at 18-, 24-, and 30-month timepoints

**5.2 Cold Chain and Shipping Qualification**

Temperature-controlled shipping qualification studies were conducted to
support the clinical supply cold chain. Cervimab drug product (2--8°C
labeled storage) was subjected to simulated shipping conditions using an
ISTA 7E-qualified temperature monitoring study (frozen gel packs,
48-hour shipping profile, summer/winter extremes). Drug product remained
within the 2--8°C range during simulated shipments, with no excursions
above 15°C or below 0°C detected. Post-shipment stability samples at 0,
1, and 3 months post-shipping showed no changes relative to pre-shipping
samples across the analytical panel.

**5.3 Mean Kinetic Temperature Excursion Policy**

A mean kinetic temperature (MKT) excursion policy has been established
for clinical trial sites. Single temperature excursions of ≤ 30°C for ≤
12 hours or ≤ 25°C for ≤ 24 hours are covered by available in-use
stability data and do not require drug product discard. Excursions
outside these ranges require notification to the Sponsor and pharmacist
assessment based on data-driven bracketing studies. All excursions must
be documented in temperature monitoring logs maintained at the clinical
site.
