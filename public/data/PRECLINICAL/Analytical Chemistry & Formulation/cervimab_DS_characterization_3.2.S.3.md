**1. INTRODUCTION AND PURPOSE**

This Drug Substance Characterization Report (DSCR) for Cervimab has been
prepared in accordance with ICH Q6B (Specifications: Test Procedures and
Acceptance Criteria for Biotechnological/Biological Products) and ICH
Q5E (Comparability of Biotechnological/Biological Products Subject to
Changes in Their Manufacturing Process). The report fulfills the
regulatory requirements for Module 3.2.S.3 of the Common Technical
Document (CTD) submitted to the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA)
as part of the Investigational New Drug (IND) application for Cervimab.

Cervimab is a recombinant humanized anti-PD-1 IgG4 monoclonal antibody
intended for the treatment of adult patients with mismatch
repair-deficient (dMMR) or microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H)
metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC). Cervimab targets the Programmed
Death-1 (PD-1) receptor on activated T lymphocytes, blocking its
interaction with PD-L1 and PD-L2 ligands, thereby restoring anti-tumor
immune activity.

The characterization studies described herein were conducted using
Cervimab drug substance manufactured under Good Manufacturing Practice
(GMP) conditions at Example BioPharma Manufacturing, Inc. (Facility ID:
\[XXXXX\]) using a Chinese Hamster Ovary (CHO) cell expression system.
Studies utilized a state-of-the-art panel of physicochemical,
biochemical, and biological assays to elucidate the primary,
higher-order, and functional structure of the molecule.

**1.1 Scope of Characterization**

Comprehensive characterization was performed on three representative GMP
lots of Cervimab drug substance to establish the following:

- Primary structure: amino acid sequence, disulfide bond linkage, and
  glycosylation sites

- Higher-order structure: secondary, tertiary, and quaternary structure
  confirmation

- Post-translational modifications (PTMs): N- and O-linked glycosylation
  profiles, deamidation, oxidation, C-terminal lysine clipping,
  N-terminal pyroglutamate formation

- Charge heterogeneity: isoelectric point distribution and acidic/basic
  species profiling

- Size heterogeneity: aggregation, fragmentation, and molecular size
  confirmation

- Biological activity: PD-1 binding affinity (SPR), FcRn binding, FcγR
  binding, complement activation

- Extinction coefficient determination

**2. DRUG SUBSTANCE DESCRIPTION AND GENERAL PROPERTIES**

**2.1 Molecule Description**

Cervimab is a recombinant humanized IgG4 subclass monoclonal antibody
directed against the human Programmed Death-1 (PD-1; CD279) receptor.
The molecule consists of two identical heavy chains (HC) and two
identical light chains (LC) connected by interchain disulfide bonds. A
critical S228P substitution in the hinge region of the IgG4 Fc domain is
incorporated to prevent Fab-arm exchange (half-antibody formation),
conferring molecular stability consistent with other approved IgG4
therapeutic antibodies.

**3.1.2 Analytical Ultracentrifugation --- Sedimentation Velocity
(AUC-SV)**

AUC-SV was performed at 40,000 rpm (An-50 Ti rotor, Beckman Optima AUC)
at 20°C in PBS pH 7.4. Sedimentation coefficient distributions were
calculated using the continuous c(s) model in SEDFIT. Results confirmed
the predominant monomer species at S20,w ≈ 6.4 S with no significant
ultracentrifugation-resolved aggregates observed above 0.5% threshold.

**3.1.3 Dynamic Light Scattering (DLS)**

DLS measurements (Zetasizer Ultra, Malvern Panalytical) in 20 mM
histidine, 140 mM NaCl, 0.04% PS80, pH 5.8 (drug substance formulation
buffer) yielded a Z-average hydrodynamic diameter of 11.2 ± 0.4 nm with
polydispersity index (PDI) ≤ 0.08, consistent with a monodisperse
monoclonal antibody preparation.

**3.2 Charge Heterogeneity**

**3.2.1 Imaged Capillary Isoelectric Focusing (icIEF)**

icIEF analysis was performed using the iCE3 system (ProteinSimple) with
Pharmalyte 3-10 carrier ampholytes. Two pI markers (pI 7.00 and pI 9.22)
were employed for calibration. Cervimab exhibits a major isoform cluster
at pI 8.3--8.5, with acidic variants (pI \< 8.2) attributable primarily
to deamidation and sialylation, and basic variants (pI \> 8.6)
attributable to C-terminal lysine retention and N-terminal cyclization

**3.2.2 Cation Exchange Chromatography (CEX-HPLC)**

CEX was performed on a ProPac WCX-10 column (Thermo Fisher Scientific)
using a linear gradient of 25 mM MES to 200 mM NaCl, pH 5.6. Charge
variant profiles were consistent with icIEF results, with the main peak
eluting at approximately 12.5 minutes under the established gradient
conditions. Method provides orthogonal confirmation of icIEF charge
heterogeneity data.

**3.3 Higher-Order Structure**

**3.3.1 Secondary Structure --- Far-UV Circular Dichroism (CD)**

Far-UV CD spectra (190--260 nm) were collected at 0.5 mg/mL in 10 mM
phosphate buffer pH 7.4 using a Jasco J-1500 spectrometer with 1 mm path
length cuvette. Deconvolution using CDSSTR algorithm against the SP175
reference dataset yielded secondary structure composition consistent
with a predominantly β-sheet protein:

- β-sheet: 41.2 ± 2.1%

- β-turn: 22.8 ± 1.4%

- Random coil: 26.5 ± 1.9%

- α-helix: 9.5 ± 1.2%

Results are consistent with expected IgG4 secondary structure
composition reported in the literature and are reproducible across all
three characterized lots (overlay RMS deviation \< 5%).

**3.3.2 Tertiary Structure --- Intrinsic Fluorescence and Near-UV CD**

Intrinsic fluorescence emission spectra (excitation 280 nm; emission
300--400 nm) demonstrate a λmax at 336 nm at 0.1 mg/mL in PBS pH 7.4,
consistent with partially buried tryptophan residues in the native
folded IgG4 framework. Near-UV CD spectra (250--320 nm) confirm a rigid,
folded tertiary environment around aromatic residues (Trp, Tyr, Phe)
characteristic of correctly folded IgG structure.

**3.3.3 Hydrogen-Deuterium Exchange Mass Spectrometry (HDX-MS)**

HDX-MS analysis was performed using an Acquity UPLC M-Class system
coupled to a SYNAPT G2-Si mass spectrometer (Waters). HDX labeling was
conducted at 25°C in deuterated PBS (pD 7.4) for 0.5, 5, 30, and 120
minutes. Differential HDX maps across the variable regions (VH/VL),
hinge, and Fc domains are consistent with the expected accessibility
profiles for a properly folded IgG4. No anomalous deuterium uptake
indicative of conformational instability or unexpected domain exposure
was detected.

**3.3.4 Thermal Stability --- Differential Scanning Fluorimetry (DSF)
and DSC**

Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC) was performed at 1 mg/mL in
formulation buffer using a MicroCal VP-Capillary DSC (Malvern). Two
well-resolved thermal unfolding transitions were observed:

- Tm1: 71.4 ± 0.3°C (attributed to CH2 domain unfolding)

- Tm2: 84.6 ± 0.4°C (attributed to Fab/CH3 domain unfolding)

These melting temperatures indicate robust conformational stability of
the Cervimab molecule under formulation conditions and are consistent
with values reported for IgG4 antibodies bearing the S228P hinge
stabilization mutation.

**4.3 Disulfide Bond Analysis**

Disulfide bond mapping was performed by non-reduced peptide mapping
under acidic quench conditions. Twelve expected disulfide bonds were
confirmed with ≥98% pairing fidelity across all characterized lots. No
free thiol species above 0.2 mol/mol antibody were detected by Ellman\'s
assay (5,5\'-dithiobis-(2-nitrobenzoic acid)), consistent with complete
and correct disulfide bond formation.

**5.2 Ligand Blocking Activity**

A cell-free ELISA-based competition assay was developed to assess
Cervimab\'s ability to block PD-1/PD-L1 and PD-1/PD-L2 interactions.
PD-1-ECD was coated at 1 µg/mL; biotinylated PD-L1-ECD or PD-L2-ECD was
added as competitor ligand after Cervimab pre-incubation (0.001 -- 100
nM). IC50 values were calculated by 4-parameter logistic regression.

- PD-1/PD-L1 blockade IC50: 0.48 ± 0.06 nM (Cervimab)

- PD-1/PD-L2 blockade IC50: 0.71 ± 0.09 nM (Cervimab)

These values confirm dual blockade of both PD-1 ligands at sub-nanomolar
concentrations.

**5.3 Cell-Based Functional Activity**

A Jurkat T cell NFAT-luciferase reporter assay co-cultured with CHO
cells expressing PD-L1 (Promega PD-1/PD-L1 Blockade Bioassay Kit) was
used to assess functional restoration of T cell activation. Cervimab
dose-dependently restored NFAT-driven luciferase activity with an EC50
of 0.92 ± 0.14 nM and a maximum fold-induction of 5.8 ± 0.4 relative to
no-antibody control.

**5.4 FcRn and FcγR Binding**

FcRn binding at pH 6.0 (endosomal) was confirmed by SPR (KD = 0.42 ±
0.05 µM at pH 6.0; no binding at pH 7.4), consistent with expected IgG4
neonatal Fc receptor-mediated recycling behavior and predicting a normal
IgG4 half-life in vivo (\~21 days).

FcγR binding panel (FcγRI, FcγRIIa, FcγRIIb, FcγRIIIa-F158,
FcγRIIIa-V158) was assessed by SPR at pH 7.4. Cervimab IgG4 demonstrates
the expected low FcγR binding relative to IgG1, with detectable FcγRI
binding only (KD \~32 nM), consistent with the reduced effector function
profile of IgG4 subclass antibodies. Complement C1q binding by ELISA was
below the assay limit of detection, confirming negligible ADCC/CDC
activity.

**5.5 Cross-Reactivity Panel**

Cervimab binding specificity was confirmed by ELISA against a panel of
32 human tissue antigens and 8 structurally related immune checkpoint
receptors (LAG-3, TIM-3, CTLA-4, CD28, BTLA, VISTA, TIGIT, ICOS). No
off-target binding above 10% of PD-1 signal was observed for any panel
member, confirming high target specificity.

**8. CONCLUSIONS**

Comprehensive physicochemical, biochemical, and biological
characterization of Cervimab drug substance confirms that the molecule
is structurally well-defined, highly pure, and functionally active as an
anti-PD-1 IgG4 monoclonal antibody. The following key conclusions are
drawn from characterization studies conducted across three
representative GMP drug substance lots:

- Primary structure was confirmed with ≥99.5% sequence coverage by
  peptide mapping LC-MS/MS; all 12 disulfide bonds were correctly formed
  and confirmed

- Cervimab is predominantly monomeric (≥98.5%) with negligible
  aggregation and fragmentation

- Glycosylation profile is consistent and reproducible across
  manufactured lots, with a typical IgG4 CHO-derived complex biantennary
  glycan distribution

- Post-translational modifications (deamidation, oxidation, C-terminal
  Lys clipping, N-terminal pyroglutamate) are within acceptable ranges
  and consistent with expected IgG4 modifications

- Cervimab binds human PD-1 with high affinity (KD ≈ 0.33 nM) and blocks
  both PD-L1 and PD-L2 ligand interactions at sub-nanomolar
  concentrations

- Cell-based functional assay confirms potent restoration of T cell
  activation via PD-1/PD-L1 checkpoint blockade (EC50 ≈ 0.92 nM)

- Process-related impurities (HCP, hcDNA, Protein A, Benzonase,
  endotoxin) are well below established safety-based limits

- Lot-to-lot consistency across three characterized lots supports robust
  and reproducible manufacturing

These characterization data provide the foundation for
specification-setting activities (Module 3.2.S.4) and support the
clinical safety and efficacy profile of Cervimab as described in the IND
submission.
