**1. INTRODUCTION AND BACKGROUND**

This Formulation Development Report (FDR) describes the pharmaceutical
development activities conducted to support the clinical formulation of
Cervimab, a recombinant humanized anti-PD-1 IgG4 monoclonal antibody, in
the drug product presentation of 25 mg/mL sterile concentrate solution
for intravenous (IV) infusion, supplied in Type I borosilicate glass
vials with bromobutyl rubber stoppers. This document is prepared in
accordance with ICH Q8(R2) (Pharmaceutical Development), ICH Q9 (Quality
Risk Management), and ICH Q10 (Pharmaceutical Quality System) to fulfill
CTD Module 3.2.P.2 requirements of the IND application.

Cervimab is indicated for the treatment of adult patients with
dMMR/MSI-H metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC) as a monotherapy. The
proposed clinical dose range for Phase I/II is 1--10 mg/kg IV
administered every 3 weeks (Q3W), with the recommended Phase II dose to
be determined from the dose-escalation study.

The formulation development strategy was guided by the following
objectives:

- Maximize protein conformational and colloidal stability over the
  intended shelf life

- Prevent aggregation, fragmentation, deamidation, and oxidative
  degradation

- Achieve compatibility with IV infusion bags (0.9% NaCl, D5W) and
  administration sets

- Support clinical-scale manufacturing via aseptic filtration and
  fill-finish

- Minimize patient risk by selecting excipients with well-established
  safety profiles

**1.1 Summary of Formulation Development History**

Formulation development proceeded in three sequential phases: (1)
preformulation biophysical characterization of the drug substance to map
instability risk; (2) Design of Experiment (DoE)-based screening of
buffer, pH, and stabilizer space; and (3) confirmatory stability
evaluation of the selected formulation under accelerated and long-term
conditions. The resulting Phase I formulation is summarized in Table 1
of Section 2.

**2.2 Excipient Rationale**

**2.2.1 Histidine Buffer System (pH 5.8)**

Histidine was selected as the primary buffer based on preformulation
screening data demonstrating superior protein conformational stability
(lower Tagg by DLS, higher Tm1 by DSF) at pH 5.5--6.0 compared to
acetate, citrate, and phosphate buffer systems evaluated at equivalent
ionic strength (5 mM -- 25 mM). Histidine additionally provides
antioxidant activity that may protect methionine residues (Met252,
Met428) from oxidation during the protein\'s shelf life. The
dual-histidine/histidine-HCl system provides a well-buffered pH 5.8
formulation using a combination of free base and hydrochloride salt to
achieve target pH without the addition of mineral acids.

**2.2.2 Sucrose (8.5% w/v)**

Sucrose was selected as the primary stabilizer and isotonicity agent.
Its inclusion at 8.5% (w/v) provides: (1) preferential exclusion from
the protein surface (Timasheff mechanism), contributing to
conformational stabilization; (2) sufficient tonicity (\~340--360
mOsm/kg) compatible with IV administration without additional tonicity
adjusters; and (3) cryoprotection if the protein concentration or
long-term stability data later necessitate a lyophilized backup
formulation. Trehalose was evaluated as an alternative but offered no
statistically significant stability advantage in 6-month accelerated
stress studies and presents greater viscosity at higher concentrations.

**2.2.3 Polysorbate 80 (0.04% w/v)**

PS80 is included at 0.04% (w/v) to prevent interfacial stress-induced
aggregation during formulation, filling, shipping, and IV
administration. The surfactant concentration was optimized to provide
maximum agitation protection (sub-visible particle formation on
stirring/shaking) without excess PS80 that could lead to
peroxide-mediated methionine or tryptophan oxidation on storage. The
peroxide specification for the PS80 excipient lot is set at ≤ 0.03 mEq/g
to mitigate oxidation risk.

**3. PREFORMULATION CHARACTERIZATION**

**3.1 pH Stability Screen**

Cervimab was buffer-exchanged into nine buffer conditions (pH 4.5, 5.0,
5.5, 5.8, 6.0, 6.5, 7.0, 7.4) at 5 mM buffer concentration and 5 mg/mL
protein concentration using Amicon Ultra centrifugal concentrators.
Samples were incubated at 40°C for 4 weeks. Stability was assessed by
SEC-HPLC (% monomer), icIEF (charge variants), and turbidity (OD400).

  -------- --------------- ----------------- -------------- -------------
   **pH**    **Buffer**       **% Monomer      **ΔAcidic     **Turbidity
                            (4-wk, 40°C)**   Species (%)**    ΔOD400**

    4.5        Acetate           87.2             +8.4          0.142

    5.0        Acetate           94.1             +5.1          0.062

    5.5       Histidine          97.8             +2.6          0.018

    5.8       Histidine          98.3             +2.1          0.012

    6.0       Histidine          98.1             +2.3          0.014

    6.5        Citrate           97.6             +3.8          0.021

    7.0       Phosphate          94.9             +5.6          0.055

    7.4       Phosphate          91.8             +7.2          0.094
  -------- --------------- ----------------- -------------- -------------

*pH 5.5--6.0 in histidine buffer demonstrated the optimal stability
window. pH 5.8 was selected as the formulation target, providing a
safety margin from both the acidic-instability region and the higher-pH
aggregation/deamidation-prone region.*

**3.2 Buffer Type and Concentration Screen**

At the selected pH 5.8, five buffer systems were compared: histidine
(10, 20, 35 mM), acetate (10, 20, 35 mM), citrate (5, 10, 20 mM),
succinate (5, 10, 20 mM), and phosphate (10, 20, 35 mM) using 4-week
40°C accelerated stress. Histidine at 20--35 mM provided the best
combination of monomer retention, turbidity suppression, and deamidation
inhibition. A concentration of 35 mM (combining histidine free base +
HCl salt) was selected for buffer capacity at the target pH 5.8.

**3.3 Stabilizer Screening (DoE)**

A 2-factor, 3-level central composite face-centered Design of Experiment
was executed evaluating sucrose (0 -- 10% w/v), trehalose (0 -- 10%
w/v), NaCl (0 -- 150 mM), arginine-HCl (0 -- 100 mM), and combinations
thereof at 4-week 40°C stress. Responses included: % monomer (SEC), %
main peak (icIEF), Tm1/Tm2 by nanoDSF (Prometheus NT.48, NanoTemper),
Tagg by DLS (Zetasizer), and viscosity at 25 mg/mL.

Key DoE findings:

- Sucrose ≥ 6% w/v provided significant stabilization vs. NaCl; 8.5%
  sucrose was identified as the optimal concentration balancing
  stability, viscosity (\<5 cP at 25°C), and osmolality

- NaCl ≥ 75 mM decreased colloidal stability (lower kD by DLS) without
  conferring conformational benefit

- Arginine-HCl at 25--50 mM provided marginal additional benefit over
  sucrose alone and was dropped to minimize formulation complexity

- Trehalose and sucrose were statistically equivalent in stabilizing
  Cervimab under all conditions tested

- PS80 (0.02--0.08%) demonstrated dose-dependent protection from
  agitation-induced aggregation with no stability benefit differences
  between 0.04% and 0.08%

**3.4 Protein Concentration Feasibility**

Viscosity was assessed across protein concentrations of 5--50 mg/mL in
the selected formulation buffer. At 25 mg/mL, viscosity measured 3.8 ±
0.2 cP (cone-and-plate rheometry at 25°C, shear rate 100 s⁻¹), well
below the 10 cP threshold for IV administration. At 50 mg/mL, viscosity
reached 12.4 ± 0.6 cP, approaching limits of acceptable syringe
injectability. The 25 mg/mL Phase I target dose supports the full
clinical dose range (1--10 mg/kg for a 70-kg patient ≈ 70--700 mg)
within a 10 mL vial with dilution into a 50--250 mL infusion bag.

**4. CONTAINER CLOSURE SYSTEM**

**4.1 Vial Selection and Compatibility**

Type I borosilicate glass vials (15 mL capacity, 20 mm neck) were
selected based on regulatory guidance (USP \<660\> Containers --- Glass,
FDA Guidance on Container Closure Systems for Packaging Human Drugs and
Biologics). Chemical resistance testing following USP \<660\> and ISO
4802 confirmed no significant leachable metals (including barium, boron,
silicon, sodium, zinc) above safety concern thresholds after 30-day
contact at 2--8°C and 25°C.

**4.2 Stopper Selection and Extractables/Leachables**

West FluroTec®-coated bromobutyl rubber stoppers were selected to
minimize extractables relative to uncoated rubber formulations.
Extractables/leachables assessment was conducted by high-resolution
GC-MS and LC-HRMS following USP \<661\> and BPOG recommended extraction
conditions. No leachables were detected above the analytical evaluation
threshold (AET) of 0.15 µg/day based on proposed patient dose and daily
exposure calculations per PQRI guidelines. Certification of compliance
with EU REACH regulation is confirmed by the supplier.

**4.3 In-Use Compatibility Studies**

Compatibility of diluted Cervimab (0.5 and 5 mg/mL) in 0.9% NaCl and 5%
Dextrose (D5W) was evaluated in PVC and non-PVC (polyolefin) IV bags
with polyethylene administration sets:

- No significant loss of protein concentration (±5%) over 6 hours at
  15--25°C or 24 hours at 2--8°C

- No increase in sub-visible particles (MFI ≥1 µm, ≥2 µm, ≥5 µm, ≥10 µm,
  ≥25 µm) relative to undiluted product

- No increase in aggregation by DLS or SEC-HPLC

- No pH shift \> 0.3 units

- In-use stability specification: diluted product should be administered
  within 24 hours if stored at 2--8°C, or within 6 hours at room
  temperature (15--25°C)

**5.2 Filtration Development**

Protein retention and filter compatibility studies were performed using
scaled-down 0.22 µm Durapore PVDF filter discs (Millipore) at
process-representative flux rates (150--250 LMH) and volumes (up to 100
L/m² equivalent). No protein adsorption loss \> 2% was observed. Protein
recovery post-filtration was ≥ 98.5% for all filter types evaluated.
Extractables from PVDF membranes were confirmed non-detectable by
LC-HRMS above the AET at proposed processing volumes.

**5.3 Freeze-Thaw Characterization**

Drug product freeze-thaw studies were conducted using commercial
cryovessels (2L CryoWedge, Single-Use Support) at controlled cooling
rates of 0.5°C/min to -40°C. Three freeze-thaw cycles were performed;
samples were analyzed post-thaw by SEC-HPLC, DLS, sub-visible particle
analysis (MFI), and potency. No significant changes in any attribute
were observed across three cycles, supporting bulk drug substance frozen
storage at -80°C and controlled thaw during drug product manufacturing.

**6.2 Drug Substance/Drug Product Comparability**

A formal comparability exercise was conducted between Cervimab drug
substance (pre-filtration) and drug product (post-fill-finish) to
confirm that the manufacturing process does not introduce changes in
protein quality attributes. Results across the full analytical
characterization panel (SEC, CE-SDS, icIEF, potency SPR, glycan profile,
peptide mapping) demonstrated no significant differences between DS and
DP within analytical variability, confirming process comparability.

**8.2 Stability Commitments**

Real-time stability studies (2--8°C) are ongoing for all Phase I
clinical batches per ICH Q5C (Stability Testing of
Biotechnological/Biological Products). Accelerated studies at 25°C/60%
RH (6 months) and stressed studies at 40°C/75% RH (4 weeks) are
completed and support the proposed 24-month shelf life pending long-term
data. Stability update reports will be provided annually to the FDA as
required under 21 CFR 312.

**9. CONCLUSIONS**

Formulation development studies for Cervimab have culminated in a
scientifically well-justified Phase I clinical formulation: 25 mg/mL in
35 mM histidine buffer (pH 5.8), 8.5% (w/v) sucrose, 0.04% (w/v)
polysorbate 80. The formulation:

- Maximizes protein stability through an optimized histidine buffer
  system, sucrose stabilization, and PS80 anti-aggregation protection

- Is isotonic and compatible with IV administration in both 0.9% NaCl
  and D5W diluents

- Demonstrates robustness to anticipated handling and shipping stresses

- Uses well-characterized, compendial excipients with established
  clinical safety precedents in marketed monoclonal antibody products

- Container closure system (Type I glass / FluroTec stopper) meets USP,
  Ph. Eur., and FDA extractables/leachables guidance

- Manufacturing process (compounding, filtration, aseptic fill-finish)
  is well-controlled and fit-for-purpose for Phase I GMP manufacturing

The formulation and manufacturing process described herein are
considered suitable to support IND-enabling clinical trials of Cervimab
in dMMR/MSI-H metastatic colorectal cancer.
