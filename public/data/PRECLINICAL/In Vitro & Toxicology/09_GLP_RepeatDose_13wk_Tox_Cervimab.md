**2. Observations Summary**

**2.1 Mortality and Clinical Signs**

No mortality. At 3 and 10 mg/kg: no treatment-related clinical
observations. At 30 mg/kg: Grade 1 soft stool/loose feces in 3/10
animals (Days 43--71; resolved spontaneously by Day 78 without
intervention); 1/10 animal showed Grade 1 mucoid stool (Day 57; resolved
Day 64). No diarrhea grade ≥2. No animals required corticosteroid
rescue. Infusion site: no reactions at any dose.

**3. NOAEL and LOAEL Determinations**

  ---------------------- ---------------------------------------------
  **Parameter**          **13-Week Repeat-Dose Determination**

  **NOAEL:**             **10 mg/kg IV Q2W**

  **NOAEL Basis:**       No adverse findings at 10 mg/kg.
                         Immunophenotypic changes (↑ CD8+, ↑ WBC) and
                         spleen/lymph node lymphoid expansion are
                         pharmacological effects classified as
                         NON-ADVERSE. ALT elevation at 10 mg/kg was
                         minimal (≤1.4× ULN, no histopathological
                         correlate). No colitis, hepatitis, or other
                         immune-mediated adverse effects at 10 mg/kg.

  **LOAEL:**             **30 mg/kg IV Q2W**

  **LOAEL Basis:**       Grade 1 colitis in 1/10 animals (10%
                         incidence) plus ALT/AST elevation ≤2× ULN
                         with hepatic periportal infiltrate in 2/10
                         animals at 30 mg/kg. These findings, while
                         minimal/reversible, represent the lowest
                         observed adverse effects.

  **HNSTD:**             10 mg/kg (based on absence of severe toxicity
                         at LOAEL; HNSTD = highest dose without
                         severely toxic effects). Supports FIH dose =
                         1/6 × 10 = 1.67 mg/kg. Combined with MABEL of
                         0.8 mg/kg → proposed FIH = 1 mg/kg IV Q3W
                         (conservative).

  **Reversibility:**     All findings at LOAEL (30 mg/kg) were fully
                         reversed at 8-week recovery necropsy (Day
                         141). Supports reversible/manageable toxicity
                         profile.

  **Human relevance of   Colitis, hepatitis (ALT elevation), and
  findings:**            lymphocytosis are fully consistent with the
                         established irAE profile of approved PD-1
                         inhibitors (pembrolizumab, nivolumab). These
                         class effects are managed clinically with
                         corticosteroids and dose modifications.
