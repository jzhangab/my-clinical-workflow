**1. Introduction: Receptor Occupancy as PK/PD Link**

Receptor occupancy (RO) is the mechanistic link between Cervimab plasma
concentration and pharmacodynamic effect. For PD-1 inhibitors, the
relevant RO is the fraction of PD-1 receptors on circulating CD8+ T
cells occupied by the antibody, which determines the extent of
PD-1/PD-L1 blockade and restoration of T-cell effector function. RO
measurement was central to the FIH dose selection using the MABEL
(Minimum Anticipated Biological Effect Level) approach. The precedent
for RO-based dose justification for anti-PD-1 antibodies was established
by Brahmer et al. in the first-in-human MDX-1106 (nivolumab precursor)
study, which used RO to justify initial dose levels ^\[6\]^. Published
data for comparator anti-PD-1 antibodies in cynomolgus monkeys
demonstrate RO \>90% at ≥1 mg/kg ^\[1\]^, ^\[2\]^, ^\[3\]^, which frames
the Cervimab RO profile in translational context.

**2. In Vitro Receptor Occupancy: Dose-Response**

**2.1 Competitive Saturation Assay --- Primary Human CD8+ T Cells**

Receptor occupancy was measured by competitive saturation flow cytometry
using primary human PBMCs (CD8+ T cells activated 72h with anti-CD3/CD28
beads + IL-2 100 U/mL to maximize PD-1 expression). Detection antibody:
EH12.2H7-PE (anti-PD-1; epitope distinct from Cervimab paratope,
confirmed by non-competing SPR). This assay approach provides a direct
quantitative estimate of the fraction of cell-surface PD-1 receptors
occupied by Cervimab at equilibrium, following the formula: RO% = \[(%
PD-1+ after in vivo Cervimab) / (% PD-1+ after ex vivo saturation)\] ×
100. This formula was validated for clinical use with pembrolizumab in
humanized PD-1 mouse models ^\[4\]^.

**3. In Vivo Receptor Occupancy --- Cynomolgus Monkey**

**3.1 Single-Dose PK/RO Study (Non-GLP, MOS-PK-2021-004)**

monkeys (n=3M+3F per dose group: 1, 5, 20 mg/kg IV over 60 min) at
predose, 0.0 (end of infusion), 24h, 168h, 336h, 504h, 672h, and 1008h

^\[1\]^; P1801 collected at 0, 24h, 168h, 336h, 672h, 1008h, 1344h

**4.1 PK-RO Relationship (Phase 1 Pooled, 1--400 mg)**

The relationship between Cervimab plasma concentration (Cmax and Cmin)
and CD8+ PD-1 RO follows a sigmoidal Emax model (Km_RO = 0.186 µg/mL;
hill coefficient γ = 1.28), estimated from the combined in vitro (n=6
donor EC₅₀) and in vivo Phase 1 data (n=36 with RO measurements). This
Hill model is consistent with the MM-TMDD framework used for modeling
anti-CD33 antibody RO by Figueroa et al. ^\[7\]^ and the PD-1 RO
modeling approach described for pembrolizumab by Lindauer et al.
^\[9\]^.
