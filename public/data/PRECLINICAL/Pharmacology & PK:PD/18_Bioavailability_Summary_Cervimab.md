**1. Introduction: Bioavailability of IgG4 mAbs**

Cervimab is administered exclusively by intravenous (IV) infusion. For
IV therapeutics, absolute bioavailability is definitionally 100% --- all
administered dose enters the systemic circulation. This report documents
the IV bioavailability characterization, infusion rate assessment,
absolute F confirmation, and dose linearity across the preclinical PK
program. Subcutaneous (SC) formulation development is not part of the
current development plan for Cervimab; SC bioavailability is therefore
not assessed in this program. This is consistent with the IV-only
administration of pembrolizumab and nivolumab at approval, though SC
formulations have subsequently been developed for some PD-1/PD-L1
inhibitors ^\[2\]^.

**2. Absolute Bioavailability: IV Reference**

**2.1 Confirmation of Complete Systemic Absorption**

Absolute bioavailability was confirmed by comparing AUC₀₋∞ following IV
bolus-equivalent (60-min infusion) administration across all doses
tested in cynomolgus monkeys (1, 5, 20, 30, 100 mg/kg). Dose-normalized
AUC (AUC₀₋∞/Dose) was compared across doses to confirm
dose-proportionality (a necessary condition for F=100% for IV
administration) and absence of saturable absorption mechanisms (not
applicable for IV, but confirms no target-mediated pre-systemic
elimination) ^\[2\]^.

**2.2 Infusion Rate Assessment**

Cervimab is formulated at 10 mg/mL and administered as a 60-minute IV
infusion. The 60-minute infusion duration was selected based on: (1)
preclinical safety (no infusion-site reactions at any dose in cynomolgus
monkeys), (2) clinical class precedent (pembrolizumab 30 min; nivolumab
60 min; approved infusion times for IgG4 anti-PD-1 agents), (3) rate of
rise of Cmax limited to reduce acute infusion reactions. No
infusion-related reactions were observed in either the single-dose or
13-week repeat-dose GLP toxicology studies at infusion rates up to 100
mg/kg over 60 minutes (equivalent to \~16.7 mg/kg/min).

**3.2 Effects of Covariates on Cervimab Bioavailability/Exposure**

Covariate analysis was performed in the Phase 1 popPK analysis (N=146,
Phases 1+2a pooled). The following covariates were tested: body weight
(BW), sex, age, ECOG PS, baseline albumin, renal function (eGFR), and
hepatic function (Child-Pugh). For large IgG4 mAbs, FcRn-mediated
clearance is the dominant elimination pathway; factors affecting FcRn
saturation or expression are the primary PK determinants ^\[1\]^. Renal
function does not affect mAb clearance (no renal filtration for MW 144
kDa) ^\[6\]^.
