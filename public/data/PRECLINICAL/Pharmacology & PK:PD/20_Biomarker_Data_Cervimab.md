**1. Biomarker Strategy Overview**

This report documents the preclinical biomarker data package for
Cervimab, organized by biomarker type: (1) patient selection/predictive
biomarkers (dMMR/MSI-H, TMB, PD-L1), (2) pharmacodynamic biomarkers
(receptor occupancy, serum IFN-γ, CD8+ T-cell phenotype), (3) response
biomarkers (ctDNA, TCF7+ progenitor TIL), and (4) safety biomarkers
(inflammatory cytokines). The biomarker strategy is informed by the
clinical evidence base for PD-1 inhibitors in dMMR/MSI-H colorectal
cancer ^\[3\]^, ^\[4\]^, ^\[5\]^, and by mechanistic studies defining
the TIL phenotype that predicts response to PD-1 blockade ^\[1\]^,
^\[2\]^, ^\[6\]^.

**2. Predictive / Patient Selection Biomarkers**

**2.1 dMMR and MSI-H as Primary Selection Biomarkers**

Mismatch repair deficiency (dMMR) and microsatellite instability-high
(MSI-H) status are the primary enrollment criteria for the Cervimab
Phase 2/3 program. The clinical rationale is established by the landmark
pembrolizumab KEYNOTE-177 trial demonstrating 43.8% ORR vs 33.1% for
chemotherapy in 1L dMMR/MSI-H mCRC ^\[5\]^, and nivolumab CheckMate 142
demonstrating 31.1% ORR in ≥2L disease ^\[4\]^. PD-1 blockade efficacy
in this population is mechanistically driven by the high neoantigen
burden (median TMB 47 mut/Mb in our patient specimens vs \~5 mut/Mb in
pMMR), which drives robust CD8+ TIL infiltration and PD-1 upregulation
^\[3\]^.

**2.2 TMB Distribution in Clinical Trial Cohort**

Whole-exome sequencing (WES) of 48 CRC specimens (dMMR n=24, pMMR n=24;
MOS-IRB-2020-012) confirmed the strong enrichment of high TMB in the
dMMR subpopulation. These data support dMMR/MSI-H (rather than TMB
alone) as the primary enrollment criterion, as the overlap between dMMR
and TMB-H is \~96% in this population, making redundant TMB testing at
enrollment unnecessary ^\[3\]^.

**3. Pharmacodynamic Biomarkers**

**3.1 PD-1 Receptor Occupancy as Primary PD Biomarker**

Receptor occupancy (RO) on circulating CD8+ T cells is the primary
pharmacodynamic biomarker linking Cervimab concentration to on-target
mechanism of action. The competitive saturation flow cytometry assay
using a non-competing site-labeled detection antibody (clone EH12.2H7;
epitope distinct from Cervimab paratope) measures the fraction of CD8+
PD-1 receptors occupied by Cervimab at each sampling timepoint. This
approach was validated for anti-PD-1 antibodies in clinical studies
^\[4\]^ and provides the mechanistic PD readout for MABEL-based dose
justification. At the proposed 400 mg Q3W dose, PD-1 RO exceeds 99% at
Cmax and maintains \>90% at Cmin (see MOS-PKPD-2022-002).

**3.2 Serum IFN-γ as Pharmacodynamic Response Biomarker**

Serum IFN-γ is the most established pharmacodynamic biomarker of PD-1
blockade, reflecting restoration of effector T-cell cytokine secretion.
The Emax model relating Cervimab AUC to IFN-γ response (EC₅₀ = 6,840
µg·h/mL; Emax = 4.18×) places the proposed 400 mg Q3W dose (AUC₀₋₂₁d
\~14,820 µg·h/mL) at \~90% of the Emax, consistent with the
pharmacodynamic saturation observed with pembrolizumab and nivolumab at
therapeutic doses ^\[7\]^.

**3.3 TIL Phenotyping: TCF7+ Progenitor-Exhausted CD8+ T Cells as
Response Biomarker**

The density of TCF7+PD-1+CD8+ \'progenitor exhausted\' TILs (also termed
stem-like exhausted TILs or Tex_progenitor) has been identified as a key
predictor of response to PD-1 checkpoint blockade. These cells retain
proliferative capacity and expand into terminally differentiated
effectors upon PD-1 blockade, mediating durable antitumor responses
^\[2\]^, ^\[6\]^. In the dMMR human CRC specimens (n=24), TCF7+PD-1+CD8+
TIL density was 84 ± 38 cells/mm², vastly exceeding pMMR tumors (6 ± 4
cells/mm²; p\<0.0001), providing mechanistic rationale for the dMMR
biomarker-selected population.

**4. ctDNA as Pharmacodynamic and Response Biomarker**

Circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) clearance is an emerging pharmacodynamic
biomarker for PD-1 checkpoint blockade in dMMR CRC. Wang et al.
demonstrated that ctDNA clearance at Week 8 (ctDNA negativity) predicted
PFS benefit in anti-PD-1-treated dMMR mCRC patients with 78.6%
sensitivity and 85.7% specificity for PFS \>12 months ^\[7\]^. This
supports ctDNA (Guardant360 assay) as an exploratory PD/efficacy
biomarker in the Cervimab Phase 2 study.
