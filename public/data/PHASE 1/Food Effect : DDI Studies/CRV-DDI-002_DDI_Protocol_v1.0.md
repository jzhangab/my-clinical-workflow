**1. INTRODUCTION AND BACKGROUND**

**1.1 Background on Cervimab and Rationale for DDI Study**

Cervimab is an IgG4 anti-PD-1 monoclonal antibody under clinical
development for dMMR/MSI-H metastatic colorectal cancer and other solid
tumors. Like all programmed death pathway inhibitors currently approved
or in development, cervimab is co-administered with chemotherapy,
targeted therapy, and/or supportive care medications in the oncology
setting. Patients with advanced mCRC routinely receive FOLFOX
(oxaliplatin/5-fluorouracil/leucovorin) or FOLFIRI ± bevacizumab or
cetuximab, alongside analgesics, anticoagulants, antiemetics, and
corticosteroids --- agents that are substrates, inhibitors, or inducers
of major cytochrome P450 (CYP) enzymes.

While monoclonal antibodies are not metabolized by CYP enzymes and thus
are not expected to directly inhibit or induce CYP-mediated drug
metabolism, there is growing recognition that inflammatory cytokines
modulated by immunotherapy can regulate hepatic CYP enzyme expression
via a phenomenon termed cytokine-mediated DDI (cDDI). Pro-inflammatory
cytokines (IL-6, TNF-α, IFN-γ) suppress CYP3A4, CYP1A2, CYP2C9, and
CYP2C19 activity in hepatocytes. By blocking PD-1 and promoting immune
activation, cervimab may alter systemic cytokine levels, potentially
modifying CYP substrate exposure.

The FDA Draft Guidance for Industry: Drug Interaction Studies --- Study
Design, Data Analysis, Implications for Dosing and Labeling (2020) and
the finalized ICH M12 Guideline (Drug Interaction Studies, 2023) address
the evaluation of cDDI potential for immunomodulatory agents. A
prospective clinical DDI study using an established probe cocktail is
the definitive approach to characterize this risk prior to Phase 3 and
NDA submission.

**1.3 Nonclinical DDI Assessment**

A comprehensive in vitro CYP inhibition and induction assessment package
was conducted for cervimab (see companion In Vitro CYP Assessment
Report, VT-DMPK-004). Key findings are summarized in Section 3. Cervimab
itself showed no direct CYP inhibition (IC50 \> 100 μg/mL for all tested
CYPs) and no time-dependent inhibition (TDI) or direct induction in
primary human hepatocyte assays. The primary DDI concern for this study
is therefore indirect, cytokine-mediated modulation of CYP activity in
vivo.

**2. STUDY OBJECTIVES AND ENDPOINTS**

**2.1 Primary Objective**

To evaluate the effect of cervimab at steady-state (400 mg Q3W IV) on
the single-dose pharmacokinetics of sensitive probe substrates for
CYP3A4 (midazolam), CYP1A2 (caffeine), and CYP2C9 (S-warfarin) in
patients with advanced solid tumors.

**2.2 Primary Endpoints**

- Midazolam: Cmax, AUC0-24, AUC0-∞

- Caffeine: Cmax, AUC0-24, AUC0-∞

- S-Warfarin: Cmax, AUC0-24, AUC0-∞

**2.3 Secondary Objectives**

To further characterize probe PK (Tmax, t½, CL/F); to assess the PK of
cervimab at steady state; to evaluate safety and tolerability; and to
assess any pharmacodynamic (PD) markers of immune activation.

**2.4 Secondary Endpoints**

- Tmax, t½, CL/F, and metabolic ratios (1-OH midazolam/midazolam;
  paraxanthine/caffeine) for each probe

- Cervimab trough concentrations (Ctrough) at Cycles 1--3 to confirm
  steady state

- International Normalized Ratio (INR) at pre-dose, 24 h, and 48 h
  post-warfarin administration in each period

- Incidence and severity of AEs (NCI-CTCAE v5.0); immune-mediated AEs;
  infusion-related reactions

- Serum cytokine panel (IL-6, IL-10, IFN-γ, TNF-α) pre- and
  post-cervimab to characterize cDDI mechanism

**4. STUDY DESIGN**

**4.1 Overall Design**

CRV-DDI-002 is a Phase 1, open-label, fixed-sequence, two-period study.
Patients who meet eligibility criteria will be enrolled and evaluated
for the DDI effect of cervimab on probe substrate PK over two defined
periods, before and after achieving steady-state cervimab exposure.

Rationale for timing: Day 43 = 3rd dose of cervimab. With a t½ of \~18
days and Q3W (every 21 days) dosing, approximately 2 doses are required

is expected to be manifest by Cycle 3 based on anti-PD-1 pharmacodynamic
data in the literature.

**4.3 Probe Cocktail Administration Instructions**

All three probe substrates will be administered simultaneously as an
oral DDI cocktail with 240 mL water after an overnight fast of ≥10
hours. Subjects must fast for ≥2 hours post-dose. The probes are
administered approximately 1 hour before the start of the cervimab IV
infusion on Period 2 Day 1 (to allow the initial distribution phase of
the probes to commence before cervimab peak exposure).

Warfarin note: Due to the anticoagulant activity of racemic warfarin,
Vitamin K1 (phytonadione) 10 mg PO will be co-administered with each
warfarin dose to attenuate the PT/INR response while preserving PK
measurability. INR will be measured at baseline, 24 h, and 48 h
post-warfarin. Antidotes (Vitamin K1 IV) and reversal agents (4-factor
PCC, FFP) must be available on site.

**5. STUDY POPULATION**

**5.1 Inclusion Criteria**

1.  Adult patients (≥18 years) with histologically or cytologically
    confirmed advanced or metastatic solid tumor(s) for which no
    satisfactory standard-of-care alternative exists, or for whom
    cervimab-based therapy is clinically indicated

2.  Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group (ECOG) Performance Status of 0 or
    1

3.  Adequate organ function at screening: ANC ≥1.0 × 10⁹/L, platelets
    ≥100 × 10⁹/L, hemoglobin ≥9.0 g/dL; ALT and AST ≤2.5× ULN (≤5× ULN
    if hepatic metastases); total bilirubin ≤1.5× ULN; creatinine ≤1.5×
    ULN or eGFR ≥50 mL/min/1.73 m²

4.  Life expectancy ≥12 weeks

5.  Willing and able to provide written informed consent

6.  Women of childbearing potential must have negative pregnancy test;
    adequate contraception required for all subjects of reproductive
    potential

**5.2 Exclusion Criteria**

7.  Active or prior history of autoimmune disease requiring systemic
    treatment within the prior 2 years, or any condition requiring
    chronic systemic steroids (\>10 mg/day prednisone equivalent) or
    other immunosuppressants within 14 days

8.  Known active or chronic hepatitis B or C, or HIV infection

9.  Concomitant use of strong CYP3A4 inhibitors or inducers within 14
    days or 5 half-lives of Day 1 and through the PK follow-up of Period
    2

10. Known allergy or hypersensitivity to midazolam, benzodiazepines,
    caffeine, warfarin, or Vitamin K1

11. Personal or family history of coagulopathy or known hemostatic
    disorders; active bleeding or high bleeding risk

12. Prior treatment with any PD-1, PD-L1, or CTLA-4 inhibitor within 6
    months of enrollment (to avoid carryover immunological effects)

13. Regular consumption of grapefruit, grapefruit juice, Seville
    oranges, or other CYP3A4-inhibiting foods within 7 days prior to
    Period 1

14. History of significant hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh B or C)

15. Active or untreated CNS metastases (patients with stable, treated
    CNS metastases ≥4 weeks off steroids are eligible)

16. Current use of medications with narrow therapeutic index that are
    sensitive CYP substrates and which cannot be safely withheld around
    the probe dosing visits

**7. STATISTICAL ANALYSIS**

**7.1 PK Analysis**

Individual PK parameters for each probe will be estimated by NCA
(Phoenix WinNonlin v8.3+). The DDI analysis will use a mixed-effects
ANOVA model on natural log-transformed primary PK parameters (Cmax,
AUC0-24, AUC0-∞) with fixed effects for period and treatment
(with/without cervimab), and a random effect for subject.

The point estimate (PE) and 90% CI of the geometric mean ratio (GMR:
cervimab + probe / probe alone) will be computed and expressed as
percentages. Per ICH M12 (2023) and FDA guidance for oncology DDI
studies conducted in patient populations where a broader CI is accepted:

- No clinically significant DDI: 90% CI of GMR within 70.00%--143.00%
  for all three probes on all primary parameters

- If the 90% CI is outside this range, dose adjustment recommendations
  will be developed for co-medications that are sensitive CYP substrates

**7.2 Metabolic Ratio Analysis**

In addition to parent drug PK, the following metabolic ratios will be
reported as supportive analyses of CYP phenotypic changes:

- CYP3A4 activity ratio: AUC1-OH midazolam / AUCmidazolam (Period 1 vs.
  Period 2)

- CYP1A2 activity ratio: AUCparaxanthine / AUCcaffeine (Period 1 vs.
  Period 2)

- CYP2C9 activity ratio: AUCS-warfarin (Period 2) vs. AUCS-warfarin
  (Period 1)

**7.3 Sample Size**

With 20 evaluable subjects and assuming intra-subject CV of 30% for
probe PK, the study has \~80% power to conclude no DDI (90% CI within
70--143%) if the true GMR is 1.0. An enrollment target of 24 subjects
accounts for up to 20% dropout/non-evaluable rate.

**8. SAFETY**

Safety will be monitored throughout the study. Of particular concern
are:

- Warfarin-associated bleeding: INR will be monitored at 24 and 48 h
  post-warfarin dose. If INR \>3.0 at any time point, Vitamin K1 5 mg PO
  will be administered as a corrective dose. Patients with INR \>4.0
  will receive IV Vitamin K1 and be evaluated for clinical bleeding.

- Midazolam sedation: All subjects receiving midazolam will be monitored
  for sedation (Ramsay Sedation Scale) for ≥4 hours post-dose.
  Flumazenil (0.2 mg IV) must be available for reversal of excessive
  benzodiazepine effect.

- Cervimab immune-related AEs: All AEs will be graded per NCI-CTCAE
  v5.0; immune-mediated AEs managed per standard ICI toxicity
  guidelines.
