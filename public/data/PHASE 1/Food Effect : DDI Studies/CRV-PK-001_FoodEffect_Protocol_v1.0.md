**1. INTRODUCTION AND RATIONALE**

**1.1 Background**

Cervimab is a fully human immunoglobulin G4 (IgG4) monoclonal antibody
targeting Programmed Cell Death Protein 1 (PD-1). It is being developed
by Vanguard Oncology Therapeutics, Inc. for the treatment of adult
patients with deficient mismatch repair (dMMR) or microsatellite
instability-high (MSI-H) metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC). Cervimab
exerts its antitumor effect by blocking the interaction between PD-1 and
its ligands PD-L1 and PD-L2, thereby restoring T-cell mediated antitumor
immunity.

Cervimab is administered as an intravenous (IV) infusion. As a
large-molecule biologic, cervimab is not expected to undergo classical
gastrointestinal absorption following oral administration. However, the
potential influence of fed state on the pharmacokinetics (PK) of
IV-administered monoclonal antibodies via Fc-receptor mediated
processes, including neonatal Fc receptor (FcRn) recycling, has been an
emerging area of regulatory interest. In addition, food intake can alter
gastric pH, gastrointestinal motility, splanchnic blood flow, and
systemic inflammatory mediators, which may indirectly influence biologic
distribution and clearance.

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) Guidance for Industry on
Food-Effect Bioavailability and Fed Bioequivalence Studies (January
2002) recommends that all new molecular entities (NMEs) requiring oral
dosing under both fed and fasted conditions be studied for food effects.
While cervimab is administered intravenously, FDA\'s Monoclonal Antibody
Development Guidance (Draft, 2022) recommends characterization of
conditions surrounding administration for any product where food-related
variability in exposure has been identified or is plausible based on
mechanism.

This protocol describes a dedicated Phase 1 pharmacokinetic food effect
study for cervimab administered as a single 400 mg IV infusion in
healthy adult subjects under fed and fasted conditions.

**1.2 Nonclinical and Clinical Experience**

Nonclinical PK studies in cynomolgus monkeys showed dose-proportional
exposure for cervimab doses of 1--30 mg/kg IV. Mean terminal half-life
(t½) was approximately 15--18 days, consistent with the expected
behavior of an IgG4 antibody with intact FcRn binding. No notable
food-related effects on PK were observed in feeding studies in primates
(Study Report VT-NC-PK-007).

In the ongoing Phase 1 dose-escalation study (CRV-101), cervimab
demonstrated a t½ of approximately 18 days following single-dose IV
administration at 200 and 400 mg in patients with advanced solid tumors.
The population PK model identified body weight as a clinically
meaningful covariate on volume of distribution. Pre-dose nutritional
status (as assessed by serum albumin) was identified as a potential
covariate on clearance; however, its contribution was not statistically
significant (p = 0.12) in the current dataset (n = 48).

A dedicated food effect study is warranted to prospectively characterize
the potential impact of fed versus fasted state on the PK of cervimab
and to support the prescribing information and Phase 3 dosing guidance.

**2. STUDY OBJECTIVES AND ENDPOINTS**

**2.1 Primary Objective**

To evaluate the effect of a high-fat, high-calorie meal on the
pharmacokinetics of cervimab following a single IV dose of 400 mg in
healthy adult subjects.

**2.2 Primary Endpoints**

- Maximum observed serum concentration (Cmax)

- Area under the serum concentration--time curve from time zero to the
  last measurable concentration (AUC0-t)

- Area under the serum concentration--time curve from time zero to
  infinity (AUC0-∞)

**2.3 Secondary Objectives**

To further characterize the PK of cervimab under fed and fasted
conditions, and to assess safety and tolerability.

**2.4 Secondary Endpoints**

- Time to maximum observed concentration (Tmax)

- Terminal elimination half-life (t½)

- Apparent total systemic clearance (CL)

- Volume of distribution at steady state (Vss)

- Incidence and severity of adverse events (AEs) per NCI-CTCAE v5.0

- Incidence of anti-drug antibodies (ADA) and neutralizing antibodies
  (nAb)

**3. STUDY DESIGN**

**3.1 Overall Design**

CRV-PK-001 is a Phase 1, open-label, randomized, two-period,
two-sequence crossover study in 24 healthy adult subjects. Subjects will
be randomized 1:1 to one of two treatment sequences:

- Sequence AB (n=12): Period 1 (Fasted) → Washout → Period 2 (Fed)

- Sequence BA (n=12): Period 1 (Fed) → Washout → Period 2 (Fasted)

Each treatment period consists of a single IV infusion of cervimab 400
mg over 60 minutes, administered under the assigned condition (fasted or
fed). Periods are separated by a washout period of ≥21 days
(approximately 5 half-lives), with timing adjusted based on emerging PK
data to ensure \<5% carryover.

buttered toast, 4 oz of hash brown potatoes, 8 oz of whole milk.
Subjects must consume ≥80% of the meal.

**3.4 Fasted Condition**

In the fasted condition, subjects will fast for ≥10 hours prior to
dosing. Water (up to 240 mL) is permitted up to 1 hour before dosing.
Fasting will continue for ≥4 hours post-dose.

**4. STUDY POPULATION**

**4.1 Inclusion Criteria**

Subjects must meet all of the following criteria:

1.  Healthy adult male or female subjects, 18 to 55 years of age,
    inclusive, at the time of signing informed consent

2.  Body mass index (BMI) ≥18.0 and ≤30.0 kg/m² and body weight ≥50 kg
    at screening

3.  Females of childbearing potential must have a negative serum
    pregnancy test at screening and a negative urine pregnancy test at
    Day -1 of each period, and must agree to use highly effective
    contraception from screening through 90 days after the last dose

4.  Male subjects must agree to use contraception from Day 1 through 90
    days after the last dose

5.  Willing and able to provide written informed consent and comply with
    all study procedures

6.  Clinically acceptable vital signs, physical examination, 12-lead
    ECG, and laboratory parameters at screening

7.  Non-smoker or former smoker who has not smoked within 3 months prior
    to screening

8.  Willing to consume the standardized high-fat, high-calorie meal as
    required

**4.2 Exclusion Criteria**

Subjects will be excluded if any of the following criteria are met:

9.  Any clinically significant medical condition including, but not
    limited to, hepatic, renal, gastrointestinal, cardiovascular,
    endocrine, pulmonary, neurologic, or psychiatric disease

10. Active or prior history of autoimmune disease requiring systemic
    treatment within the past 2 years

11. History of known or suspected immunodeficiency, or any condition
    requiring chronic systemic immunosuppressive therapy

12. History of prior therapy with any anti-PD-1, anti-PD-L1, anti-PD-L2,
    anti-CTLA-4, or other immune checkpoint inhibitor

13. Positive serology for HIV, hepatitis B surface antigen (HBsAg), or
    anti-hepatitis C virus (HCV) antibodies at screening

14. Use of any prescription or non-prescription medication (excluding
    hormonal contraceptives) within 14 days or 5 half-lives (whichever
    is longer) prior to study drug administration

15. History of severe hypersensitivity or allergic reactions to any
    monoclonal antibody or biologic product

16. Clinically significant abnormality on 12-lead ECG at screening
    (e.g., QTcF \>450 ms in males or \>470 ms in females)

17. ALT or AST \>1.5× upper limit of normal (ULN), or total bilirubin
    \>1.5× ULN at screening

18. eGFR \<60 mL/min/1.73 m² at screening

19. Participation in another clinical study with an investigational
    product within 30 days or 5 half-lives prior to screening

20. Blood donation or significant blood loss \>500 mL within 56 days
    prior to screening

21. Pregnant or breastfeeding

22. Any condition that, in the opinion of the investigator, would make
    it unsafe or unsuitable for the subject to participate in this study

**5.2 Dose Rationale**

The 400 mg dose is the proposed Phase 3 therapeutic dose based on
emerging PK/PD and preliminary efficacy data from CRV-101. Single-dose
administration is appropriate for food-effect evaluation to isolate PK
differences attributable to nutritional state without the confound of
accumulation.

**7. PHARMACOKINETIC AND STATISTICAL ANALYSES**

**7.1 PK Parameter Estimation**

Individual PK parameters will be estimated using non-compartmental
analysis (NCA) with Phoenix WinNonlin (Version 8.3 or higher). The
following parameters will be derived:

  ------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Parameter**    **Units**       **Definition**
  ---------------- --------------- ---------------------------------------
  Cmax             μg/mL           Maximum observed serum concentration

  Tmax             h               Time of maximum observed concentration

  AUC0-t           μg·h/mL         AUC from time zero to last measurable
                                   concentration (linear-log trapezoid)

  AUC0-∞           μg·h/mL         AUC from time zero to infinity
                                   (AUC0-t + Clast/λz)

  t½               h               Terminal elimination half-life
                                   (0.693/λz)

  CL               mL/h/kg         Total systemic clearance (Dose/AUC0-∞)

  Vss              mL/kg           Volume of distribution at steady state
                                   (AUMC method)

  %AUCextrap       \%              Percentage of AUC0-∞ attributed to
                                   extrapolation (AUC0-∞ − AUC0-t)/AUC0-∞
                                   × 100
  ------------------------------------------------------------------------

Terminal elimination rate constant (λz) will be estimated by log-linear
regression of the terminal portion of the concentration-time curve using
at least 3 data points with an adjusted r² ≥ 0.90. If %AUCextrap \>20%,
AUC0-∞ will not be used as a primary endpoint.

**7.2 Statistical Analysis of Food Effect**

The food effect statistical analysis will employ a mixed-effects
analysis of variance (ANOVA) model on the natural log-transformed PK
parameters (Cmax, AUC0-t, AUC0-∞). The model will include fixed effects
for sequence, period, and treatment (Fed vs. Fasted), and a random
effect for subject within sequence.

Point estimates and 90% confidence intervals (CIs) for the geometric
mean ratios (GMR: Fed/Fasted) will be back-transformed from the log
scale and reported as percent ratios with 90% CIs. A food effect is
considered absent if the 90% CI for both Cmax and AUC falls entirely
within 80.00%--125.00%, consistent with FDA bioequivalence criteria.

Tmax will be analyzed non-parametrically using the Wilcoxon signed-rank
test. All analyses will be performed using SAS Version 9.4 (or higher).

**7.3 Sample Size Justification**

A sample size of 24 subjects (12 per sequence) provides approximately
90% power to conclude the absence of a food effect (90% CI within
80--125%) for Cmax and AUC, assuming:

- True Fed/Fasted GMR of 1.00 (no food effect expected)

- Intra-subject CV of 20% (conservative estimate based on IgG4 mAb PK
  variability in the literature)

- Two-sided significance level α = 0.10 (corresponding to 90% CI)

To account for a potential dropout rate of up to 10%, a total of 26
subjects will be enrolled, targeting 24 completers.

**8. SAFETY ASSESSMENTS**

**8.1 Safety Monitoring**

Safety assessments will include: physical examinations, vital signs,
clinical laboratory tests (hematology, serum chemistry, urinalysis),
12-lead ECG, and monitoring for adverse events (AEs) including
infusion-related reactions (IRRs). AEs will be graded per NCI Common
Terminology Criteria for Adverse Events (CTCAE) v5.0. Immune-mediated
adverse events (imAEs) will be assessed using standardized case
definitions.

**8.2 Infusion-Related Reaction Management**

All subjects will be monitored for IRRs during and for ≥2 hours
post-infusion. Resuscitation equipment and medications (epinephrine,
corticosteroids, antihistamines, bronchodilators) will be immediately
available. Grade ≥3 IRRs will result in permanent discontinuation of
cervimab in the affected subject.

**8.3 Stopping Rules**

The study will be placed on clinical hold and reviewed if any of the
following events occur:

- Any Grade 4 or Grade 5 AE deemed related to cervimab

- Two or more Grade 3 immune-mediated AEs in the same subject

- Any unexpected serious adverse event that suggests a significant risk
  to participants
