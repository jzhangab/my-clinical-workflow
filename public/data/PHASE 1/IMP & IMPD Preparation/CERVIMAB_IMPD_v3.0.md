**2. QUALITY (PHARMACEUTICAL) DATA**

**2.1 Introduction and Drug Substance Overview**

Cervimab is a fully human immunoglobulin G4 (IgG4) monoclonal antibody
(mAb) that selectively binds to the Programmed Death-1 (PD-1) receptor
on T lymphocytes, blocking interaction with its ligands PD-L1 and PD-L2.
PD-1 checkpoint blockade restores anti-tumor T-cell activity and has
demonstrated robust clinical activity in deficient mismatch repair
(dMMR) / microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H) colorectal cancer
(CRC), a patient population characterized by a high somatic mutational
burden and favorable immunological microenvironment.

Cervimab is produced by recombinant DNA technology using a Chinese
Hamster Ovary (CHO) cell expression system, and purified by a validated
multi-step chromatography and filtration process. The proposed
commercial presentation is a single-use vial containing 100 mg cervimab
in 10 mL (10 mg/mL) sterile, preservative-free aqueous solution for IV
infusion after dilution.

**2.2.3 Manufacturing Process**

The manufacturing process for cervimab drug substance encompasses the
following unit operations:

- Cell Bank System: Two-tier cell banking (MCB and WCB) derived from a
  single research cell bank (RCB). CHO-DG44 host cells transfected with
  cervimab heavy and light chain expression constructs. MCB and WCB
  qualified per ICH Q5A and Q5D.

- Upstream Bioprocessing: Seed train expansion from vial thaw through
  N-1 bioreactor (500 L), to production bioreactor (2,000 L fed-batch
  culture, 14-day run). Temperature: 36.5°C; pH: 7.1; DO: 30%
  saturation. Harvest by centrifugation and depth filtration.

- Downstream Purification (5-step process):

  - Step 1: Protein A affinity chromatography (MabSelect SuRe LX resin)
    --- primary capture

  - Step 2: Low-pH viral inactivation (pH 3.5, 60 min at room
    temperature)

  - Step 3: Anion-exchange chromatography (flow-through mode, Capto Q
    resin) --- HCP and DNA clearance

  - Step 4: Cation-exchange chromatography (bind-elute mode, Capto SP
    ImpRes) --- aggregate removal

  - Step 5: Viral filtration (20 nm filter, Planova 20N) and
    ultrafiltration/diafiltration (UF/DF) into DS formulation buffer

- Final DS Bulk: Formulated at 50 mg/mL in 20 mM histidine/histidine
  HCl, 8% (w/v) sucrose, 0.04% polysorbate 80, pH 6.0. Filtered through
  0.22 µm membrane, filled into sterile stainless-steel vessels,
  snap-frozen over dry ice, and stored at ≤-70°C.

**2.3.2 Pharmaceutical Development Rationale**

Formulation development was guided by accelerated stability screening of
32 candidate formulations using Design of Experiments (DoE) methodology
per ICH Q8(R2). Key findings include:

- Histidine/His-HCl buffer at 20 mM, pH 6.0 provides optimal stability
  with minimal chemical degradation (deamidation, oxidation) over
  accelerated stability timelines.

- Sucrose at 8% w/v provides adequate tonicity (290 mOsm/kg) and
  cryoprotection; maintains monomer content ≥99% through freeze-thaw
  cycling.

- Polysorbate 80 at 0.04% w/v prevents surface-induced aggregation
  during manufacturing and shipping stress.

- The absence of antioxidants (e.g., methionine) was evaluated and found
  unnecessary given low oxidation rates at pH 6.0 and ≤-70°C storage.

**3. NON-CLINICAL DATA SUMMARY**

**3.1 Pharmacology**

**3.1.1 Primary Pharmacodynamics**

Cervimab binds human PD-1 with high affinity (KD = 0.32 nM; SPR, BIAcore
T200), effectively blocking both PD-L1 (IC50 = 1.4 nM) and PD-L2 (IC50 =
2.1 nM) interactions in competitive binding assays. In primary human
T-cell assays, cervimab restores IFN-γ secretion and T-cell
proliferation in a dose-dependent manner (EC50 ≈ 0.5 nM in PBMC
co-culture models).

In syngeneic tumor models using humanized PD-1 knock-in mice:

- MC38 colon adenocarcinoma (MSI-H, mismatch repair deficient by MLH1
  knockout): Tumor growth inhibition (TGI) = 88.4% at 10 mg/kg IP q3d ×
  4; 3/8 complete responses (CR)

- CT26 colon carcinoma (MSS): TGI = 31.2% at 10 mg/kg; 0/8 CR ---
  consistent with known clinical biology

- Combination with FOLFOX in MC38 model: Synergistic antitumor activity;
  TGI = 94.1%; 5/8 CR

**3.1.2 Secondary Pharmacodynamics**

Off-target binding screening by radioligand binding panel (44 receptors,
ion channels, enzymes) revealed no significant activity (\>50%
inhibition) at 1 µM cervimab. No binding to PD-L1 or B7-H3 direct. No
complement-dependent cytotoxicity (CDC) or antibody-dependent cellular
cytotoxicity (ADCC) due to IgG4 isotype and S228P hinge modification.

**4.3.2 Risk Management Strategy**

- Mandatory irAE monitoring and management algorithm (corticosteroid
  protocol) provided to all investigators

- Cardiac biomarker monitoring (troponin I, ECG) at baseline, Cycles
  1-3, then q3 cycles --- based on myocarditis signal

- Thyroid function tests at baseline and q6 weeks for endocrinopathy
  surveillance

- Dedicated Safety Monitoring Committee (SMC) with independent
  cardiologist and immunologist

- Proposed REMS: Prescriber and healthcare facility certification for
  cervimab administration; MedGuide for patients
