**1. INTRODUCTION AND PURPOSE OF AMENDMENT**

Acme Biosciences, Inc. (the Sponsor) submits this IND Amendment
(Amendment No. 012) to IND 123456 for Cervimab, an investigational fully
human IgG4 anti-PD-1 monoclonal antibody, in the treatment of dMMR/MSI-H
metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC). This amendment is submitted
pursuant to 21 CFR 312.30 (Protocol Amendments) and 21 CFR 312.32 (IND
Safety Reports).

The purpose of this amendment is threefold:

1.  Protocol Amendment to Study ACME-CERV-CRC-003 (Phase III, randomized
    controlled trial): Modifications to eligibility criteria (cardiac
    exclusion), addition of mandatory cardiac surveillance assessments,
    and revision of the Dose Modification Guidelines to include a
    formalized immune-mediated myocarditis management algorithm.

2.  IND Safety Report: Reporting of two (2) treatment-related cases of
    immune-mediated myocarditis, including one fatal case, identified in
    the Phase II Study ACME-CERV-CRC-001 since the last IND annual
    report submission (Amendment No. 010, September 2025).

3.  Reference to Updated Investigator Brochure: IB Version 4.0 (filed as
    separate IND submission, Amendment No. 011) incorporates updated
    cardiac safety information; this amendment cross-references IB v4.0
    as the authoritative safety reference for all ongoing studies under
    this IND.

This amendment does not modify the IND drug substance or drug product
sections (IMPD v3.0, on file). The Sponsor has notified all active
investigators per 21 CFR 312.30(b)(3) and requests FDA acknowledgment
per 21 CFR 312.30(d) prior to implementing Protocol Version 3.0.

**2.2.3 New Section 9.4 --- Immune-Mediated Myocarditis Management
Algorithm (Added to Protocol v3.0)**

A new dedicated section (Section 9.4) has been added to Protocol v3.0 to
provide a standardized, stepwise management algorithm for
immune-mediated myocarditis, replacing the brief text previously
included in Section 9 (Adverse Event Management). The full text of
Section 9.4 is provided in Appendix B of this amendment. Key elements
include:

- Grade 1 (asymptomatic troponin elevation ≤ 2×ULN): Withhold cervimab;
  serial troponin q48h; cardiology consult; echocardiography. Resume if
  troponin returns to ≤1×ULN and no symptoms.

- Grade 2 (symptomatic or troponin 2--10×ULN): Withhold cervimab; admit
  to monitored bed; methylprednisolone 1 mg/kg/day IV; cardiology
  co-management; cardiac MRI if available. Resume at Grade ≤1 only after
  multidisciplinary review.

- Grade 3--4 (hemodynamic compromise, ventricular arrhythmia, or
  troponin \>10×ULN): PERMANENTLY DISCONTINUE cervimab;
  methylprednisolone 500--1000 mg/day IV; cardiology ICU-level care;
  consider mycophenolate mofetil or IVIG for steroid-refractory cases.
  Do not rechallenge.

- Fatal / Grade 5: Autopsy strongly recommended; mandatory SAE/SUSAR
  reporting within 7 days; notify FDA.

**4.3 Risk Characterization and Clinical Context**

In the integrated safety population of the cervimab clinical program
(n=187), immune-mediated myocarditis has been observed in 3 patients
(1.6%), of which 2 were Grade 3-4 (1.1%) and 1 was fatal (0.5%). For
context, published rates of myocarditis with approved PD-1/PD-L1
inhibitors range from 0.04% to 1.4% (any grade), with Grade 3-4 rates of
0.3-1.1% and case fatality rates of approximately 40-50% for Grade 3-4
cases.

- Myocarditis has occurred as early as Cycle 2 and as late as Cycle 11
  in the cervimab program; there is no clear dose-response relationship.

- Risk factors identified in cervimab program cases: Male sex (2/3
  cases), prior anthracycline exposure (0/3 --- no prior anthracyclines
  in identified cases), absence of baseline cardiac disease in all 3
  cases (highlighting that baseline screening does not eliminate risk).

- Comprehensive review of all cardiac AEs across the cervimab program by
  an independent Cardiac Safety Committee (CSC) is underway and a full
  CSC report will be provided to FDA as a follow-up submission within 60
  days.
