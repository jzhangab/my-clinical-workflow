**2. BACKGROUND AND RATIONALE**

**2.1 Disease Overview: dMMR/MSI-H Metastatic Colorectal Cancer**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) is the third most common cancer and the second
leading cause of cancer-related mortality in the United States, with
approximately 153,000 new cases and 52,000 deaths estimated annually
(SEER 2024). Metastatic CRC (mCRC) carries a 5-year survival rate of
approximately 14%, underscoring the critical unmet medical need for
effective therapies.

Deficient mismatch repair (dMMR) or microsatellite instability-high
(MSI-H) status is present in approximately 3--5% of mCRC cases,
representing a molecularly distinct and clinically actionable subset.
dMMR/MSI-H tumors harbor substantially higher tumor mutational burden
(TMB-H) and neoantigen load compared to proficient MMR (pMMR) tumors,
creating a highly immunogenic tumor microenvironment that is amenable to
immune checkpoint blockade.

**2.2 PD-1 Pathway Biology and Therapeutic Rationale for Cervimab**

The programmed cell death protein 1 (PD-1) pathway is a central immune
checkpoint that restrains adaptive anti-tumor immunity. Tumor cells and
tumor-infiltrating immune cells upregulate PD-L1 and PD-L2 ligands,
engaging PD-1 on cytotoxic T lymphocytes (CTLs) and thereby attenuating
effector function, inducing T-cell exhaustion, and promoting immune
escape. In dMMR/MSI-H CRC, dense T-cell infiltration accompanies
prominent PD-L1 expression, providing the molecular rationale for PD-1
inhibition.

Cervimab is a fully humanized IgG4 anti-PD-1 monoclonal antibody with a
stabilizing S228P hinge mutation to prevent Fab arm exchange. Cervimab
binds with high affinity (KD \< 1 nM) to the PD-1 receptor,
competitively blocking PD-L1 and PD-L2 binding, restoring effector
T-cell function, and enabling anti-tumor immune cytotoxicity. Cervimab
does not directly induce ADCC or CDC, minimizing risk of T-cell
depletion.

**2.3 Nonclinical Summary Supporting First-in-Human Dose**

Preclinical pharmacology, toxicology, and PK studies supporting IND
submission are summarized in the Investigator\'s Brochure (IB). Key
findings:

- In vitro: Cervimab blocked PD-1/PD-L1 interaction with IC50 of 0.8 nM;
  restored T-cell proliferation and IFN-gamma secretion in suppression
  assays; demonstrated no off-target binding across a 286-member
  receptor panel.

- In vivo (MC38 syngeneic mouse model): Significant tumor growth
  inhibition observed at 1--10 mg/kg IP, with 40% complete responses at
  10 mg/kg.

- GLP Repeat-Dose Toxicology (NHP, 26-week): NOAEL established at 10
  mg/kg Q2W IV. Adverse findings above NOAEL included immune-mediated
  colitis (Grades 2--3), hepatocellular enzyme elevations, and
  pneumonitis. All findings were reversible upon cessation.

- PK/PD modeling: Predicted therapeutic PD-1 receptor occupancy (\>80%)
  at approximately 3 mg/kg in human based on cross-species allometric
  scaling and receptor occupancy modeling.

- FIH dose selection: Starting dose of 1 mg/kg IV corresponds to 1/10 of
  NOAEL (10 mg/kg) in NHP, consistent with FDA guidance for
  immunostimulatory biologics.

**2.4 Regulatory Considerations**

This study is conducted under an active IND (21 CFR Part 312). The study
design is consistent with FDA Guidance for Industry: Considerations for
the Design, Conduct, and Analysis of First-in-Human Studies for
Investigational Oncology Drugs and Biologics (2025 Draft Guidance) and
FDA Guidance on Expedited Programs for Serious Conditions (2023).
dMMR/MSI-H mCRC qualifies for Breakthrough Therapy Designation

**4. STUDY DESIGN**

**4.1 Overview**

CERV-001-SAD/MAD is a Phase 1, open-label, multicenter, dose-escalation
study conducted in two sequential parts:

- Part A (SAD): Single IV administration of Cervimab across up to 6 dose
  levels using a 3+3 design. Each cohort enrolls 3 patients. Following
  completion of the DLT assessment window, the DSMB/Safety Review
  Committee reviews safety data before escalation to the next dose
  level.

- Part B (MAD): Multiple ascending dose administration (Q3W x 4 cycles)
  at 3 selected dose levels informed by Part A. Enrollment of 3--6
  patients per cohort; 3+3 design with DLT defined within Cycle 1 (Days
  1--21).

Upon completion of the planned dose levels or upon MTD/RP2D
determination, an optional dose-expansion cohort may be initiated at the
RP2D to collect additional safety, PK, and preliminary efficacy data in
up to 20 additional patients (contingent on IND amendment).

**4.3 Dose Escalation Rules (3+3 Design)**

Dose escalation proceeds as follows:

- Cohort of 3 patients enrolled at a given dose level.

- If 0/3 patients experience a DLT: escalate to next dose level after
  DSMB review.

- If 1/3 patients experience a DLT: expand cohort to 6 patients.

- If 1/6 patients experience a DLT: escalate to next dose level.

- If ≥2/6 patients experience a DLT: MTD exceeded; declare prior dose
  level as MTD.

- If ≥2/3 patients experience a DLT: MTD exceeded; expand prior cohort
  to 6 patients to confirm MTD.

The MTD is defined as the highest dose level at which \<2/6 patients
experience a DLT. The RP2D will be selected by the DSMB, taking into
account safety, PK, PD biomarkers, and emerging efficacy signals; the
RP2D may not be the MTD.

**4.4 Accelerated Titration Option**

At Dose Levels A1 and A2 (1 and 3 mg/kg), accelerated titration (1
patient per cohort) may be employed if no dose-related toxicity ≥Grade 2
is observed. Upon any Grade 2 drug-related toxicity, the cohort reverts
to a 3+3 design. This approach is consistent with Simon et al. (1997)
and FDA guidance on dose-escalation designs.

**5. PATIENT SELECTION CRITERIA**

**5.1 Inclusion Criteria**

Patients must meet ALL of the following criteria:

- Age ≥18 years at time of informed consent.

- Histologically confirmed colorectal adenocarcinoma with documented
  distant metastases (mCRC).

- Confirmed dMMR status by IHC (loss of ≥1 MMR protein: MLH1, MSH2,
  MSH6, PMS2) AND/OR MSI-H by PCR or NGS (≥30% of tested microsatellite
  loci showing instability), performed by a CLIA-certified laboratory.

- Measurable disease per RECIST v1.1 (≥1 lesion ≥10 mm in longest
  diameter by CT/MRI or ≥15 mm for lymph nodes).

- ECOG Performance Status 0--2.

- Life expectancy ≥12 weeks per investigator assessment.

- Refractory to or intolerant of ≥1 prior line of systemic therapy for
  mCRC (no maximum prior lines).

- Adequate organ function within 14 days prior to first dose: ANC ≥1.5 x
  10\^9/L; Hgb ≥9 g/dL; Platelets ≥100 x 10\^9/L; Creatinine ≤1.5 x ULN
  or CrCl ≥50 mL/min; AST/ALT ≤2.5 x ULN (≤5 x ULN if hepatic
  metastases); Total Bilirubin ≤1.5 x ULN (≤3 x ULN if documented
  Gilbert syndrome).

- Willing to provide archival tumor tissue (FFPE block) and, if
  accessible, agree to pre- and on-treatment biopsy per protocol
  (mandatory at sites with biopsy capability).

- Female patients of childbearing potential must have negative serum
  pregnancy test within 72 hours of first dose and agree to use highly
  effective contraception during treatment and for 5 months
  post-last-dose.

- Male patients with female partners of childbearing potential must
  agree to contraception during treatment and for 7 months
  post-last-dose.

- Provision of written informed consent prior to any study procedures.

**5.2 Exclusion Criteria**

Patients will be excluded if ANY of the following apply:

- Prior treatment with any anti-PD-1, anti-PD-L1, anti-PD-L2,
  anti-CTLA-4, or other immune checkpoint inhibitory antibody or
  pathway-targeting agent.

- History of Grade ≥3 immune-mediated adverse event (irAE) with any
  immunotherapy agent.

- Active, known, or suspected autoimmune disease requiring systemic
  therapy within 2 years (vitiligo, type 1 diabetes, hypothyroidism on
  stable replacement, psoriasis not requiring systemic therapy, and
  Sjögren syndrome without systemic involvement are permitted).

- Condition requiring systemic corticosteroids (\>10 mg/day prednisone
  equivalent) or other immunosuppressive medications within 14 days of
  first dose; inhaled/topical steroids permitted.

- Active interstitial lung disease, pneumonitis, or history of
  drug-induced Grade ≥2 pneumonitis.

- Uncontrolled or symptomatic central nervous system (CNS) metastases;
  patients with stable, previously treated CNS metastases (off
  corticosteroids ≥4 weeks; no progression on MRI for ≥4 weeks prior to
  enrollment) are eligible.

- Active or chronic hepatitis B (HBsAg+) or hepatitis C (anti-HCV+ with
  detectable HCV RNA). Patients with cured HCV (undetectable RNA ≥12
  weeks post-EOT) or treated/inactive HBV are permitted with monitoring.

- Known HIV infection with CD4 count \<350 cells/mm3 or detectable viral
  load; patients with controlled HIV (CD4 ≥350, undetectable VL, on
  stable ART ≥4 weeks) may be enrolled with medical monitor approval.

- Active second malignancy within 3 years, excluding in situ carcinomas,
  basal or squamous cell skin cancer, or early-stage prostate cancer not
  requiring systemic treatment.

- Major surgical procedure or significant trauma within 4 weeks of first
  dose (wound must be fully healed; minor surgical procedures/biopsies
  are not exclusionary).

- Prior allogeneic stem cell or solid organ transplantation.

- Systemic antineoplastic therapy, radiotherapy, or investigational
  agent within 4 weeks (or 5 half-lives, whichever is shorter) prior to
  first dose.

- Known hypersensitivity to any monoclonal antibody or IV formulation
  components.

- Pregnancy, lactation, or intent to become pregnant during study
  participation.

- Psychiatric illness, substance abuse disorder, or any other condition
  that, in the investigator\'s opinion, would preclude compliance with
  study procedures or compromise patient safety.

**6.2 Pre-medications and Supportive Care**

Routine pre-medication prior to Cervimab infusion is NOT required. In
the event of Grade 1--2 infusion reaction, the infusion should be slowed
or temporarily interrupted. For recurrent Grade 1--2 or first Grade 2
infusion reactions, prophylactic antihistamine (diphenhydramine 25--50
mg IV) and/or acetaminophen 650 mg PO may be administered 30 minutes
prior to subsequent infusions. Grade 3 infusion reactions require
permanent discontinuation.

**6.3 Prohibited Concomitant Medications**

- Any other anti-cancer agent, immunotherapy, investigational drug, or
  biologic during the treatment period.

- Systemic corticosteroids \>10 mg/day prednisone equivalent (except as
  indicated for irAE management per Section 8).

- Live attenuated vaccines within 30 days prior to or during study
  treatment.

- Strong CYP3A4 inducers or inhibitors are not expected to affect the PK
  of Cervimab but should be documented.

**8. SAFETY MONITORING AND ADVERSE EVENT MANAGEMENT**

**8.1 Definitions**

**Adverse Event (AE):** Any untoward medical occurrence in a clinical
trial participant administered a medicinal product, regardless of causal
relationship (ICH E2A).

**Serious Adverse Event (SAE):** Any AE meeting any of the following:
death, life-threatening event, hospitalization or prolonged
hospitalization, persistent significant disability/incapacity,
congenital anomaly/birth defect, or any other medically important event.

**Dose-Limiting Toxicity (DLT):** A drug-related adverse event meeting
pre-specified criteria within the DLT assessment window (see Section
8.2).

**Suspected Unexpected Serious Adverse Reaction (SUSAR):** An SAE that
is unexpected per the IB and suspected to be related to Cervimab.
Expedited reporting to FDA per 21 CFR 312.32.

**8.2 Dose-Limiting Toxicity Criteria**

A DLT is defined as any of the following events occurring within the DLT
assessment window (28 days post-dose for Part A; Cycle 1 for Part B)
that is assessed as related (possibly, probably, or definitely) to
Cervimab, per NCI CTCAE v5.0:

- Any Grade ≥4 hematologic toxicity lasting \>7 days (except lymphopenia
  without clinical consequence).

- Any Grade ≥3 non-hematologic toxicity lasting \>3 days (Grade 3
  nausea/vomiting/diarrhea adequately controlled with standard
  antiemetic/antidiarrheal therapy within 72 hours is NOT a DLT).

- Grade 3 immune-mediated toxicity (colitis, hepatitis, pneumonitis,
  nephritis, endocrinopathy) not resolving to ≤Grade 1 within 14 days
  despite high-dose corticosteroids per institutional irAE protocols.

- Any Grade ≥4 immune-mediated adverse event.

- Any Grade 3 infusion-related reaction.

- Any Grade ≥3 elevation of AST/ALT (\>5 x ULN) or bilirubin (\>3 x ULN)
  possibly related to drug.

- Any other Grade ≥3 toxicity that is clinically significant in the
  medical judgment of the investigator and Sponsor medical monitor.

- Any toxicity resulting in \>21-day delay of the next scheduled dose
  (Part B only).

**8.3 irAE Management Algorithm**

Immune-mediated adverse events (irAEs) are managed per the ASCO Clinical
Practice Guideline on Management of Immune-Related Adverse Events in
Patients Treated with Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor Therapy (2021 update)
and per site-specific treatment guidelines. General principles:

- Grade 1: Continue Cervimab with close monitoring; symptomatic
  management. Endocrinopathies require hormone replacement.

- Grade 2: Withhold Cervimab; initiate oral prednisone 0.5--1 mg/kg/day
  (or equivalent); resume when resolved to ≤Grade 1 and steroids tapered
  to ≤10 mg/day. Withhold if not resolved within 6 weeks.

- Grade 3: Discontinue Cervimab; initiate methylprednisolone 1--2
  mg/kg/day IV; if no improvement in 48 hours, consider additional
  immunosuppression (infliximab, mycophenolate mofetil) per
  organ-specific protocols.

- Grade 4: Permanently discontinue Cervimab; high-dose IV
  corticosteroids; multidisciplinary subspecialty consultation.

**8.4 SAE Reporting**

All SAEs and SUSARs are reported within 24 hours of investigator
awareness to the Sponsor Safety Team. Fatal or life-threatening SUSARs
are reported to FDA within 7 calendar days (21 CFR 312.32).
Non-fatal/non-life-threatening SUSARs are reported within 15 calendar
days. Annual IND safety reports submitted per 21 CFR 312.33.

**9. STATISTICAL CONSIDERATIONS**

**9.1 Sample Size**

The study is not powered for statistical hypothesis testing; sample
sizes are based on the standard 3+3 dose-escalation design. Part A
anticipates enrollment of 3--6 patients per cohort across up to 6 dose
levels (maximum 36 patients). Part B anticipates 3--6 patients per
cohort across 3 dose levels (maximum 18 patients). Total planned
enrollment: approximately 54 patients.

**9.2 Safety Analysis**

All patients receiving at least one dose of Cervimab will be included in
the safety-evaluable population. AEs will be coded using MedDRA (current
version) and graded by NCI CTCAE v5.0. Incidence of AEs, SAEs, DLTs, and
treatment discontinuations due to AEs will be summarized by system organ
class, preferred term, and maximum grade using frequency tables. No
formal statistical comparisons will be performed.

**9.3 PK Analysis**

PK parameters will be calculated by NCA using Phoenix WinNonlin or
equivalent validated software. Descriptive statistics (n, mean, SD, CV%,
geometric mean, median, min, max) will be presented by cohort. Dose
proportionality will be assessed by power model analysis (Cmax and AUC
vs. dose). Population PK (popPK) modeling will be conducted using NONMEM
to characterize inter- and intra-individual variability and to support
exposure-response analyses.

**9.4 Interim Analyses**

The DSMB will conduct ongoing reviews of cumulative safety data
following completion of each cohort DLT assessment window. Formal
interim analyses are not planned; the DSMB may recommend study
modifications or termination at any time based on safety signals (see
DSMB Charter, separate document).
