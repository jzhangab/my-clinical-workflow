**1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE**

This Dose Escalation Plan (DEP) provides a systematic framework
governing the escalation, de-escalation, and selection of doses of
Cervimab (anti-PD-1 IgG4 monoclonal antibody) in the Phase 1
first-in-human (FIH) SAD/MAD study (Protocol CERV-001-SAD/MAD). This
document must be read in conjunction with the Clinical Study Protocol,
the Investigator\'s Brochure (IB), and the Data and Safety Monitoring
Board (DSMB) Charter.

The DEP defines: (1) dose levels and escalation increments; (2) starting
dose rationale per FDA FIH guidance; (3) escalation decision rules; (4)
DLT criteria and DLT assessment windows; (5) stopping rules and MTD/RP2D
determination criteria; and (6) mandatory DSMB data review processes.

**2. STARTING DOSE RATIONALE**

**2.1 Preclinical Basis**

The selection of the starting dose follows FDA guidance for
first-in-human dose determination for immunostimulatory biologics,
emphasizing selection based on the minimum anticipated biological effect
level (MABEL) and the no observed adverse effect level (NOAEL) in the
most sensitive relevant species.

The 1 mg/kg IV starting dose is 10-fold below the NHP NOAEL (10 mg/kg),
is above the estimated MABEL (0.1 mg/kg), and is expected to yield
measurable pharmacodynamic activity (PD-1 receptor occupancy) while
providing an adequate safety margin. This starting dose is consistent
with the approach used for approved PD-1 inhibitors during development.

**Dose Escalation Increment Rule:** Dose escalation increments are
limited to no greater than 3-fold between consecutive cohorts in the
standard 3+3 phase. Smaller increments (e.g., intermediate doses) may be
inserted at the discretion of the DSMB based on emerging safety or PK/PD
data.

**Maximum Planned Dose:** 300 mg/kg IV x1 (Cohort A6). This dose is 30x
above the NHP NOAEL and represents an extreme pharmacological saturation
scenario. Escalation to A6 is contingent on the absence of DLTs and
favorable safety profile through A5.

**4. DOSE ESCALATION DECISION RULES**

**4.1 Standard 3+3 Escalation Algorithm**

+-----------------------------------------------------------------+
| **3+3 ESCALATION DECISION TREE**                                |
|                                                                 |
| COHORT of 3 patients: 0/3 DLTs → ESCALATE \| 1/3 DLTs → EXPAND  |
| to 6 \| ≥2/3 DLTs → STOP (MTD exceeded at this dose) EXPANDED   |
| COHORT of 6: 0-1/6 DLTs → ESCALATE \| ≥2/6 DLTs → STOP (MTD =   |
| prior dose level) MTD CONFIRMATION: If MTD identified, expand   |
| prior dose to 6 patients to confirm; if 0-1/6 DLTs confirmed →  |
| MTD declared.                                                   |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------+

**4.2 Accelerated Titration (Cohorts A1 and A2 Only)**

For Cohorts A1 and A2, accelerated titration may be employed:

- Enroll 1 patient per cohort initially (if institutional SOP and DSMB
  agree at study initiation).

- If no drug-related Grade ≥2 toxicity observed within 14 days: escalate
  to next dose with 1 patient.

- If drug-related Grade 2 toxicity occurs: expand current cohort to 3
  patients (standard 3+3 resumes).

- If drug-related Grade ≥3 toxicity occurs in any patient: expand cohort
  to 6 patients (standard 3+3 rules apply).

**4.3 Intra-Patient Dose Escalation**

Intra-patient dose escalation is NOT permitted in Part A (SAD). In Part
B (MAD), intra-patient dose escalation is NOT permitted within a cohort
cycle. After completing all 4 cycles at the assigned dose level,
patients demonstrating clinical benefit and no drug-related toxicity
\>Grade 1 may optionally receive continued treatment at the same dose
level in an extension phase pending amendment.

**4.4 DSMB Review Process Before Each Escalation**

Before enrollment of any new dose cohort (Part A or B), the DSMB must
convene a formal safety review meeting (in person or by teleconference)
within 7 business days of completion of the DLT assessment window for
the current cohort. The DSMB review package includes:

- Individual patient DLT listings with narrative descriptions and
  causality assessments.

- AE summaries by system organ class and grade (all patients enrolled to
  date).

- Preliminary PK parameters (Cmax, AUC, t1/2) for current and all prior
  cohorts.

- PD-1 receptor occupancy data by dose level.

- Infusion reaction, cytokine, and clinical laboratory summaries.

- Proposed dose for next cohort and justification.

The DSMB renders a written recommendation within 3 business days of the
review meeting: Escalate / Hold pending additional data / De-escalate /
Stop. Escalation cannot proceed without documented DSMB written
approval.

**6. MTD, RP2D, AND STOPPING RULES**

**6.1 Maximum Tolerated Dose (MTD) Definition**

The MTD is defined as the highest dose level at which ≤1/6 evaluable
patients experiences a DLT within the DLT assessment window, immediately
below the dose level at which ≥2/6 patients experience DLTs. The MTD
must be confirmed in ≥6 evaluable patients.

**6.2 Recommended Phase 2 Dose (RP2D)**

The RP2D will be determined by the DSMB and Sponsor in a formal RP2D
selection meeting following completion of all dose escalation cohorts.
The RP2D will integrate the following data:

- Safety: DLT incidence and severity across all cohorts; cumulative
  Grade ≥2 AEs in repeat-dose cohorts.

- PK: Dose proportionality assessment; predicted Css at Q3W dosing;
  target therapeutic exposure range.

- PD: PD-1 receptor occupancy (target: ≥80% persistent RO); tumor
  biomarker changes on treatment biopsies.

- Preliminary Efficacy: Any objective responses or stable disease
  observed in Part B cohorts (per RECIST 1.1).

- Precedent: Approved PD-1 inhibitor dose levels (typically 200 mg flat
  dose or 2 mg/kg Q3W) considered as external benchmark.

The RP2D may be lower than the MTD if pharmacodynamic plateau (PD-1
receptor saturation) is reached at a dose below MTD, consistent with FDA
guidance that biological activity, not toxicity, should drive dose
selection for immunomodulatory agents.

**6.3 Mandatory Study Stopping Rules**

+-----------------------------------------------------------------+
| **IMMEDIATE STUDY HOLD --- MANDATORY**                          |
|                                                                 |
| The study must be immediately placed on clinical hold (all      |
| enrollment and dosing suspended) and the FDA notified within 24 |
| hours if ANY of the following events occur: 1. Any patient      |
| death possibly related to Cervimab. 2. ≥2 patients in any       |
| cohort experiencing Grade 4 immune-mediated AEs (colitis,       |
| hepatitis, pneumonitis, or neurotoxicity) simultaneously. 3.    |
| Any Grade 5 (fatal) AE suspected to be related to study         |
| treatment. 4. Any unexpected serious safety signal not covered  |
| by the protocol that, in the Sponsor medical monitor\'s         |
| opinion, requires immediate cessation of dosing. 5. Cytokine    |
| Release Syndrome ≥Grade 4 in any patient.                       |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------+

For events not meeting mandatory hold criteria, the DSMB may recommend a
voluntary hold pending safety review at any time. The DSMB has
unilateral authority to recommend study termination per its Charter.

**6.4 Dose De-escalation**

If a dose level is declared to have exceeded the MTD, the Sponsor and
DSMB will review all available data and may: (a) re-enroll at the prior
dose level to expand the cohort to 6 patients for MTD confirmation; (b)
propose an intermediate dose between the exceeded level and the prior
level if PK data suggest a potentially safer sub-therapeutic increment;
(c) declare the prior dose level as the MTD without further escalation.

**7. EVALUABILITY FOR DLT ASSESSMENT**

A patient is considered DLT-evaluable if:

- Part A: Received the full planned single dose of Cervimab AND
  completed the full 28-day DLT observation period without major
  protocol deviations preventing safety assessment, OR experienced a DLT
  at any time during the window.

- Part B: Received ≥80% of the planned Cycle 1 dose (i.e., completed
  infusion) AND completed at least 18 of the 21-day observation window,
  OR experienced a DLT.

Patients who are not DLT-evaluable due to early withdrawal for reasons
unrelated to drug toxicity (e.g., disease progression, withdrawal of
consent, non-drug-related SAE) must be replaced to ensure ≥3 evaluable
patients per cohort before escalation decisions are made.

**9. SPECIAL ESCALATION CONSIDERATIONS**

**9.1 Cytokine Release Syndrome (CRS)**

Given the mechanism of action of Cervimab as a T-cell activator, CRS is
an anticipated risk, particularly at higher dose levels or in patients
with high tumor burden. CRS will be graded using the ASTCT Consensus
Criteria (Lee et al., 2019). Grade 1--2 CRS is NOT a DLT and is managed
with supportive care per site protocols. Grade 3 CRS IS a DLT. Grade 4
CRS triggers an immediate study hold.

**9.2 Immune-Related AEs (irAEs) --- Escalation Watchpoint**

Given that irAEs may develop after the formal DLT window closes
(particularly thyroiditis, adrenal insufficiency, and Type 1 diabetes
mellitus), emerging irAEs in patients from prior cohorts will be
reviewed at each DSMB meeting regardless of DLT window status. A pattern
of late-onset Grade 3--4 irAEs across cohorts may prompt DSMB to
recommend a halt to escalation even without formal DLTs.

**9.3 Sentinel Dosing Strategy**

For Cohorts A3--A6 (≥10 mg/kg), a sentinel dosing strategy will be
employed: the first patient in each cohort will be dosed and observed
for a minimum of 7 days before the second patient is dosed, and the
second patient observed for 7 days before the third patient is dosed.
This staggered approach allows early safety signals to be detected
before the full cohort is enrolled.
