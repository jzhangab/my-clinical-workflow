**1. INTRODUCTION AND PURPOSE**

The Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) --- also referred to as the
Safety Review Committee (SRC) or Data Monitoring Committee (DMC) --- is
an independent expert body established by the Sponsor to provide ongoing
oversight of participant safety and data integrity for the Cervimab
Phase 1 First-in-Human SAD/MAD clinical study (Protocol
CERV-001-SAD/MAD).

This Charter defines the DSMB\'s purpose, composition, independence
requirements, responsibilities, decision-making authority, meeting
cadence, data access procedures, reporting obligations, and
confidentiality requirements. This Charter is a legally binding
agreement between DSMB members and the Sponsor and governs all DSMB
activities for the duration of the study.

Given the investigational nature of Cervimab as a first-in-human
immunostimulatory PD-1 inhibitor, the DSMB has heightened
responsibilities to protect study participants from unforeseen
immune-mediated toxicity, cytokine release, and other
immune-oncology-related adverse events throughout the dose-escalation
phases.

**2.2 Independence Requirements**

DSMB members must be fully independent of the Sponsor, Principal
Investigators, sub-investigators, CRO, contract statisticians, and any
entity with a financial interest in the study outcome. The following
relationships are DISQUALIFYING:

- Current or pending employment, consulting, or advisory relationships
  with the Sponsor.

- Equity ownership, stock options, or financial instruments related to
  the Sponsor or competitive companies.

- Serving as investigator or sub-investigator in this study or any
  related Cervimab study.

- Prior professional or personal relationships that could compromise
  objectivity.

All DSMB members must submit a signed Conflict of Interest (COI)
Disclosure Form prior to the first meeting and annually thereafter. COI
disclosures will be reviewed by the Sponsor Legal/Compliance team and a
determination of independence confirmed in writing before each meeting.

**2.3 Confidential Statistical Analyst**

An Independent Statistical Analyst (ISA), who is neither the trial
statistician nor the Sponsor\'s internal statistician, will be appointed
to prepare unblinded statistical reports for closed DSMB sessions. The
ISA is bound by the same confidentiality obligations as DSMB members and
signs a Data Access and Confidentiality Agreement.

**2.4 DSMB Support (Non-Voting Attendees)**

The following Sponsor representatives may attend open sessions of DSMB
meetings but are EXCLUDED from closed sessions:

- Sponsor Medical Monitor (Chief Medical Officer or designee)

- Clinical Development Lead / Study Team Lead

- Regulatory Affairs representative (as needed)

- CRO Project Manager (at discretion of DSMB Chair)

**3. DSMB RESPONSIBILITIES AND AUTHORITY**

**3.1 Core Responsibilities**

The DSMB has the following core responsibilities throughout the duration
of Protocol CERV-001-SAD/MAD:

1.  Review cumulative safety data (AEs, SAEs, irAEs, DLTs, laboratory
    findings, PK data, immunogenicity data) following completion of each
    dose cohort DLT assessment window and at scheduled interim safety
    reviews.

2.  Render formal written escalation decisions (Proceed / Hold /
    De-escalate / Stop) prior to initiation of each new dose cohort, in
    accordance with the Dose Escalation Plan (Document
    CERV-001-DEP-001).

3.  Identify emerging safety signals, unexpected toxicity patterns, or
    evidence of unacceptable harm and communicate findings to the
    Sponsor Medical Monitor in a timely manner.

4.  Advise the Sponsor on modifications to the DLT assessment window,
    sentinel dosing strategy, or protocol procedures if emerging safety
    data warrant.

5.  Review all SUSARs (Suspected Unexpected Serious Adverse Reactions)
    as they occur between scheduled meetings.

6.  Provide input on MTD determination and RP2D selection based on
    integrated safety, PK, and PD data.

7.  Recommend study termination if participant safety is unacceptably
    compromised, following the stopping rules in the Dose Escalation
    Plan.

8.  Review and adjudicate disputed AE causality assessments when the
    investigator and Sponsor medical monitor disagree.

9.  Ensure that participants are not subjected to undue risk beyond the
    accepted benefit-risk profile for the indication.

**4.2 Meeting Procedures --- Open and Closed Sessions**

Each routine safety review meeting will consist of two distinct
sessions:

**OPEN SESSION:** Sponsor attendees permitted. The Principal
Investigator or Sponsor Medical Monitor presents a summary of
enrollment, protocol adherence, AE overview, outstanding data queries,
and any operational issues. DSMB members may ask questions. Individual
patient-level data and unblinded group comparisons are NOT presented in
the Open Session.

**CLOSED SESSION:** DSMB members and Independent Statistical Analyst
ONLY. Sponsor representatives must leave the meeting room/call. Detailed
patient-level safety listings, unblinded cumulative AE tables, PK data
by cohort, PD biomarker summaries, and DLT assessments are reviewed.
DSMB deliberation and vote occurs in Closed Session. All Closed Session
discussions are strictly confidential.

**4.3 Quorum Requirements**

- Routine and Interim Reviews: All five voting members, or a minimum of
  four (4) of five voting members required (a quorum). In case of
  non-attendance, the absent member must provide written input prior to
  meeting.

- Emergency Meetings: Minimum of three (3) voting members including the
  Chairperson (or designated co-chair) constitutes quorum. Remaining
  members must provide written concurrence within 48 hours.

- Organizational and RP2D meetings: All voting members required; no
  proxy attendance.

**4.4 Voting and Recommendation Protocol**

DSMB decisions shall be reached by simple majority vote among attending
voting members. In case of tie, the Chairperson casts the deciding vote.
The Chairperson communicates the DSMB recommendation to the Sponsor
Medical Monitor verbally within 2 hours of meeting close, followed by
formal written communication within 3 business days. Dissenting opinions
are documented in the meeting minutes.

Recommendations shall be issued using the following formal language:

- PROCEED --- No dose-limiting toxicity or safety concerns precluding
  escalation; escalation to the next dose level approved.

- HOLD --- Escalation suspended pending additional data collection,
  query resolution, or further DSMB review within \[specified
  timeframe\].

- MODIFY --- Escalation approved subject to specified protocol
  modifications (e.g., extended DLT window, reduced increment,
  additional monitoring, sentinel dosing).

- DE-ESCALATE --- Return to prior dose level; current dose level
  discontinued.

- STOP --- Study terminated; all dosing ceased immediately; Sponsor
  notified for FDA communication.

**5.2 Emergency Safety Notifications**

The Sponsor Medical Monitor must notify the DSMB Chairperson within 24
hours of becoming aware of any of the following:

- Any patient death (on-study or within 90-day follow-up period)
  possibly related to Cervimab.

- Any Grade 4 or 5 treatment-emergent adverse event (CTCAE v5.0) that is
  possibly related.

- Any SUSAR (fatal or non-fatal/non-life-threatening).

- Any FDA Information Request, Clinical Hold, or Safety-Related IND
  Amendment.

- Any event triggering a mandatory study hold per the Dose Escalation
  Plan.

Emergency notifications will be delivered by phone (Chairperson) and
email (all members simultaneously) and include: patient identifier,
cohort and dose level, event description, CTCAE grade, causality
assessment, and any immediate actions taken.

**5.3 DSMB Meeting Minutes and Recommendations**

Detailed minutes of all DSMB meetings (open and closed sessions) will be
drafted by a designated DSMB Secretary (provided by the Sponsor,
excluded from closed sessions --- minutes from closed sessions drafted
by the Chairperson or designated DSMB member). Minutes will include:
attendance, quorum confirmation, topics discussed, vote tallies, formal
recommendations, and action items. Meeting minutes are finalized and
signed by the Chairperson within 10 business days of each meeting and
retained in the Trial Master File.

**6. STOPPING RULES AND ESCALATION DECISIONS**

**6.1 Mandatory Escalation Conditions**

The DSMB MUST approve escalation before any new cohort is initiated.
Escalation may be approved if ALL of the following conditions are met:

- DLT assessment window complete for all evaluable patients in the
  current cohort.

- DLT incidence below the threshold for MTD declaration (≤1/6 DLTs per
  standard 3+3 rules).

- No emerging safety signals, SAEs, or serious irAEs that, in the
  DSMB\'s judgment, warrant additional observation before escalation.

- PK data reviewed and no anomalous or unexpected PK findings (e.g.,
  non-proportional exposure, unusually prolonged t1/2) that would
  warrant extended DLT observation.

- Written DSMB recommendation issued; Sponsor acknowledgment received.

**6.3 Criteria for Study Termination**

The DSMB may recommend permanent study termination if:

- The evidence of harm from Cervimab is overwhelming and a compelling
  benefit-risk case for continued investigation cannot be established.

- The MTD is determined to be at or below the estimated MABEL level,
  rendering the therapeutic window unacceptably narrow.

- Nonclinical safety findings arise (from concurrent studies) that
  fundamentally alter the benefit-risk assessment.

- A pattern of serious irAEs suggests a class effect that negates the
  anticipated therapeutic benefit.

- The study cannot be conducted in accordance with GCP due to systematic
  site or data quality issues.

**7.2 Integrated Benefit-Risk Assessment**

At the RP2D selection meeting, the DSMB will conduct a formal integrated
benefit-risk assessment incorporating: cumulative safety data; PK/PD
modeling; PD-1 receptor occupancy targets; any preliminary efficacy
signals; and external clinical context (benchmark PD-1 inhibitor safety
and efficacy data from the literature). The benefit-risk assessment will
be documented in the formal DSMB RP2D Recommendation Report, which is
appended to the study record.

**8. CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA HANDLING**

**8.1 Confidentiality Obligations**

All information reviewed by the DSMB --- including unblinded patient
data, safety listings, statistical reports, and deliberation minutes ---
is strictly confidential. DSMB members agree not to disclose any study
information to parties outside the DSMB, including colleagues,
co-investigators, or external parties, except as required by law or
regulatory obligation.

All DSMB members and support staff must execute a Confidentiality and
Data Access Agreement with the Sponsor prior to receiving any study
data. These agreements will be maintained in the Trial Master File.

**8.2 Data Security**

Electronic data packages will be transmitted via a secure, encrypted
file-sharing platform designated by the Sponsor. DSMB members must use
Sponsor-provided access credentials; data packages must not be forwarded
or stored on non-encrypted devices. Physical copies of meeting materials
must be returned to the Sponsor or destroyed following each meeting.

**8.3 Communication of Decisions**

DSMB recommendations are communicated exclusively to the Sponsor Medical
Monitor (and designated Sponsor team members as specified by the Medical
Monitor). DSMB recommendations shall NOT be communicated to: site
investigators, clinical research coordinators, IRBs (unless a
safety-related protocol amendment requires IRB notification), or any
commercial or external parties. Publication or public disclosure of DSMB
deliberations is prohibited during the study. Summary information
(without patient-level data) may be included in regulatory filings.

**9.2 DSMB Annual Report to FDA**

A summary of DSMB activities and safety findings will be incorporated
into the Annual IND Safety Report submitted to FDA per 21 CFR 312.33.
The summary will include: number of DSMB meetings convened; escalation
decisions made; significant safety findings reviewed; and any
recommendations for protocol modifications.

**9.3 Charter Amendments**

This Charter may be amended by mutual agreement of the Sponsor Medical
Monitor and DSMB Chairperson. Any amendment must be documented in
writing, executed by both parties, and filed as an amendment to the
Trial Master File. Amendments that materially alter DSMB authority,
composition, or escalation criteria must be submitted to IRB as a
protocol amendment if they affect participant safety or rights.

**10. COMPENSATION AND ADMINISTRATIVE PROVISIONS**

**10.1 Compensation**

DSMB members will receive compensation per an independent consulting
agreement with the Sponsor at a rate commensurate with their level of
expertise and time commitment, as agreed prior to the first meeting.
Compensation is not contingent on study outcomes or escalation decisions
and will not exceed FDA-recommended advisory committee payment limits.
Reimbursement for travel, accommodation, and related expenses for
in-person meetings will be provided per Sponsor travel policy.

**10.2 Term**

DSMB membership commences upon execution of the Confidentiality
Agreement and terminates upon: (a) issuance of the final DSMB
Recommendation Report; (b) study termination; (c) member resignation
with 30 days written notice; or (d) removal for cause by Sponsor in
consultation with the DSMB Chairperson. Replacement of departing members
requires DSMB and Sponsor agreement and will not delay scheduled reviews
beyond 14 days.

**10.3 Publication**

DSMB members may not independently publish DSMB deliberations, unblinded
data, or meeting minutes. DSMB members may be acknowledged in study
publications and may co-author safety or methodology papers derived from
DSMB activities, subject to authorship criteria (ICMJE guidelines) and
Sponsor agreement.
