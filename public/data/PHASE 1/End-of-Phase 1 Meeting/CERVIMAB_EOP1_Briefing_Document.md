**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

OncoBioLogics, Inc. presents this briefing document to support the
End-of-Phase 1 meeting for CERVIMAB, a novel humanized anti-PD-1 IgG4
monoclonal antibody being developed for the treatment of dMMR/MSI-H
metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC). CERVIMAB has demonstrated a
clinically meaningful and durable antitumor response in the Phase 1
expansion cohort, with an ORR of 43.1% in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC patients who
had received at least one prior line of therapy, and a tolerability
profile consistent with the known class of PD-1 inhibitors.

The Sponsor proposes to advance CERVIMAB into a randomized, open-label
Phase 2 trial (CERVIMAB-CRC-002) comparing CERVIMAB 400 mg IV Q3W versus
investigator\'s choice of second-line standard-of-care chemotherapy
(FOLFIRI ± bevacizumab) in approximately 200 dMMR/MSI-H mCRC patients.
The primary endpoint is progression-free survival (PFS) per RECIST v1.1
by BICR. This briefing document provides a comprehensive summary of the
data supporting Phase 2 initiation and the specific questions for FDA
discussion.

Population PK modeling (one-compartment model with first-order
elimination) identified body weight as the only significant covariate;
however, the impact on exposure was not clinically meaningful,
supporting flat dosing at 400 mg Q3W. No dose adjustment is required for
mild-to-moderate hepatic or renal impairment.

**1.2 Pharmacodynamics**

Pharmacodynamic assessments included serial measurements of PD-1
receptor occupancy on circulating T cells (flow cytometry), serum
cytokine profiles, and tumor-infiltrating lymphocyte (TIL) density in
paired pre- and on-treatment biopsies.

- PD-1 receptor occupancy: Near-complete (\>95%) sustained occupancy at
  doses ≥200 mg Q3W throughout the dosing interval

- Peripheral blood: Dose-dependent increases in activated CD8+ T cells
  (Ki-67+ CD8+ T cells; 3.2-fold increase at 400 mg Q3W vs. baseline)

- Tumor biopsies: Significant increase in TIL density (CD8+ T cells) in
  paired pre/on-treatment biopsies (on-treatment vs. baseline fold
  change: 2.8; p = 0.003) at 400 mg Q3W

- Serum IFN-γ: 4.1-fold increase from baseline at Cycle 2 Day 1,
  consistent with T-cell activation

- No evidence of paradoxical immunosuppression or regulatory T-cell
  expansion at any dose level

**2. NONCLINICAL DATA SUMMARY**

**2.1 In Vitro Pharmacology**

CERVIMAB demonstrated specific, high-affinity binding to human PD-1 (KD
= 0.32 nM) and cynomolgus monkey PD-1 (KD = 0.41 nM), with no detectable
binding to murine PD-1 (KD \> 1,000 nM). CERVIMAB fully blocked
PD-1/PD-L1 (IC50 = 1.2 nM) and PD-1/PD-L2 (IC50 = 1.8 nM) interactions,
restoring T-cell proliferation and IFN-γ production in PD-L1-expressing
co-culture assays. No agonist activity was observed.

**2.3 Genotoxicity and Other Studies**

No genotoxicity studies were conducted, consistent with FDA guidance for
monoclonal antibodies (ICH S6(R1); mAbs do not interact with DNA). A
tissue cross-reactivity (TCR) study in normal human tissues demonstrated
binding restricted to PD-1-expressing immune cells with no unexpected
off-target staining. No carcinogenicity studies are planned per ICH
S6(R1).

**3. PHASE 1 CLINICAL DATA**

**3.1 Study Design (CRV-001)**

Study CRV-001 was a Phase 1, open-label, multicenter, first-in-human
study of CERVIMAB in adult patients with advanced solid tumors. The
study comprised two parts: Part A (dose-escalation using a standard 3+3
design) and Part B (dose-expansion in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC). Key eligibility
criteria for Part B included:

- Histologically confirmed dMMR/MSI-H mCRC (central confirmation
  required)

- 1--2 prior lines of systemic therapy including at least 1
  fluoropyrimidine-based regimen

- No prior anti-PD-1, anti-PD-L1, or anti-CTLA-4 therapy

- ECOG PS 0--1

- Adequate organ function (hepatic, renal, hematologic)

- Measurable disease per RECIST v1.1

**4. CMC SUMMARY**

**4.1 Drug Substance**

CERVIMAB drug substance (DS) is produced by fed-batch cell culture using
a Chinese Hamster Ovary (CHO) cell line engineered to express the
humanized anti-PD-1 IgG4 antibody. The manufacturing process has been
validated at 2,000-L bioreactor scale at a qualified GMP contract
manufacturing organization (CMO). Key DS attributes:

- Purity (SEC-HPLC): ≥99.2% monomer; HMW species ≤0.5%

- Potency (cell-based PD-1 blocking assay): 85--115% relative potency
  specification

- Glycan profile: Consistent across ≥12 consecutive GMP lots
  (afucosylation \<5%)

- Host cell protein (HCP): ≤5 ppm

- Residual protein A: ≤2 ppm

- Container closure: Stainless steel biocontainer; DS stability
  demonstrated at -70°C ≥36 months

**4.2 Drug Product**

CERVIMAB drug product (DP) is formulated at 10 mg/mL in 25 mM histidine,
150 mM NaCl, 0.02% polysorbate 80, pH 5.8. The DP is filled into 30 mL
Type I borosilicate glass vials (50 mg/5 mL per vial). DP stability has
been established for 24 months at 2--8°C; an in-use stability of 6 hours
at room temperature and 24 hours at 2--8°C post-dilution (0.9% NaCl) has
been demonstrated.

**4.3 Process Change and Comparability**

A scale-up manufacturing change was implemented between Phase 1 (200-L)
and proposed Phase 2 (2,000-L) production. Comprehensive analytical
comparability testing was performed per ICH Q5E, demonstrating
equivalence in CQAs including glycan profile, charge variant
distribution, potency, and binding kinetics. PK bridging data in
cynomolgus monkeys (N=6/group) confirmed comparable exposure profiles
(geometric mean Cmax ratio: 1.04; AUC ratio: 1.06; 90% CIs within
80--125% bounds).

**5. PROPOSED PHASE 2 STRATEGY**

**5.1 Rationale for Advancing to Phase 2**

The Sponsor believes the totality of Phase 1 evidence strongly supports
Phase 2 advancement:

- Clinically meaningful and durable ORR of 43.1% in a patient population
  with unmet need and no current approved second-line immunotherapy
  standard of care

- Favorable safety profile: Grade ≥3 TRAE rate of 23.1%, no
  treatment-related deaths, treatment discontinuation rate of 6.2%

- Near-complete, sustained PD-1 receptor occupancy at RP2D, providing
  mechanistic confidence in dose selection

- Emerging OS signal (12-month OS rate of 68.2%) with median OS not yet
  reached

- Durable responses with 79% of responders still in response at 12
  months

**5.3 Statistical Considerations**

The primary analysis will compare PFS between treatment arms using a
stratified log-rank test (alpha = 0.05, two-sided). The study is powered
at 90% to detect a hazard ratio of 0.55 (corresponding to an improvement
in median PFS from approximately 4.5 months on SOC to approximately 8.2
months on CERVIMAB). Based on KEYNOTE-177 and available real-world data
for FOLFIRI in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC patients who progressed on first-line
therapy, the assumed control arm PFS is conservative.

An interim analysis for futility is planned when approximately 50% of
PFS events have occurred (O\'Brien-Fleming boundaries). No interim
analysis for superiority is planned given the Phase 2 confirmatory
intent; OS data will be collected through the end of the study as a key
secondary endpoint and to power the Phase 3 confirmatory trial OS
analysis.

**6. REGULATORY PATHWAY DISCUSSION**

**6.1 Accelerated Approval**

The Sponsor believes CERVIMAB is eligible for Accelerated Approval under
21 CFR 601.41, based on PFS as a reasonably likely surrogate endpoint.
Precedent exists within dMMR/MSI-H solid tumors (pembrolizumab initial
approval in this indication under Accelerated Approval based on
ORR/DoR). The proposed Phase 2 is specifically designed to generate the
confirmatory evidence required, with OS as the primary endpoint of the
planned confirmatory Phase 3.

**6.2 Breakthrough Therapy Designation**

Based on the Phase 1 ORR of 43.1% with 79% ongoing response at 12 months
in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC patients who received at least one prior line of
therapy, and given the absence of approved immunotherapy options
specifically in the second-line setting following progression on
first-line checkpoint inhibitor in BTD-naïve patients (note: CERVIMAB
targets a patient population without prior anti-PD-1 therapy), the
Sponsor will submit a formal BTD request contemporaneously with this IND
amendment. FDA feedback at the EOP1 meeting on the evidentiary basis for
BTD would be welcomed.

**6.3 Special Protocol Assessment**

The Sponsor is interested in pursuing a Special Protocol Assessment
(SPA) for the Phase 2 protocol. Given that CERVIMAB-CRC-002 is designed
both as a pivotal trial for Accelerated Approval and to generate key
operational data informing Phase 3, alignment with FDA on the protocol
under an SPA would provide important regulatory certainty. The Sponsor
requests FDA\'s view on SPA eligibility and timing.
