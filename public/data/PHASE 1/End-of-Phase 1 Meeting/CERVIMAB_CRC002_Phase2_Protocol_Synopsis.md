**3. STUDY POPULATION**

**3.1 Key Inclusion Criteria**

- Age ≥ 18 years at the time of informed consent

- Histologically or cytologically confirmed metastatic or locally
  advanced unresectable colorectal adenocarcinoma

- dMMR/MSI-H status confirmed by central laboratory testing using an
  FDA-approved companion diagnostic (VENTANA MMR RxDx Panel or
  FoundationOne CDx) on tumor tissue from primary or metastatic site

- Received exactly 1 prior line of systemic therapy for metastatic
  disease; must have received a fluoropyrimidine and
  oxaliplatin-containing regimen (FOLFOX, CAPOX, or equivalent)

- Radiographically confirmed disease progression during or following the
  last prior therapy

- No prior treatment with anti-PD-1, anti-PD-L1, anti-PD-L2,
  anti-CTLA-4, or any other checkpoint inhibitor

- Measurable disease per RECIST v1.1 (≥1 target lesion; prior irradiated
  lesions not acceptable as target unless confirmed progression
  post-radiation)

- ECOG Performance Status 0 or 1

- Adequate bone marrow function: ANC ≥1,500/μL, platelets ≥100,000/μL,
  Hgb ≥9 g/dL

- Adequate hepatic function: AST/ALT ≤2.5x ULN (≤5x ULN if liver
  metastases), total bilirubin ≤1.5x ULN

- Adequate renal function: eGFR ≥30 mL/min/1.73m² (CKD-EPI)

- Adequate coagulation: INR ≤1.5 (≤3.0 if on therapeutic anticoagulation
  for non-study indications)

- Women of childbearing potential (WOCBP): Negative serum pregnancy test
  within 72 hours prior to first dose; must agree to highly effective
  contraception during treatment and for 6 months after last dose

- Males with female partners of childbearing potential: Must agree to
  use barrier contraception during treatment and for 90 days after last
  dose

- Ability to understand and willingness to sign written informed
  consent; must be able to comply with study visits and procedures

**3.2 Key Exclusion Criteria**

- Active known or suspected autoimmune disease (exceptions: type I
  diabetes mellitus, autoimmune hypothyroidism on stable hormone
  replacement, vitiligo, psoriasis not requiring systemic treatment,
  Sjögren\'s syndrome without major organ involvement)

- Active systemic therapy with corticosteroids (\>10 mg/day prednisone
  or equivalent) or any other immunosuppressive medication within 14
  days prior to randomization

- Known active hepatitis B virus (HBV) or hepatitis C virus (HCV)
  infection (chronic inactive infection permitted if viral load
  undetectable)

- Known HIV infection with CD4 count \<350 cells/μL or on antiretroviral
  therapy that is not compatible with study drug PK

- History of interstitial lung disease (ILD), non-infectious
  pneumonitis, or other lung conditions requiring corticosteroids

- Active CNS metastases or leptomeningeal carcinomatosis (treated,
  stable CNS metastases permitted if asymptomatic, no edema on MRI, no
  corticosteroids for ≥28 days, no seizures for ≥60 days)

- Prior organ transplant or allogeneic hematopoietic stem cell
  transplant

- Live attenuated vaccine within 30 days prior to first dose or planned
  during study participation

- Unresolved toxicity from prior therapy \> Grade 1 per CTCAE v5.0
  (except alopecia, peripheral neuropathy Grade ≤2)

- Known hypersensitivity to any component of CERVIMAB formulation or to
  monoclonal antibody therapy

- Pregnancy or breastfeeding

- Any psychiatric disorder, substance use disorder, or other condition
  that in the Investigator\'s judgment would impair the patient\'s
  ability to comply with study procedures or increase the safety risk

- Participation in another interventional clinical study within 4 weeks
  prior to randomization (observational studies or long-term follow-up
  studies without active treatment permitted)

- Prior malignancy within 3 years (exceptions: curatively treated basal
  or squamous cell carcinoma, in situ carcinoma of any site, or low-risk
  prostate cancer on active surveillance)

**4.2 Crossover**

Crossover from Arm B to Arm A is not permitted following primary
progression. Patients in Arm B who discontinue study treatment for
reasons other than PD (e.g., unacceptable toxicity) may receive
subsequent therapy at the discretion of the treating physician, but this
will be captured as a secondary resistance endpoint. Patients in Arm A
who achieve a CR and complete 2 years of therapy may be re-treated upon
confirmed radiographic progression per protocol-specified criteria.

**4.3 Permitted Concomitant Medications**

Supportive care medications are permitted per institutional practice,
including antiemetics, G-CSF (for febrile neutropenia prophylaxis in Arm
B per ASCO guidelines), bisphosphonates/denosumab for bone metastases,
and analgesics. Systemic corticosteroids are permitted for irAE
management per the irAE management algorithm in the full protocol.
Corticosteroids for other indications at doses \>10 mg/day prednisone
equivalent require medical monitor approval.

**5. STUDY ASSESSMENTS**

**5.1 Screening and Baseline**

Screening period: up to 28 days prior to randomization. Required
assessments include: informed consent; medical history and physical
examination; ECOG PS; vital signs; 12-lead ECG; CT/MRI
chest-abdomen-pelvis (baseline tumor imaging); brain MRI (if clinically
indicated); central dMMR/MSI-H testing (mandatory from archival or fresh
biopsy tissue); laboratory panels (CBC with diff, CMP, LFTs, TFTs,
lipase, amylase, HbA1c, urinalysis, urine pregnancy test); serology
(HIV, HBV, HCV); EORTC QLQ-C30 and EQ-5D-5L baseline PROs; archival FFPE
tumor block or 15 unstained slides (for biomarker analysis); and fresh
tumor biopsy (strongly encouraged; mandatory at sites with accessible
lesions).

**6. STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN OVERVIEW**

**6.1 Primary Analysis**

The primary efficacy analysis will compare PFS between Arm A (CERVIMAB)
and Arm B (SOC) in the intent-to-treat (ITT) population using a
stratified log-rank test (stratification factors: prior bevacizumab use
\[yes/no\] and geographic region \[North America/Western
Europe/Other\]). Hazard ratios and 95% confidence intervals will be
estimated using a stratified Cox proportional hazards model with the
same stratification factors. The Kaplan-Meier method will be used for
PFS curve estimation and median PFS with 95% CIs.

Power and sample size: A sample size of approximately 200 patients (100
per arm) provides approximately 90% power to detect an HR of 0.55
(corresponding to a median PFS improvement from 4.5 months \[Arm B;
based on historical data for FOLFIRI in second-line dMMR/MSI-H mCRC\] to
approximately 8.2 months \[Arm A\]) at a two-sided alpha level of 0.05,
assuming 145 events have occurred by the time of the primary analysis.

**6.2 Secondary and Sensitivity Analyses**

OS will be analyzed using the same stratified log-rank test approach.
ORR and DCR will be analyzed using logistic regression with confidence
intervals from the normal approximation. DoR will be analyzed by
Kaplan-Meier in the subset of patients achieving confirmed response.
Pre-specified sensitivity analyses include: (a) PFS by investigator
assessment, (b) PFS with censoring for subsequent anticancer therapies,
(c) OS in patients without crossover. Subgroup analyses are
pre-specified for key biomarker subgroups (TMB-H vs. TMB-L; PD-L1 CPS ≥1
vs. \<1; KRAS/NRAS/BRAF status) and clinical subgroups (age ≥65, prior
bevacizumab, ECOG 0 vs. 1, tumor sidedness).

**6.3 Interim Analysis**

A single interim analysis for futility will be conducted when
approximately 50% of the required PFS events (n ≈ 73 events) have
accrued. The futility boundary is set at a conditional power threshold
of \<20% of rejecting the null hypothesis at the final analysis. No
stopping for superiority is planned at interim. An independent Data
Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) will review safety data approximately
every 6 months throughout the study.

**7. SAFETY MONITORING AND REPORTING**

**7.1 Adverse Event Management**

All TEAEs will be graded using NCI CTCAE v5.0. Immune-related adverse
events (irAEs) will be managed according to a standardized irAE
management algorithm (provided as a separate appendix to the full
protocol), which is based on ASCO and NCCN guidelines for immune
checkpoint inhibitor toxicity management. Algorithms are provided for:
immune-mediated colitis, hepatitis, pneumonitis, endocrinopathies
(thyroid, adrenal, pituitary), nephritis, dermatologic reactions, and
neurologic irAEs.

**7.2 Reporting Requirements**

SUSARs (Suspected Unexpected Serious Adverse Reactions) will be reported
to FDA within 7 days (life-threatening) or 15 days
(non-life-threatening) per 21 CFR 312.32. All SAEs will be reported to
the Sponsor within 24 hours of the site becoming aware. Annual IND
safety reports will be submitted per 21 CFR 312.33. All sites will
report to their local IRB/IEC per applicable regulations.

**7.3 Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB)**

An independent DSMB comprising at least 5 members (2 oncologists with
GI/immunotherapy expertise, 1 biostatistician, 1 patient advocate, 1
safety scientist) will be convened. The DSMB charter (separate document)
defines meeting frequency (semi-annual or triggered by safety signals),
stopping rules, and voting procedures. DSMB recommendations will be
shared with FDA.

**8. BIOMARKER AND COMPANION DIAGNOSTIC STRATEGY**

**8.1 Required Biomarker (Enrollment Criterion)**

Central confirmation of dMMR/MSI-H status is a mandatory enrollment
criterion. The following FDA-approved diagnostic tests will be used at
central laboratories designated by the Sponsor:

- VENTANA MMR RxDx Panel (Roche Diagnostics) --- IHC for MLH1, MSH2,
  MSH6, PMS2 on FFPE tissue

- FoundationOne CDx (Foundation Medicine) --- NGS-based MSI and
  biomarker analysis

Local dMMR/MSI-H testing may be used for pre-screening purposes;
however, all patients must have central confirmation prior to
randomization. Patients with discordant local vs. central results (dMMR
by local, MMR-proficient by central) will be excluded and their cases
reviewed by a biomarker review committee.

**8.2 Exploratory Biomarkers**

The following exploratory biomarkers will be evaluated in a
pre-specified translational research plan (separate document):

- Tumor mutational burden (TMB) --- FoundationOne CDx; TMB-H defined as
  ≥10 mut/Mb

- PD-L1 CPS --- Dako 22C3 pharmDx; scored by central pathologist

- T-cell infiltration (CD8+ TIL density) --- IHC, multiplex
  immunofluorescence (mIF)

- ctDNA (plasma cfDNA) --- FoundationOne Liquid CDx for molecular
  response and MRD assessment

- ctMSI --- liquid biopsy-based MSI determination for longitudinal
  monitoring

- TCR clonality --- bulk TCR sequencing (Adaptive Biotechnologies
  ImmunoSEQ)

- RNA-seq transcriptomic profiling --- pre-treatment biopsies for immune
  gene expression signatures

**9. ETHICAL AND REGULATORY CONSIDERATIONS**

**9.1 Informed Consent**

Written informed consent will be obtained from all patients prior to any
study-specific procedures. The consent process will comply with ICH
E6(R2), 21 CFR 50, and applicable local regulations. A patient-friendly
lay summary will be provided. Patients will be informed of the
investigational nature of CERVIMAB, the risks of immune-related adverse
events, the requirement for central biomarker testing, and the
collection and use of biological samples for research purposes.

**9.2 IRB/IEC Review**

The protocol, consent forms, and patient-facing materials require
approval from an Institutional Review Board (IRB) or Independent Ethics
Committee (IEC) at each site prior to enrollment. Continuing review will
occur at least annually. Any protocol amendments affecting patient
safety or study procedures will require prospective IRB/IEC approval
before implementation.

**9.3 Protocol Amendments**

Protocol modifications will be handled per the Sponsor\'s standard
operating procedures and submitted to FDA as IND amendments. Substantial
protocol amendments (as defined by ICH E6(R2) and 21 CFR 312.30) will
also require prospective IRB/IEC approval. Non-substantial
administrative amendments will be documented and reported at the next
annual IND report.

**10. PUBLICATION AND DATA POLICY**

Results of this study will be submitted for publication in a
peer-reviewed journal within 12 months of the primary analysis data
lock, regardless of outcome. Interim or exploratory analyses may be
presented at scientific meetings. All publications will follow CONSORT
reporting standards. De-identified patient-level data may be shared via
a controlled access platform (e.g., Vivli) following regulatory approval
and BLA submission.
