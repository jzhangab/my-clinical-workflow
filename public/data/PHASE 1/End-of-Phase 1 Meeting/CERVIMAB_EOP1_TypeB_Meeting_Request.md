**1. PURPOSE OF MEETING**

OncoBioLogics, Inc. (\"Sponsor\") respectfully requests an End-of-Phase
1 (EOP1) Type B meeting with the FDA/CDER to discuss the clinical
development of CERVIMAB, an investigational humanized IgG4 monoclonal
antibody targeting the PD-1 checkpoint receptor, for the treatment of
patients with mismatch repair deficient (dMMR) and/or microsatellite
instability-high (MSI-H) metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC).

The Sponsor has completed Phase 1 dose-escalation and dose-expansion
studies (Study CRV-001) and is prepared to advance to a randomized Phase
2/3 program. The primary objectives of this meeting are:

- To review Phase 1 safety and efficacy data and confirm adequacy of
  dose/schedule selection for Phase 2

- To align on the proposed Phase 2 trial design, primary endpoint, and
  patient population

- To discuss the regulatory pathway, including potential for Accelerated
  Approval and Breakthrough Therapy Designation (BTD)

- To align on biomarker strategy (MSI testing, TMB, PD-L1) and companion
  diagnostic (CDx) requirements

- To confirm adequacy of the nonclinical package to support Phase 2
  initiation

**2. BACKGROUND**

**2.1 Disease Overview**

Colorectal cancer is the third most common malignancy and the second
leading cause of cancer death in the United States, with approximately
153,000 new cases and 52,000 deaths projected in 2026 (ACS). An
estimated 4--5% of all mCRC patients harbor dMMR/MSI-H tumors,
corresponding to approximately 7,500--8,500 patients annually in the
U.S. who progress on standard first-line therapy.

Despite approval of pembrolizumab (KEYNOTE-177) and nivolumab
(CheckMate-142) in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC, approximately 30--40% of patients do
not respond to currently approved PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitors, and among
initial responders, acquired resistance remains a major clinical
challenge. There is therefore significant unmet need for novel
checkpoint inhibitors with potentially differentiated mechanisms of
action, improved tolerability, and durable responses in this population.

**2.2 Drug Description**

CERVIMAB is a humanized IgG4 monoclonal antibody that binds with high
affinity and selectivity to PD-1, blocking interactions with both PD-L1
and PD-L2 ligands, thereby restoring T-cell-mediated anti-tumor
immunity. Key molecular and pharmacological features include:

**4. QUESTIONS FOR FDA**

**4.1 Phase 2 Trial Design**

1.  Does FDA agree that the proposed randomized Phase 2 trial comparing
    CERVIMAB (400 mg IV Q3W) versus standard-of-care second-line
    chemotherapy (FOLFIRI ± bevacizumab) in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC patients who
    progressed on first-line therapy constitutes an adequate and
    well-controlled study to support a biologics license application
    (BLA)?

2.  Does FDA agree that PFS per RECIST v1.1 by blinded independent
    central review (BICR) is acceptable as the primary endpoint for the
    proposed Phase 2 trial, with OS as a key secondary endpoint, to
    support Accelerated Approval followed by verification via OS in a
    confirmatory Phase 3?

3.  Does FDA concur that the proposed sample size of approximately 200
    patients (1:1 randomization; 100 per arm) provides adequate
    statistical power (90% power, two-sided alpha 0.05, assumed HR =
    0.55) to detect the anticipated treatment benefit?

**4.2 Regulatory Pathway**

4.  Has CERVIMAB received, or is it eligible for, Breakthrough Therapy
    Designation (BTD) based on Phase 1 data? If BTD has not yet been
    granted, does FDA agree that the Phase 1 data are sufficiently
    compelling to support a BTD request?

5.  Does FDA agree that the proposed Phase 2 program, if successful,
    would support an Accelerated Approval submission based on ORR/DOR or
    PFS as a reasonably likely surrogate endpoint, with OS as the
    confirmatory endpoint in a subsequent Phase 3 trial?

6.  Is a Special Protocol Assessment (SPA) for the Phase 2 protocol
    appropriate and feasible at this stage?

**4.3 Biomarker and CDx Strategy**

7.  Does FDA agree that prospective central confirmation of dMMR/MSI-H
    status by an FDA-approved companion diagnostic test (e.g., VENTANA
    MMR RxDx Panel or Foundation Medicine CDx) is required as an
    enrollment criterion, and would FDA consider allowing the use of
    validated laboratory-developed tests (LDTs) as a backup during trial
    conduct?

8.  Should TMB (tumor mutational burden) and PD-L1 CPS be co-collected
    as exploratory biomarkers, and does FDA have any specific
    recommendations on TMB thresholds for subgroup analyses?

**4.4 CMC and Nonclinical**

9.  Does FDA concur that the current nonclinical package (GLP toxicology
    studies in cynomolgus monkeys, completed DART study demonstrating no
    reproductive/developmental toxicity signal at exposures up to 10x
    the human RP2D) is adequate to support Phase 2 initiation and,
    ultimately, BLA submission?

10. Is the proposed drug product specification package (release and
    shelf-life limits) and drug substance comparability data (pre- and
    post-process change lots) adequate to support Phase 2 clinical
    manufacturing?
