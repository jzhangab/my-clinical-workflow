**1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

This Process Development Report (PDR) summarizes the pharmaceutical
development activities conducted for Cervimab Drug Product (DP), a
sterile, preservative-free, liquid formulation of an anti-PD-1
monoclonal antibody (IgG4 isotype) intended for intravenous infusion.
Cervimab targets the Programmed Cell Death Protein 1 (PD-1) receptor to
restore T-cell-mediated anti-tumor immunity and is being developed for
the treatment of mismatch repair-deficient (dMMR) / microsatellite
instability-high (MSI-H) metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC).

This report supports the Phase 1 Investigational New Drug (IND)
Application submitted to the United States Food and Drug Administration
(FDA) and documents compliance with 21 CFR Parts 210, 211, and 600
series; ICH Q8(R2), Q9, Q10; and FDA guidance documents including the
2019 CMC guidance for early-phase INDs.

**Key outcomes of Phase 1 process development include:**

- Defined a robust liquid formulation (histidine buffer, pH 6.0, sucrose
  as stabilizer, PS-80 as surfactant) supporting 18-month storage at
  2--8°C.

- Established a scalable aseptic fill-finish process at bench scale
  (2.0 L) suitable for Phase 1 manufacturing.

- Characterized critical quality attributes (CQAs) of the drug product
  through forced degradation and stress studies.

- Demonstrated container closure integrity and compatibility of Cervimab
  with Type I borosilicate glass vials and FluroTec-coated stoppers.

- Established preliminary specifications consistent with ICH Q6B and FDA
  guidance for Phase 1.

**3. FORMULATION DEVELOPMENT**

**3.1 Development Strategy and Rationale**

Formulation development was guided by the physicochemical properties of
Cervimab and industry knowledge of IgG4 mAb behavior. A Quality by
Design (QbD) approach per ICH Q8(R2) was applied, with formulation
screening focused on protein stability, minimizing aggregation, and
maintaining potency under relevant stress conditions. The target product
profile (TPP) for Phase 1 specified a ready-to-dilute liquid concentrate
for IV infusion with a minimum shelf life of 18 months at 2--8°C.

**3.2 Buffer and pH Screening**

A systematic pH and buffer screening study was conducted at 5 mg/mL
protein concentration. Accelerated stability (40°C/4 weeks) and
freeze-thaw stress (5 cycles, -80°C/+25°C) were used to rank
formulations. Three buffer systems were evaluated: citrate (pH
5.0--6.5), histidine (pH 5.5--6.5), and phosphate (pH 6.0--7.2).

**3.5 Protein Concentration Optimization**

concentrate as the optimal balance between vial count per patient and
formulation risk.

**4. DRUG PRODUCT MANUFACTURING PROCESS DEVELOPMENT**

**4.1 Process Overview**

The Cervimab DP manufacturing process consists of the following unit
operations: (1) excipient solution preparation; (2) drug substance thaw
and dilution/formulation compounding; (3) pre-filtration bioburden
reduction (0.45 μm, optional); (4) sterilizing filtration (0.22 μm, 2x
in series); (5) aseptic vial filling (time-pressure filling under ISO 5
/ Grade A LAF); (6) stoppering and aluminum capping; (7) 100% visual
inspection; (8) labeling and quarantine.

**4.2 Mixing and Hold Studies**

Mixing studies at 2.0 L scale confirmed adequate homogeneity with
overhead stirring at 50--100 rpm in a 3.0 L jacketed stainless steel
vessel. An in-use hold study was conducted to evaluate protein stability
in the formulation vessel prior to filling. Samples were collected at 0,
4, 8, and 12 hours at 2--8°C under mixing (50 rpm).

**8. CONCLUSIONS AND PHASE 1 READINESS STATEMENT**

Based on the pharmaceutical development activities summarized in this
report, Cervimab Drug Product has been successfully developed as a 20
mg/mL sterile concentrate for IV infusion. The Phase 1 formulation (10
mM histidine pH 6.0, 8% sucrose, 0.04% PS-80) provides robust
physicochemical and biological stability, is compatible with standard IV
diluents, and is manufactured using an established, scalable aseptic
fill-finish process.
