**1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE**

This Container Closure Integrity (CCI) Validation Report documents the
qualification of the primary container closure system selected for
Cervimab Drug Product: a 20 mL Type I borosilicate glass vial sealed
with a 20 mm West Pharmaceutical Services FluroTec®-coated bromobutyl
rubber stopper and aluminum crimp seal.

The purpose of this study is to:

- Demonstrate that the container closure system provides adequate
  protection against microbial ingress and preserves the sterility of
  the product throughout its proposed shelf life.

- Demonstrate that the container closure system prevents oxygen ingress
  and liquid leakage that could compromise product quality (oxidative
  degradation, concentration change).

- Support the Phase 1 IND filing per FDA requirements and in compliance
  with USP \<1207\>, ICH Q1A(R2), and relevant FDA guidance documents.

- Establish validated CCI methodology for routine batch release and
  stability testing.

This report covers: (1) container closure system description and
materials characterization; (2) deterministic CCI test method
development and qualification (vacuum decay); (3) probabilistic
confirmatory method (dye ingress); (4) worst-case condition testing; and
(5) stability-linked CCI data.

**2. REGULATORY BASIS AND APPLICABLE STANDARDS**

> USP \<1207\> Guidance (2016) established deterministic methods (vacuum
> decay, high-voltage leak detection, mass extraction/headspace
> analysis) as preferred over probabilistic methods (dye ingress,
> microbial challenge) for CCI validation of sterile products. FDA
> expects CCI data in INDs for sterile injectable biologic products.

**5. VACUUM DECAY METHOD DEVELOPMENT AND VALIDATION**

**5.1 Vacuum Decay Principle**

Vacuum decay testing (ASTM F2338) measures the rate of vacuum decay in a
hermetically sealed test chamber containing the vial under test. A
defective package (containing a leak path) results in measurable
increase in the test chamber pressure due to gas or liquid flux from the
package. The method is non-destructive, operator-independent, and
provides a quantitative sensitivity threshold in absolute leak size (μm
equivalent).

confirmatory tool per USP \<1207\> and is not intended for routine
release testing. Positive control vials (10 μm, 20 μm, 50 μm defects)

**8.1 CCI on Stability Samples**

protocol (CVM-CMC-STA-001) as a stability-indicating test. Both vacuum

shelf life.

**11. CONCLUSIONS**

This Container Closure Integrity Validation Report demonstrates that the
container closure system selected for Cervimab Drug Product (20 mL Type
I borosilicate glass vial, 20 mm West FluroTec® stopper, aluminum crimp
seal) provides robust and validated protection against microbial
ingress, oxygen ingress, and liquid leakage throughout the proposed
Phase 1 shelf life.

Key conclusions:

- The vacuum decay method was validated with a 10 μm minimum detectable
  leak size, well below the threshold for microbial ingress.

- Zero false positives were observed in 30 intact control vials,
  confirming high specificity.

- Inter-analyst reproducibility met all criteria (%RSD ≤20%).

- Orthogonal confirmation by dye ingress validated vacuum decay
  sensitivity.

- Container closure integrity was maintained under worst-case crimping
  force variation, 5 freeze-thaw cycles, and ISTA 2A shipping
  simulation.

- Headspace oxygen remained stable over 6 months at real-time
  conditions, confirming long-term seal integrity.

- The validated CCI test procedure is suitable for routine Phase 1 batch
  release and stability testing.

This CCI validation supports the Phase 1 IND CMC filing and complies
with USP \<1207\>, ICH Q1A(R2), 21 CFR 211.94, and FDA guidance for
container closure integrity of sterile biologic products.
