**1. INTRODUCTION AND BACKGROUND**

Incurred Sample Reanalysis (ISR) is a critical component of
bioanalytical method performance assessment for clinical studies. ISR
evaluates whether subject-derived plasma samples, which contain the
actual drug in a complex biological matrix including metabolites,
co-medications, and endogenous interferences, can be quantitatively
reproduced using the validated bioanalytical method.

This ISR Report covers the re-analysis of subject plasma samples from
Protocol CRV-001-PH1-001 (Cervimab Phase 1 Dose Escalation Study in
dMMR/MSI-H Metastatic Colorectal Cancer). The ISR was conducted in
accordance with:

- FDA Guidance for Industry: Bioanalytical Method Validation (May 2018)

- ICH M10 Bioanalytical Method Validation and Study Sample Analysis (May
  2022)

- CRV-BA-VAL-001-v1.0: Cervimab Bioanalytical Method Validation Report

- CRV-BA-SOP-003 Rev 2: Sample Processing and Plate-based LBA/ECL for
  Cervimab Quantification

The bioanalytical method is a validated sandwich ligand-binding assay
(LBA) using electrochemiluminescence (ECL) detection on the Meso Scale
Discovery (MSD) platform. The validated concentration range is 0.100
µg/mL to 100 µg/mL in human K2-EDTA plasma.

**2. OBJECTIVES**

The objectives of this ISR assessment are:

- To confirm the reproducibility and reliability of the validated
  LBA/ECL method using actual incurred study samples from the Cervimab
  Phase 1 study.

- To confirm that the original PK concentration data reported for study
  subjects are reproducible within the specified acceptance criterion.

- To identify and investigate any incurred sample failures that may
  indicate a systemic assay problem, sample handling issue, or
  subject-specific biological variability.

- To support the integrity of PK and PK/PD data that will be submitted
  to the FDA as part of the Cervimab Phase 1 IND/CTD regulatory package.

**4. ANALYTICAL PROCEDURE**

**4.1 Method**

ISR samples were analyzed using the validated LBA/ECL method documented
in CRV-BA-VAL-001-v1.0 and per CRV-BA-SOP-003 Rev 2. All ISR samples
were processed and analyzed identically to the original samples.

**4.2 Run Design**

ISR samples were analyzed in separate analytical runs from the original
runs. Each ISR run included:

- A full 8-point calibration curve (CS1--CS8)

- QC samples at LLOQ, QCL, QCM, and QCH (minimum 6 QC samples per level
  per run)

- ISR study samples analyzed in duplicate (mean value reported)

**5. ISR ACCEPTANCE CRITERION**

The ISR acceptance criterion for Cervimab (CRV-001) in human K2-EDTA
plasma, as established in the Bioanalytical Analysis Plan
(CRV-BA-PLAN-001) and consistent with FDA Guidance (2018) and ICH M10,
is:

**≥67% of ISR samples must have percent difference within ±20% of the
original result**

Percent Difference (%Diff) is calculated as:

***%Diff = \[(ISR Conc − Mean Original Conc) / Mean Original Conc\] ×
100***

where Mean Original Conc is the mean of the duplicate original assay
results for the sample.

Overall ISR study acceptance: If \<67% of samples pass the ±20%
criterion, the ISR fails and a full investigation is required prior to
reporting or submitting PK data to FDA.

**8. DISCUSSION**

The ISR pass rate of 92.1% (70 of 76 samples within ±20%) substantially
exceeds the FDA and ICH M10 threshold of ≥67%, demonstrating excellent
method reproducibility across the concentration range and study
timepoints evaluated.

The \${failures} ISR failures represent \${failRate}% of all ISR
samples. Each failure was independently investigated and attributed to
non-systemic, sample-specific causes (pipetting error, partial thermal
excursion, plate contamination, and exceeded freeze-thaw cycle limit).
No failure suggests a deficiency in the validated LBA/ECL assay itself.

Notably, ISR performance was consistent across dose cohorts and
concentration ranges, with no concentration-dependent bias or trend in
%Diff values. The median absolute %Diff of 3.0% is well within expected
variability for a validated LBA method applied to complex biological
matrices.

The Cohort 4 (10 mg/kg) pass rate of 89.3% --- lower than lower-dose
cohorts --- was investigated; the 3 failures in Cohort 4 are
attributable to discrete sample handling events rather than any drug
concentration-related matrix effect. High-concentration samples (near
ULOQ) were confirmed to be within the validated dilution integrity range
(1:50 maximum dilution validated).

All CAPAs identified during ISR investigations have been documented,
assigned, and tracked to closure per PharmaKinetics Bioanalytical
Services CAPA SOP (CRV-QA-CAPA-001). The freeze-thaw cycle LIMS tracking
update represents the most impactful process improvement arising from
this ISR exercise.
