2\. BACKGROUND AND RATIONALE

2.1 Disease Background

Colorectal cancer (CRC) is the third most common cancer and the second
leading cause of cancer-related mortality worldwide, with an estimated
1.9 million new cases annually (GLOBOCAN 2022). Approximately 5% of
patients with metastatic CRC harbor dMMR/MSI-H tumors, representing a
genetically and immunologically distinct subgroup characterized by high
tumor mutational burden (TMB-H), abundant tumor-infiltrating
lymphocytes, and sensitivity to immune checkpoint blockade.

Despite the approval of pembrolizumab as first-line therapy for
dMMR/MSI-H mCRC (KEYNOTE-177), the majority of patients eventually
develop resistance, and there is a high unmet medical need for effective
therapies in the second-line and beyond setting. Currently available
options after progression on first-line immunotherapy include cytotoxic
chemotherapy (FOLFIRI ± bevacizumab), regorafenib, and
trifluridine/tipiracil, with modest ORRs (5--12%) and median PFS of
approximately 3--5 months.

2.2 Rationale for Cervimab in This Setting

Cervimab is a fully humanized IgG4 monoclonal antibody that binds PD-1
with high affinity (KD = 0.18 nM), blocking both PD-L1 and PD-L2
ligation. Nonclinical data demonstrate potent CD8+ T-cell reinvigoration
and anti-tumor activity in dMMR tumor models. Phase 2 data from
CERVIX-02 demonstrate clinically meaningful activity of cervimab in
previously treated dMMR/MSI-H mCRC (ORR 45.5%; mDOR not reached; mPFS
9.8 months), supporting the conduct of a randomized Phase 3 study.

4\. PATIENT POPULATION

4.1 Key Inclusion Criteria

- Adults ≥18 years of age

- Histologically or cytologically confirmed colorectal adenocarcinoma
  with unresectable locally advanced or metastatic disease

- Documented dMMR/MSI-H status by a CLIA-certified laboratory using IHC,
  PCR, or validated NGS panel

- Measurable disease per RECIST v1.1

- ECOG Performance Status 0, 1, or 2

- Received at least 1 but no more than 3 prior lines of systemic therapy
  for mCRC, including at least one fluoropyrimidine-containing regimen

- Adequate organ function (hematologic, hepatic, renal, and coagulation
  parameters per protocol)

- Life expectancy ≥12 weeks

4.2 Key Exclusion Criteria

- Prior treatment with any PD-1, PD-L1, or CTLA-4 inhibitor

- Active autoimmune disease requiring systemic therapy within past 2
  years (with exceptions for well-controlled thyroid disease, type 1
  diabetes, stable skin conditions)

- Active or history of non-infectious pneumonitis requiring steroids, or
  Grade ≥3 immune-mediated adverse event on prior immunotherapy

- Active brain metastases (stable treated brain metastases permitted
  after 4 weeks of definitive treatment without steroids)

- Systemic corticosteroids \>10 mg prednisone equivalent/day within 14
  days of first dose

- Concurrent active malignancy (except non-melanoma skin cancer,
  cervical carcinoma in situ)

- Known MSS/pMMR tumor status (historical testing accepted; re-testing
  required if \>12 months prior)

- Pregnant or breastfeeding women

6\. EFFICACY ASSESSMENTS

- Radiologic tumor assessments: CT chest/abdomen/pelvis with contrast
  (or MRI if CT contraindicated) at Baseline, Week 9, Week 18, then Q12
  weeks until PD or end of study

- Response assessment per RECIST v1.1; all scans reviewed centrally by
  BICR

- Investigator-assessed tumor response collected in parallel (not for
  primary endpoint)

- PET scan not required; optional per institutional standard

- CEA and CA19-9: collected at baseline and every 2 cycles (exploratory)

7\. SAFETY ASSESSMENTS

- Physical examination, vital signs, ECOG PS: at each cycle

- Hematology and serum chemistry panels: at each cycle; additional
  monitoring as clinically indicated for imAEs

- Thyroid function (TSH, free T4): Q6W for cervimab arm; Q12W for ICC
  arm

- AEs graded per NCI CTCAE v5.0; imAEs graded and managed per protocol
  algorithms

- Cardiac monitoring: ECG at baseline; troponin at baseline, Cycle 3,
  and as clinically indicated

- DSMB: independent DSMB to review safety data at regular intervals;
  first DSMB meeting after 75 patients enrolled

8\. STATISTICAL CONSIDERATIONS

8.1 Sample Size and Power

Based on the following assumptions for the primary endpoint (PFS):

- Median PFS in ICC arm: 4.2 months (based on published KEYNOTE-164,
  CHECKMATE 142 control arm estimates)

- Target HR = 0.60 (cervimab vs. ICC); median PFS cervimab estimated 7.0
  months

- One-sided α = 0.025 (equivalent to two-sided α = 0.05)

- Power = 90%

- One PFS interim analysis at \~60% information fraction (α spending per
  Lan-DeMets O\'Brien-Fleming)

Required: 316 PFS events; approximately 450 patients enrolled (2:1;
accounting for 10% dropout rate). Enrollment period: 24 months; minimum
follow-up: 12 months after last patient enrolled.

8.2 Analysis Populations

- Intent-to-Treat (ITT) Population: All randomized patients. Primary
  analysis population for PFS and OS.

- Per-Protocol (PP) Population: ITT minus major protocol deviations.
  Sensitivity analysis.

- Safety Population: All patients who received ≥1 dose of study
  treatment.

- Biomarker-Evaluable Population: Patients with adequate baseline tumor
  sample for exploratory biomarker analyses.

8.3 Multiplicity Control

A hierarchical testing procedure will control the overall family-wise
error rate at two-sided α = 0.05:

- Step 1: PFS (primary) --- tested first

- Step 2: OS (key secondary) --- tested if PFS significant

- Step 3: ORR (key secondary) --- tested if OS significant

- Step 4: HRQoL (key secondary) --- tested if ORR significant

If any step is not significant, subsequent endpoints are descriptive
only.

9\. BIOMARKER AND TRANSLATIONAL RESEARCH PLAN

- Mandatory archival tissue (FFPE) submission for central dMMR/MSI-H
  confirmation, TMB, and PD-L1 IHC (22C3 pharmDx assay)

- Optional fresh biopsy at baseline and at confirmed PD for
  transcriptomic and spatial profiling

- Serial blood samples for ctDNA (liquid biopsy) at Baseline, C1D1,
  C3D1, and at PD

- Germline testing for Lynch syndrome mismatch repair gene variants
  (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2, EPCAM) with separate consent

- T-cell receptor (TCR) sequencing on PBMC and tumor-infiltrating
  lymphocytes (exploratory)
