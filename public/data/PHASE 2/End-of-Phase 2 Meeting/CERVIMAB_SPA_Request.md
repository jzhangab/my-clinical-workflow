1\. BASIS FOR SPECIAL PROTOCOL ASSESSMENT REQUEST

Vexion Therapeutics, Inc. (\"Sponsor\") hereby requests a Special
Protocol Assessment (SPA) for the pivotal Phase 3 protocol CERVIX-03.
The Sponsor submits this SPA request pursuant to 21 CFR § 312.82(b) and
the FDA Guidance for Industry: Special Protocol Assessment (May 2002,
updated June 2024). An SPA is being requested for the following reasons:

- CERVIX-03 is designed as a pivotal, randomized, active-controlled
  Phase 3 study intended to support a New Drug Application (NDA) /
  Biologics License Application (BLA) for cervimab in previously treated
  dMMR/MSI-H mCRC

- The Sponsor requests written agreement with FDA that the design,
  endpoints, and statistical analysis plan of CERVIX-03 are adequate to
  support a marketing application for the claimed indication

- An SPA agreement will provide regulatory certainty for the Sponsor and
  investors and facilitate efficient Phase 3 execution

- The Sponsor acknowledges that an SPA agreement is binding under 21 CFR
  § 312.82(e) and commits to substantive discussion with FDA prior to
  any material protocol amendments

1.1 Eligibility for SPA

Per FDA guidance, SPA is available for studies intended to provide
evidence of effectiveness or safety for a marketing application.
CERVIX-03 satisfies this criterion as a pivotal efficacy study with a
randomized controlled design, prospective statistical analysis plan, and
primary endpoint (PFS) that has been established as a basis for full or
accelerated approval in oncology.

2\. PROTOCOL SUMMARY

The complete Phase 3 protocol synopsis is submitted as Attachment A to
this SPA request. Key elements are summarized below for FDA\'s review:

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Protocol Element**   **Summary**
  ---------------------- ----------------------------------------------
  Study Design           Randomized (2:1), open-label,
                         active-controlled, multicenter Phase 3

  Population             Adults ≥18 years; confirmed dMMR/MSI-H mCRC;
                         ≥1 prior fluoropyrimidine-based therapy; ECOG
                         PS 0--2; measurable disease per RECIST v1.1

  Treatment Arms         Arm A: Cervimab 400 mg IV Q6W Arm B: ICC
                         (FOLFIRI ± bevacizumab, regorafenib, or
                         trifluridine/tipiracil)

  Primary Endpoint       PFS per RECIST v1.1 by BICR (time from
                         randomization to first progression or death)

  Key Secondary          OS; ORR; DOR; HRQoL (EORTC QLQ-C30)
  Endpoints              

  Sample Size            \~450 patients; 316 PFS events required; 90%
                         power; HR = 0.60

  Alpha                  Two-sided α = 0.05; hierarchical testing: PFS
                         → OS → ORR → HRQoL

  Stratification         Prior lines of therapy; BRAF V600E status;
                         geographic region

  Interim Analyses       One PFS interim at \~60% events; one OS
                         interim at \~50% OS events; O\'Brien-Fleming
                         alpha spending

3\. SPECIFIC QUESTIONS FOR SPA AGREEMENT

The Sponsor requests written FDA agreement on the following specific
protocol elements. The Sponsor is prepared to revise the protocol prior
to SPA submission to incorporate any feedback received at the EOP2
meeting.

3.1 Primary Endpoint Agreement

SPA Question 1: Does FDA agree that PFS per RECIST v1.1 assessed by BICR
is an acceptable primary endpoint for CERVIX-03 to support marketing
approval (regular or accelerated) for cervimab in previously treated
dMMR/MSI-H mCRC? Specifically:

- Is a PFS HR of 0.60 with a median PFS improvement from 4.2 months
  (ICC) to 7.0 months (cervimab) considered a clinically meaningful
  benefit?

- Is BICR assessment as the primary efficacy assessment acceptable, with
  investigator assessment as a supportive analysis?

- Is censoring per the proposed composite strategy (see Estimands
  Document, Section 2) acceptable?

3.2 Statistical Analysis Plan Agreement

SPA Question 2: Does FDA agree with the following statistical
methodology:

- Primary test: stratified log-rank test (stratified by the three
  stratification factors)

- Sample size: 316 PFS events providing 90% power at HR = 0.60 with
  two-sided α = 0.05

- Interim analysis: one PFS interim at 60% events using O\'Brien-Fleming
  alpha spending; boundary for early stopping approximately HR ≤ 0.56, p
  \< 0.012

- OS alpha allocation from hierarchical testing: OS tested after PFS
  with remaining alpha per group sequential design

- Multiplicity control hierarchy: PFS → OS → ORR → HRQoL

3.3 Patient Population and Biomarker Testing Agreement

SPA Question 3: Does FDA agree with the following patient eligibility
and biomarker testing requirements:

- Eligibility based on local dMMR/MSI-H testing (IHC, PCR, or NGS) from
  a CLIA-certified laboratory, with central confirmation performed
  retrospectively

- ECOG PS 0--2 (rather than restricting to ECOG PS 0--1)

- Up to 3 prior lines of systemic therapy (rather than a cap of 2 prior
  lines)

- No restriction on presence of prior VEGF/VEGFR-targeted therapy

3.4 Control Arm Agreement

SPA Question 4: Does FDA agree that the proposed active control arm
(investigator\'s choice among FOLFIRI ± bevacizumab, regorafenib, or
trifluridine/tipiracil) adequately represents the current standard of
care in previously treated dMMR/MSI-H mCRC for purposes of regulatory
approval? Does the inclusion of bevacizumab as optional with FOLFIRI
introduce material confounding that would affect the regulatory
interpretation?

3.5 OS Data Maturity at Primary Analysis

SPA Question 5: Does FDA agree that a final OS analysis at approximately
70% of required OS events (planned approximately 36 months after last
patient enrolled) with concurrent final PFS analysis is sufficient to
support full approval if both PFS and OS results are positive?
Alternatively, if only PFS meets the primary endpoint at the planned
analysis, would accelerated approval with confirmatory OS data be an
acceptable pathway?
