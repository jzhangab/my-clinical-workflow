1\. BACKGROUND AND PURPOSE OF MEETING

Vexion Therapeutics, Inc. (\"Sponsor\") respectfully requests a Type B
End of Phase 2 (EOP2) meeting with the Food and Drug Administration
(FDA), Division of Oncology Products 2 (DOP2), to discuss the Phase 3
development program for cervimab, an investigational humanized IgG4
monoclonal antibody targeting programmed cell death protein-1 (PD-1), in
patients with deficient mismatch repair (dMMR) or microsatellite
instability-high (MSI-H) metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC).

**The primary objectives of this meeting are:**

- To obtain FDA feedback on the adequacy of the Phase 2 data package to
  support initiation of a pivotal Phase 3 trial

- To align on the Phase 3 trial design, including primary endpoint, key
  secondary endpoints, and estimand framework

- To discuss the feasibility of a Special Protocol Assessment (SPA) for
  the proposed Phase 3 protocol

- To obtain agreement on the biomarker strategy and dMMR/MSI-H testing
  requirements

- To discuss CMC readiness and drug supply for Phase 3

5\. PROPOSED PHASE 3 DESIGN AND RATIONALE

5.1 Trial Overview

  --------------------- -----------------------------------------------
  **Study Title:**      A Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled
                        Phase 3 Study of Cervimab vs. Investigator\'s
                        Choice Chemotherapy in Previously Treated
                        dMMR/MSI-H mCRC (CERVIX-03)

  **Design:**           Randomized, active-controlled, open-label,
                        multicenter, global Phase 3

  **Primary Endpoint:** Progression-Free Survival (PFS) per RECIST v1.1
                        by BICR

  **Key Secondary       Overall Survival (OS); ORR; DOR;
  Endpoints:**          Patient-reported outcomes (PRO)

  **Randomization:**    2:1 (cervimab : ICC)

  **Stratification      Prior lines of therapy (1 vs. ≥2); BRAF V600E
  Factors:**            status (mutant vs. WT); Geographic region

  **Sample Size:**      \~450 patients (300 cervimab; 150 ICC)

  **Patient             Adults ≥18 years; dMMR/MSI-H mCRC confirmed by
  Population:**         local CLIA-certified testing; ECOG PS 0--2; ≥1
                        prior fluoropyrimidine-based chemotherapy

  **Control Arm:**      Investigator\'s Choice Chemotherapy: FOLFIRI ±
                        bevacizumab OR regorafenib OR
                        trifluridine/tipiracil

  **Primary Statistical Log-rank test; HR estimated by Cox proportional
  Test:**               hazards model; two-sided α = 0.05
  --------------------- -----------------------------------------------

5.2 Rationale for Active-Controlled Design

The Sponsor acknowledges FDA\'s preference for randomized controlled
evidence in the dMMR/MSI-H mCRC setting, particularly given the approval
of pembrolizumab as first-line therapy. The proposed active control
reflects the current standard of care for patients progressing after
first-line immune checkpoint blockade or chemotherapy. The proposed 2:1
randomization is intended to maximize safety data collection for
cervimab while maintaining a feasible sample size.

5.3 dMMR/MSI-H Biomarker Testing Strategy

All patients will be required to have dMMR/MSI-H status confirmed prior
to enrollment using a validated, CLIA-certified assay. Acceptable
testing platforms include:

- IHC for MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2 (dMMR defined as loss of any protein)

- PCR-based MSI testing (MSI-H defined as ≥2 of 5 NCI markers unstable)

- Next-generation sequencing (NGS) with validated MSI-H call per
  CAP/ASCO guidelines

*Central confirmatory testing will be performed retrospectively on
archival tissue for all enrolled patients. A companion diagnostic (CDx)
strategy for cervimab is under development in parallel.*

7\. STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN OVERVIEW

The statistical analysis plan (SAP) for CERVIX-03 is under development.
Key elements are summarized below for FDA review and comment:

- Primary analysis: log-rank test for PFS, stratified by randomization
  factors; two-sided α = 0.05

- OS: tested at final analysis with α allocation from group sequential
  design (Lan-DeMets O\'Brien-Fleming spending function); planned at
  \~70% of required OS events

- Multiplicity control: hierarchical testing procedure --- PFS → OS →
  ORR → additional secondary endpoints

- Sensitivity analyses for PFS: (1) unstratified log-rank; (2) censoring
  at cross-over; (3) PFS2 as sensitivity for OS

- Interim analyses: one PFS interim analysis at \~60% of events (alpha
  spending per O\'Brien-Fleming); one OS interim at \~50% OS events

- Sample size: 316 PFS events required for 90% power to detect HR =
  0.60, assuming median PFS control arm = 4.2 months (HR 0.60 → median
  PFS cervimab ≈ 7.0 months)

**Question for FDA: The Sponsor proposes a PFS HR threshold of 0.65 as
the minimum clinically meaningful benefit. Please confirm whether FDA
agrees with this threshold and the proposed sample size / event count.**

9\. MEETING QUESTIONS

The Sponsor respectfully requests FDA\'s responses to the following
questions. Questions are organized by topic and numbered for reference.

9.1 Phase 2 Data Adequacy

1.  Q1. Does FDA agree that the Phase 2 data from CERVIX-02 Cohort A
    (N=112; ORR 45.5% by BICR; mDOR not reached; mPFS 9.8 months) are
    adequate to support initiation of a pivotal Phase 3 trial in
    previously treated dMMR/MSI-H mCRC, without a need for additional
    Phase 2 data?

2.  Q2. FDA is requested to comment on the adequacy of biomarker testing
    methodology for dMMR/MSI-H confirmation in Phase 3 (IHC, PCR, or
    NGS) and whether a single validated CDx platform will be required
    for approval.

9.2 Phase 3 Design

1.  Q3. Does FDA agree with PFS per RECIST v1.1 by BICR as the primary
    endpoint for the pivotal Phase 3 trial (CERVIX-03) in previously
    treated dMMR/MSI-H mCRC, with OS as a key secondary endpoint?

2.  Q4. Does FDA agree with the proposed estimand framework per ICH
    E9(R1), including the composite intercurrent event strategy for PFS
    and the hypothetical strategy sensitivity analysis for OS?

3.  Q5. Does FDA agree with the proposed 2:1 randomization ratio
    (cervimab : ICC), stratification factors, and active control arm
    (FOLFIRI ± bevacizumab, regorafenib, or trifluridine/tipiracil per
    investigator\'s choice)?

4.  Q6. Does FDA agree with the proposed sample size of approximately
    450 patients and the target of 316 PFS events to achieve 90% power
    at HR = 0.60? If FDA believes a different effect size or event count
    is warranted, please advise.

9.3 Accelerated Approval / Regulatory Pathway

1.  Q7. Given the established precedent for accelerated approval of PD-1
    inhibitors in MSI-H malignancies, does FDA consider ORR and DOR from
    CERVIX-02 as a potential basis for accelerated approval in
    previously treated dMMR/MSI-H mCRC, while CERVIX-03 provides
    confirmatory evidence?

2.  Q8. Does FDA agree that CERVIX-03 would qualify for the Special
    Protocol Assessment (SPA) process? The Sponsor intends to submit an
    SPA request concurrently with or shortly following this EOP2
    meeting.

9.4 Safety and Benefit-Risk

1.  Q9. Does FDA agree that the safety database from CERVIX-01 and
    CERVIX-02 (N=218 patients with cervimab; minimum follow-up 12
    months) is adequate to characterize the safety profile of cervimab
    to support initiation of Phase 3? Are there any additional safety
    assessments or risk mitigation strategies FDA recommends be
    incorporated into CERVIX-03?

9.5 CMC

1.  Q10. Does FDA have any concerns with the proposed Phase 3
