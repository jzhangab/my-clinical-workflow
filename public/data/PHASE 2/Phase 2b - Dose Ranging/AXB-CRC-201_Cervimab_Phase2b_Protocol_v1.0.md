**5. INTRODUCTION AND RATIONALE**

**5.1 Disease Background**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) is the third most commonly diagnosed cancer
worldwide and the second leading cause of cancer-related mortality,
accounting for approximately 1.9 million new cases and 935,000 deaths
globally in 2020 (GLOBOCAN 2020). In the United States, approximately
153,000 new cases and 52,000 deaths are projected annually. At initial
diagnosis, approximately 20-25% of patients present with metastatic
disease (mCRC), and an additional 50% of patients with localized disease
will develop metastases. The five-year survival rate for metastatic
disease remains approximately 13-17%.

Deficient mismatch repair (dMMR) and its molecular correlate,
microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H), represent a clinically critical
biomarker subset occurring in approximately 3-5% of mCRC cases. Tumors
with dMMR/MSI-H status are characterized by a hypermutated phenotype
with high tumor mutational burden (TMB), abundant tumor-infiltrating
lymphocytes (TILs), and high expression of immune checkpoint molecules
including PD-1 and PD-L1. This immunogenic microenvironment renders
dMMR/MSI-H tumors uniquely susceptible to PD-1/PD-L1 axis blockade.

The landmark KEYNOTE-177 trial established single-agent pembrolizumab as
the standard of care for first-line treatment of dMMR/MSI-H mCRC,
demonstrating a progression-free survival benefit versus standard
chemotherapy (PFS HR 0.60; 95% CI 0.45-0.80; p=0.0002) and an ORR of
43.8%. Subsequent data from nivolumab (CheckMate 142) and the
combination of nivolumab plus ipilimumab further validated this class of
agents. Despite these advances, approximately 40-60% of patients with
dMMR/MSI-H mCRC fail to respond to first-line anti-PD-1 monotherapy,
highlighting the need for optimized dosing regimens and predictive
biomarkers.

**5.2 Investigational Product: Cervimab (AXB-7219)**

Cervimab (AXB-7219) is a humanized IgG4 kappa monoclonal antibody that
binds to the extracellular domain of human Programmed Death-1 (PD-1)
with high affinity (KD \~0.3 nM), blocking its interaction with PD-L1
and PD-L2 ligands. The IgG4 subclass was selected to minimize
Fc-mediated effector functions (ADCC/CDC) while preserving hinge region
stability through the S228P substitution (Eu numbering). Cervimab
restores T-cell-mediated antitumor immunity by preventing PD-1-mediated
T-cell exhaustion within the tumor microenvironment.

Non-clinical characterization has demonstrated: (i) picomolar binding
affinity to human PD-1 with cross-reactivity to cynomolgus monkey PD-1;
(ii) absence of binding to murine PD-1; (iii) potent restoration of
T-cell proliferation and IFN-gamma production in co-culture and MLR
assays; (iv) in vivo antitumor activity in humanized PD-1 knock-in
syngeneic mouse models including MC38 (MSI-H) and CT26; (v) a favorable
toxicological profile in cynomolgus monkeys at doses up to 100 mg/kg
with no treatment-related mortalities; and (vi) a predicted human PK
profile consistent with flat dosing at 200-400 mg Q2W-Q3W based on PK/PD
modeling.

**5.3 Clinical Experience to Date**

The Phase 1/1b first-in-human study (AXB-CRC-101; IND \[XXXXX\])
evaluated cervimab in 68 patients with advanced solid tumors across four
dose cohorts (1, 3, 10, and 20 mg/kg IV Q3W), followed by two flat-dose
expansion cohorts (200 mg and 400 mg Q3W) each enrolling 20 patients.
Key findings include:

- The maximum tolerated dose (MTD) was not reached up to 20 mg/kg Q3W

- The recommended Phase 2 dose (RP2D) range was defined as 200-400 mg
  Q3W based on PK/PD modeling targeting receptor occupancy \>90% at
  trough

- Dose-proportional PK was observed across the dose range; half-life
  \~25 days; Ctrough at steady-state: 18.2 mcg/mL (200 mg Q3W) and 41.4
  mcg/mL (400 mg Q3W)

- Grade 3-4 treatment-related adverse events (TRAEs) occurred in 17.6%
  of patients; most frequent were immune-related: pneumonitis (4.4%),
  colitis (2.9%), hepatitis (2.9%)

- No Grade 5 TRAEs were observed; no dose-limiting toxicities at doses
  ≤400 mg Q3W

- Anti-drug antibody (ADA) incidence: 8.8% (6/68 patients); no
  neutralizing antibodies detected; no apparent impact on PK or safety

- Confirmed ORR in the dMMR/MSI-H CRC subset (n=14): 50% (7/14 patients;
  95% CI 23.0-77.0%), with 2 complete responses

**5.4 Rationale for Phase 2b Dose-Ranging Study**

While preliminary anti-tumor activity has been observed across the RP2D
range, the optimal dose and schedule for cervimab in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC has
not been established in a randomized setting. Key scientific questions
motivating this Phase 2b study include:

- Dose-response relationship: Whether 200 mg Q3W achieves similar
  efficacy to 400 mg Q3W (would reduce drug exposure and potentially
  improve tolerability)

- Schedule optimization: Whether more frequent dosing (400 mg Q2W)
  provides pharmacokinetic/pharmacodynamic advantages through higher
  Ctrough and more sustained PD-1 receptor occupancy that translates
  into clinical benefit

- Biomarker refinement: Identification of predictive biomarkers beyond
  MSI-H/dMMR status (e.g., TMB-H, PD-L1 CPS, TIL density, ctDNA
  dynamics) that may identify patients most likely to benefit from each
  dose level

The Phase 2b dose-ranging design employs a Bayesian adaptive framework
with a pre-specified interim analysis after 100 patients to enable early
dose selection while maintaining scientific rigor. This design is
consistent with FDA Guidance for Industry: Adaptive Designs for Clinical
Trials of Drugs and Biologics (2019) and provides flexibility to
optimize the probability of success in a subsequent confirmatory Phase 3
trial.

**6. STUDY OBJECTIVES AND ENDPOINTS**

**6.1 Primary Objective**

To evaluate the antitumor efficacy of three cervimab dose regimens (200
mg Q3W, 400 mg Q3W, and 400 mg Q2W) as measured by objective response
rate (ORR) per RECIST v1.1 at Week 24 in patients with previously
untreated dMMR/MSI-H mCRC, as assessed by blinded independent central
review (BICR).

**6.2 Key Secondary Objectives**

- To compare progression-free survival (PFS) across dose arms

- To assess overall survival (OS) through 36 months

- To characterize duration of response (DOR) and disease control rate
  (DCR)

- To evaluate the pharmacokinetic (PK) profiles of each dosing regimen
  at steady state

- To assess immunogenicity (anti-drug antibody formation)

- To evaluate patient-reported outcomes (PROs) and health-related
  quality of life (HRQoL) using EORTC QLQ-C30

**6.3 Exploratory Objectives**

- To explore biomarker correlates of response: PD-L1 CPS, TMB-H, TIL
  density, MSI-H allele frequency, HLA diversity

- To evaluate ctDNA dynamics as an early predictor of response and
  resistance

- To characterize pharmacokinetic/pharmacodynamic (PK/PD) relationships
  including PD-1 receptor occupancy

- To assess correlation between tumor immune microenvironment (TIME)
  characteristics and clinical outcomes

**7. STUDY DESIGN**

**7.1 Overview**

This is a Phase 2b, randomized, open-label, multicenter, three-arm,
parallel-group, dose-ranging study of cervimab in patients with
previously untreated dMMR/MSI-H mCRC. Approximately 150 eligible
patients will be randomized 1:1:1 to one of three dose arms using a
central interactive web response system (IWRS) with stratified
randomization.

**7.2 Stratification Factors**

Randomization will be stratified by the following pre-specified factors:

- ECOG performance status: 0 vs. 1

- Geographic region: North America/Europe vs. Rest of World

- Prior adjuvant chemotherapy: Yes vs. No

- Number of metastatic sites: 1 vs. ≥2

**7.4 Adaptive Design and Interim Analysis**

This study employs a Bayesian adaptive design with a pre-specified
dose-selection interim analysis. After approximately 100 patients have
completed the Week 24 tumor assessment (or have discontinued earlier),
the DSMB will review unblinded ORR data by dose arm and provide a
dose-selection recommendation per the pre-specified Bayesian decision
rules. The sponsor may eliminate a clearly inferior arm or select a
preferred arm for Phase 3 planning. The study will continue per protocol
until the full 150-patient cohort completes Week 24 assessment. Full
details are provided in the separate Statistical Analysis Plan (SAP).

**7.5 Study Duration**

- Screening Period: Up to 28 days prior to Cycle 1 Day 1

- Treatment Period: Up to 24 months (or until disease progression,
  unacceptable toxicity, or withdrawal)

- End-of-Treatment Visit: Within 30 days of last dose

- Safety Follow-up: 30 days post-EOT visit

- Survival Follow-up: Quarterly for up to 36 months from first patient
  enrolled

- Estimated total study duration: Approximately 36 months (12 months
  enrollment + 24 months follow-up)

**8. STUDY POPULATION --- INCLUSION AND EXCLUSION CRITERIA**

All eligibility criteria must be confirmed prior to randomization. A
patient must meet ALL inclusion criteria and NONE of the exclusion
criteria to be eligible for enrollment.

**8.1 Inclusion Criteria**

1.  Age ≥18 years at time of informed consent

2.  Histologically or cytologically confirmed adenocarcinoma of the
    colon or rectum with documented metastatic disease (Stage IV per
    AJCC 8th edition)

3.  Confirmed dMMR status by local immunohistochemistry (IHC) testing
    (absence of MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, or PMS2 protein expression) AND/OR
    confirmed MSI-H by local PCR or NGS panel. Central laboratory
    confirmation required but need not be available prior to
    randomization; however, patients with centrally confirmed pMMR/MSS
    will be withdrawn.

4.  Measurable disease as defined by RECIST v1.1 (at least one target
    lesion not previously irradiated)

5.  No prior systemic therapy for metastatic disease. Patients who
    received prior adjuvant chemotherapy are eligible if the last dose
    was \>6 months prior to development of metastatic disease.

6.  No prior therapy with anti-PD-1, anti-PD-L1, anti-PD-L2,
    anti-CTLA-4, or any other immune checkpoint inhibitor

7.  ECOG performance status of 0 or 1

8.  Life expectancy of at least 3 months, in the judgment of the
    investigator

9.  Adequate bone marrow, hepatic, and renal function within 14 days
    prior to randomization:

    - Absolute neutrophil count (ANC) ≥1.5 × 10⁹/L

    - Platelet count ≥100 × 10⁹/L

    - Hemoglobin ≥9.0 g/dL (transfusion permitted if stable for ≥2
      weeks)

    - Total bilirubin ≤1.5 × ULN (≤3 × ULN for patients with Gilbert
      syndrome or liver metastases)

    - AST and ALT ≤2.5 × ULN (≤5 × ULN for patients with liver
      metastases)

    - Serum creatinine ≤1.5 × ULN OR calculated creatinine clearance ≥40
      mL/min (Cockcroft-Gault)

10. Thyroid function: TSH within institutional normal range (patients on
    stable thyroid replacement therapy are eligible)

11. Women of childbearing potential (WOCBP) must have a negative serum
    beta-hCG within 72 hours prior to Cycle 1 Day 1 and must agree to
    use highly effective contraception during study treatment and for 5
    months after last dose of cervimab

12. Male patients must agree to use effective contraception during study
    treatment and for 7 months after last dose

13. Provide signed written informed consent prior to any study-related
    procedures

14. Willing and able to comply with scheduled visits, treatment plan,
    and study procedures

15. Availability of archival tumor tissue (FFPE block or minimum 10

    strongly encouraged

**8.2 Exclusion Criteria**

16. Active or prior documented autoimmune disease within the past 2

22. Active Hepatitis B (HBsAg positive) or active Hepatitis C (HCV RNA

**9. INVESTIGATIONAL PRODUCT: CERVIMAB**

**9.1 Description**

Cervimab (AXB-7219) is a recombinant, humanized immunoglobulin G4 (IgG4)
kappa monoclonal antibody directed against human PD-1. It is expressed
in Chinese Hamster Ovary (CHO) cells via a recombinant DNA process. The
molecule consists of two heavy chains (MW \~49 kDa each) and two light
chains (MW \~23 kDa each) linked by disulfide bonds. Molecular weight:
approximately 144 kDa.

**9.2 Formulation and Supply**

Cervimab is supplied by Axiom Biosciences as a sterile,
preservative-free concentrate for solution for infusion:

- Presentation: 10 mL single-use vials containing 250 mg/10 mL (25
  mg/mL) solution

- Excipients: L-histidine, L-histidine monohydrochloride, polysorbate
  80, sucrose, Water for Injection

- pH: 5.8; Osmolality: 280-320 mOsm/kg

- Storage: 2-8°C (refrigerated); protect from light; do not freeze

- Stability: 24 months from manufacture date under refrigerated
  conditions

- Prepared infusion bag stability: 24 hours at 2-8°C or 6 hours at room
  temperature (15-25°C) after dilution in 0.9% NaCl or 5% Dextrose

**9.3 Preparation and Administration**

Cervimab must be prepared in a laminar airflow hood or equivalent
aseptic environment by qualified pharmacy personnel. Detailed
preparation instructions are provided in the Investigator\'s Brochure
(IB) and Pharmacy Manual.

- Dilute appropriate volume from vial(s) in 100 mL of 0.9% Sodium
  Chloride Injection, USP or 5% Dextrose Injection, USP

- Administer via IV infusion through a sterile, non-pyrogenic,
  low-protein binding 0.2 or 0.22 micron in-line or add-on filter

- Infusion duration: 60 minutes (±10 minutes) for Cycle 1; may reduce to
  30 minutes for subsequent cycles if Cycle 1 is well tolerated

- No in-line filter is required if filter is incorporated into the
  administration set

- Cervimab should NOT be mixed with or administered concomitantly with
  other medicinal products through the same intravenous line

**9.4 Pre-Medications**

Routine pre-medication is not required prior to cervimab infusion.
Prophylactic antihistamines, antipyretics, or corticosteroids should NOT
be administered unless required for management of prior infusion
reactions. Management of infusion reactions should follow institutional
guidelines and the irAE management algorithms in Appendix A.

**10. DOSE RATIONALE AND DOSE-RANGING SCHEMA**

**10.1 Dose Selection Rationale**

Three dose regimens have been selected for evaluation based on a
comprehensive review of Phase 1 PK/PD data, population PK modeling, and
receptor occupancy simulations:

**10.1.1 Arm A: Cervimab 200 mg Q3W**

Population PK modeling predicts Ctrough at steady-state of approximately
15-22 mcg/mL (90% CI), which corresponds to approximately 88-92% PD-1
receptor occupancy. This dose is predicted to provide clinically
meaningful antitumor activity in immunogenic tumors while potentially
reducing the cumulative drug exposure and associated toxicity profile
compared to 400 mg Q3W. Importantly, Phase 1 dose-expansion data in the
200 mg cohort (n=20) showed an ORR of 40% across solid tumor
histologies, with a Grade 3-4 TRAE rate of 10%, suggesting a favorable
benefit-risk profile.

**10.1.2 Arm B: Cervimab 400 mg Q3W (Reference Dose)**

The 400 mg Q3W dose is designated as the reference arm, consistent with
the dose range explored in the Phase 1/1b study and aligned with the
approved dosing of comparator PD-1 inhibitors. Predicted Ctrough at
steady-state is approximately 38-47 mcg/mL (90% CI), achieving \>95%
PD-1 receptor occupancy throughout the dosing interval. The Phase 1 ORR
at this dose across solid tumors was 45%, with a Grade 3-4 TRAE rate of
15%.

**10.1.3 Arm C: Cervimab 400 mg Q2W**

The Q2W schedule at 400 mg is designed to evaluate whether increased
dosing frequency and higher Ctrough levels (predicted 65-80 mcg/mL at
steady-state) confer additional efficacy benefit. Immunological modeling
suggests that sustained near-complete PD-1 receptor occupancy throughout
the dosing interval may improve depth of response in a subset of
patients. The Phase 1 14-day dosing window data (n=8, dose escalation
cohort) showed tolerability comparable to Q3W at equivalent dose levels.

**10.2 Dose Modifications for Toxicity**

Cervimab does not undergo dose reductions. Dose modifications for
toxicity involve dose delays (treatment holds) and, if toxicity does not
resolve, permanent discontinuation. Specific guidance for immune-related
adverse events (irAEs) is detailed in Appendix A. General principles:

- Grade 1-2 AEs: Continue dosing with enhanced monitoring; initiate
  supportive care as clinically indicated

- Grade 3 AEs: Hold cervimab; initiate systemic corticosteroids
  (prednisone 1-2 mg/kg/day or equivalent) for irAEs; resume after
  recovery to ≤Grade 1 and corticosteroid taper to ≤10 mg/day prednisone
  equivalent

- Grade 4 AEs (most): Permanently discontinue cervimab; initiate
  high-dose corticosteroids ± additional immunosuppression per irAE
  guidelines

- Grade 3-4 infusion reactions: Permanently discontinue cervimab

- Maximum dose delay: 12 weeks. If unable to resume within 12 weeks due
  to toxicity, permanently discontinue.

**12. EFFICACY ASSESSMENTS**

**13. SAFETY ASSESSMENTS**

**13.1 Adverse Event Definitions and Classification**

All adverse events (AEs) will be recorded from the time of signed
informed consent through 30 days after the last dose of cervimab.
Adverse events will be graded according to the NCI Common Terminology
Criteria for Adverse Events (CTCAE), Version 5.0. Causality assessment
(related vs. not related to cervimab) will be made by the investigator.

**13.2 Serious Adverse Events**

A serious adverse event (SAE) is any AE that: results in death; is
life-threatening; requires inpatient hospitalization or prolongation of
existing hospitalization; results in persistent or significant
incapacity or substantial disruption of the ability to conduct normal
life functions; or is a congenital anomaly/birth defect. The
investigator must report all SAEs to Axiom Biosciences within 24 hours
of becoming aware of the event, using the SAE reporting form in the
study eTMF.

**13.3 Immune-Related Adverse Events (irAEs)**

Given the mechanism of action of cervimab, immune-related adverse events
(irAEs) are an anticipated class effect of PD-1 blockade. Common irAEs
include, but are not limited to: immune-related pneumonitis, colitis,
hepatitis, endocrinopathies (thyroiditis, hypophysitis, adrenal
insufficiency, type 1 diabetes mellitus), nephritis, dermatitis, and
neurologic events. A comprehensive irAE management algorithm, including
corticosteroid dosing guidance and criteria for additional
immunosuppressive therapy (e.g., infliximab for severe colitis,
mycophenolate mofetil for hepatitis), is provided in Appendix A.

Investigators should maintain a high index of suspicion for irAEs. All
patients should receive written education materials regarding the signs
and symptoms of irAEs and carry an alert card at all times during study
participation. Investigators should establish an escalation pathway for
after-hours irAE management at each site.

**13.4 Data Safety Monitoring Board**

An independent Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) will be established
prior to study initiation. The DSMB will meet at minimum every 6 months
and following the pre-specified interim analysis. The DSMB will review
unblinded safety data and may recommend study pause, protocol
modifications, or early termination based on pre-specified stopping
rules. DSMB composition, charter, and stopping rules are defined in the
separate DSMB Charter document.

**13.5 Laboratory Safety Monitoring**

Clinically significant laboratory abnormalities must be followed until
resolution or documentation of a chronic baseline value. Any Grade ≥3
laboratory abnormality considered related to cervimab must be reported
as an AE. Of particular importance are liver function tests (AST, ALT,
bilirubin) for hepatitis monitoring, thyroid function tests, fasting
glucose and HbA1c, and morning cortisol for endocrinopathy surveillance.

**14. PHARMACOKINETICS AND IMMUNOGENICITY**

**14.1 Pharmacokinetic Sampling**

Serial blood samples for PK analysis will be collected per the Schedule
of Assessments. Intensive PK sampling at Cycle 1 Day 1 will allow
characterization of single-dose PK parameters. Trough and
end-of-infusion samples at subsequent cycles will be used to
characterize PK at steady state and evaluate dose-proportionality across
arms.

Key PK parameters to be estimated: Cmax (maximum concentration), AUCtau
(area under the concentration-time curve over the dosing interval),
Ctrough (pre-dose trough concentration), t1/2 (terminal half-life), CL
(systemic clearance), Vd (volume of distribution at steady state).
Population PK modeling will be performed to characterize
inter-individual variability and identify covariates.

**14.2 Immunogenicity Assessment**

Serum samples for anti-drug antibody (ADA) testing will be collected
pre-dose at Baseline, Cycle 3 Day 1, Cycle 5 Day 1, and every 12 weeks
thereafter, as well as at the EOT visit. ADA-positive samples will be
further characterized by titer, isotype, and neutralizing antibody (NAb)
testing. The impact of ADA on PK, efficacy, and safety will be assessed.
All immunogenicity assays will be performed using a validated
electrochemiluminescence (ECL) bridging assay at a Sponsor-designated
central laboratory.

**16. STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN OVERVIEW**

**16.1 Sample Size**

The target enrollment of 150 patients (50 per arm) is based on the
following assumptions for the primary endpoint (ORR at Week 24):

- Expected ORR in the reference arm (Arm B, 400 mg Q3W): 45% based on
  Phase 1 data and published pembrolizumab data in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC

- Minimum clinically meaningful difference detectable: 15% absolute
  difference between arms

- The dose-selection Bayesian adaptive design provides approximately 80%
  probability of correctly selecting the arm with the highest ORR when
  the true difference is ≥15%

- Assuming 10% non-evaluable rate, 50 randomized patients per arm
  provides 45 evaluable patients per arm

- This sample size also provides 80% power to detect a 20% absolute
  difference in ORR between the selected dose arm and the reference arm
  in the final confirmatory analysis (one-sided alpha = 0.10)

**16.2 Analysis Populations**

- Intent-to-Treat (ITT) Population: All randomized patients, analyzed
  according to assigned dose arm; primary efficacy analysis population

- Per-Protocol (PP) Population: All ITT patients who received ≥2 doses
  of cervimab and had ≥1 post-baseline tumor assessment

- Safety Population: All patients who received ≥1 dose of cervimab,
  analyzed according to actual dose received

- PK Analysis Population: All patients in the Safety Population with ≥1
  evaluable PK sample

**16.3 Primary Efficacy Analysis**

The primary endpoint (ORR at Week 24 per BICR) will be analyzed using an
exact binomial test for each pairwise comparison versus the reference
arm (Arm B). A 90% confidence interval (Wilson method) will be
calculated for ORR in each arm. The Bayesian dose-selection analysis
will use a beta-binomial model with non-informative priors. Multiplicity
adjustment will be performed using the Bonferroni method for the two
pairwise comparisons (alpha = 0.05 per comparison at one-sided alpha =
0.10).

**16.4 Secondary Efficacy Analyses**

- PFS and OS: Kaplan-Meier method with log-rank test for pairwise
  comparisons; Cox proportional hazards model for hazard ratios with 95%
  CI; censoring rules per RECIST v1.1

- DOR: Kaplan-Meier analysis in responders

- DCR: Exact binomial proportions with 90% CI

- PRO analyses: Mixed-effects model for repeated measures (MMRM);
  analysis performed separately for EORTC QLQ-C30 global health score
  and individual domain scores

**16.5 Subgroup and Exploratory Analyses**

Pre-specified subgroup analyses will be performed for ORR and PFS by:
PD-L1 CPS (\<1, 1-10, ≥10), TMB (\< vs. ≥10 mut/Mb), ECOG PS (0 vs. 1),
KRAS mutation status, and number of metastatic sites. Biomarker analyses
will explore associations between baseline and on-treatment biomarkers
(ctDNA, PD-L1, TMB, TIL) and clinical outcomes using logistic
regression, Cox regression, and landmark analyses as appropriate.

**Non-Target Lesions**

- All other lesions/sites of disease not designated as target lesions

- Record as present, absent, or unequivocal progression

**New Lesions**

- Appearance of any new lesion constitutes progressive disease
  regardless of change in target lesion measurements

- FDG-PET progression should be confirmed with CT

**APPENDIX D: PATIENT-REPORTED OUTCOME INSTRUMENTS**

**D.1 EORTC QLQ-C30**

The EORTC QLQ-C30 (version 3.0) is a validated 30-item cancer-specific
HRQoL questionnaire. It incorporates five functional scales (physical,
role, cognitive, emotional, social), three symptom scales (fatigue,
pain, nausea/vomiting), a global health status/QoL scale, and six single
items. Scores are linearly transformed to a 0-100 scale. Higher scores
on functional/global scales = better HRQoL; higher scores on symptom
scales = greater symptom burden.

**D.2 EORTC QLQ-CR29**

The EORTC QLQ-CR29 is the colorectal cancer-specific supplementary
module to the QLQ-C30. It contains 29 items addressing symptoms and
functional issues specific to CRC patients, including those with stomas.
Items cover urinary frequency, blood/mucus in stool, stool frequency,
embarrassment, abdominal pain, buttock pain, bloating, dry mouth, hair
loss, taste, anxiety, and weight. It will be used as an exploratory PRO
measure.

**D.3 Administration and Scoring**

PRO questionnaires must be completed by the patient independently
(without assistance from site staff or caregivers) at the designated
time points, BEFORE any clinical assessments or discussion of results.
Questionnaires should be completed in the patient\'s native language
using the validated linguistic version. Scoring will be performed
centrally per the EORTC Scoring Manual. Minimally important difference
(MID) for the QLQ-C30 global health status scale will be defined as a
change of ≥10 points.
