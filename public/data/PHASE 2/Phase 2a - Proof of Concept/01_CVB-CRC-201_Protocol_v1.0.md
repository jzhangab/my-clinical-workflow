**1. BACKGROUND AND RATIONALE**

**1.1 Disease Overview**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) is the third most commonly diagnosed cancer and
the second leading cause of cancer-related mortality worldwide, with an
estimated 1.93 million new cases and 935,000 deaths annually (GLOBOCAN
2022). Approximately 20-25% of patients present with metastatic disease
at the time of diagnosis, and an additional 25-30% will develop
metastatic disease over their clinical course. Five-year survival for
metastatic CRC (mCRC) remains approximately 14% with standard
chemotherapy-based regimens.

Deficiency in the DNA mismatch repair (dMMR) pathway, identified either
through immunohistochemistry (IHC) or high microsatellite instability
(MSI-H) by molecular testing, occurs in approximately 3-5% of mCRC
cases. These tumors accumulate a high burden of frameshift mutations
generating neoantigens that can be recognized by the immune system,
making them uniquely susceptible to immune checkpoint blockade.

**1.2 Scientific Rationale for Immunotherapy in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC**

The biological basis for enhanced immunotherapy responsiveness in
dMMR/MSI-H tumors includes: (1) high tumor mutational burden (TMB)
leading to abundant neoantigens; (2) dense tumor-infiltrating lymphocyte
(TIL) infiltration; (3) elevated expression of PD-L1 and other immune
checkpoint molecules; and (4) enrichment of effector memory T cells.
These features collectively create an immunologically \'hot\' tumor
microenvironment amenable to PD-1/PD-L1 blockade.

Pembrolizumab (KEYNOTE-158, KEYNOTE-177) and nivolumab (CheckMate 142)
have demonstrated durable objective responses (ORR \~40%) and
significant improvement in progression-free and overall survival
compared to chemotherapy in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC patients. These landmark
results led to FDA approvals of pembrolizumab as first-line monotherapy
and nivolumab with or without ipilimumab.

**1.3 Cervimab: Investigational Agent**

Cervimab is a fully humanized IgG4 monoclonal antibody that binds with
high affinity and specificity to the PD-1 receptor on T lymphocytes,
blocking interaction with its ligands PD-L1 and PD-L2. Cervimab was
engineered with a stabilizing Fc region mutation (S228P) to prevent
Fab-arm exchange and reduce Fc-mediated effector functions, consistent
with its mechanism as an immune checkpoint blocker rather than an
antibody-dependent cellular cytotoxicity (ADCC) agent.

Preclinical data demonstrate potent PD-1 blockade with an IC50 of 0.8 nM
in T-cell activation assays, favorable pharmacokinetic profile with a
predicted human half-life of \~25 days, and no off-target binding to
other immune receptors in comprehensive cross-reactivity panels.

**1.4 Prior Clinical Experience with Cervimab**

Cervimab has been evaluated in a Phase 1 dose-escalation study (CVB-001)
in patients with advanced solid tumors (N=56). The recommended Phase 2
dose (RP2D) was established as 200 mg IV every 3 weeks based on
acceptable safety, PK/PD data, and preliminary anti-tumor activity.
Specifically, in patients with dMMR/MSI-H tumors within the Phase 1
cohort (N=8), 3 confirmed partial responses were observed (ORR 37.5%),
providing proof-of-concept for the current study.

The safety profile of cervimab is consistent with the class effects of
PD-1 inhibitors, with immune-related adverse events (irAEs) observed in
approximately 60% of patients (all grades) and approximately 15% (Grade
3-4). No unexpected safety signals were observed, and no
treatment-related deaths occurred.

**1.5 Rationale for Phase 2a Study Design**

This Phase 2a study is designed as a single-arm, open-label study
utilizing a two-stage Simon optimal design, which provides an efficient
and ethically sound approach to assess the preliminary efficacy of
cervimab in the dMMR/MSI-H mCRC population. The single-arm design is
appropriate given: (1) strong proof-of-concept from Phase 1 data; (2)
the established efficacy of PD-1 inhibitors in this biomarker-defined
population serving as historical benchmark; (3) ethical considerations
precluding a placebo arm given available active therapies; and (4)
regulatory alignment with FDA Breakthrough Therapy Designation pathways
for biomarker-enriched oncology studies.

**2. STUDY OBJECTIVES AND ENDPOINTS**

**2.1 Primary Objective**

To evaluate the antitumor activity of cervimab 200 mg IV Q3W, as
measured by objective response rate (ORR), in patients with previously
treated dMMR/MSI-H mCRC.

**2.2 Primary Endpoint**

Objective Response Rate (ORR), defined as the proportion of patients
achieving confirmed complete response (CR) or partial response (PR) per
Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumors version 1.1 (RECIST v1.1)
as assessed by blinded independent central review (BICR).

**3. STUDY DESIGN**

**3.1 Overview**

CVB-CRC-201 is a Phase 2a, open-label, single-arm, multicenter study
employing a two-stage Simon Optimal design to evaluate the efficacy and
safety of cervimab in patients with dMMR/MSI-H mCRC who have received at
least one prior line of systemic therapy.

**3.2 Simon Two-Stage Design**

The study utilizes the Simon Optimal design to minimize the expected
sample size under the null hypothesis while controlling Type I and Type
II error rates.

- Null hypothesis (H0): ORR \<= 20% (p0 = 0.20; consistent with
  historical chemotherapy benchmark)

- Alternative hypothesis (H1): ORR \>= 40% (p1 = 0.40; clinically
  meaningful threshold)

- Alpha (one-sided): 0.05

- Power (1-beta): 0.80

- Stage 1: Enroll 19 patients. If \<= 5 responses: stop for futility. If
  \>= 6: proceed to Stage 2.

- Stage 2: Enroll additional 21 patients (total N=40). Reject H0 if \>=
  13 responses observed in 40 patients.

**3.3 Study Schema**

Screening (up to 28 days) -\> Enrollment & Baseline -\> Treatment:
Cervimab 200 mg IV Q3W (Cycles 1-up to 32 cycles \[\~24 months\]) -\>
End of Treatment (EOT) Visit -\> Safety Follow-Up (30 days post last
dose) -\> Survival Follow-Up (Q12W up to 24 months).

**3.4 Study Duration**

- Enrollment period: Approximately 18 months

- Treatment duration per patient: Up to 24 months (or until
  PD/unacceptable toxicity)

- Follow-up period: Up to 24 months post-treatment

- Total study duration: Approximately 5 years

**3.5 Number of Sites**

Approximately 10-15 US clinical sites with demonstrated oncology
research infrastructure, dMMR/MSI-H testing capabilities, and experience
with immune checkpoint inhibitor administration.

**4. STUDY POPULATION**

**4.1 Inclusion Criteria**

Patients must meet ALL of the following criteria to be eligible:

1.  Histologically or cytologically confirmed adenocarcinoma of the
    colon or rectum with evidence of metastatic or unresectable disease.

2.  dMMR/MSI-H status confirmed by one of the following: (a) Loss of MMR
    protein expression (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, and/or PMS2) by IHC performed
    in a CLIA-certified laboratory; OR (b) MSI-H by PCR or NGS panel in
    a CLIA-certified laboratory. Testing performed on primary or
    metastatic tumor tissue is acceptable.

3.  Measurable disease per RECIST v1.1, as assessed by CT or MRI within
    28 days prior to enrollment.

4.  Prior treatment with at least one line of systemic chemotherapy for
    metastatic disease (e.g., FOLFOX, FOLFIRI, CAPOX). Prior targeted
    therapy (e.g., bevacizumab, cetuximab, panitumumab where applicable)
    is permitted.

5.  Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group (ECOG) Performance Status of 0 or
    1.

6.  Age \>= 18 years at time of informed consent.

7.  Adequate organ function within 14 days prior to enrollment: ANC \>=
    1.5 x 10\^9/L, Platelets \>= 100 x 10\^9/L, Hemoglobin \>= 9.0 g/dL,
    Creatinine \<= 1.5 x ULN OR CrCl \>= 50 mL/min, AST/ALT \<= 2.5 x
    ULN (\<= 5 x ULN if liver mets), Total bilirubin \<= 1.5 x ULN (\< 3
    x ULN if Gilbert\'s or liver mets).

8.  Willing and able to provide written informed consent prior to any
    study procedures.

9.  Women of childbearing potential (WOCBP) must have a negative serum
    or urine pregnancy test within 72 hours prior to first dose and must
    agree to use highly effective contraception during treatment and for
    5 months after last cervimab dose.

10. Male patients must agree to use contraception during treatment and
    for 7 months after last dose.

**4.2 Exclusion Criteria**

Patients meeting ANY of the following criteria are NOT eligible:

11. Prior treatment with any anti-PD-1, anti-PD-L1, anti-PD-L2, or
    anti-CTLA-4 antibody or any other immune checkpoint inhibitor.

12. Active autoimmune disease requiring systemic treatment within the
    past 2 years, except: vitiligo, alopecia, controlled type 1
    diabetes, hypothyroidism on stable hormone replacement therapy.

13. Active or prior documented inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn\'s
    disease, ulcerative colitis).

14. Systemic corticosteroid therapy (\>10 mg/day prednisone or
    equivalent) within 14 days prior to first dose. Physiologic
    replacement doses and inhaled/topical steroids are permitted.

15. Known central nervous system (CNS) metastases unless treated and
    neurologically stable for \>= 4 weeks with no requirement for
    corticosteroids within 14 days prior to enrollment.

16. Uncontrolled intercurrent illness including: active infection
    requiring IV antibiotics, symptomatic congestive heart failure (NYHA
    Class III/IV), unstable angina, cardiac arrhythmia, or psychiatric
    illness/social situations that would limit protocol compliance.

17. Known history of active Mycobacterium tuberculosis infection.

18. Known HIV infection with CD4+ count \<200 cells/uL or detectable
    viral load within 6 months of enrollment.

19. Active hepatitis B (HBsAg+) or hepatitis C (HCV RNA+) infection.

20. Receipt of live vaccine within 30 days prior to first dose.

21. Prior malignancy within 2 years, except adequately treated
    non-melanoma skin cancer, in situ cervical cancer, or other
    curatively treated malignancy with no evidence of active disease.

22. Pregnancy or breastfeeding.

23. Known hypersensitivity to cervimab or any of its excipients.

24. Prior organ or bone marrow transplantation.

**5.2 Dose Modifications**

Cervimab dose reductions are NOT permitted. Dose delays and treatment
discontinuation are the primary management strategies for AEs.
Immune-related AEs (irAEs) are managed per protocol-defined
immune-mediated adverse event management guidelines detailed in Appendix
B.

**5.2.1 Dose Delay Criteria**

- Grade \>= 3 non-immune-related toxicity: Hold until resolution to
  Grade \<= 1 or baseline, then resume with close monitoring.

- Grade 3 immune-related AE: Hold; initiate corticosteroids (prednisone
  1-2 mg/kg/day or equivalent); resume after resolution to Grade \<= 1
  and corticosteroid taper to \<= 10 mg/day prednisone equivalent.

- Any Grade 4 laboratory abnormality that is transient and not
  clinically significant: May resume with medical monitor approval.

**5.2.2 Permanent Discontinuation Criteria**

- Grade 4 immune-related AE (any organ) or Grade 3 immune-related AE
  that does not resolve to \<= Grade 1 within 12 weeks with adequate
  immunosuppression.

- Recurrent Grade 3 immune-related AE of the same organ/type.

- Grade 3 or 4 pneumonitis, myocarditis (any grade), or immune-related
  encephalitis.

- Grade 3 or 4 infusion-related reaction not manageable with
  premedication.

- Maximum dose delay of 12 weeks from scheduled date.

- Confirmed radiographic progressive disease per RECIST v1.1
  (investigator\'s determination, pending BICR confirmation).

**5.3 Prohibited and Permitted Medications**

Prohibited: Systemic corticosteroids (\>10 mg/day prednisone equivalent)
for non-irAE purposes; any other anticancer therapy (chemotherapy,
targeted therapy, hormonal therapy, radiation therapy); other
investigational agents; live vaccines. Permitted: Supportive care
medications (antiemetics, analgesics, growth factors for Grade 4
neutropenia); physiologic steroid replacement;
inhaled/topical/intranasal steroids; proton pump inhibitors;
bisphosphonates/denosumab.

**6.2 Tumor Response Assessments**

Radiographic tumor assessments will be performed by CT scan of the
chest, abdomen, and pelvis (with IV contrast unless contraindicated) or
MRI every 6 weeks (2 cycles) for the first 24 weeks, then every 12 weeks
(4 cycles) thereafter. All imaging will be submitted for Blinded
Independent Central Review (BICR) by an independent radiology vendor.
Response will be assessed per RECIST v1.1 criteria. Immune-related
RECIST (iRECIST) will be applied as a supplementary exploratory
assessment.

Complete or partial responses must be confirmed by repeat imaging no
sooner than 4 weeks after initial response criteria are met.

**7. SAFETY MONITORING AND REPORTING**

**7.1 Adverse Event Definitions**

All AEs will be graded per NCI Common Terminology Criteria for Adverse
Events Version 5.0 (CTCAE v5.0). Severity (Grade 1-5), causality
(definitely related, probably related, possibly related, unlikely
related, not related), and seriousness will be assessed.

A Serious Adverse Event (SAE) is any adverse event that: results in
death; is life-threatening; requires inpatient hospitalization or
prolongation of existing hospitalization; results in persistent or
significant disability/incapacity; is a congenital anomaly/birth defect;
OR is a medically important event that may jeopardize the patient or may
require intervention to prevent one of the outcomes listed above.

**7.3 Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB)**

An independent Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) comprising 3
independent oncologists and 1 independent biostatistician will be
established prior to study initiation. The DSMB will conduct
pre-specified interim safety reviews after every 10 patients have
completed at least 2 treatment cycles. The DSMB will have authority to
recommend study pause, modification, or termination based on safety or
futility findings.

**7.4 Immune-Related Adverse Event (irAE) Management**

Detailed irAE management guidelines are provided in Appendix B and are
based on current American Society of Clinical Oncology (ASCO) and
Society for Immunotherapy of Cancer (SITC) guidelines. General
principles include: (1) early recognition and grading; (2) withholding
cervimab for Grade \>= 2 irAEs; (3) initiation of systemic
corticosteroids for Grade \>= 2 irAEs; (4) consideration of specialist
consultation; (5) escalation to high-dose corticosteroids and/or
additional immunosuppression for Grade \>= 3 irAEs.

**8. STATISTICAL METHODS**

**8.1 Sample Size Justification**

As detailed in Section 3.2, the two-stage Simon Optimal design requires
a total of 40 evaluable patients (19 in Stage 1, 21 in Stage 2) to
achieve 80% power at a one-sided alpha of 0.05. The null hypothesis (p0
= 0.20) reflects the ORR benchmark for second-line chemotherapy in
unselected mCRC patients; the alternative hypothesis (p1 = 0.40) is
considered clinically meaningful based on observed ORRs with approved
PD-1 inhibitors in dMMR/MSI-H CRC. To account for up to 10%
non-evaluability due to withdrawal before first tumor assessment, up to
45 patients may be enrolled.

**8.2 Analysis Populations**

- Intent-to-Treat (ITT) Population: All enrolled patients who received
  at least one dose of cervimab.

- Efficacy-Evaluable (EE) Population: All ITT patients with at least one
  post-baseline tumor assessment OR who discontinued due to disease
  progression prior to first assessment. This is the primary efficacy
  analysis population.

- Safety Population: All ITT patients who received at least one dose of
  cervimab (same as ITT).

- PK Population: All Safety Population patients with at least one
  evaluable PK sample.

- Biomarker Population: All patients with evaluable biomarker sample
  (PD-L1, TMB, ctDNA).

**8.3 Primary Efficacy Analysis**

The primary endpoint, ORR per BICR-assessed RECIST v1.1, will be
analyzed in the EE population. ORR and its exact two-sided 95%
Clopper-Pearson confidence interval will be calculated. The null
hypothesis (H0: ORR \<= 20%) will be rejected if \>= 13 confirmed
responses are observed among 40 evaluable patients (equivalent to an
observed ORR \>= 32.5%), consistent with the Simon design stopping rule.

**8.4 Secondary Efficacy Analyses**

DoR and PFS will be estimated using the Kaplan-Meier method with 95% CIs
using Greenwood\'s formula. OS will be analyzed similarly with longer
follow-up. All time-to-event endpoints will be censored at the date of
last known alive/progression-free status for patients who have not
experienced the event at time of analysis. DCR will be summarized
similarly to ORR with exact CIs.

**8.5 Safety Analyses**

AEs will be coded using MedDRA and summarized by system organ class
(SOC) and preferred term (PT), grade, severity, and relationship to
treatment. Treatment-related AEs (TRAEs), Grade \>= 3 AEs, SAEs, irAEs,
AEs leading to dose delay, AEs leading to discontinuation, and deaths
will be summarized. Laboratory values, vital signs, and ECOG PS changes
will be tabulated over time.

**9. DATA MANAGEMENT**

All study data will be collected in a validated electronic data capture
(EDC) system. Source data verification (SDV) will be performed per a
risk-based monitoring plan. Data will be subject to automated range
checks, logical consistency edits, and query generation. Database lock
will occur following resolution of all outstanding data queries and
completion of all protocol-required data reviews.
