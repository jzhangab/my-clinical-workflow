**1. INTRODUCTION AND REGULATORY CONTEXT**

This IND Annual Report (AR) is submitted pursuant to 21 CFR 312.33 and
covers the reporting period from October 1, 2023, through September 30,
2024. This report summarizes all clinical, preclinical, manufacturing,
and safety information accumulated during Phase 2 development of
Cervimab in patients with deficient mismatch repair (dMMR) /
microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H) metastatic colorectal cancer
(mCRC).

Regulatory references: 21 CFR Part 312 (IND regulations), ICH E6(R2)
(GCP), ICH E3 (CSR structure), FDA Guidance for Industry: IND Annual
Reports (2018).

**4.3 Deaths**

Four patients died during the reporting period. None of the deaths were
attributed to Cervimab:

- Patient 201-012: Disease progression (CRC) --- Day 142

- Patient 201-031: Disease progression (CRC) --- Day 187

- Patient 201-047: Pulmonary embolism, assessed as unrelated to study
  drug --- Day 203

- Patient 201-058: Acute myocardial infarction, pre-existing
  cardiovascular risk, unrelated --- Day 94

**4.4 Serious Adverse Events Narratives Summary**

Twenty-two SAEs were reported across 22 patients. Selected narratives
for treatment-related Grade ≥3 SAEs are summarized in Appendix A. SAE
causality assessments were reviewed by the Medical Monitor and are
consistent with the known PD-1 inhibitor safety profile.

**5. PRECLINICAL STUDIES**

No new preclinical studies were initiated or completed during the
current reporting period specifically for Cervimab. Previously submitted
chronic toxicology data (NHP 26-week repeat-dose), reproductive
toxicology, and genotoxicity studies remain the basis for ongoing safety
monitoring. No new non-clinical findings require updates to the
Investigator Brochure (IB) beyond those captured in IB Version 3.0 (July
2024).

**6. MANUFACTURING CHANGES**

One manufacturing change was implemented during this reporting period:

- Drug substance manufacturing scale-up from 500 L to 2,000 L bioreactor
  (approved via CBE-30 supplement, submitted August 2024). Comparability
  studies confirmed no impact on drug product quality attributes,
  pharmacological activity, or safety profile.

Certificate of Analysis and comparability data are included in Appendix
B.

**7. INVESTIGATOR BROCHURE UPDATES**

Investigator Brochure Version 3.0 was issued on July 15, 2024. Key
updates from Version 2.1 include:

- Addition of immune-mediated myocarditis as a rare but potentially
  serious irAE based on spontaneous post-marketing reports from a
  related PD-1 inhibitor (class effect); 2 cases total in Cervimab
  clinical program (non-serious, resolved with corticosteroids)

- Revised guidance on corticosteroid management algorithms for Grade
  2--4 irAEs

- Updated pharmacokinetic summary incorporating CERVI-201 interim PK
  data

- Addition of biomarker section summarizing PD-L1 CPS correlation data

**8. PROTOCOL AMENDMENTS**

Two protocol amendments were filed during this reporting period:

- Amendment 2 (January 2024): Expansion of inclusion criteria to permit
  patients with controlled autoimmune disease on stable low-dose
  immunosuppression (prednisone ≤10 mg/day equivalent). Implemented
  following safety data review at Protocol Review Committee meeting.

- Amendment 3 (July 2024): Added optional biopsy substudy for
  exploratory tumor microenvironment biomarker analysis. No changes to
  dosing or safety monitoring.

**10. SUMMARY AND CONCLUSIONS**

Cervimab continues to demonstrate a favorable benefit-risk profile in
patients with dMMR/MSI-H mCRC. The interim ORR of 42.7% compares
favorably to historical data in this population. The safety profile is
consistent with the known class effects of PD-1 inhibitors, with no
treatment-related deaths and manageable irAEs. Enrollment is proceeding
on schedule, with completion anticipated Q1 2025. The program remains in
good standing with all regulatory obligations fulfilled.
