**PART I --- PRODUCT OVERVIEW**

**I.1 Active Substance and Product Information**

Cervimab is a humanized immunoglobulin G4 (IgG4) monoclonal antibody
directed against programmed cell death protein 1 (PD-1). It functions by
blocking the interaction of PD-1 with its ligands PD-L1 and PD-L2,
thereby restoring T-cell immune activity against tumor cells. The drug
is administered as a 200 mg intravenous infusion every 3 weeks (Q3W).

**I.2 Therapeutic Indication**

Cervimab is under investigation for the treatment of adult patients with
deficient mismatch repair (dMMR) or microsatellite instability-high
(MSI-H) metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC) who have received at least
one prior line of fluoropyrimidine-based chemotherapy.

**I.3 Pharmacological Class and Mechanism**

PD-1 is expressed on activated T cells and, upon binding to PD-L1/PD-L2
expressed on tumor cells and antigen-presenting cells, delivers
inhibitory signals that suppress immune responses. By blocking PD-1,
Cervimab reverses T-cell exhaustion and promotes anti-tumor immunity.
The immunostimulatory mechanism of action underlies the potential for
immune-related adverse events (irAEs).

**II.3 Missing Information**

- Long-term safety beyond 24 months (insufficient follow-up data
  available)

- Safety in patients with ECOG PS ≥3 (excluded from current protocol)

- Safety in patients with prior allogeneic stem cell transplant

- Safety in pediatric population (age \<18 years; see Pediatric
  Investigation Plan)

- Safety in patients with severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh C)

- Drug-drug interaction potential with immunosuppressants and strong CYP
  modulators

- Safety during pregnancy and lactation (women excluded from current
  study)

**PART III --- PHARMACOVIGILANCE PLAN**

**III.1 Routine Pharmacovigilance Activities**

All investigational sites are required to report adverse events per 21
CFR 312.32 and ICH E6(R2). The sponsor\'s Global Drug Safety (GDS) team
conducts:

- Continuous real-time monitoring of incoming AE/SAE reports

- Expedited IND safety reports: 7-day for fatal/unexpected serious;
  15-day for unexpected non-fatal serious

- Monthly signal detection meetings using the sponsor\'s validated
  pharmacovigilance database (MedWatch-compatible)

- Quarterly safety data listings reviewed by the Medical Monitor

- Semi-annual unblinded DSMB safety reviews

- Annual aggregate safety review in the IND Annual Report

**III.3 DSMB Charter Summary**

An independent Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) comprising 3
oncologists, 1 cardiologist, 1 biostatistician, and 1 patient advocate
was convened prior to first patient enrollment. The DSMB meets
semi-annually and reviews unblinded safety data. Stopping rules are
pre-specified for: (1) treatment-related mortality ≥3%; (2) Grade 4
myocarditis (any case); (3) unexpected SAE rate \>25% treatment-related.

**IV.2 Additional Risk Minimization**

- Restricted distribution: Cervimab is supplied only to IND-registered
  investigators through a controlled supply chain

- Patient Monitoring Card: A wallet-sized irAE symptom recognition card
  is provided to all enrolled patients

- 24/7 Sponsor Medical Hotline: Site personnel and investigators can
  contact the sponsor medical team at any time for real-time irAE
  management guidance

- Mandatory site qualification visits: All sites must complete a
  qualification visit with irAE management training before site
  activation

**PART V --- SUMMARY OF BENEFIT-RISK CONCLUSIONS**

Cervimab\'s benefit-risk profile is considered favorable for the target
population of patients with dMMR/MSI-H mCRC who have limited therapeutic
options. The identified risks --- primarily immune-related adverse
events --- are manageable with established protocols and are consistent
with the known PD-1 inhibitor class. The clinical benefit (ORR \~43%,
durable responses) substantially outweighs the risk in this setting.
Ongoing pharmacovigilance and risk minimization activities are
appropriate and proportionate to the risk profile.
