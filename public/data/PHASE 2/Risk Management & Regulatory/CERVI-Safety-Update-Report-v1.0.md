**1. OVERVIEW AND PURPOSE**

This Safety Update Report (SUR) is submitted pursuant to 21 CFR
312.32(c) and ICH E2F guidance. This report covers the period January 1,
2024 through March 31, 2024 (Q1 2024 reporting period). The purpose of
this SUR is to provide FDA with an interim update on the accumulating
safety database for Cervimab in the ongoing Phase 2 study CERVI-201.

This SUR is submitted because (1) the aggregate blinded safety data
review identified a new potential signal for immune-mediated
myocarditis; and (2) the sponsor is proactively updating the FDA per its
pharmacovigilance commitment.

**3. NEW SAFETY SIGNAL: IMMUNE-MEDIATED MYOCARDITIS**

**3.1 Signal Description**

Two cases of immune-mediated myocarditis have been identified in study
CERVI-201 as of the Q1 2024 data cut. This represents a reporting rate
of 3.0% (2/67 treated patients). Both cases were Grade 2 per CTCAE v5.0,
clinically manageable, and resolved with high-dose corticosteroid
therapy. Neither case resulted in hospitalization beyond
protocol-required monitoring.

**3.2 Case Narratives**

**Case 1 --- Patient 201-039**

A 58-year-old male with dMMR mCRC presented with chest discomfort and
palpitations on Day 43 (after Cycle 3). ECG showed non-specific ST
changes. Troponin I was 2.1x ULN. Cardiac MRI confirmed focal myocardial
inflammation consistent with immune-mediated myocarditis (Grade 2).
Cervimab was held. The patient received methylprednisolone 1 mg/kg/day
for 4 weeks with taper. Troponin normalized by Day 63. Cardiac MRI on
Day 90 showed complete resolution. Cervimab was permanently discontinued
per protocol. The event was assessed as possibly related to Cervimab.

**Case 2 --- Patient 201-054**

A 64-year-old female with dMMR mCRC presented with new dyspnea and
fatigue on Day 72 (after Cycle 5). BNP was elevated at 340 pg/mL.
Echocardiogram revealed mildly reduced LVEF of 48% (baseline 62%).
Cardiac MRI was performed confirming myocardial edema consistent with
myocarditis (Grade 2). Cervimab was held and the patient received
prednisone 1 mg/kg/day for 3 weeks with taper. LVEF normalized to 58% at
6-week follow-up. The event was assessed as probably related to
Cervimab.

**3.3 Regulatory Assessment and Actions Taken**

The sponsor has taken the following actions in response to this signal:

- Both cases were reported as expedited IND safety reports (7-day and
  15-day reports) consistent with 21 CFR 312.32

- Investigator Brochure Version 3.0 has been updated to add
  immune-mediated myocarditis as a potential serious irAE

- Protocol Amendment 2 has been filed to update cardiac monitoring
  requirements (baseline and Q3W troponin monitoring for first 6 cycles)

- A Dear Investigator letter was issued to all active sites on February
  12, 2024

- Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) conducted an unscheduled review on
  March 1, 2024 and recommended continued enrollment with enhanced
  monitoring

**6. BENEFIT-RISK ASSESSMENT**

The emerging safety profile of Cervimab remains consistent with the
anti-PD-1 antibody class. The new signal of immune-mediated myocarditis
(2 cases, both Grade 2, both resolved) represents a known class effect
that has been addressed through protocol and IB amendments. No
treatment-related deaths have occurred. Interim efficacy data continue
to demonstrate clinically meaningful anti-tumor activity with an ORR of
40.3% in the 67 evaluable patients, supporting a favorable benefit-risk
profile for continued enrollment with enhanced cardiac monitoring.

**7. PROPOSED ACTIONS AND REGULATORY COMMITMENTS**

- Continue enrollment with enhanced cardiac safety monitoring per
  Amendment 2

- Conduct DSMB review after every 25 additional patients enrolled

- Provide updated SUR if additional myocarditis cases are identified

- IB Version 3.0 (submitted separately) fully reflects current safety
  profile

- Sponsor will conduct pooled cardiac safety analysis as part of the
  planned interim CSR (Q3 2024)
