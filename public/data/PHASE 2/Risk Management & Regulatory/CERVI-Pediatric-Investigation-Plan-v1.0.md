**1. INTRODUCTION AND REGULATORY BASIS**

This Pediatric Investigation Plan (PIP) has been developed in accordance
with FDA requirements under the Pediatric Research Equity Act (PREA), 21
USC § 355a, and 21 CFR 312 Subpart D, for Cervimab (anti-PD-1 monoclonal
antibody). This PIP applies to the proposed indication of dMMR/MSI-H
advanced/metastatic colorectal cancer and other dMMR/MSI-H solid tumors.

Under PREA, sponsors of drugs for a new active ingredient, new
indication, new dosage form, new dosing regimen, or new route of
administration must submit pediatric studies unless a waiver or deferral
has been obtained. Oncovance Therapeutics is submitting this PIP to
fulfill PREA obligations in advance of a planned New Drug Application
(NDA).

**2. SCOPE OF PEDIATRIC INVESTIGATION**

**2.1 Target Pediatric Population**

The pediatric investigation addresses patients from 2 years to less than
18 years of age with dMMR/MSI-H advanced solid tumors, including
colorectal cancer, endometrial cancer, gastric cancer, and other tumors
with confirmed dMMR/MSI-H status. The dMMR/MSI-H biomarker is a
tumor-agnostic predictive marker, making a broad pediatric indication
scientifically appropriate.

**2.2 Scientific Rationale for Pediatric Investigation**

- Constitutional mismatch repair deficiency (CMMRD) syndrome causes dMMR
  tumors in children and adolescents, including high-grade gliomas,
  colorectal adenomas/carcinomas, and hematologic malignancies

- Sporadic MSI-H CRC is rare but documented in patients aged 12--17; the
  dMMR/MSI-H biomarker is age-independent as a predictor of PD-1
  response

- PD-1 is expressed on pediatric T cells; nonclinical data suggest a
  similar pharmacological mechanism in juvenile animals

- Extrapolation of adult PK/PD to adolescents (≥12 years) is supported
  by FDA guidance on pediatric extrapolation (2016) given similar
  body-weight-adjusted clearance for IgG4 antibodies

**4. WAIVERS AND DEFERRALS**

**4.1 Full Waiver Request --- Neonates/Infants (Birth to \<2 Years)**

Oncovance Therapeutics requests a full waiver from pediatric studies in
neonates and infants (birth to \<2 years) on the following grounds per
21 CFR 314.55(c)(1):

1.  Necessary studies are impossible or highly impracticable ---
    dMMR/MSI-H tumors do not occur in neonates or infants; this age
    group would not be enrolled.

2.  There is evidence that the drug would be ineffective or unsafe in
    this age group --- the neonatal immune system differs fundamentally;
    theoretical risks of immune dysregulation, thymus disruption, and
    impaired immune development are disproportionate to any potential
    benefit given the absence of indication in this age group.

3.  The disease or condition does not occur in neonates --- MSI-H CRC
    and CMMRD-associated tumors requiring systemic therapy have not been
    documented in patients under 2 years.

**4.2 Deferral Request --- Pediatric Studies (Ages 2--\<18 Years)**

Oncovance Therapeutics requests a deferral for all pediatric studies
until after completion of the adult Phase 2 program (CERVI-201) and
submission of the adult NDA. Grounds for deferral per 21 CFR
314.55(c)(2):

4.  The adult indication has not yet been approved; completion of adult
    development is necessary to establish proof-of-concept before
    pediatric investigation.

5.  The proposed pediatric study CERVI-301P cannot be completed before
    NDA submission given the time required for site setup at pediatric
    oncology centers and enrollment of a rare pediatric population.

6.  There is no immediate unmet pediatric need that cannot be addressed
    through FDA\'s existing accelerated approval and expanded access
    mechanisms in the interim.

Pediatric studies will be completed and submitted as a post-approval
pediatric study commitment (PSC) within 3 years of adult NDA approval.

**5. PEDIATRIC FORMULATION CONSIDERATIONS**

The current adult formulation (200 mg/20 mL solution for IV infusion) is
appropriate for adolescent patients ≥12 years and patients ≥40 kg. For
younger children requiring weight-based dosing, the following
formulation work is planned:

- Development of a lower-concentration solution (10 mg/mL) to enable
  precise weight-based dosing in smaller patients

- Compatibility and stability studies for diluted concentrations in
  pediatric-appropriate IV bags (25 mL, 50 mL)

- Palatability and administration route studies are not applicable (IV
  only)

Formulation development will be completed prior to first patient
enrollment in CERVI-301P.

**6. EXTRAPOLATION STRATEGY**

For the adolescent cohort (12--\<18 years), a pharmacokinetic
extrapolation approach will be employed consistent with FDA\'s Pediatric
Extrapolation Guidance (2016). The following assumptions support
extrapolation:

- The disease in adolescents with dMMR/MSI-H tumors shares the same
  pathophysiology as adult dMMR/MSI-H tumors

- The response to PD-1 blockade is driven by the same biomarker
  (dMMR/MSI-H) and the same immune mechanism

- Population PK modeling from adult CERVI-201 PK data will be used to
  simulate adolescent exposure profiles; dose adjustment will target
  adolescent AUC within 80--125% of the adult target AUC

- A limited PK assessment (rich sampling for first 3 patients per age
  cohort) will confirm model predictions before expanding enrollment

**8. PEDIATRIC EXPERTISE AND COLLABORATION**

The pediatric investigation will be conducted in collaboration with the
Children\'s Oncology Group (COG) and the New Approaches to Neuroblastoma
Therapy (NANT) consortium. A dedicated Pediatric Advisory Committee
comprising two pediatric oncologists, a pediatric pharmacologist, and a
patient advocate will provide ongoing scientific oversight. Oncovance
has engaged Pediatric Oncology Consultants International (POCI) as
external pediatric regulatory and scientific advisors.
