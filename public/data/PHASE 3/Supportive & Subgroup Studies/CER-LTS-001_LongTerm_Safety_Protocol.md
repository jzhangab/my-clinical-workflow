**2. BACKGROUND AND RATIONALE**

**2.1 Disease Background**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) is the third most commonly diagnosed cancer and
the second leading cause of cancer-related mortality in the United
States, with approximately 153,000 new cases annually. Metastatic CRC
(mCRC) carries a 5-year survival rate of approximately 14%.
Approximately 4--5% of unselected mCRC cases harbor deficient mismatch
repair (dMMR) or high microsatellite instability (MSI-H), a biomarker
associated with elevated tumor mutational burden, high immunogenicity,
and markedly enhanced sensitivity to immune checkpoint inhibitor
therapy.

PD-1/PD-L1 axis inhibition has demonstrated robust and durable clinical
responses in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC. The pivotal Phase 3 CER-301 study
established that CERVIMAB significantly prolongs progression-free
survival versus standard-of-care chemotherapy (FOLFOX/FOLFIRI +
bevacizumab). Given the potential for durable remissions, some subjects
may continue CERVIMAB for extended periods or may require ongoing safety
surveillance for delayed immune-related adverse events (irAEs) following
treatment discontinuation.

**2.2 Rationale for Long-Term Safety Follow-Up**

Anti-PD-1 therapy is associated with a broad spectrum of immune-related
adverse events (irAEs) that can occur at any time during or after
treatment. Key safety considerations warranting systematic long-term
follow-up include:

- Late-onset irAEs (endocrinopathies, pneumonitis, colitis, hepatitis,
  nephritis) that may manifest months to years after last dose

- Persistent or chronic irAEs requiring ongoing immunosuppressive
  management

- Secondary malignancies associated with chronic immune dysregulation

- Rare but serious delayed events: aplastic anemia, myocarditis,
  encephalitis, Guillain-Barré syndrome

- Impact of prior irAE treatment (systemic corticosteroids, biologics)
  on long-term immune function

- Infections resulting from persistent immunosuppression

This Long-Term Safety Follow-Up (LTSF) protocol satisfies the FDA
requirement under 21 CFR §312.32 and Guidance for Industry: Long-Term
Follow-Up After Administration of Human Gene Therapy Products (where
applicable), as well as ICH E2E pharmacovigilance planning obligations
for novel biologics with a mechanistically immunomodulatory mechanism of
action.

**3. STUDY OBJECTIVES AND ENDPOINTS**

**3.1 Primary Safety Objective**

To characterize the cumulative incidence, severity, time-to-onset,
resolution, and clinical management of long-term and late-onset adverse
events attributable to CERVIMAB, including immune-related adverse events
(irAEs), in subjects with dMMR/MSI-H mCRC over a minimum 5-year
follow-up period.

**3.2 Primary Safety Endpoints**

- Incidence, severity (per CTCAE v5.0), and time-to-onset of any-grade
  and Grade ≥3 irAEs emerging ≥90 days after last CERVIMAB dose

- Incidence of new-onset autoimmune conditions (e.g., type 1 diabetes
  mellitus, thyroid dysfunction, adrenal insufficiency, autoimmune
  hepatitis, inflammatory bowel disease)

- All-cause mortality and cause-specific mortality

- Incidence of new primary malignancies

- Incidence of serious infections (Grade ≥3 or requiring
  hospitalization)

**3.3 Secondary Objectives and Endpoints**

Secondary safety endpoints include:

- Duration and resolution of irAEs requiring systemic corticosteroids or
  immunosuppressive agents

- Cumulative exposure to corticosteroids (mg prednisone-equivalent) and
  immunosuppressive biologics (infliximab, mycophenolate) in the
  follow-up period

- Laboratory abnormalities: thyroid function (TSH, free T4),
  cortisol/ACTH stimulation, liver enzymes (ALT, AST), creatinine/eGFR,
  CBC with differential

- Immune reconstitution markers: CD4+/CD8+ T-cell counts, immunoglobulin
  levels (IgG, IgA, IgM) at Years 1, 3, and 5

Secondary efficacy endpoints include:

- Overall survival (OS) from first CERVIMAB dose

- Duration of response (DoR) per RECIST v1.1 in subjects with prior
  objective response

- Proportion of subjects alive and progression-free at Years 1, 2, 3,
  and 5

Patient-reported outcomes:

- EQ-5D-5L Health Utility Index at each annual visit

- FACT-C (Functional Assessment of Cancer Therapy --- Colorectal) at
  each annual visit

**4. STUDY POPULATION**

**4.1 Inclusion Criteria**

Subjects must meet ALL of the following criteria:

1.  Enrollment in and receipt of ≥1 dose of CERVIMAB in the parent
    CER-301 Phase 3 study

2.  Provision of written informed consent for long-term safety follow-up
    prior to any study procedures

3.  Willingness and ability to comply with scheduled follow-up visits
    and telephone assessments

4.  For subjects electing optional continued treatment: adequate organ
    function per CER-301 eligibility criteria (e.g., ECOG PS 0--2,
    adequate renal and hepatic function)

**4.2 Exclusion Criteria**

5.  Withdrawal of consent from CER-301 parent study for reasons that
    preclude further contact

6.  Prisoners or subjects under legal compulsion per 21 CFR §50.44

7.  Any condition that, in the investigator\'s judgment, would
    compromise subject safety or protocol compliance

**4.3 Subject Disposition Tracking**

All subjects rolling over from CER-301 will be assigned a Long-Term
Follow-Up (LTFU) subject identification number. Reasons for
non-enrollment (e.g., death prior to rollover, withdrawal of consent,
lost to follow-up) will be captured in the disposition log.

**5.3 Safety Monitoring**

All adverse events occurring during the follow-up period will be
assessed for:

- Severity per NCI CTCAE v5.0

- Relationship to prior CERVIMAB exposure (definitely related, probably
  related, possibly related, unlikely related, not related)

- Immune-related attribution per protocol-defined irAE criteria

- Seriousness (SAE criteria per 21 CFR §312.32 and ICH E6)

An independent Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) with expertise in
oncology, immunology, and biostatistics will review unblinded cumulative
safety data on a semi-annual basis. The DSMB Charter (separate document)
specifies stopping rules and safety review procedures.

**6.2 Serious and Unexpected irAE Reporting**

All Grade 3--4 irAEs and any Grade 2 irAEs that are unexpected (unlisted
in current IB) must be reported as Serious Adverse Events (SAEs) per 21
CFR §312.32:

- Fatal or life-threatening: IND Safety Report within 7 calendar days
  (initial) and 15 days (complete)

- Serious, unexpected, related: IND Safety Report within 15 calendar
  days

- Serious, expected, related: Aggregate reporting in annual IND report

**7. STATISTICAL CONSIDERATIONS**

**7.1 Sample Size**

The LTFU study will enroll all eligible subjects from CER-301 (up to
N=420). No formal power calculation is required for this descriptive
safety follow-up study. The sample size is determined by the parent
study enrollment.

**7.2 Analysis Populations**

- Long-Term Safety Population (LTSP): All subjects who received ≥1 dose
  of CERVIMAB and enrolled in CER-LTS-001

- Extended Treatment Population (ETP): Subjects in Track B who receive
  ≥1 additional dose of CERVIMAB

- Observational Cohort: All subjects in Track A

**7.3 Primary Analyses**

Safety analyses will be primarily descriptive:

- Cumulative incidence of irAEs presented as n (%), incidence rate per
  100 patient-years with 95% Poisson CI

- Time-to-onset and duration of irAEs summarized using Kaplan-Meier
  methodology

- Secondary malignancy rates compared to age-sex-standardized background
  cancer incidence rates (SEER database)

- Overall survival estimated by Kaplan-Meier method with log-rank test
  for comparison between continued treatment and observation cohorts

- HRQoL longitudinal changes modeled using mixed-effects model for
  repeated measures (MMRM)

**7.4 Data Cutoffs and Reports**

Annual interim safety reports will be provided to the DSMB and submitted
to the FDA as required. A final integrated safety report will be
prepared at study closure for submission per NDA or BLA safety update
requirements.
