**2. BACKGROUND AND SCIENTIFIC RATIONALE**

**2.1 CERVIMAB Pharmacokinetics Overview**

CERVIMAB is a humanized IgG4κ monoclonal antibody targeting the PD-1
receptor. As a large molecular weight protein therapeutic (\~147 kDa),
CERVIMAB is primarily eliminated via proteolytic catabolism rather than
renal filtration or hepatic enzymatic metabolism. Nonetheless, FDA and
EMA guidance documents require dedicated pharmacokinetic studies in
patients with organ impairment for all novel biologics seeking
regulatory approval.

Population PK data from the Phase 1/2 CER-101 study (N=186) and the
Phase 3 CER-301 study (N=420) suggest that mild-to-moderate renal
impairment (eGFR 30--89 mL/min/1.73 m²) does not meaningfully alter
CERVIMAB systemic exposure. However, data in subjects with severe renal
impairment (eGFR \<30 mL/min/1.73 m², not on dialysis) and end-stage
renal disease (ESRD) are limited (n\<10 total across studies).
Similarly, subjects with moderate-to-severe hepatic impairment
(Child-Pugh B and C) have been systematically excluded from prior
CERVIMAB studies.

**2.2 Regulatory Requirement**

Per FDA Guidance for Industry --- Pharmacokinetics in Patients with
Impaired Renal Function: Study Design, Data Analysis, and Impact on
Dosing and Labeling (2010) and the associated draft update (2020), a
dedicated clinical study is required when:

1.  The drug or active metabolites are renally excreted OR renal
    impairment may affect drug disposition through indirect mechanisms
    (protein binding, secondary organ effects)

2.  Existing data do not adequately characterize the PK across the full
    spectrum of impairment

3.  Post-marketing labeling will include dosing guidance for impaired
    populations

The study design follows the FDA-recommended parallel-group approach
(preferred over the reduced study design given the planned broad patient
population in the CERVIMAB NDA).

**3. STUDY DESIGN**

**3.1 Overview**

CER-SP-002 is an open-label, parallel-group, multicenter study enrolling
subjects across 8 organ function cohorts (4 renal + 4 hepatic), as
detailed below. Each subject receives a single intravenous dose of
CERVIMAB 400 mg (the intended commercial dose), followed by dense PK
sampling over 70 days and an optional continued treatment period for
subjects with cancer.

Cohorts are classified per FDA guidance and KDIGO criteria:

**4. ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA**

**4.1 Inclusion Criteria --- All Cohorts**

4.  Adults ≥18 years of age

5.  Histologically or cytologically confirmed solid tumor malignancy
    (dMMR/MSI-H mCRC preferred; other advanced solid tumors allowed for
    organ impairment cohorts if clinically appropriate)

6.  Willingness to provide written informed consent

7.  ECOG Performance Status 0--3 (ECOG 3 permitted only with medical
    monitor approval for severe impairment cohorts)

8.  Life expectancy ≥3 months (≥6 months for optional continued
    treatment)

9.  Adequate contraception in subjects of childbearing potential

**4.2 Cohort-Specific Inclusion Criteria**

**Renal Impairment Cohorts (R2--R4):**

- Confirmed stable chronic kidney disease (CKD) per KDIGO criteria; eGFR
  must be stable (±20%) over 3 months prior to enrollment

- Not requiring acute dialysis initiation within 3 months of enrollment

**Hepatic Impairment Cohorts (H2--H4):**

- Confirmed chronic liver disease (CLD) of any etiology with Child-Pugh
  classification as specified per cohort

- Stable liver function for ≥4 weeks prior to enrollment (no acute
  hepatic decompensation)

- For H4 (Child-Pugh C): Albumin ≥2.0 g/dL; INR ≤3.0; No active Grade ≥2
  hepatic encephalopathy at screening

**4.3 Exclusion Criteria**

10. Active, uncontrolled autoimmune disease requiring systemic
    immunosuppressive therapy

11. Prior exposure to any anti-PD-1, anti-PD-L1, or anti-CTLA-4 agent

12. Active or prior documented hepatitis B (unless on suppressive
    antiviral therapy with undetectable viral load) or hepatitis C
    (unless achieving sustained virologic response)

13. Known HIV infection with CD4 count \<350 cells/μL or detectable
    viral load

14. Solid organ transplant or allogeneic stem cell transplant

15. Acute renal failure or acute-on-chronic kidney disease within 4
    weeks of screening

16. Concurrent enrollment in any interventional clinical study with
    experimental therapy

17. Pregnancy or breastfeeding

18. Any condition that, in the investigator\'s judgment, would preclude
    safe participation

**5. PHARMACOKINETIC SAMPLING AND ASSESSMENTS**

**5.1 PK Sampling Schedule**

All subjects will receive a single dose of CERVIMAB 400 mg IV (60-minute
infusion) on Day 1, with the following PK sample collection schedule:

  -------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Timepoint**   **Sample Collection** **Notes**
  --------------- --------------------- -----------------------------
  Day 1 Pre-dose  1 sample              Within 60 min before infusion
                                        start

  Day 1 End of    1 sample              Within 5 min of infusion
  Infusion                              completion

  Day 1 Post-dose 4 samples: 2h, 4h,    Dense early sampling for Cmax
                  6h, 12h               characterization

  Day 2, 3, 4     1 sample/day          

  Day 7, 10, 14   1 sample/day          

  Day 21, 28, 42, 1 sample/day          Terminal phase
  56, 70                                characterization; Day 70 for
                                        t1/2 estimation

  Early           1 sample              Regardless of scheduled
  Termination                           timepoint
  -------------------------------------------------------------------

Total PK blood volume: approximately 90 mL over the 70-day PK period (3
mL per time point). An additional 15 mL will be collected at pre-dose
and Day 70 for immunogenicity (ADA assay) and exploratory biomarkers.

**5.2 Primary PK Parameters**

- AUC0-inf: Area under the serum concentration-time curve from time 0 to
  infinity

- AUC0-t: AUC to last quantifiable time point

- Cmax: Maximum observed serum concentration

- tmax: Time to Cmax

- t1/2: Terminal elimination half-life

- CL: Systemic clearance

- Vd (Vss, Vz): Volume of distribution at steady state and terminal
  phase

- ADA Incidence: Anti-drug antibody titer and status at pre-dose and Day
  70

**5.3 Statistical Analysis --- PK**

Non-compartmental analysis (NCA) will be performed using Phoenix
WinNonlin v8.x (Certara). Key analyses:

- Geometric mean ratio (GMR) with 90% CI for AUC0-inf and Cmax (test
  \[impaired\] vs. reference \[normal\]) per FDA guidance

- No equivalence boundaries are pre-specified for this descriptive PK
  characterization; GMR and 90% CI will be presented for labeling
  guidance

- Population PK (PopPK) modeling will be performed combining CER-SP-002
  data with CER-301 dataset (NONMEM v7.5); renal function (eGFR) and
  hepatic function covariates will be evaluated

- FDA will be provided with labeling recommendations for dose adjustment
  (or confirmation of no dose adjustment required) based on PopPK
  simulation results

**6.2 Stopping Rules for Hepatic Impairment Cohorts**

The following pre-specified stopping rules will trigger immediate
medical monitor notification and potential cohort hold:

19. Any Grade 4 hepatic adverse event (ALT/AST \>20× ULN or total
    bilirubin \>10× ULN) in any subject in H3 or H4

20. Any Grade 3 hepatic event in ≥2/8 subjects in H3 or H4 within the
    same cohort

21. New or worsening Grade ≥2 hepatic encephalopathy in any H4 subject
    within 30 days post-dose

22. Any fatal hepatic adverse event assessed as related to CERVIMAB

**6.3 Dose Modification Guidance**

CERVIMAB dose modifications are not anticipated for this single-dose PK
study. If a subject transitions to optional continued treatment, dose
modifications will follow the CER-301 protocol guidelines. Per current
IB data, no dose adjustment is expected for mild-to-moderate renal or
hepatic impairment; this study will either confirm or modify this
guidance for labeling.

**7. REGULATORY DELIVERABLES**

**7.1 Labeling Impact**

Results from CER-SP-002 will directly support the CERVIMAB BLA/NDA
labeling sections including:

- Section 12.3 (Pharmacokinetics) --- PK parameters by organ function
  group with GMR and 90% CI

- Section 8.6 (Renal Impairment) --- Dosing guidance or confirmation no
  dose adjustment required

- Section 8.7 (Hepatic Impairment) --- Dosing guidance by Child-Pugh
  category

- Section 17 (Patient Counseling) --- Information for patients with
  impaired organ function

**7.2 Study Report Timeline**

The Clinical Study Report (CSR) for CER-SP-002 will be completed within
12 months of last subject last visit and submitted to FDA as part of the
NDA/BLA rolling submission package. A brief interim analysis will be
provided to FDA after completion of all Cohort R4 and H4 subjects if
significant PK differences are observed that may require urgent labeling
guidance.
