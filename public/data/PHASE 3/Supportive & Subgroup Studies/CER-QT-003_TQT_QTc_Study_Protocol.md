**2. BACKGROUND AND REGULATORY RATIONALE**

**2.1 ICH E14 Requirements**

The International Conference on Harmonisation (ICH) E14 guideline,
Effect of Test Drugs on the QT/QTc Interval Including Lead for the
Detection of Potential Proarrhythmic Effects, requires that sponsors
conduct a thorough QT/QTc study (TQT) as part of the non-clinical and
clinical cardiac safety evaluation of a new drug prior to regulatory
approval. The study is designed to determine whether the drug has a
threshold pharmacological effect on cardiac repolarization, defined as a
mean QTcF prolongation of \>10 ms above placebo-corrected baseline, with
an upper bound of the two-sided 90% CI exceeding 10 ms (the regulatory
threshold of clinical concern).

Under the 2012 and 2017 FDA E14 Q&A updates, a dedicated TQT study may
be waived (concentration-QTc \[C-QTc\] analysis substituted) if: (1)
adequate QTc data with PK are available from early phase studies at
supratherapeutic exposures, and (2) the drug mechanism does not suggest
hERG channel liability. However, given that CERVIMAB is a novel
anti-PD-1 antibody with a distinct immune-modulatory mechanism and that
PD-1 pathway activation has been associated with myocarditis and cardiac
conduction changes in post-marketing surveillance, NovaMed elects to
conduct a formal TQT/concentration-QTc integrated analysis.

**2.2 Cardiac Safety Considerations for CERVIMAB**

Anti-PD-1 immune checkpoint inhibitors have been associated with
immune-mediated cardiotoxicity, including:

- Immune-mediated myocarditis (incidence \~1--2%; can be fatal in
  \~25--50% of affected subjects)

- Pericarditis, pericardial effusion

- Arrhythmias: atrial fibrillation, complete heart block, ventricular
  tachycardia

- Troponin elevation without overt cardiac symptoms

Beyond direct cardiac irAEs, immune-mediated endocrinopathies (e.g.,
thyroiditis, adrenal insufficiency) may secondarily alter QTc through
electrolyte changes (hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia). The TQT study will
explicitly evaluate QTc at matched PK timepoints to differentiate direct
pharmacological effects from secondary electrolyte-mediated changes.

**2.3 Known PK and QTc Data at Time of Protocol Development**

Across the CER-101 and CER-301 studies, 12-lead ECG data collected at
pre-dose and 1--2 time points post-dose showed no signal of QTcF
prolongation at therapeutic doses (400 mg IV Q6W; mean Cmax \~124
μg/mL). However, these data were not collected according to TQT
standards (i.e., no continuous Holter monitoring, single time-point
post-dose ECG only, no moxifloxacin positive control). This dedicated
TQT study provides the regulatory-grade assessment required for the
NDA/BLA package.

**4. ECG METHODOLOGY**

**4.1 ECG Collection Standards**

All ECGs will be collected per TQT-grade standards:

- Triplicate 12-lead digital ECGs collected at each time-matched
  post-dose timepoint (within 5-minute window per replicate)

- Subjects supine for ≥10 minutes before ECG acquisition; ECG collected
  before blood sampling to avoid positional/movement artifact

- Continuous 12-lead Holter monitoring (24-hour digital Holter) for the
  first 4 days post-dose for time-matched QTcF extraction

- Central ECG laboratory: All ECGs transmitted digitally to an
  independent, FDA-qualified central ECG reading laboratory (blinded to
  treatment arm, timepoint, and PK data)

- QTcF (Fridericia correction) is the primary correction method; QTcB
  and QTci will be analyzed as supportive

**4.3 Positive Control Validation (Arm D --- Moxifloxacin)**

Moxifloxacin 400 mg oral will be administered as a single dose to Arm D
subjects on Day 1. ECGs will be collected at: pre-dose, 0.5h, 1h, 2h,
3h, 4h, 6h, 8h, and 12h post-dose. The assay is considered validated
(i.e., sensitive) if the lower bound of the 90% CI for ΔΔQTcF exceeds 5
ms at any time point within 1--4h post-dose (the expected moxifloxacin
Tmax window). Failure to demonstrate sensitivity will require
investigation and protocol amendment before primary analysis.

**5. PHARMACOKINETIC AND CONCENTRATION-QTc ANALYSIS**

**5.1 PK Assessment**

Serum CERVIMAB concentration measurements will be performed from blood
samples collected at all ECG timepoints (time-matched PK-ECG pairs
required). Validated bioanalytical assay (ELISA, LLOQ ≤1 ng/mL)
developed per FDA Guidance for Industry: Bioanalytical Method Validation
(2018).

**5.2 Concentration-QTc (C-QTc) Modeling**

As per the 2017 FDA E14 Q&A and ICH E14 R3 update, a pre-specified
linear mixed-effects concentration-QTc (C-QTc) model will be fit to all
time-matched PK-QTc data:

  ------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Parameter**    **Specification**
  ---------------- -------------------------------------------------
  Model            ΔQTcF = α + β × \[CERVIMAB\] + γ × TIME + δ ×
                   \[CERVIMAB:TIME interaction (if significant)\] +
                   random effects

  PK Metric        Serum CERVIMAB concentration (μg/mL) at each
                   time-matched ECG timepoint

  Primary          Slope β: change in QTcF (ms) per μg/mL increase
  Interpretation   in CERVIMAB concentration

  Decision         If upper bound of 90% CI for predicted ΔΔQTcF at
  Threshold        geometric mean Cmax \<10 ms, no QT concern
                   identified

  Software         R v4.x (nlme, lme4 packages) or SAS v9.4;
                   pre-specified analysis code lodged in SAP

  Sensitivity      Emax model as pre-specified sensitivity analysis;
  Analysis         QTcB and QTci as supportive corrections
  ------------------------------------------------------------------

**5.3 Primary Statistical Endpoint**

The primary analysis is the largest mean ΔΔQTcF (placebo-corrected
change from time-matched baseline QTcF) observed at any timepoint in
Arms A and B, with corresponding two-sided 90% CI. The study will be
considered to demonstrate absence of clinically meaningful QTc
prolongation if:

- The upper bound of the two-sided 90% CI of the mean ΔΔQTcF at each
  pre-specified timepoint is below 10 ms

- The upper bound of the 90% CI for the C-QTc model-predicted ΔΔQTcF at
  2× geometric mean Cmax is below 10 ms

If either criterion is not met, additional analysis, labeling language,
and risk mitigation strategy will be developed in consultation with FDA
Cardiac Safety team.

**6.2 Cardiac Immune-Related Adverse Events**

Immune-mediated myocarditis is of particular concern given the mechanism
of action of anti-PD-1 therapy. All subjects will have troponin I
(high-sensitivity), BNP/NT-proBNP, and ECG collected at Screening, Day 1
pre-dose, Day 7, Day 15, and any unscheduled visit for cardiac symptoms.
If troponin elevation ≥2× ULN or new significant ECG changes are
observed:

1.  Immediately hold study drug and all systemic anticancer therapy

2.  Obtain urgent 12-lead ECG, echocardiogram, and cardiac MRI (if
    feasible)

3.  Initiate high-dose methylprednisolone 1--2 mg/kg/day IV immediately
    if myocarditis is clinically suspected

4.  Report as SAE to sponsor within 24 hours; expedited IND Safety
    Report within 7 days if fatal or life-threatening

5.  Cardiology co-management required for all confirmed cardiac irAEs

**6.3 Independent Cardiac Safety Review**

A qualified independent Cardiac Safety Review Committee (CSRC),
consisting of at least two board-certified cardiologists with
electrophysiology expertise and one clinical pharmacologist, will review
all serious cardiac events, ECG outliers (QTcF \>480 ms), and any
troponin elevations in real-time. The CSRC will advise the medical
monitor and sponsor on subject continuation and protocol modifications.

**7. STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN OVERVIEW**

**7.1 Sample Size Justification**

A sample size of n=18 per arm (N=72 total) provides ≥90% power to detect
the absence of a mean ΔΔQTcF \>10 ms at any timepoint, assuming a true
ΔΔQTcF of 0 ms (no effect), SD of QTcF change of 9 ms (based on
literature data for oncology TQT studies with IV biologics), and
applying a one-sample t-test approach for the ΔΔQTcF at the primary
timepoint. Allowance for up to 20% dropout provides an enrollment target
of n=23 per arm.

**7.2 ECG Data Analysis**

- ECG data processing: Central ECG laboratory will provide digitally
  adjudicated measurements of RR, PR, QRS, QT, QTcF, QTcB, QTci
  intervals; median of triplicates used

- Time-matched baseline: Pre-dose ECG at corresponding nominal clock
  time on the day prior to dosing (Holter-extracted)

- Primary analysis: Baseline-adjusted and placebo-corrected (ΔΔQTcF) by
  the intersection-union principle with 90% CI at each pre-specified
  timepoint

- Outlier analysis: Proportion of subjects with QTcF \>450, \>480, \>500
  ms and ΔQTcF \>30, \>60 ms tabulated per arm

- Categorical analysis: Fisher\'s exact test for proportion with any
  QTcF threshold-exceeding event

- C-QTc analysis: As specified in Section 5.2; pre-specified analysis
  code filed with FDA prior to database lock

**7.3 Analysis Populations**

- Full Analysis Set (FAS): All randomized subjects who received ≥1 dose
  and have ≥1 post-dose ECG

- PK-QTc Analysis Set: All FAS subjects with ≥1 matched PK-ECG pair
  within the specified time window

- Positive Control Validation Set: Arm D subjects with ≥1 post-dose ECG
  in the 1--4h Tmax window
