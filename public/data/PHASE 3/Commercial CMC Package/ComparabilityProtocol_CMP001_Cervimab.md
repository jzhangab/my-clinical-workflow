**1. INTRODUCTION AND BACKGROUND**

**1.1 Purpose**

This Comparability Protocol (CMP-001) defines the criteria and
analytical testing strategy to assess whether cervimab drug substance
manufactured at the commercial scale (2,000 L fed-batch bioreactor, Site
A) is comparable to drug substance manufactured at the Phase 3 clinical
scale (200 L fed-batch bioreactor, Site A) used in the pivotal Phase 3
clinical trial (Study CVM-301). The objective is to demonstrate that the
manufacturing process change has not adversely affected the safety,
efficacy, or quality of cervimab.

**1.2 Description of Manufacturing Changes**

Three manufacturing changes were introduced between Phase 3 and
commercial-scale manufacturing:

The formulation, downstream purification chemistry, and container
closure system are UNCHANGED between Phase 3 and commercial
manufacturing. The production bioreactor process conditions
(temperature, pH, DO, nutrient feeds, culture duration) are within the
established proven acceptable ranges (PARs) and characterized design
space.

**1.3 Regulatory Context**

Under ICH Q5E, the purpose of comparability assessment is to establish
that the pre- and post-change product has a similar profile with respect
to quality, safety, and efficacy. If comparability is established at the
quality level, clinical bridging is not required. This protocol defines
the approach to demonstrating quality comparability. Safety and efficacy
comparability are supported by the unchanged clinical indication, dosing
regimen, and formulation.

**2.2 Batch Disposition for Comparability**

Pre-change: Three Phase 3 clinical-scale drug substance batches
(CVM-DS-P3-001, P3-002, P3-003), manufactured at 200 L scale under the
Phase 3 process.

Post-change: Three commercial-scale PPQ drug substance batches
(CVM-DS-PPQ-V01, V02, V03), manufactured at 2,000 L scale.

All six batches were manufactured at the same site (Acme Biologics, RTP)
using the same CHO-K1 cell bank (MCB-Gen3-Lot001). Side-by-side testing
was conducted from retained samples stored at \<=−70°C.

**4. PRE-DEFINED ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA AND DECISION TREE**

**4.1 Overall Comparability Conclusion Criteria**

Comparability is established (product is \'comparable\') when ALL of the
following conditions are met:

- ALL Tier 1 critical attributes meet their pre-defined acceptance
  criteria

- ALL Tier 2 important attributes show no statistically significant
  differences AND all individual values are within the approved
  specification

- ALL Tier 3 supportive attributes show qualitative similarity with no
  unexpected differences

- No new impurities are identified in the post-change material above
  reporting thresholds

- No new structural variants are identified by mass spectrometry that
  are absent from the pre-change material

If ANY Tier 1 criterion is not met, comparability is not established and
a clinical bridging study may be required.

If any Tier 2 or Tier 3 finding is unexpected, a root cause
investigation will be conducted and the impact on product quality and
clinical safety/efficacy will be assessed prior to a final comparability
determination.

**5.2 New Impurities Assessment**

No new product-related or process-related impurities were identified in
the post-change (commercial PPQ) material above ICH Q6B reporting
thresholds. Mass spectrometric peptide mapping (n=3 batches each)
confirmed identical variant profiles between pre- and post-change
material. No new HCP species were identified by 2D-DIGE or mass
spectrometry-based HCP characterization in the post-change material.

**5.3 Comparability Conclusion**

Based on the totality of the analytical evidence, cervimab drug
substance manufactured at the commercial scale (2,000 L fed-batch, Acme
Biologics) is COMPARABLE to the Phase 3 clinical material (200 L scale)
used in the pivotal Phase 3 trial (CVM-301). All Tier 1, Tier 2, and
Tier 3 acceptance criteria are satisfied. A minor, statistically
non-significant improvement in HCP clearance was observed at commercial
scale, which is attributed to the improved process control at larger
scale and is not considered an adverse change.

*No clinical bridging study is required. This comparability package,
including the full Comparability Report (CRM-001), will be submitted as
part of the BLA Module 3.*

**6. IMPACT ON CLINICAL SAFETY AND EFFICACY**

The comparability assessment supports the conclusion that the
manufacturing process change has not adversely affected the safety or
efficacy of cervimab:

- Biological potency (PD-1/PD-L1 blockade) is comparable, supporting
  equivalent in vivo activity

- No change in PD-1 binding affinity (KD) was observed, confirming the
  target engagement profile is unchanged

- Glycosylation profile is comparable, supporting equivalent Fc effector
  function (minimal ADCC, consistent FcRn binding)

- Aggregate and particle content is comparable or improved, supporting
  equivalent immunogenicity risk

- Process impurity levels (HCP, DNA) are within specifications with a
  non-adverse decrease at commercial scale

- Higher-order structure (CD, DSC, HDX-MS, intact mass) is
  indistinguishable between pre- and post-change material

These data, in conjunction with the established pharmacological
mechanism of PD-1 inhibition, provide high confidence that clinical
outcomes observed in Study CVM-301 are fully applicable to cervimab
manufactured at the commercial scale.
