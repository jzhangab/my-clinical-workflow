**1. INTRODUCTION**

**1.1 Purpose**

This Shelf Life Determination Report (SLD-001) summarizes the stability
data generated for cervimab drug substance (DS) and drug product (DP) in
support of the proposed 24-month shelf life at 2-8 deg C. Stability
studies were conducted in accordance with ICH Q5C (Stability Testing of
Biotechnological/Biological Products), ICH Q1A(R2), ICH Q1E (Evaluation
of Stability Data), and FDA Guidance for Industry: Stability Testing of
Biological/Biotechnological Products (1996).

**1.2 Stability Indicating Methods**

Stability-indicating analytical methods were selected based on their
sensitivity to detect product changes under stability conditions.
Methods were validated per ICH Q2(R2) and demonstrated to be
stability-indicating through forced degradation studies. Key
stability-indicating methods include:

**5. STATISTICAL ANALYSIS AND SHELF LIFE DETERMINATION**

**5.1 Statistical Methodology**

Shelf life was determined using a linear regression analysis of
stability data per ICH Q1E. For each stability-indicating attribute, the
rate of change was estimated by linear regression of the
time-versus-response data pooled across batches (per ICH Q1E poolability
testing). The shelf life was estimated as the time at which the 95%
one-sided confidence interval of the regression line intersects the
specification limit.

**5.3 Drug Product vs. Drug Substance Stability Comparison**

Drug product stability at 2-8 deg C is comparable to drug substance
stability under the same conditions. Minor differences in polysorbate 20
content (gradual decrease of \~0.006 mg/mL over 24 months, within
specification throughout) were attributed to oxidation of polysorbate in
the presence of residual peroxides and are not considered adverse. The
rate of change in all other attributes is indistinguishable between DS
and DP.

**6. CONCLUSIONS AND PROPOSED LABELING**

**6.1 Drug Substance Shelf Life**

- Proposed shelf life: 24 months at 2-8 deg C (refrigerated; do not
  freeze)

- Alternative storage: \<= -70 deg C for long-term bulk DS storage with
  \<= 3 freeze-thaw cycles permitted

- Supported by: 24-month real-time stability data from 6 batches;
  accelerated data consistent with Arrhenius prediction of \> 28 months
  at 2-8 deg C

- Photostability: cervimab DS is photosensitive; store in original
  container, protected from light

**6.2 Drug Product Shelf Life**

- Proposed shelf life: 24 months at 2-8 deg C (refrigerated; do not
  freeze)

- In-use stability: diluted solution (0.9% NaCl, 1-10 mg/mL) is stable
  for 24 hours at 2-8 deg C or 8 hours at 25 deg C (room temperature);
  do not store diluted product beyond these time limits

- Compatible diluents: 0.9% Sodium Chloride Injection, USP (normal
  saline); D5W is NOT compatible

- Compatible containers: PVC and non-PVC IV bags and infusion sets

- Photostability: store in outer carton until time of use; administer
  within 8 hours of preparation if stored at 25 deg C

**6.3 Proposed Labeling Statement**

*Store CERVIMAB at 2 degrees C to 8 degrees C (36 degrees F to 46
degrees F) in original carton until time of use to protect from light.
Do not freeze or shake. Discard vial if frozen. After dilution, the
infusion solution may be stored for up to 24 hours at 2 degrees C to 8
degrees C or for up to 8 hours at room temperature (up to 25 degrees
C/77 degrees F), including infusion time. Discard any unused portion
remaining in the vial.*

**6.4 Commitment to Continue Stability Studies**

Ongoing stability studies will continue through the proposed shelf life
period and beyond, per the FDA and ICH commitments made at time of
initial BLA submission. Annual stability reports will be submitted to
the BLA. If any out-of-specification or unexpected trends are observed,
an immediate notification will be filed with FDA per 21 CFR 314.81.

Stability studies are governed by the approved Stability Protocol
(SP-CVM-001) and conducted in compliance with GMP and applicable FDA/ICH
guidelines.
