**3.2.S.1.2 Structure**

Cervimab is a fully human immunoglobulin G4 (IgG4) monoclonal antibody
targeting the extracellular domain of human Programmed Cell Death
Protein 1 (PD-1; CD279). The molecule consists of two identical heavy
chains (gamma-4 isotype) and two identical light chains (kappa isotype)
linked by inter-chain disulfide bonds.

**3.2.P.2 Pharmaceutical Development**

**3.2.P.2.1 Components of the Drug Product**

Formulation development studies evaluated the impact of buffer type and
concentration (histidine, citrate, acetate at 10--50 mM), tonicity
agents (sucrose, mannitol, trehalose), surfactant type and concentration
(polysorbate 20 vs. 80 at 0.01--0.04% w/v), and pH (5.0--6.5) on
cervimab stability. The final formulation (histidine/sucrose/polysorbate
20, pH 5.8) was selected based on optimal colloidal stability (DLS,
SEC-HPLC), chemical stability (icIEF, peptide mapping), and
compatibility with IV administration. Forced degradation studies
confirmed the formulation\'s robustness under thermal, oxidative,
freeze-thaw, and mechanical stress conditions.

**3.2.P.2.2 Drug Product (Formulation Design)**

No preservatives are included in the formulation. The 200 mg/10 mL
single-use vial configuration was selected based on clinical
dose-finding results (fixed dose 400 mg Q3W) and minimizes multi-dose
manipulation risk consistent with USP \<1\> guidelines for parenterals.
Overfill of 0.5 mL (10 mL nominal fill; 10.5 mL actual fill) is included
to ensure extractable volume ≥ 10 mL.

**3.2.P.7 Container Closure System**

Cervimab Injection 200 mg/10 mL is packaged in a 15 mL Type I
borosilicate glass vial (EP/USP compliant; Schott Fiolax clear) with a
20 mm bromobutyl rubber stopper (West Pharmaceuticals 4432/50 Gray,
fluoropolymer-coated) and an aluminum flip-off seal. The container
closure system has been qualified per USP \<1\> and \<1207\>.
Container/closure compatibility and extractables/leachables studies
demonstrate compliance with ICH Q3D (parenteral route; PDEs not
exceeded) and applicable FDA/EMA guidance. Compatibility of the drug
product with IV administration materials (PVC/non-PVC infusion bags and
tubing) was confirmed in in-use studies (0.9% NaCl and D5W dilution to
1--10 mg/mL, 25°C, up to 24 hours).

**3.2.P.8 Stability**

Stability studies for cervimab drug product are conducted in accordance
with ICH Q5C. The proposed shelf life is 24 months at 2--8°C in the
commercial container closure system. In-use stability: diluted cervimab
(in 0.9% NaCl, 1--10 mg/mL) is stable for up to 24 hours at 2--8°C or 8
hours at room temperature (25°C). Full stability data and statistical
analysis are presented in the Shelf Life Determination Report (Appendix
SLD-001).
