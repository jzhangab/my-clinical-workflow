**MODULE 2 --- COMMON TECHNICAL DOCUMENT SUMMARIES**

**2.5 Clinical Overview**

**2.5.1 Product Development Rationale**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) is the third most commonly diagnosed cancer and
the second leading cause of cancer-related death globally, with
approximately 1.9 million new cases and 935,000 deaths estimated in 2020
(Sung et al., CA Cancer J Clin, 2021). In the United States,
approximately 106,590 new cases of colon cancer and 46,220 new cases of
rectal cancer are expected in 2023.

Approximately 3--5% of metastatic CRC patients harbor dMMR/MSI-H tumors,
a molecularly distinct subset characterized by somatic mutations in DNA
MMR genes (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2) or MLH1 promoter hypermethylation.
These tumors exhibit markedly elevated tumor mutational burden (TMB-H),
extensive neoantigen generation, and a tumor microenvironment rich in
tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes (TILs) --- features that strongly predict
responsiveness to PD-1/PD-L1 checkpoint inhibition.

Prior to CERVIMAB, pembrolizumab (KEYTRUDA) and nivolumab (OPDIVO)
received approvals in dMMR/MSI-H CRC; however, both are administered
every 3--6 weeks with infusion times of 30--60 minutes and require
prespecified dose modifications. CERVIMAB addresses the unmet need for a
PD-1 inhibitor with a more convenient Q6W dosing schedule, a robust
biomarker strategy, and an optimized pharmacokinetic (PK) profile
supporting extended dosing intervals.

**2.5.2 Overview of Biopharmaceutics**

Cervimab is administered as an IV infusion; no oral or subcutaneous
formulation has been developed. Three Phase 1 PK studies (CERVIMAB-101,
101-Ext, 102-Renal) characterized the PK profile in healthy volunteers
and in patients with mild, moderate, and severe renal impairment.
Population PK (PopPK) analysis of 892 patients across Phase 1/2/3
studies confirmed dose-proportional PK at doses ≥200 mg and supported
the 400 mg Q6W regimen.

**2.5.3 Overview of Clinical Pharmacology**

Cervimab binds the PD-1 receptor with high affinity (KD \~0.8 nM) and
selectivity, blocking interactions with both PD-L1 and PD-L2. Target
occupancy modeling demonstrated \>98% PD-1 receptor occupancy sustained
through the full 6-week dosing interval at the 400 mg Q6W dose,
supporting selection of this regimen for Phase 3.

The exposure-response (E-R) analysis demonstrated statistically
significant relationships between Cycle 1 trough cervimab concentrations
(Ctrough) and ORR (logistic regression) and OS (parametric survival
model). No clinically meaningful differences in efficacy or safety were
observed across the first--fourth quartiles of cervimab exposure,
supporting the fixed 400 mg Q6W dose without weight-based adjustment.

**2.5.4 Overview of Efficacy**

The efficacy of CERVIMAB is primarily supported by the pivotal
RESTORE-CRC (CERVIMAB-301) Phase 3 RCT described in Section 14 of the
Prescribing Information. Supportive data are provided from the Phase 2
CERVIMAB-201 single-arm study (n=142; ORR 41.5%) which formed the basis
for the FDA Breakthrough Therapy Designation request.

Subgroup analyses from RESTORE-CRC demonstrated consistent OS and PFS
benefit across pre-specified subgroups including:

- BRAF V600E status (mutant vs. wild-type): OS HR 0.41 vs. 0.46

- Prior bevacizumab use (yes vs. no): OS HR 0.43 vs. 0.45

- ECOG PS 0 vs. 1--2: OS HR 0.38 vs. 0.47

- PD-L1 CPS ≥1 vs. \<1: OS HR 0.40 vs. 0.51 (no formal interaction; CPS
  not required for patient selection)

- MSI-H by NGS vs. dMMR by IHC: Comparable outcomes; concordance rate
  96.3%

**2.5.5 Overview of Safety**

The integrated safety analysis included 1,206 patients who received at
least one dose of CERVIMAB across CERVIMAB-101, 201, and 301. Median
treatment duration was 8.4 months (range 0.5--36.1).

The safety profile of CERVIMAB is consistent with the class of PD-1
inhibitors and is characterized primarily by immune-mediated adverse
reactions (imAEs). The incidence of Grade 3--4 treatment-related adverse
events (TRAEs) was 22.1%, with no Grade 5 TRAEs definitively attributed

  (\>10,000 nM)

- Blockade of PD-1/PD-L1 and PD-1/PD-L2 interactions in cell-free
  competitive binding assays (IC50 0.6 nM and 0.9 nM respectively)

- Enhancement of T cell activation (IL-2 and IFN-γ production) in mixed
  lymphocyte reactions (MLR) and SEB-stimulated whole blood assays

- In vivo anti-tumor activity in a huPD-1/huPD-L1 knock-in syngeneic
  mouse model (MC38-hPD-L1): significant tumor growth inhibition (TGI
  74% vs. isotype control, p\<0.001) and 3 complete responses out of 10

**Efficacy Results --- Final OS Analysis**

**Primary Endpoint --- Overall Survival:**

At the final OS analysis (data cutoff: April 30, 2024; median follow-up
27.4 months; 376 OS events), CERVIMAB demonstrated a statistically
significant and clinically meaningful improvement in OS versus
investigator\'s choice:

- Median OS: 20.4 months (95% CI: 17.8--23.6) vs. 9.3 months (95% CI:
  7.9--11.1)

- HR: 0.44 (95% CI: 0.35--0.55); p \< 0.0001 (two-sided stratified
  log-rank test)

- 24-month OS rate: 42.1% (CERVIMAB) vs. 18.3% (control)

- 36-month OS rate: 28.7% (CERVIMAB) vs. 8.9% (control)

The pre-specified significance boundary for the final OS analysis was α
= 0.0227 (two-sided), based on a Lan-DeMets O\'Brien-Fleming
alpha-spending function with one prior interim analysis. The observed
p-value was far below this boundary.

**Patient-Reported Outcomes (PROs)**

CERVIMAB demonstrated statistically significant improvements versus
control in time to definitive deterioration (TDD) on the FACT-C Total
Score (HR 0.56; 95% CI: 0.44--0.71; p\<0.0001) and the EQ-5D-5L VAS
score (mean difference +9.4 points at Week 12; p=0.0008), reflecting
meaningful preservation of health-related quality of life (HRQoL).

**Biomarker Analyses**

Pre-specified exploratory biomarker analyses evaluated the predictive
value of PD-L1 CPS, TMB, BRAF/KRAS mutation status, and gut microbiome
composition on CERVIMAB outcomes. Key findings:

- PD-L1 CPS: A numerical trend toward improved ORR in CPS ≥10 subgroup
  (ORR 45.2% vs. 33.8% for CPS \<10); however, OS benefit was maintained
  regardless of CPS, supporting PD-L1 as a prognostic but not predictive
  biomarker in this dMMR/MSI-H population

- TMB: Patients with TMB ≥20 mut/Mb showed numerically higher ORR
  (51.3%) vs. TMB 10--19 (35.4%); OS benefit maintained across TMB
  strata

- BRAF V600E: Present in 38.6% of enrolled patients; ORR slightly lower
  in BRAF-mutant (32.1% vs. 42.8% WT) but meaningful OS benefit
  maintained (HR 0.41 BRAF-mut vs. 0.46 BRAF-WT)
