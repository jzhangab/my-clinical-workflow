**1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

Nexagen Biotherapeutics, Inc. (the Sponsor) submits this revised Risk
Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy (REMS) Proposal for CERVIMAB
(cerviximab-dryx) in response to FDA\'s Complete Response Letter (CRL)
deficiency regarding the adequacy of the initial REMS proposal. This
revised REMS has been substantially redesigned in response to FDA\'s
feedback and is consistent with existing PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitor REMS
frameworks where applicable.

The primary risks addressed by this REMS are serious and potentially
fatal immune-mediated adverse events (imAEs), particularly
immune-mediated pneumonitis, immune-mediated hepatitis, and
immune-mediated colitis, which require early recognition and prompt
management by trained oncology healthcare providers. The REMS does not
include a restricted distribution program (ETASU) given the established
oncology prescribing infrastructure and the availability of management
guidance through the proposed HCP training program.

**2. REMS GOALS**

This REMS is designed to achieve the following goals:

1.  Goal 1 --- HCP Education: Inform healthcare providers who prescribe
    CERVIMAB about: (a) the serious risks of immune-mediated adverse
    reactions, including immune-mediated pneumonitis, hepatitis,
    colitis, endocrinopathies, and nephritis; (b) the importance of
    early recognition of symptoms of immune-mediated AEs; (c) the
    appropriate management algorithm (withholding, dose modification,
    corticosteroid therapy, and permanent discontinuation criteria); (d)
    the importance of monitoring laboratory parameters per the CERVIMAB
    Prescribing Information.

2.  Goal 2 --- Patient Education: Ensure patients who receive CERVIMAB
    understand: (a) the risks of immune-mediated adverse reactions; (b)
    the specific symptoms that require immediate medical evaluation
    (dyspnea, persistent cough, severe diarrhea, jaundice, severe rash,
    fever); (c) the importance of not self-managing symptoms and seeking
    prompt medical care.

3.  Goal 3 --- Pharmacovigilance Enhancement: Facilitate early
    identification and reporting of serious imAEs through a structured
    safety surveillance framework, supporting ongoing benefit-risk
    monitoring.

**3. REMS ELEMENTS**

**3.1 Element 1: Medication Guide**

FDA requires a Medication Guide for CERVIMAB pursuant to 21 CFR Part
208, given that patient labeling could help prevent serious adverse
outcomes. The CERVIMAB Medication Guide:

- Is written at an 8th-grade reading level (validated by Fry Readability
  Scale)

- Describes serious immune-mediated AEs in plain language and provides
  specific symptom checklists for patient self-monitoring

- Is available in English and Spanish at launch; additional languages
  (Mandarin, Portuguese, Korean) within 6 months of launch

- Will be provided to patients at each dispensing via specialty pharmacy
  network and hospital pharmacy

- Is available for download at www.cervimab.com/REMS and via the HCP
  portal

The Medication Guide must be distributed to patients prior to every
administration in the inpatient/outpatient infusion setting, and at each
prescription dispensing for oral take-home kits (not applicable for
CERVIMAB IV formulation). The revised Medication Guide text is attached
as REMS Appendix A.

**3.2 Element 2: Communication Plan (HCP-Targeted)**

A Healthcare Provider Communication Plan is a required element of this
REMS. The Communication Plan consists of a Dear Healthcare Provider
(DHCP) letter to be sent to all oncologists, hematologists,
gastroenterologists, pulmonologists, and hepatologists in the United
States who are likely to encounter patients receiving CERVIMAB or
managing imAEs. The DHCP letter addresses:

- Summary of approved indication and patient selection criteria
  (dMMR/MSI-H testing requirement)

- Serious immune-mediated AEs: incidence, severity, and outcomes in
  CERV-301

- Early recognition: key symptoms and laboratory findings for each imAE
  category

- Management algorithm: withhold/discontinue criteria; corticosteroid
  dosing; specialist referral guidance

- Monitoring requirements: timing of LFTs, TSH, creatinine, and chest
  imaging as per PI

- REMS training resources and how to access the HCP Education Portal

**3.3 Element 3: HCP Education and Training Program**

The Sponsor proposes a voluntary, accredited (CME/CE credit-eligible)
online training program for HCPs. This program is not mandatory for
prescribing but is strongly encouraged and will be promoted through the
Communication Plan and MSL outreach. The HCP Training Program includes:

- Module 1 (30 min): CERVIMAB Mechanism of Action, Clinical Data Summary
  (CERV-301), and Patient Selection

- Module 2 (45 min): Immune-Mediated AE Recognition, Grading, and
  Management --- Case-based learning format

- Module 3 (20 min): REMS Requirements, Medication Guide Overview, and
  Patient Counseling

- Post-module assessment: 80% passing threshold to receive CME/CE credit

Training materials are hosted on the secure CERVIMAB HCP portal
(www.cervimabHCP.com). The portal will also provide downloadable HCP
resources: imAE Quick Reference Card, Patient Monitoring Checklist, and
Management Algorithm Decision Tree.

**3.4 Element 4: Patient Wallet Card**

A patient wallet card will be distributed with each Medication Guide.
The wallet card includes:

- Patient name and prescribing oncologist contact information
  (patient-completed)

- Drug name, dose, and dosing schedule

- Key symptoms requiring emergency evaluation (fever, shortness of
  breath, severe diarrhea, yellowing of skin/eyes, rash)

- 24/7 CERVIMAB Medical Information Hotline: 1-800-XXX-XXXX

- CERVIMAB REMS program website: www.cervimab.com/REMS
