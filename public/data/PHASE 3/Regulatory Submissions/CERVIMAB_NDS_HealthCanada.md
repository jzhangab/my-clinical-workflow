**Therapeutic Indications**

CERVIMAB (cervimab) is indicated as monotherapy for the treatment of
adult patients with unresectable or metastatic mismatch repair-deficient
(dMMR) or microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H) colorectal cancer
(CRC) that has progressed following treatment with fluoropyrimidine-,
oxaliplatin-, and irinotecan-based chemotherapy.

Patient selection should be based on tumour dMMR/MSI-H status confirmed
by a validated diagnostic test (see CLINICAL TRIALS --- Patient
Selection and WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS --- Monitoring and Laboratory
Tests).

**Dosage and Administration**

Recommended Dose and Dosage Adjustment

The recommended dose of CERVIMAB is 400 mg administered as an IV
infusion over 30 minutes every 6 weeks (Q6W). Treatment should be
continued until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity.

Immune-related adverse reactions: No dose reductions are recommended for
CERVIMAB. Dose interruption (withholding) and/or permanent
discontinuation are required for immune-related adverse reactions based
on severity. Refer to the Dosage Adjustment table in the full Product
Monograph (Table 1, PM Section 2).

**Reconstitution and Administration**

- Inspect the vial visually for particulate matter and discoloration.
  Discard if cloudy, discolored, or if visible particles are present.

- Withdraw the required volume from CERVIMAB vial(s) into an IV bag
  containing 0.9% Sodium Chloride Injection (USP/BP) or 5% Dextrose
  Injection (USP/BP) to prepare a diluted infusion with a final
  concentration between 1 mg/mL and 10 mg/mL.

- Administer diluted CERVIMAB using an IV infusion set with an in-line
  or add-on sterile, non-pyrogenic, low-protein-binding filter (0.2--5
  micrometers pore size).

- Do not co-administer other medicinal products through the same
  infusion line.

- Administer over 30 minutes.

- Storage after dilution: room temperature up to 25°C for no more than 6
  hours (including infusion time), or refrigerated at 2°C--8°C for no
  more than 24 hours from time of dilution.

**Contraindications**

CERVIMAB is contraindicated in patients with a known hypersensitivity to
cervimab or to any ingredient in the formulation, including any
non-medicinal ingredient, or component of the container. See DOSAGE
FORMS, COMPOSITION AND PACKAGING.

**Warnings and Precautions --- SERIOUS WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS BOX**

**SERIOUS WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS**

- Immune-related adverse reactions (irARs), including severe and fatal
  cases, have been reported with CERVIMAB. These irARs can involve any
  organ system, may occur at any time during or after treatment, and may
  require corticosteroid therapy, other immunosuppressants, hormone
  replacement therapy, and/or discontinuation of CERVIMAB. (see WARNINGS
  AND PRECAUTIONS, Immune-related Adverse Reactions)

- Severe or fatal infusion-related reactions, including anaphylaxis,
  have been reported with CERVIMAB. (see WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS,
  Infusion-related Reactions)

- CERVIMAB can cause fetal harm based on its mechanism of action and
  nonclinical data. Advise female patients of reproductive potential to
  use effective contraception during treatment with CERVIMAB and for at
  least 4 months after the last dose. (see WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS,
  Special Populations --- Pregnant Women; and CLINICAL PHARMACOLOGY ---
  Pharmacokinetics --- Special Populations)

**Adverse Reactions --- Overview**

The safety of CERVIMAB was evaluated in 1,206 patients who received at
least one dose across clinical studies CERVIMAB-101, 201, and 301 (the
pivotal RESTORE-CRC trial). The median duration of exposure was 8.4
months (range: 0.5--36.1 months). The data described in the ADVERSE
REACTIONS section reflect exposure to CERVIMAB in the RESTORE-CRC trial
(n=307 randomized to CERVIMAB).

The most common adverse reactions (≥20% of patients) in the RESTORE-CRC
trial (CERVIMAB vs. comparator) were fatigue (38.2% vs. 28.7%), diarrhea
(22.5% vs. 31.4%), nausea (21.8% vs. 27.9%), and rash (19.5% vs. 11.1%).
The most common Grade 3--4 adverse reactions in CERVIMAB-treated
patients were diarrhea/colitis (4.8%), hepatitis (3.3%), fatigue (3.1%),
and pneumonitis (2.1%).

**Clinical Trials (Pivotal Study --- RESTORE-CRC/CERVIMAB-301)**

Study Design: RESTORE-CRC (CERVIMAB-301) was a global, randomized,
open-label, active-controlled Phase 3 trial of CERVIMAB 400 mg IV Q6W
versus investigator\'s choice of regorafenib or TAS-102 in adult
patients with previously treated dMMR/MSI-H unresectable or metastatic
CRC. Patients were randomized 1:1 and stratified by prior bevacizumab
use, ECOG PS, and geographic region. A total of 612 patients were
enrolled (307 CERVIMAB; 305 control) across 148 sites in 22 countries.

**CANADIAN PATIENT ENROLLMENT IN RESTORE-CRC:**

Fourteen (14) Canadian sites participated in RESTORE-CRC across 5
provinces (Ontario, British Columbia, Quebec, Alberta, Nova Scotia). A
total of 52 Canadian patients were enrolled (CERVIMAB: n=27; control:
n=25). The Canadian subgroup efficacy and safety data are provided in
the CSR Addendum (Module 5, Appendix CA-1):

**SECTION 2 --- PRIORITY REVIEW REQUEST**

**2.1 Priority Review Justification**

Archon Biotherapeutics Canada Inc. submits this Priority Review (PR)
request for CERVIMAB pursuant to Health Canada\'s \'Priority Review of
Drug Submissions\' guidance (2012). To qualify for Priority Review, a
submission must meet one of the following criteria:

- Criterion 1 --- Serious condition for which no drug is marketed in
  Canada: Not applicable (regorafenib and TAS-102 are marketed in Canada
  for 3L mCRC, and nivolumab (OPDIVO) is marketed for dMMR/MSI-H CRC in
  Canada)

- Criterion 2 --- Serious condition for which existing therapy is not
  satisfactory, and the new drug provides evidence of clinical benefit:
  APPLICABLE --- see rationale below

**Priority Review Rationale --- Criterion 2:**

Condition Severity: dMMR/MSI-H metastatic CRC is a serious,
life-threatening malignancy. Following failure of
fluoropyrimidine/oxaliplatin/irinotecan-based therapy, median OS with
available treatments is approximately 9 months (regorafenib: mOS
6.4--8.8 months; TAS-102: mOS 7.1--7.8 months per CORRECT and RECOURSE
trials). Nivolumab, while approved for this indication in Canada (2021),
has not been directly compared to regorafenib/TAS-102 in a randomized
trial in this specific refractory setting.

Evidence of Clinical Benefit over Existing Therapy:

- CERVIMAB demonstrated OS HR of 0.44 (p\<0.0001) versus
  regorafenib/TAS-102 in the first-ever randomized, active-controlled
  Phase 3 trial in this setting, providing direct comparative evidence
  of clinical benefit

- Median OS improvement: 20.4 months vs. 9.3 months --- a clinically
  meaningful improvement of 11.1 months

- Two-year OS rate: 42.1% vs. 18.3% --- doubling of 2-year survival

- Q6W dosing: superior dosing convenience compared to currently approved
  agents in Canada (nivolumab: Q2W or Q4W; pembrolizumab: Q3W or Q6W
  with 30-min infusion --- CERVIMAB offers the same 30-min/Q6W schedule
  with pivotal Phase 3 RCT evidence against regorafenib/TAS-102 which
  pembrolizumab lacks)

- HRQoL benefit: statistically significant improvement in FACT-C TDD (HR
  0.56) and EQ-5D-5L VAS --- important to Canadian patients, physicians,
  and reimbursement bodies (CADTH, INESSS)
