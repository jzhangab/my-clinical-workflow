**1.3 Product Information**

**1.3.1 Summary of Product Characteristics (SmPC) --- Draft**

**1. NAME OF THE MEDICINAL PRODUCT**

CERVIMAB 200 mg/4 mL concentrate for solution for infusion

**2. QUALITATIVE AND QUANTITATIVE COMPOSITION**

Each vial contains 200 mg cervimab in 4 mL (50 mg/mL concentrate).

After dilution, the final cervimab concentration is between 1 mg/mL and
10 mg/mL.

Cervimab is produced by recombinant DNA technology using Chinese hamster
ovary (CHO) cells.

Excipients with known effect: Each vial contains approximately 70 mg
trehalose and 0.4 mg polysorbate 80.

For the full list of excipients, see section 6.1.

**3. PHARMACEUTICAL FORM**

Concentrate for solution for infusion. Clear to slightly opalescent,
colourless to slightly yellow solution.

**4. CLINICAL PARTICULARS**

4.1 Therapeutic Indications

CERVIMAB as monotherapy is indicated for the treatment of adult patients
with unresectable or metastatic mismatch repair deficient (dMMR) or
microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H) colorectal cancer (CRC) that has
progressed following treatment with fluoropyrimidine-, oxaliplatin-, and
irinotecan-based chemotherapy.

Selection of patients for treatment with CERVIMAB should be based on
tumour dMMR or MSI-H status confirmed by a validated test (see section
5.1).

4.2 Posology and Method of Administration

Treatment with CERVIMAB should be initiated and supervised by a
physician experienced in the treatment of cancer.

Posology

The recommended dose of CERVIMAB is 400 mg administered as an
intravenous infusion over 30 minutes every 6 weeks (Q6W) until disease
progression or unacceptable toxicity.

Dose modifications for adverse reactions: No dose reduction is
recommended. Management of immune-related adverse reactions may require
dose interruption, withholding, or permanent discontinuation. Refer to
Table 1 in the full SmPC for immune-related adverse reaction management
guidelines.

Special populations:

- Elderly patients (≥65 years): No dose adjustment is required. See
  section 5.2.

- Renal impairment: No dose adjustment is required in patients with mild
  to severe renal impairment. See section 5.2.

- Hepatic impairment: No dose adjustment is required in patients with
  mild hepatic impairment. Limited data in moderate hepatic impairment.
  Not recommended in severe hepatic impairment. See section 5.2.

- Paediatric population: The safety and efficacy of CERVIMAB in children
  below 18 years have not been established. No data are available (see
  section 4.8).

4.3 Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to the active substance or to any of the excipients
listed in section 6.1.

4.4 Special Warnings and Precautions for Use

Immune-related adverse reactions

Immune-related adverse reactions (irAEs), which may be severe or fatal,
have been observed with cervimab. They can occur in any organ or tissue
and can appear at any time after initiation, including after
discontinuation of cervimab. They typically present with inflammation
and can be managed through interruption, withholding, or permanent
discontinuation of cervimab, together with administration of
corticosteroids and/or other immunosuppressants. The clinical
presentation of immune-related adverse reactions can be complex;
concomitant endocrinopathies should be considered. Immune-related
adverse reactions and their management are described below.

- Immune-related pneumonitis: Withhold for moderate (Grade 2)
  pneumonitis. Permanently discontinue for severe (Grade 3) or
  life-threatening (Grade 4) pneumonitis.

- Immune-related colitis: Withhold for moderate (Grade 2) colitis;
  permanently discontinue for severe (Grade 3--4) colitis.

- Immune-related hepatitis: Withhold for Grade 2 ALT/AST elevation;
  permanently discontinue for Grade 3--4.

- Immune-related endocrinopathies (hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism,
  adrenal insufficiency, type 1 DM): Manage endocrine toxicities with
  hormone replacement as indicated; consider dose interruption for Grade
  3--4.

- Immune-related nephritis: Withhold for Grade 2; permanently
  discontinue for Grade 3--4.

Infusion-related reactions

Severe infusion-related reactions, including anaphylaxis, have been
reported. Interrupt infusion for Grade 1--2 reactions; permanently
discontinue for Grade 3--4.

**4.8 Undesirable Effects (Summary)**

The safety population included 1,206 patients treated with CERVIMAB
across clinical studies. The most frequently reported adverse reactions
were fatigue (38.2%), diarrhoea/colitis (27.4%), rash (24.1%), nausea
(22.8%), hypothyroidism (18.3%), arthralgia (16.7%), and hepatitis ---
liver enzyme elevation (14.2%).

Immune-related adverse reactions occurred in 42.6% of patients (any
grade); Grade 3--4 imAEs occurred in 10.4%.

**5. PHARMACOLOGICAL PROPERTIES**

5.1 Pharmacodynamic Properties

Pharmacotherapeutic group: Antineoplastic agents, monoclonal antibodies,
PD-1 inhibitors, ATC code: L01FF07 (proposed)

Mechanism of action: Cervimab is a human immunoglobulin G4 (IgG4)
monoclonal antibody that binds with high affinity and selectivity to the
programmed cell death receptor 1 (PD-1), blocking the interactions of
PD-1 with its ligands, PD-L1 and PD-L2. PD-1 signalling inhibits T cell
activation. By blocking PD-1, cervimab enhances T cell-mediated
anti-tumour responses.

**MODULE 2 --- CTD SUMMARIES (EU-SPECIFIC ELEMENTS)**

**2.5 Clinical Overview --- EU-Specific Considerations**

**2.5.1 Regulatory Basis and Benefit-Risk Assessment**

This MAA is submitted under Article 8(3) of Directive 2001/83/EC and
evaluated under the centralised procedure (Regulation (EC) No 726/2004).
The proposed indication satisfies the criteria for the centralised
procedure as a medicinal product indicated for the treatment of
oncological diseases.

The Applicant\'s benefit-risk assessment concludes that CERVIMAB offers
a favourable benefit-risk profile for the target population (adult
patients with dMMR/MSI-H metastatic CRC following failure of standard
chemotherapy). The pivotal evidence from RESTORE-CRC (CERVIMAB-301)
demonstrates:

- A statistically significant and clinically meaningful OS benefit: HR
  0.44, p\<0.0001; median OS 20.4 vs. 9.3 months

- A statistically significant and clinically meaningful PFS benefit: HR
  0.34, p\<0.0001; median PFS 6.9 vs. 2.2 months

- Superior ORR: 38.4% vs. 4.9%, p\<0.0001

- Preservation of HRQoL (FACT-C TDD HR 0.56; EQ-5D-5L VAS improvement
  +9.4 points)

- A manageable safety profile consistent with the PD-1 inhibitor class,
  with no new safety signals identified

The Applicant notes that two approved PD-1 inhibitors (pembrolizumab,
nivolumab) already hold EU marketing authorisations in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC.
The differentiation of CERVIMAB lies in the Q6W dosing schedule (vs.
Q3W/Q6W pembrolizumab and Q2W/Q4W nivolumab), which offers a reduced
infusion frequency and potential benefit for patient convenience and
healthcare resource utilisation, as supported by direct comparison in
study CERVIMAB-301.

**2.5.2 EU-Specific Unmet Medical Need Assessment**

Across the EU Member States, approximately 140,000 patients are
diagnosed with CRC each year (ECCO/ESMO guidelines, 2023). The
dMMR/MSI-H subgroup (3--5% of metastatic CRC) comprises approximately
4,200--7,000 new patients annually within the EU. Despite approval of
pembrolizumab and nivolumab, real-world utilisation of checkpoint
inhibitors in 2L+ dMMR mCRC remains suboptimal due to:

- Infusion centre capacity constraints --- frequent Q2W--Q3W visits
  place a burden on oncology infusion centres across EU Member States

- Geographic access disparities --- less frequent dosing with Q6W
  CERVIMAB may improve access in healthcare systems with limited
  infusion infrastructure

- Active comparator data gap --- RESTORE-CRC provides the first
  head-to-head randomised data comparing a PD-1 inhibitor to the current
  EU-approved 3L standard of care (regorafenib, TAS-102) in this
  population, directly informing clinical decision-making
