**3. DEFICIENCY 1: OVERALL SURVIVAL DATA --- STUDY CERV-301**

**3.1 Background**

At the time of NDA submission (data cutoff: March 31, 2023), the primary
endpoint of progression-free survival (PFS) per BICR demonstrated a
statistically significant and clinically meaningful result (HR 0.43; 95%
CI 0.35--0.53; p\<0.0001; median PFS 16.5 months vs. 5.4 months).
Overall survival data were immature, with 32% events (115/360 required),
below the pre-specified interim analysis threshold of 60%. FDA requested
updated, mature OS data before approval could be granted.

**3.2 Updated Overall Survival Analysis**

An updated OS analysis was conducted with a data cutoff of January 15,
2026. At this data cutoff, 63% of pre-specified OS events had been
observed (229/360 deaths). The Kaplan-Meier analysis of OS in the
intent-to-treat (ITT) population demonstrated the following results:

  ---------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Parameter**           **CERVIMAB (N=180)**   **Investigator\'s
                                                 Choice Chemo (N=180)**
  ----------------------- ---------------------- ----------------------
  Events, n (%)           106 (58.9%)            123 (68.3%)

  Median OS (months)      24.8 (95% CI:          13.7 (95% CI:
                          21.3--28.4)            11.1--16.2)

  Hazard Ratio (95% CI)   0.62 (0.51--0.75)      Reference

  P-value (log-rank)      \<0.0001               ---

  12-month OS rate        75.4% (95% CI:         54.1% (95% CI:
                          68.3--81.2%)           46.7--61.0%)

  24-month OS rate        51.2% (95% CI:         27.8% (95% CI:
                          43.7--58.3%)           21.4--34.6%)
  ---------------------------------------------------------------------

The OS results are consistent with, and confirmatory of, the PFS
results. The observed HR of 0.62 represents a clinically meaningful 38%
reduction in the risk of death. Subgroup analyses were consistent with
the ITT results across all pre-specified subgroups including age, ECOG
PS, prior lines of therapy, tumor sidedness, and MSI/MMR testing
modality.

**3.3 Updated Safety Data**

The updated safety analysis (N=360; median follow-up 28.4 months) did
not identify any new safety signals compared to those reported in the
original NDA. The incidence of treatment-related adverse events of
special interest (immune-mediated AEs) remained consistent with the
known PD-1 inhibitor class profile. Grade 3-4 immune-mediated AEs
occurred in 18.3% of CERVIMAB-treated patients vs. 3.3% of chemotherapy
patients, consistent with the product class. No new fatal
immune-mediated events were identified in the updated dataset.

**3.4 Conclusion --- Deficiency 1**

The updated OS analysis with a data cutoff of January 15, 2026,
demonstrates a statistically significant and clinically meaningful
improvement in OS (HR 0.62; p\<0.0001; median OS benefit 11.1 months).
The Sponsor believes this addresses FDA\'s concern regarding OS data
maturity. All relevant updated clinical study report sections,
statistical analysis datasets, and patient narratives are provided in
Module 5.3.5.1 of this submission.

**4. DEFICIENCY 2: CHEMISTRY, MANUFACTURING, AND CONTROLS (CMC)**

**4.1 Summary of FDA\'s CMC Concern**

The CRL identified that the impurity profile for process-related
impurity \[REDACTED\] at Drug Substance manufacturing Step 4 at the
\[REDACTED\] facility exceeded the proposed specification limit in 2 of
6 validation batches (Batches DS-2024-003 and DS-2024-005). FDA
requested process improvement, revalidation, and updated specification
justification.

**4.2 Root Cause Investigation**

A thorough root cause analysis was conducted by \[REDACTED\] in
collaboration with Nexagen\'s CMC team. The investigation identified the
root cause as variability in the \[REDACTED\] step reaction temperature
control, which led to increased formation of the impurity under
non-nominal conditions. The investigation included:

- Full batch record review of all Phase 3 clinical and commercial-scale
  batches

- Comprehensive analytical method re-qualification (HPLC-MS/UV, ICH
  Q2(R1) compliant)

- Process parameter risk assessment (FMEA) per ICH Q9

- Design of Experiments (DoE) to identify critical process parameters
  (CPPs) governing impurity formation

**4.3 Process Improvements and Revalidation**

Based on the root cause investigation findings, the following process
improvements were implemented:

1.  Temperature control system upgrade: Replacement of \[REDACTED\] step
    temperature control system with \[REDACTED\], with tighter control
    limits (±0.5°C vs. prior ±2.0°C)

2.  Revised process parameter specification: \[REDACTED\] step
    temperature set point narrowed from \[REDACTED\]°C to \[REDACTED\]°C
    range

3.  Enhanced in-process control: Addition of in-process testing at
    \[REDACTED\] step with hold specification of NMT \[REDACTED\]%

Three consecutive process validation batches (DS-2026-001, DS-2026-002,
DS-2026-003) at commercial scale were completed. All three batches met
the revised impurity specification of NMT \[REDACTED\]%, demonstrating
process reproducibility and control. Full process validation data are
provided in Module 3.2.S.2.5.

**7. UPDATED BENEFIT-RISK ASSESSMENT**

With the addition of mature OS data (HR 0.62; median OS 24.8 vs. 13.7
months; p\<0.0001), the benefit-risk profile of CERVIMAB for the
treatment of dMMR/MSI-H mCRC has been further strengthened. The OS
benefit is consistent across pre-specified subgroups and is supported by
the durability of PFS responses (median PFS 16.5 months).

The safety profile remains consistent with the known PD-1 inhibitor
class, with well-characterized and manageable immune-mediated adverse
events. The proposed REMS further mitigates serious risks through
structured HCP education and patient monitoring frameworks.

dMMR/MSI-H mCRC represents a molecularly-defined subpopulation with high
unmet medical need, characterized by limited long-term benefit from
cytotoxic chemotherapy. CERVIMAB represents a clinically meaningful
advance for this patient population, with a favorable benefit-risk
profile that supports approval.
