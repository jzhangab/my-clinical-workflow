**1. INTRODUCTION AND PURPOSE OF THIS DOCUMENT**

Nexagen Biotherapeutics, Inc. submits this Briefing Document to the
Oncologic Drugs Advisory Committee (ODAC) in support of NDA 761XXX for
CERVIMAB (cerviximab-dryx) for the treatment of adult patients with
unresectable or metastatic mismatch repair deficient (dMMR) or
microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H) colorectal cancer (CRC) that has
progressed following treatment with fluoropyrimidine-, oxaliplatin-, and
irinotecan-based chemotherapy.

The Committee is asked to consider whether the efficacy and safety data
from the Phase 3 CERV-301 trial, in the context of the unmet need in
this patient population, support a favorable benefit-risk assessment for
CERVIMAB in this indication.

**2. DISEASE BACKGROUND AND UNMET NEED**

**2.1 Colorectal Cancer Epidemiology**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) is the third most common cancer and the second
leading cause of cancer-related mortality in the United States, with an
estimated 154,270 new cases and 52,180 deaths in 2023 (Siegel et al.,
2023). Approximately 50% of patients ultimately develop metastatic
disease, for which 5-year survival remains poor (\~15%).

**2.2 dMMR/MSI-H as a Distinct Molecular Subtype**

Approximately 3.5--5% of mCRC tumors harbor dMMR/MSI-H status,
representing approximately 5,000--7,000 patients per year in the United
States. dMMR/MSI-H tumors are characterized by defects in the mismatch
repair (MMR) system, resulting in high somatic mutation burden and
neoantigen load --- the molecular basis for immunotherapy
responsiveness.

In the first-line setting, pembrolizumab (KEYNOTE-177) has demonstrated
superiority to chemotherapy in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC. However, following
progression on or after first-line PD-1/PD-L1 therapy and standard
chemotherapy, no approved immune checkpoint inhibitor therapy exists for
this molecularly-defined population, representing a critical unmet
medical need.

**2.3 Current Standard of Care in Third-Line+ dMMR/MSI-H mCRC**

Following progression on standard first- and second-line therapy
(fluoropyrimidine/oxaliplatin + bevacizumab or cetuximab;
irinotecan-based regimen), the standard of care consists of regorafenib
or TAS-102 (trifluridine/tipiracil), both of which provide only modest
clinical benefit (median OS \~7-9 months; ORR \<5%) and are associated
with significant toxicity. Clinical outcomes are substantially worse in
dMMR/MSI-H patients who have progressed on prior PD-1 therapy,
reflecting both primary and acquired resistance mechanisms.

**3. CERVIMAB: MECHANISM OF ACTION AND PRECLINICAL PROFILE**

**3.1 Mechanism of Action**

Cerviximab-dryx (CERVIMAB) is a fully human IgG4 monoclonal antibody
that binds to the programmed death-1 (PD-1) receptor on T cells,
blocking the interaction between PD-1 and its ligands PD-L1 and PD-L2.
This blockade releases PD-1 pathway-mediated inhibition of T cell
function, restoring anti-tumor immune activity.

CERVIMAB is distinguished from marketed PD-1 inhibitors by a novel
Fc-engineered variant (S228P stabilization mutation) and binding domain
optimized for sub-nanomolar affinity (KD \< 0.2 nM) with reduced
off-rate kinetics, resulting in sustained receptor occupancy in the
tumor microenvironment at doses as low as 200 mg Q3W.

**3.2 Summary of Preclinical and Phase 1/2 Data**

Phase 1 dose-escalation (Study CERV-101; N=68) established the
recommended Phase 2 dose (RP2D) of 200 mg Q3W IV. The Phase 2 basket
study (CERV-201; N=124) demonstrated an ORR of 38.7% (95% CI:
29.9--48.0%) in dMMR/MSI-H mCRC patients, supporting advancement to
Phase 3. PK/PD modeling confirmed target receptor occupancy \>97% at
trough at 200 mg Q3W.

**6.2 Immune-Mediated Adverse Events of Special Interest**

Immune-mediated AEs (imAEs) are a known class effect of PD-1 inhibitors.
In CERV-301, the most common imAEs by category were: hypothyroidism
(12.8%), immune-mediated pneumonitis (8.4%), immune-mediated hepatitis
(6.7%), immune-mediated colitis (5.6%), and skin toxicity
(pruritus/rash) (14.5%). Grade 3-4 immune-mediated pneumonitis occurred
in 2.8% of patients. Three fatal imAEs were observed: 2 cases of
immune-mediated pneumonitis and 1 case of immune-mediated hepatic
failure. All three fatal cases occurred in patients who experienced
delayed medical evaluation (\>14 days from symptom onset). This finding
underpins the REMS requirements for early recognition and management
education.

**7.2 Questions for the Advisory Committee**

The FDA requests the Committee address the following questions:

1.  Has the Sponsor adequately demonstrated the efficacy of CERVIMAB
    based on the PFS results from CERV-301 (HR 0.43; p\<0.0001)? If so,
    do the results represent a clinically meaningful benefit in the
    context of dMMR/MSI-H mCRC?

2.  Is the safety profile of CERVIMAB, in particular the incidence and
    severity of immune-mediated adverse events, acceptable given the
    clinical benefit observed? Are there specific safety concerns that
    require additional evaluation or labeling?

3.  Does the proposed REMS adequately mitigate the risks associated with
    immune-mediated AEs? Are there additional risk mitigation measures
    the Committee recommends?

4.  In the context of the immature OS data at the time of NDA filing
    (32% events), does the Committee consider the totality of evidence
    (PFS, ORR, DoR, OS trend) sufficient to support a benefit-risk
    determination, or should full OS maturity be required prior to
    approval?

5.  Are there specific post-marketing study commitments the Committee
    recommends to further characterize the long-term safety and efficacy
    of CERVIMAB?
