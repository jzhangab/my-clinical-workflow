**3.2 Disputed Item --- Treatment Duration Cap**

Disputed Item: FDA has requested inclusion of a 24-month maximum
treatment duration. Sponsor\'s Position: CERV-301 did not have a
protocol-mandated treatment duration cap. Among patients with ongoing
response at 24 months (N=22), 14 patients elected to continue CERVIMAB
with continued clinical benefit and no emergent safety signals; 8
patients elected to stop per institutional preference. Inclusion of a
24-month cap is not supported by clinical data and could deprive
patients of continued benefit. Sponsor proposes: \'Continue CERVIMAB
until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity.\'

FDA Response (Cycle 2 IR): FDA is reviewing the 24-month continuation
data presented by the Sponsor. Pending final FDA response. This item is
marked as OPEN and will be resolved in labeling alignment meeting
scheduled April 3, 2026.

**3.3 Dose Modifications (Section 2.2)**

Dose modification guidance for immune-mediated AEs has been agreed by
both parties and is based on NCI CTCAE v5.0 grading (confirmed per CRL
Complete Response). The dose modification table (Table 1 of the PI) has
been agreed in full. Key elements:

- Grade 1 imAE: No dose modification required; monitor

- Grade 2 imAE: Withhold CERVIMAB; initiate corticosteroids (prednisone
  0.5--1 mg/kg/day or equivalent); resume upon recovery to Grade ≤1

- Grade 3 imAE: Withhold CERVIMAB; initiate high-dose corticosteroids
  (prednisone 1--2 mg/kg/day or equivalent); consider additional
  immune-modulating therapy

- Grade 4 or Grade 3 recurrent imAE: Permanently discontinue CERVIMAB

- Grade 5 (fatal) imAE: Permanently discontinue CERVIMAB

**5. ADVERSE REACTIONS (Section 6)**

**5.1 Section 6.1 --- Clinical Trials Experience**

The adverse reactions table (Table 2) has been agreed in full. FDA and
Sponsor agreed on the following formatting conventions:

- All AEs occurring in ≥5% of CERVIMAB-treated patients are listed
  (regardless of causal relationship)

- AEs are listed by SOC (System Organ Class, MedDRA v24) and PT
  (Preferred Term)

- Table includes both \'any grade\' and \'Grade 3-4\' columns per FDA
  convention

- AEs of special interest (imAEs) are flagged with footnotes

Disputed Item in Section 6.1: The Sponsor proposed including an imAE
summary subsection immediately following Table 2, referencing the REMS
assessment criteria. FDA requested this be deferred to Section 5
(Warnings and Precautions) cross-reference only, not repeated in Section
6. RESOLVED in favor of FDA\'s position.

**5.2 SmPC Section 4.8 Alignment**

The EMA SmPC Section 4.8 (Undesirable Effects) uses MedDRA CIOMS
frequency conventions (Very common ≥1/10; Common ≥1/100 to \<1/10;
Uncommon ≥1/1,000 to \<1/100). Frequency category assignments have been
mapped from CERV-301 incidence data and are consistent with the US PI
adverse reactions table. SmPC includes the additional EU-required
\'Reporting of suspected adverse reactions\' section directing HCPs to
national pharmacovigilance systems. Transatlantic alignment:
satisfactory.
