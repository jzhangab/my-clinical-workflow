**1. BACKGROUND AND RATIONALE**

**1.1 Disease Overview**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) is the third most commonly diagnosed cancer and
the second leading cause of cancer-related mortality worldwide, with an
estimated 1.9 million new cases and over 930,000 deaths annually
(GLOBOCAN 2022). Approximately 20-25% of patients present with
metastatic disease at initial diagnosis, and an additional 30-40% of
patients with localized CRC will develop distant metastases. The 5-year
survival rate for metastatic CRC remains approximately 14%.

Deficiency in mismatch repair (dMMR) or microsatellite instability-high
(MSI-H) status occurs in approximately 4-5% of metastatic CRC cases.
This molecular subtype is characterized by a hypermutated phenotype,
high neoantigen burden, dense tumor-infiltrating lymphocyte (TIL)
infiltration, and upregulated immune checkpoint ligand expression ---
features that render these tumors exquisitely sensitive to immune
checkpoint blockade.

**1.2 Cervimab: Nonclinical and Clinical Background**

Cervimab is a fully human immunoglobulin G4 (IgG4) monoclonal antibody
directed against the programmed cell death protein 1 (PD-1) receptor. By
blocking the PD-1/PD-L1 and PD-1/PD-L2 inhibitory axes, Cervimab
restores T-cell-mediated antitumor immunity.

In nonclinical studies, Cervimab demonstrated high-affinity binding to
human PD-1 (KD \~0.3 nM), potent blockade of PD-L1/PD-L2 interactions,
and robust T-cell reactivation in co-culture assays. In xenograft models
bearing dMMR tumors, Cervimab produced complete tumor regression in 70%
of animals at 10 mg/kg.

In the Phase 1/2 dose-escalation study (CERVIMAB-101), Cervimab
demonstrated a manageable safety profile with an ORR of 45% in
dMMR/MSI-H mCRC patients at 400 mg Q3W flat dosing --- supporting the
dose selected for this Phase 3 investigation.

**1.3 Rationale for Dose and Schedule**

Cervimab 400 mg IV Q3W was selected based on population PK/PD modeling
demonstrating sustained receptor occupancy (\>95%) throughout the dosing
interval. Flat dosing eliminates weight-based calculation complexity and
is consistent with established practice for IgG4 immune checkpoint
inhibitors. This dose and schedule will be employed as monotherapy in
the experimental arm.

**1.4 Rationale for Study Design**

The current standard of care for first-line treatment of metastatic CRC
is FOLFOX-4 plus bevacizumab, which produces a median PFS of
approximately 9.5 months and median OS of approximately 21 months. Phase
2 data suggest that cervimab produces superior PFS and OS in the
dMMR/MSI-H subpopulation. This Phase 3 study is designed to formally
demonstrate this superiority and support a New Drug Application (NDA)
submission to the FDA under 21 CFR Part 312.

**2. STUDY OBJECTIVES AND ENDPOINTS**

**2.1 Primary Objective**

To demonstrate the superiority of Cervimab versus FOLFOX-4 plus
Bevacizumab in terms of Progression-Free Survival (PFS) as assessed by
Blinded Independent Central Review (BICR) per RECIST v1.1 criteria in
patients with previously untreated dMMR/MSI-H metastatic colorectal
cancer.

**2.2 Secondary Objectives**

- To evaluate Overall Survival (OS) of Cervimab versus the control arm

- To evaluate Objective Response Rate (ORR) by BICR per RECIST v1.1

- To evaluate Duration of Response (DoR) in responding patients

- To evaluate Disease Control Rate (DCR) at 24 weeks

- To assess Patient-Reported Outcomes (PROs) including EORTC QLQ-C30 and
  EQ-5D-5L

- To evaluate the safety and tolerability of Cervimab per NCI CTCAE v5.0

**2.3 Exploratory Objectives**

- To evaluate PFS and OS by PD-L1 expression levels (TPS and CPS)

- To assess tumor mutational burden (TMB) as a biomarker of response

- To characterize the pharmacokinetics (PK) of Cervimab in this
  population

- To assess anti-cervimab antibody formation (immunogenicity)

- To evaluate KRAS, NRAS, and BRAF mutational subgroup outcomes

**3. STUDY DESIGN**

**3.1 Overall Design**

CERVIMAB-301 is a Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled,
multicenter, international study. Approximately 480 patients will be
randomized 1:1 to receive either Cervimab 400 mg IV Q3W plus
chemotherapy placebo, or FOLFOX-4 plus Bevacizumab plus cervimab
placebo. Double-dummy design will be employed to maintain blinding.

Randomization will be stratified by ECOG Performance Status (0 vs 1),
Geographic Region (North America/Western Europe vs. Rest of World), and
RAS/BRAF mutation status (wild-type vs. mutant) using an Interactive Web
Response System (IWRS).

**3.2 Study Phases**

**3.2.1 Screening (Days -28 to -1)**

Eligibility will be confirmed through medical history, physical
examination, laboratory tests, tumor imaging (CT chest/abdomen/pelvis
with contrast), and central confirmation of dMMR/MSI-H status by IHC/PCR
at the sponsor-designated central laboratory.

**3.2.2 Treatment Period**

Arm A (Experimental): Cervimab 400 mg IV Q3W. Patients receive Cervimab
every 21 days (each cycle = 21 days) until disease progression,
unacceptable toxicity, patient withdrawal, or investigator decision to
discontinue. Maximum treatment duration is 2 years.

Arm B (Control): FOLFOX-4 every 14 days plus Bevacizumab 5 mg/kg IV Q2W
until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity.

**3.2.3 Response Assessment**

Tumor assessments will be performed by CT (or MRI where CT is
contraindicated) at Screening, then Q6 weeks from Cycle 1 Day 1 for the
first 12 months, and Q12 weeks thereafter. All scans will be assessed by
BICR per RECIST v1.1. Investigator assessment will also be performed to
guide clinical management.

**3.2.4 End of Treatment and Follow-Up**

End of Treatment (EOT) visit within 30 days of last dose. Safety
follow-up at 90 days post-last dose. Long-term survival follow-up every
3 months until death, lost to follow-up, or study closure.

**3.3 Dose Modifications**

Dose modifications for Cervimab are not permitted. In the event of
immune-related adverse events (irAEs), treatment interruption,
corticosteroid management, and protocol-specific algorithms will be
followed per Section 5. FOLFOX-4 and Bevacizumab dose modifications
follow the respective standard of care guidelines.

**4. PATIENT SELECTION**

**4.1 Inclusion Criteria**

Patients must meet ALL of the following criteria to be eligible:

- Age \>= 18 years at time of informed consent

- Histologically or cytologically confirmed colorectal adenocarcinoma
  with unresectable metastatic or locally advanced disease

- dMMR/MSI-H status confirmed by IHC (loss of MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, and/or
  PMS2) and/or PCR-based MSI testing at the central laboratory

- No prior systemic therapy for unresectable metastatic CRC (prior
  adjuvant therapy completed \>= 6 months before randomization is
  permitted)

- At least one measurable lesion per RECIST v1.1 on CT/MRI performed
  within 28 days of randomization

- ECOG Performance Status 0 or 1

- Adequate organ function: ANC \>= 1.5 x 10\^9/L, Platelets \>= 100 x
  10\^9/L, Hemoglobin \>= 9.0 g/dL, Total bilirubin \<= 1.5 x ULN (or
  \<= 3 x ULN for Gilbert syndrome), AST and ALT \<= 2.5 x ULN (or \<= 5
  x ULN with hepatic metastases), Serum creatinine \<= 1.5 x ULN or CrCl
  \>= 50 mL/min

- Negative serum pregnancy test within 72 hours prior to first dose
  (women of childbearing potential \[WOCBP\])

- Willingness to use effective contraception from Day 1 through 6 months
  after last dose (WOCBP and male patients with WOCBP partners)

- Provision of signed and dated written informed consent prior to any
  study-specific procedures

**4.2 Exclusion Criteria**

Patients will be excluded if ANY of the following criteria apply:

- Prior treatment with an anti-PD-1, anti-PD-L1, anti-PD-L2,
  anti-CTLA-4, or any other immune checkpoint inhibitor

- Active autoimmune disease requiring systemic treatment within the past
  2 years (replacement therapy is permissible)

- Active or prior documented inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn\'s
  disease or ulcerative colitis)

- Known history of Grade 3 or 4 immune-mediated reactions with prior
  immunotherapy

- Systemic corticosteroids (\> 10 mg prednisone/day equivalent) or other
  immunosuppressive therapy within 14 days prior to randomization

- Known CNS metastases (unless previously treated, asymptomatic, and
  stable for \>= 4 weeks)

- Known microsatellite stable (MSS) or pMMR tumor status

- History of interstitial lung disease, non-infectious pneumonitis, or
  pulmonary fibrosis

- Known HIV infection with CD4+ count \< 350 cells/uL, or requiring
  antiretroviral therapy (stable HIV is permitted)

- Active Hepatitis B (HBsAg+) or Hepatitis C (HCV RNA+) infection

- Major surgery within 28 days prior to randomization

- Known hypersensitivity to Chinese hamster ovary (CHO)-derived
  biologics, oxaliplatin, 5-fluorouracil, leucovorin, or bevacizumab

- Pregnancy or breastfeeding

- Any prior malignancy within 3 years except adequately treated basal
  cell carcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma of the skin, or carcinoma in
  situ

- Participation in another therapeutic clinical trial within 28 days
  prior to randomization

**5.2 Cervimab Administration**

Cervimab 400 mg will be diluted in 100 mL 0.9% sodium chloride for
injection and administered as a 30-minute IV infusion. Pre-medication is
not required but may be administered at investigator discretion after
the first infusion if IRR management is needed.

**5.3 FOLFOX-4 Administration**

Oxaliplatin 85 mg/m2 IV over 2 hours (Day 1), Leucovorin 200 mg/m2 IV
over 2 hours (Days 1-2), 5-FU 400 mg/m2 IV bolus then 600 mg/m2
continuous infusion over 22 hours (Days 1-2), repeated every 14 days.

**5.4 Management of Immune-Related Adverse Events (irAEs)**

- Grade 1 irAE: Continue treatment; close monitoring; consider topical
  or local therapies

- Grade 2 irAE: Withhold Cervimab; initiate prednisone 1 mg/kg/day;
  resume when resolved to Grade \<= 1

- Grade 3 irAE: Withhold Cervimab; initiate IV methylprednisolone 1-2
  mg/kg/day; resume when resolved to Grade \<= 1 if within 12 weeks

- Grade 4 or recurrent Grade 3 irAE: Permanently discontinue Cervimab

**5.5 Permitted and Prohibited Concomitant Medications**

Permitted: Supportive care including G-CSF, antiemetics,
bisphosphonates, analgesics. Best supportive care for palliative intent.
Antibiotics for infectious complications.

Prohibited: Other immunotherapy agents, other systemic anticancer
therapy, live attenuated vaccines (within 30 days), immunosuppressants
(except as required for irAE management).

**6.2 Tumor Response Assessment**

Tumor response will be assessed by CT scan of the chest, abdomen, and
pelvis with IV contrast (or MRI if CT is contraindicated) at the
intervals defined in the Schedule of Assessments. All imaging will be
assessed by BICR per RECIST v1.1. Response categories: Complete Response
(CR), Partial Response (PR), Stable Disease (SD), Progressive Disease
(PD).

**6.3 Biomarker Assessments**

Archival tumor tissue (FFPE block or minimum 10 unstained slides, 4-5
micron) is mandatory for all patients. Fresh biopsy is strongly
encouraged but not mandatory. Central laboratory testing will include:
dMMR/MSI-H confirmation (IHC + PCR), PD-L1 TPS and CPS by Dako 22C3
immunohistochemistry, and TMB by validated NGS panel.

**7. STATISTICAL CONSIDERATIONS**

**7.1 Sample Size**

The primary endpoint is PFS by BICR. Assuming a median PFS of 17 months
for Cervimab versus 9.5 months for FOLFOX-4 (HR = 0.56), an exponential
distribution, two-sided log-rank test at alpha = 0.025 (after adjustment
for interim analyses per Lan-DeMets alpha-spending with O\'Brien-Fleming
boundary), and 90% power, approximately 280 PFS events are required.
With 480 patients enrolled over 24 months and minimum 24 months
follow-up, the required events will be observed approximately 42 months
after first patient enrolled.

**7.2 Analysis Populations**

- Intent-to-Treat (ITT) Population: All randomized patients (primary
  analysis)

- Per-Protocol (PP) Population: All ITT patients without major protocol
  deviations

- Safety Population: All patients who received at least one dose of
  study treatment

- Biomarker-Evaluable Population (BEP): Patients with evaluable baseline
  biomarker data

**7.3 Primary Analysis**

The primary analysis will compare PFS between arms in the ITT population
using a stratified log-rank test. Hazard ratio and 95% CI will be
estimated from a stratified Cox proportional hazards model. Kaplan-Meier
methodology will be used to estimate median PFS and survival rates at
prespecified time points.

**7.4 Interim Analyses**

Two pre-planned interim analyses are planned: the first at approximately
50% of PFS events (for futility and efficacy), and the second at
approximately 75% of PFS events (for efficacy). The Data Safety
Monitoring Board (DSMB) will review each interim analysis. Efficacy
boundaries will be set per the Lan-DeMets alpha-spending function with
O\'Brien-Fleming type boundaries.

**7.5 Multiple Testing Procedure**

A hierarchical testing procedure will be used: (1) PFS, (2) OS, (3) ORR,
(4) HRQoL. Each subsequent endpoint will only be tested if the preceding
endpoint reaches statistical significance, preserving the overall Type I
error rate at 0.05 (two-sided).

**8. SAFETY MONITORING AND REPORTING**

**8.1 Adverse Event Definitions and Grading**

All adverse events (AEs) will be graded using the National Cancer
Institute Common Terminology Criteria for Adverse Events (NCI CTCAE)
Version 5.0. An adverse event of special interest (AESI) list specific
to immune checkpoint inhibitor therapy has been predefined and includes:
immune-related pneumonitis, colitis, hepatitis, endocrinopathies
(thyroiditis, adrenal insufficiency, hypophysitis), nephritis,
dermatitis/rash, and infusion-related reactions.

**8.2 Serious Adverse Events**

Any SAE must be reported to the sponsor within 24 hours of the
investigator\'s first awareness. Fatal and unexpected SAEs suspected to
be related to study treatment must be reported to the FDA as 7-day and
15-day expedited IND safety reports, respectively, per 21 CFR 312.32.

**8.3 Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB)**

An independent DSMB consisting of at least 3 experts in oncology,
biostatistics, and clinical trial methodology will review unblinded
safety and efficacy data at prespecified intervals and at the interim
analyses. The DSMB charter governs all DSMB activities and decision
criteria.

**Appendix B: RECIST v1.1 Response Criteria Summary**

Complete Response (CR): Disappearance of all target lesions and all
non-target lesions; normalization of tumor marker levels.

Partial Response (PR): \>= 30% decrease in the sum of diameters of
target lesions, taking as reference the baseline sum of diameters.

Progressive Disease (PD): \>= 20% increase in the sum of diameters of
target lesions (absolute increase of \>= 5 mm required), or unequivocal
progression of non-target lesions, or appearance of new lesions.

Stable Disease (SD): Neither sufficient shrinkage to qualify for PR nor
sufficient increase to qualify for PD.
