**2.1 Disease Context & Unmet Need**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) ranks as the third most diagnosed malignancy
worldwide with an estimated 1.9 million new cases annually (GLOBOCAN
2022). Approximately 4--5% of metastatic CRC tumors exhibit dMMR/MSI-H
status --- a hypermutated phenotype uniquely sensitive to immune
checkpoint blockade due to high neoantigen burden, dense TIL
infiltration, and upregulated PD-L1/PD-L2 expression. Despite the
approval of pembrolizumab (KEYNOTE-177, 2021) and nivolumab ± ipilimumab
(CheckMate 142), cervimab\'s differentiated IgG4 backbone, sub-nanomolar
PD-1 affinity (KD \~0.3 nM), and Phase 1/2 ORR of 45% at the 400 mg Q3W
flat dose position it as a competitive entrant in this market.

**3.1 Strategic Regulatory Framework**

CERVIMAB-301 is designed as a pivotal, registration-enabling study to
support simultaneous NDA (US FDA), MAA (EU EMA), and J-NDA/BLA
(PMDA/NMPA) submissions. The regulatory strategy capitalizes on
available Breakthrough Therapy Designation (BTD) pathways in the US and
PRIME eligibility in the EU, given the unmet need and early clinical
evidence of substantial improvement over standard of care.

**4.4 Site Competition Risk --- Enrollment Impact**

Multiple concurrent PD-1 trials targeting identical dMMR/MSI-H mCRC
patients create significant site enrollment competition. Sites actively
running KEYNOTE-177 follow-on studies, CheckMate 8HW extensions, or
emerging Chinese PD-1 trials will face competing protocol obligations.
Mitigation strategies:

- Select sites with documented dMMR/MSI-H screening capability AND not
  exclusively committed to competing protocols

- Offer competitive site payments, screen failure reimbursement
  (dMMR/MSI-H testing is costly), and central lab support

- Prioritize sites in geographies underserved by existing approved PD-1
  therapies (Latin America, Eastern Europe, Southeast Asia)

- Fast-track site selection: targeting site activation within 6 months
  of protocol finalization

**7.4 dMMR/MSI-H Screening & Screen Failure Management**

The key enrollment bottleneck in dMMR/MSI-H studies is molecular
confirmation of tumor MSI status. To address this:

- Central laboratory partnership (Foundation Medicine, NovaBay, or
  equivalent) will provide IHC + PCR-based dMMR/MSI-H confirmation
  within 7 business days of tissue receipt

- Reflex testing protocol: all consenting mCRC patients at sites will
  undergo upfront MSI-H reflex testing from tumor tissue obtained at
  standard-of-care procedures --- minimizing dedicated screening burden

- Local lab pathway: Sites with validated local MSI-H testing (IHC
  panel: MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2) may use local testing with central lab
  confirmation of equivocal results

- Screen failure cost subsidy: Sponsor will reimburse sites for
  dMMR/MSI-H testing costs for screen failures --- a key site incentive
  given \~\$800--1,200 per test cost

- Screen failure log: All screen failures tracked by reason; rolling
  analysis to optimize I/E criteria and site coaching

**7.5 Site-Level Enrollment Tracking & Performance Management**

An enrollment performance dashboard will be maintained in real-time via
the EDC system and sponsor operational platform (Veeva Vault/Medidata
integration). Monthly enrollment reviews will be held with all CRAs and
site-level targets established:

- Green flag: Site enrolling ≥80% of target rate → routine monitoring

- Yellow flag: Site enrolling 50--79% of target rate → biweekly CRA
  check-in; root cause analysis

- Red flag: Site enrolling \<50% of target rate for 2 consecutive months
  → site review board; potential Tier re-classification; possible
  replacement with backup site

         disease           insulin, steroids for adrenal) does NOT
