**1. INTRODUCTION**

**1.1 Background and Rationale**

Colorectal cancer (CRC) is the third most common cancer worldwide and
the second leading cause of cancer-related mortality. Mismatch repair
deficiency (dMMR) and microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H) status
occurs in approximately 4-5% of patients with metastatic CRC (mCRC) and
is associated with distinct tumor biology characterized by high tumor
mutational burden (TMB), elevated neoantigen load, and increased
tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes. These features render dMMR/MSI-H tumors
particularly susceptible to immune checkpoint inhibition.

Cervimab is a fully human IgG4 anti-PD-1 monoclonal antibody (mAb)
developed by NovaPharma Oncology, Inc. with sub-nanomolar affinity for
PD-1. In preclinical studies, cervimab demonstrated potent blockade of
PD-1/PD-L1 and PD-1/PD-L2 interactions, restoring T-cell effector
function within the tumor microenvironment. Phase 1/2 data (Protocol
CERV-101; Protocol CERV-201) demonstrated durable objective responses
(ORR 49%), a manageable safety profile, and median PFS of 19.4 months in
dMMR/MSI-H mCRC, providing clinical proof of concept and supporting dose
selection of 400 mg Q4W.

The current standard of care for first-line (1L) mCRC includes
fluoropyrimidine-based doublet chemotherapy (e.g., FOLFOX-6) combined
with biologic agents such as bevacizumab or cetuximab (RAS/RAF
wild-type). Despite initial response rates of 50-60%, patients
inevitably experience disease progression, and median OS remains
approximately 28-30 months. An unmet medical need exists for treatments
that can achieve durable remissions or prolonged disease control in the
dMMR/MSI-H subpopulation.

**1.2 Study Objectives**

**1.2.1 Primary Objective**

To demonstrate the superiority of cervimab over FOLFOX-6 plus
bevacizumab with respect to progression-free survival (PFS) as assessed
by blinded independent central review (BICR) per RECIST v1.1 in patients
with previously untreated dMMR/MSI-H mCRC.

**1.2.2 Key Secondary Objectives**

- To compare overall survival (OS) between treatment arms

- To compare objective response rate (ORR) and duration of response
  (DoR) per RECIST v1.1 by BICR

- To compare disease control rate (DCR) at 16 weeks

- To evaluate health-related quality of life (HRQoL) using EORTC QLQ-C30
  and EQ-5D-5L

- To characterize the safety and tolerability profile of cervimab versus
  control

**1.2.3 Exploratory Objectives**

- To evaluate the relationship between PD-L1 combined positive score
  (CPS), TMB, and clinical outcomes

- To assess pharmacokinetic/pharmacodynamic (PK/PD) correlations

- To evaluate minimal residual disease (MRD) by circulating tumor DNA
  (ctDNA)

**2. STUDY DESIGN**

**2.1 Overview of Study Design**

CERV-301 is a Phase 3, multicenter, open-label, randomized, controlled
trial enrolling patients with previously untreated dMMR/MSI-H mCRC.
Patients are randomized 1:1 to cervimab 400 mg IV Q4W (Arm A) or
mFOLFOX-6 plus bevacizumab 5 mg/kg IV Q2W (Arm B). Randomization is
stratified by ECOG performance status (0 vs. 1-2), geographic region
(North America/Western Europe vs. rest of world), and prior adjuvant
chemotherapy (yes vs. no). Treatment continues until confirmed
radiographic progression per RECIST v1.1, unacceptable toxicity, or
withdrawal of consent. Tumor assessments are performed at baseline and
every 8 weeks through Week 40, then every 12 weeks thereafter.

**2.2 Sample Size and Justification**

The sample size calculation assumes a median PFS of 9 months in the
control arm (based on published FOLFOX-6/bevacizumab data in dMMR/MSI-H
mCRC), a hazard ratio (HR) of 0.55 (corresponding to a 45% reduction in
risk of progression or death), and a two-sided alpha of 0.05 with 80%
power. Using a log-rank test, 320 PFS events are required. Accounting
for a 20% dropout rate and assuming 36 months of enrollment followed by
12 months of additional follow-up, a total of 440 patients (220 per arm)
are to be randomized. A single interim analysis for efficacy and
futility is planned at approximately 50% of events (160 events).

**3. ANALYSIS POPULATIONS**

**3.1 Intent-to-Treat (ITT) Population**

The ITT population consists of all randomized patients regardless of
treatment received, protocol deviations, or withdrawal from study.
Patients will be analyzed in the treatment arm to which they were
randomized. The ITT population is the primary population for all
efficacy analyses including PFS, OS, ORR, and DCR. This is consistent
with ICH E9 and FDA guidance on the primary analysis population for
superiority trials.

**3.2 Modified Intent-to-Treat (mITT) Population**

The mITT population consists of all randomized patients who received at
least one dose of study treatment and had at least one post-baseline
tumor assessment or died before the first assessment. The mITT
population will be used for sensitivity analyses of PFS and ORR.

**3.3 Per-Protocol (PP) Population**

The PP population consists of all randomized patients who completed at
least two treatment cycles, had no major protocol deviations affecting
efficacy assessment, and had confirmatory disease status per dMMR/MSI-H
testing. Major protocol deviations are defined in the Protocol Deviation
Management Plan (PDMP). The PP population will be used for supportive
efficacy analyses.

**3.4 Safety Population**

The safety population consists of all patients who received at least one
dose of study treatment. Patients will be analyzed according to the
treatment actually received. All safety analyses will be conducted using
the safety population.

**3.5 PK Population**

The PK population consists of all patients in the safety population with
at least one evaluable post-dose cervimab concentration measurement.

**4.2 Key Secondary Estimand --- OS**

The OS estimand uses the same population and treatment definitions. The
primary estimand strategy for OS employs a treatment-policy strategy for
all intercurrent events (i.e., all post-randomization events including
subsequent therapies are incorporated into the OS estimate). A
hypothetical sensitivity estimand (censoring at initiation of
post-progression therapy) will be conducted as a secondary analysis.

**5. STATISTICAL METHODS --- PRIMARY ENDPOINT**

**5.1 Primary Endpoint: Progression-Free Survival**

**5.1.1 Definition and Measurement**

PFS is defined as the time (in months) from the date of randomization to
the date of first documented disease progression per RECIST v1.1 as
assessed by BICR, or death from any cause, whichever occurs first.
Patients without progression or death at the time of analysis will be
censored at the date of the last evaluable tumor assessment.

Censoring rules will follow FDA guidance (\'Guidance for Industry:
Clinical Trial Endpoints for the Approval of Cancer Drugs and
Biologics,\' May 2007):

- Patient dies or progresses after two or more missed tumor assessments:
  censored at the last evaluable assessment date prior to the missed
  assessments

- Patient withdraws consent without documented progression: censored at
  the last evaluable tumor assessment

- Patient starts new anti-cancer therapy without prior documented
  progression: censored at the last evaluable assessment before the new
  therapy

- Patient was never assessed after randomization: censored at
  randomization + 1 day

**5.1.2 Primary Analysis**

The primary analysis of PFS will be a two-sided stratified log-rank test
at the significance level alpha = 0.025 (final analysis, adjusted for
interim analysis per Lan-DeMets O\'Brien-Fleming spending function).
Stratification factors used in the randomization procedure (ECOG PS,
geographic region, prior adjuvant chemotherapy) will be incorporated as
strata. The Kaplan-Meier (KM) method will be used to estimate median PFS
and PFS rates at 6, 12, 18, and 24 months with 95% confidence intervals
(Brookmeyer-Crowley method).

The primary measure of treatment effect will be the hazard ratio (HR)
estimated from a stratified Cox proportional hazards model with
treatment as the sole covariate and stratification factors as strata.
The proportional hazards assumption will be evaluated using Schoenfeld
residuals and log-log survival plots. If the assumption is violated,
restricted mean survival time (RMST) differences at 24 months will serve
as the primary measure of effect.

**5.1.3 Alpha Spending and Interim Analysis**

A single interim analysis (IA) for both efficacy and futility is planned
at approximately 160 PFS events (50% information fraction). Alpha will
be allocated using the Lan-DeMets O\'Brien-Fleming spending function. At
the interim analysis, the efficacy boundary is estimated at z = 2.797
(two-sided p \< 0.0052) and futility boundary at z = 0.400. At the final
analysis (320 events), the efficacy boundary is z = 1.960 (two-sided p
\< 0.0455). All alpha is protected such that the overall two-sided type
I error rate does not exceed 0.05.

**6. STATISTICAL METHODS --- SECONDARY ENDPOINTS**

**6.1 Overall Survival**

OS is defined as time from randomization to death from any cause.
Patients alive at the time of analysis will be censored at the date last
known alive. The OS analysis will use the same methods as PFS
(stratified log-rank test, stratified Cox model, Kaplan-Meier
estimation). To protect the overall type I error rate, OS will be tested
hierarchically following demonstration of PFS superiority per the fixed
sequence testing procedure.

**6.2 Objective Response Rate**

ORR is defined as the proportion of patients with confirmed complete
response (CR) or partial response (PR) per RECIST v1.1 by BICR. A
response must be confirmed by a repeat assessment no less than 4 weeks
after the initial response criteria are met. The difference in ORR
between arms will be estimated with 95% CI using the Newcombe-Wilson
method (stratified). The Cochran-Mantel-Haenszel test stratified by
randomization factors will be used for hypothesis testing.

**6.3 Duration of Response**

DoR is defined (in responders only) as the time from the date of first
confirmed response to the date of first documented progression or death
from any cause. The KM method will be used to estimate median DoR with
95% CI. Responders without documented progression or death will be
censored at the date of last evaluable tumor assessment.

**6.4 Disease Control Rate**

DCR at Week 16 is defined as the proportion of patients with CR, PR, or
stable disease (SD) lasting at least 16 weeks from randomization. The
same Newcombe-Wilson / CMH framework used for ORR will be applied.

**6.5 Health-Related Quality of Life**

HRQoL will be assessed using the EORTC QLQ-C30 (global health status/QoL
scale and functional/symptom sub-scales) and EQ-5D-5L (utility index and
VAS). Analyses will use a mixed-effects model for repeated measures
(MMRM) with treatment, visit, treatment-by-visit interaction, baseline
score, stratification factors, and baseline-by-visit interaction as
covariates. An unstructured covariance matrix will be used. The primary
HRQoL endpoint is time to definitive clinically meaningful deterioration
(TTCD) in the EORTC QLQ-C30 global health status scale (defined as a \>=
10-point decrease from baseline confirmed at next assessment or followed
by death within 3 weeks).

**7. MULTIPLICITY CONTROL**

**7.1 Hierarchical Testing Procedure**

To control the family-wise error rate at 0.05 (two-sided), a
fixed-sequence hierarchical testing procedure will be employed for the
primary and key secondary endpoints:

  ---------- --------------------------------- ------------- ----------------
  **Step**   **Endpoint**                      **Alpha**     **Population**

  1          PFS by BICR (Primary)             0.05          ITT
                                               (two-sided)   

  2          OS (Key Secondary)                0.05 (carried ITT
                                               over)         

  3          ORR by BICR                       0.05 (carried ITT
                                               over)         

  4          TTCD EORTC QLQ-C30                0.05 (carried ITT
                                               over)         
  ---------- --------------------------------- ------------- ----------------

Each step is tested only if all previous steps achieved statistical
significance. If a step fails, subsequent testing is considered
exploratory and p-values are not considered confirmatory.

**8. SENSITIVITY AND EXPLORATORY ANALYSES**

**8.1 Sensitivity Analyses for PFS**

- Unstratified log-rank test and Cox model (ignoring stratification
  factors)

- PFS using investigator assessment rather than BICR

- Alternative censoring rules: progressive censoring strategy where
  patients with missing assessments are treated as events

- mITT population analysis

- RMST analysis at 18 and 24 months as alternative PFS summary measures

- Analysis using PP population

- Tipping-point analysis to assess impact of non-random missing data on
  PFS

**8.2 Subgroup Analyses**

Subgroup analyses will be conducted for PFS and OS using a forest plot
framework. Pre-specified subgroups include:

- PD-L1 CPS (\< 1, 1 to \< 10, \>= 10)

- TMB (low \[\< 10 mutations/Mb\], high \[\>= 10 mutations/Mb\])

- ECOG PS (0 vs. 1-2)

- Geographic region

- Age (\< 65 vs. \>= 65 years)

- Sex (male vs. female)

- Prior adjuvant chemotherapy (yes vs. no)

- KRAS/NRAS/BRAF mutational status

- Tumor sidedness (left vs. right colon vs. rectum)

- Synchronous versus metachronous metastases

Subgroup analyses are exploratory and no multiplicity adjustment will be
applied. Interactions between subgroup and treatment will be evaluated
using a treatment-by-subgroup interaction term in the Cox model.

**8.3 Biomarker Exploratory Analyses**

Correlations between baseline biomarker levels (PD-L1 CPS, TMB, ctDNA,
tumor-infiltrating lymphocyte \[TIL\] density) and PFS/ORR will be
explored using Spearman rank correlation and logistic regression (ORR)
or Cox regression (PFS). Receiver operating characteristic (ROC) curves
and area under the curve (AUC) will be calculated for candidate
biomarker thresholds.

**9. SAFETY ANALYSES**

**9.1 Adverse Events**

All adverse events will be coded using MedDRA (version 26.0) and graded
per NCI CTCAE v5.0. AEs occurring from the first dose of study treatment
to 90 days after the last dose (or start of new anti-cancer therapy,
whichever is earlier) will be attributed to study treatment. The
following AE summaries will be presented by treatment arm using the
safety population:

- Any AE, treatment-related AE (TRAE), serious AE (SAE), AE leading to
  discontinuation, AE leading to dose modification (cervimab arm), AE
  leading to death

- AE incidence by MedDRA SOC and PT, sorted by frequency in cervimab arm

- Grade 3/4/5 AEs by MedDRA PT

- Immune-mediated AEs (imAEs) by PT and grade --- with special focus on
  colitis, hepatitis, pneumonitis, endocrinopathy, nephritis, dermatitis

- Time to onset and time to resolution of imAEs (KM methodology)

**9.2 Clinical Laboratory, Vital Signs, and ECG**

Laboratory parameters (hematology, serum chemistry, thyroid function,
liver function) will be summarized by treatment arm and visit using
descriptive statistics and worst-grade shift tables (baseline to
on-treatment). Shifts from Grade 0-1 to Grade 3-4 will be highlighted.
Vital signs and 12-lead ECG parameters (QTcF) will be summarized using
descriptive statistics.

**9.3 Exposure and Compliance**

Treatment exposure (number of cycles, cumulative dose, dose intensity,
actual/relative dose intensity) and reasons for discontinuation will be
summarized. For cervimab, time to first dose modification and proportion
of patients receiving steroid management for imAEs will be reported.

**10. HANDLING OF MISSING DATA**

**10.1 General Principles**

Missing data handling follows ICH E9(R1) principles. The primary
analysis uses available data under the assumption that censoring is
non-informative (missing completely at random, MCAR, or missing at
random, MAR). Sensitivity analyses address departures from this
assumption. Baseline covariate imputation will use single imputation by
median (continuous variables) or mode (categorical variables) where
missing rates are \< 5%; multiple imputation by chained equations (MICE,
50 imputed datasets, 10 iterations) will be used for covariates with
missing rates \>= 5%.

**10.2 Missing Primary Endpoint Data**

Patients with no post-baseline tumor assessment will be censored at
randomization + 1 day. If a patient withdraws for a reason potentially
related to disease progression (e.g., clinical deterioration), a
sensitivity analysis will treat this as a PFS event.

**10.3 Missing Patient-Reported Outcomes**

For HRQoL analyses, MMRM handles missing data under a MAR assumption. A
pattern mixture model (PMM) sensitivity analysis will be conducted. The
primary sensitivity analysis assumes a delta = -5 point offset (i.e.,
patients with missing data had scores 5 points worse than observed
patients with similar covariates).

**11. SOFTWARE AND PROGRAMMING**

**11.1 Statistical Software**

All statistical analyses will be performed using SAS version 9.4 (SAS
Institute Inc., Cary, NC, USA) and R version 4.4.x (R Foundation for
Statistical Computing, Vienna, Austria). Kaplan-Meier analyses and Cox
regression will use SAS PROC LIFETEST and PROC PHREG. MMRM analyses will
use SAS PROC MIXED. All R packages will be version-locked using the
\'renv\' package and listed in Appendix D.

**11.2 CDISC Data Standards**

Study data will be prepared per CDISC standards (SDTM v1.9, ADaM v2.1)
in compliance with FDA 21 CFR Part 11 electronic records requirements.
Analysis datasets will include ADSL (subject-level), ADTTE
(time-to-event), ADRS (response), ADAE (adverse events), ADLB
(laboratory), and ADQOL (quality of life). Dataset specifications are
documented in the Analysis Data Model Implementation Guide (ADOMIG)
maintained separately from this SAP.

**11.3 Validation and Audit Trail**

All analysis programs will undergo independent double programming and
validation. A validation report documenting all discrepancies and
resolutions will be archived with the final analysis programs. All
analysis environments will be archived per 21 CFR Part 11 requirements
to support regulatory inspection.

**APPENDIX A: RANDOMIZATION AND STRATIFICATION**

Randomization will be performed centrally using an Interactive Web
Response System (IWRS; Medidata Rave RTSM). The randomization list will
be generated by an independent biostatistician not involved in the
conduct of the trial using a permuted block algorithm with varying block
sizes of 4, 6, and 8. The randomization list will be held by the IWRS
vendor and the independent statistician. Stratification factors and
their levels are:

- ECOG Performance Status: 0 versus 1-2

- Geographic Region: North America/Western Europe versus
  Asia-Pacific/Rest of World

- Prior Adjuvant Chemotherapy: Yes versus No

**APPENDIX B: RECIST v1.1 ASSESSMENT SCHEDULE**

Tumor imaging (CT scan with contrast or MRI) will be performed at
baseline (within 28 days of randomization) and every 8 weeks (+ 7 days
window) through Week 40, then every 12 weeks (+ 14 days window)
thereafter until confirmed progression, initiation of new anti-cancer
therapy, withdrawal of consent, or death. All images will be transmitted
to the central imaging vendor (Bio-Imaging Research Inc.) within 5
business days of acquisition for BICR assessment.

**APPENDIX C: IMMUNE-MEDIATED AE DEFINITIONS**

Immune-mediated adverse events (imAEs) are defined as AEs of potential
immunological etiology requiring systemic corticosteroid management or
other immunosuppressive therapy, occurring in the absence of an obvious
alternative cause. Pre-specified imAE categories and their preferred
management pathways are defined in the Cervimab Immune-Mediated Toxicity
Management Guide (CERTIMAB-IMG-001). Specific imAE monitoring schedules
(thyroid function, liver function, glucose, amylase/lipase) are defined
in the Protocol Schedule of Assessments.
