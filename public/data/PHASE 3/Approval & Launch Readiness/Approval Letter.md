**1. INDICATIONS AND USAGE**

CERVIMAB is a programmed death receptor-1 (PD-1) blocking antibody
indicated for the treatment of adult patients with:

- Unresectable or metastatic mismatch repair deficient (dMMR) or
  microsatellite instability-high (MSI-H) colorectal cancer (CRC) that
  has progressed following fluoropyrimidine-, oxaliplatin-, and
  irinotecan-based chemotherapy. (1.1)

*This indication is approved under accelerated approval based on overall
response rate (ORR). Continued approval for this indication may be
contingent upon verification and description of clinical benefit in the
confirmatory trial. (1.1)*

**2. DOSAGE AND ADMINISTRATION**

**2.1 Patient Selection**

Select patients for treatment based on dMMR or MSI-H tumor status.
FDA-approved tests for dMMR or MSI-H status are available. Information
on FDA-approved tests for the detection of dMMR and MSI-H tumor status
is available at: http://www.fda.gov/CompanionDiagnostics.

**2.2 Recommended Dosage**

The recommended dose of CERVIMAB is 400 mg administered as an
intravenous infusion over 30 minutes every 6 weeks until disease
progression or unacceptable toxicity.

**2.4 Preparation and Administration**

Prepare and administer CERVIMAB as follows:

- Do not shake the vial.

- Visually inspect for particulate matter and discoloration prior to
  administration. Discard if solution is cloudy, discolored, or if
  visible particles are observed.

- Dilute CERVIMAB with 0.9% Sodium Chloride Injection, USP or 5%
  Dextrose Injection, USP to a final concentration ranging from 1 mg/mL
  to 10 mg/mL.

- Administer diluted solution intravenously over 30 minutes through an
  IV line containing a sterile, non-pyrogenic, low-protein binding 0.2
  or 0.22 micron in-line filter.

- The diluted solution may be stored at room temperature for no more
  than 6 hours from the time of preparation. Alternatively, store at 2°C
  to 8°C (36°F to 46°F) for no more than 24 hours.

**5. WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS**

**5.1 Immune-Mediated Pneumonitis**

CERVIMAB can cause immune-mediated pneumonitis, which is fatal in some
cases. Monitor patients for signs and symptoms of pneumonitis. Evaluate
patients with suspected pneumonitis with radiographic imaging.
Administer corticosteroids (prednisone 1--2 mg/kg/day or equivalent)
followed by corticosteroid taper for Grade 2 or greater pneumonitis.
Withhold CERVIMAB for Grade 2 and permanently discontinue for Grade 3 or
4 or recurrent Grade 2 pneumonitis \[see Dosage and Administration
(2.3)\].

In CERVIMAB-301, immune-mediated pneumonitis occurred in 3.4% (9/261) of
patients receiving CERVIMAB, including Grade 3 (0.8%), with no Grade 4
or fatal cases reported.

**5.2 Immune-Mediated Colitis**

CERVIMAB can cause immune-mediated colitis. Monitor patients for signs
and symptoms of colitis. Administer corticosteroids for Grade 2 or
greater colitis. Withhold CERVIMAB for Grade 2--3 and permanently
discontinue for Grade 4 or recurrent Grade 3 colitis \[see Dosage and
Administration (2.3)\].

In CERVIMAB-301, immune-mediated colitis occurred in 4.6% (12/261) of
patients receiving CERVIMAB, including Grade 3 (1.5%) and Grade 4
(0.4%).

**5.3 Immune-Mediated Hepatitis**

CERVIMAB can cause immune-mediated hepatitis. Monitor patients for
changes in liver function tests. Administer corticosteroids for Grade 2
or greater hepatitis and permanently discontinue CERVIMAB for Grade 3 or
4 hepatitis \[see Dosage and Administration (2.3), Adverse Reactions
(6.1)\].

**5.4 Immune-Mediated Endocrinopathies**

CERVIMAB can cause primary or secondary adrenal insufficiency,
immune-mediated hypophysitis, immune-mediated thyroid disorders, and
Type 1 diabetes mellitus. Administer hormone replacement therapy as
needed. Withhold CERVIMAB for Grade 2 or higher adrenal insufficiency or
hypophysitis. Withhold CERVIMAB for Grade 3 or 4 hyperglycemia.

**5.5 Immune-Mediated Nephritis with Renal Dysfunction**

CERVIMAB can cause immune-mediated nephritis. Monitor patients for
elevated serum creatinine. Administer corticosteroids for Grade 2 or
greater nephritis. Permanently discontinue CERVIMAB for Grade 4
nephritis.

**5.6 Immune-Mediated Dermatologic Reactions**

CERVIMAB can cause immune-mediated rash or dermatitis. Exfoliative
dermatitis, including Stevens-Johnson syndrome (SJS), toxic epidermal
necrolysis (TEN), drug rash with eosinophilia and systemic symptoms
(DRESS), and bullous pemphigoid have been reported. Topical emollients
and/or topical corticosteroids may be adequate to treat mild to moderate
non-exfoliative rashes. Withhold or permanently discontinue CERVIMAB
depending on severity.

**5.7 Immune-Mediated Myocarditis**

CERVIMAB can cause immune-mediated myocarditis, which may be fatal.
Monitor patients with cardiac symptoms and troponin levels. Permanently
discontinue CERVIMAB for Grade 2 or greater myocarditis.

**5.8 Immune-Mediated Neurological Toxicities**

CERVIMAB can cause immune-mediated neurological toxicities, including
myasthenic syndrome/myasthenia gravis, Guillain-Barré syndrome,
encephalitis, and meningitis. Withhold CERVIMAB for Grade 2 neurological
toxicities. Permanently discontinue CERVIMAB for Grade 3 or 4
toxicities.

**5.9 Other Immune-Mediated Adverse Reactions**

Other clinically significant immune-mediated adverse reactions,
including uveitis/iritis, pancreatitis, arthritis, hemolytic anemia,
sarcoidosis, and vasculitis have been reported. Evaluate patients based
on clinical presentation, initiate appropriate workup, and administer
corticosteroids as clinically indicated.

**5.10 Immune-Mediated Adverse Reactions in Solid Organ Transplant
Recipients**

Fatal and other serious complications of immune-mediated adverse
reactions have occurred in patients receiving PD-1 blocking antibodies
as allografts. These complications included acute and hyperacute
allograft rejection. Advise patients of the risk of organ transplant
rejection.

**5.11 Infusion-Related Reactions**

Severe or life-threatening infusion-related reactions occurred in
patients receiving CERVIMAB. Monitor patients for signs and symptoms.
For Grade 1--2 infusion-related reactions, interrupt or slow the rate of
infusion. For Grade 3--4 reactions, permanently discontinue CERVIMAB and
administer epinephrine as indicated.

**5.12 Complications of Allogeneic Hematopoietic Stem Cell
Transplantation**

Fatal and other serious complications can occur in patients who receive
allogeneic hematopoietic stem cell transplantation (HSCT) before or
after being treated with a PD-1 receptor blocking antibody.
Graft-versus-host disease (GVHD), hepatic veno-occlusive disease (VOD),
and graft failure have been observed. Follow patients closely for
evidence of these complications.

**5.13 Embryo-Fetal Toxicity**

Based on its mechanism of action and data from animal studies, CERVIMAB
can cause fetal harm when administered to a pregnant woman. Advise
females of reproductive potential to use effective contraception during
treatment with CERVIMAB and for 4 months after the last dose.

**6.2 Immunogenicity**

As with all therapeutic proteins, there is potential for immunogenicity.
The detection of antibody formation is highly dependent on the
sensitivity and specificity of the assay. Additionally, the observed
incidence of antibody (including neutralizing antibody) positivity in an
assay may be influenced by several factors. In CERVIMAB-301, 2.3% of
patients (6/261) tested positive for anti-cervimab antibodies; none of
the anti-cervimab antibody-positive patients tested positive for
neutralizing antibodies.

**7. DRUG INTERACTIONS**

No formal drug interaction studies have been conducted with CERVIMAB.

**7.1 Effect of Other Drugs on CERVIMAB**

Systemic corticosteroids or immunosuppressants administered prior to
starting CERVIMAB may reduce the pharmacodynamic activity of CERVIMAB
and therefore should be used only with caution prior to initiating
CERVIMAB.

**8. USE IN SPECIFIC POPULATIONS**

**8.1 Pregnancy**

Risk Summary: Based on its mechanism of action, CERVIMAB can cause fetal
harm when administered to a pregnant woman. There are no available data
on CERVIMAB in pregnant women. Animal models link disruption of the
PD-1/PD-L1 pathway to an increased risk of immune-mediated rejection of
the developing fetus resulting in fetal death. Advise women of the
potential risk to a fetus.

**8.2 Lactation**

Risk Summary: There are no data on the presence of CERVIMAB in human
milk, the effects on the breastfed infant, or the effects on milk
production. Because of the potential for serious adverse reactions in
breastfed infants, advise patients not to breastfeed during treatment
and for 4 months after the last dose.

**8.3 Females and Males of Reproductive Potential**

Pregnancy Testing: Verify pregnancy status in females of reproductive
potential prior to initiating CERVIMAB. Contraception: Advise females of
reproductive potential to use effective contraception during treatment
and for 4 months after the last dose.

**8.4 Pediatric Use**

Safety and effectiveness of CERVIMAB have not been established in
pediatric patients.

**8.5 Geriatric Use**

Of the 261 patients who received CERVIMAB in CERVIMAB-301, 34% were 65
years or older and 9% were 75 years or older. No clinically meaningful
differences in safety and efficacy were observed between older and
younger patients.

**8.6 Hepatic Impairment**

No dosage modification is recommended for patients with mild (total
bilirubin ≤ ULN and AST \> ULN, or total bilirubin 1--1.5× ULN) or
moderate (total bilirubin 1.5--3× ULN and any AST) hepatic impairment.
CERVIMAB has not been studied in patients with severe hepatic impairment
(Child-Pugh C).

**8.7 Renal Impairment**

No clinically significant differences in the pharmacokinetics of
CERVIMAB were observed in patients with mild to moderate renal
impairment (eGFR 30--89 mL/min/1.73 m²). CERVIMAB has not been studied
in patients with severe renal impairment.

**11. DESCRIPTION**

CERVIMAB is a humanized immunoglobulin G4 (IgG4) kappa monoclonal
antibody that binds to the programmed death receptor-1 (PD-1). CERVIMAB
is produced in Chinese Hamster Ovary (CHO) cells and has an approximate
molecular weight of 146 kDa.

CERVIMAB injection is a sterile, preservative-free, clear to slightly
opalescent, colorless to slightly yellow solution at pH 5.8 ± 0.5. Each
single-dose vial contains 800 mg/16 mL (50 mg/mL) cervimab, L-histidine
(0.45 mg/mL), L-histidine monohydrochloride monohydrate (1.76 mg/mL),
sucrose (84.6 mg/mL), polysorbate 80 (0.2 mg/mL), and Water for
Injection, USP.

**12. CLINICAL PHARMACOLOGY**

**12.1 Mechanism of Action**

Programmed death receptor-1 (PD-1) is a negative regulator of T-cell
activity. Engagement of PD-1 by its ligands, PD-L1 and PD-L2, results in
inhibition of T-cell proliferation, cytokine secretion, and cytolytic
activity. In tumor microenvironments, PD-1 pathway signaling allows
tumors to evade immune surveillance. CERVIMAB is a humanized anti-PD-1
IgG4 antibody that blocks the interaction between PD-1 and its ligands,
PD-L1 and PD-L2, thereby restoring anti-tumor immune activity.

**12.3 Pharmacokinetics**

Following administration of 400 mg CERVIMAB Q6W, steady-state was
reached by approximately Cycle 3 (12 weeks). At steady-state, the mean
(CV%) peak concentration (Cmax) was 286 (31%) μg/mL and the mean trough
concentration (Cmin) was 28 (48%) μg/mL. The estimated terminal
half-life (t½) was approximately 27 days. CERVIMAB pharmacokinetics are
dose-proportional over a dose range of 1--20 mg/kg.

**13. NONCLINICAL TOXICOLOGY**

**13.1 Carcinogenesis, Mutagenesis, Impairment of Fertility**

No studies have been performed to evaluate the carcinogenic or mutagenic
potential of CERVIMAB. Based on its mechanism of action, CERVIMAB may
impair fertility in males and females of reproductive potential.

**17. PATIENT COUNSELING INFORMATION**

Advise the patient to read the FDA-approved patient labeling (Patient
Package Insert).

- Inform patients of the risk of immune-mediated adverse reactions that
  may require corticosteroid treatment and interruption or
  discontinuation of CERVIMAB.

- Advise patients to contact their healthcare provider immediately for
  new or worsening cough, chest pain, shortness of breath (pneumonitis);
  diarrhea, severe abdominal pain (colitis); yellowing of skin or whites
  of eyes, dark urine (hepatitis); rapid weight gain or loss, feeling
  hot or cold all the time, excessive tiredness, hair loss
  (endocrinopathies).

- Advise patients that infusion-related reactions can occur and to
  immediately report chills, shaking, fever, flushing, rash,
  hypotension, or shortness of breath during or following an infusion.

- Advise females of reproductive potential to use effective
  contraception during treatment and for 4 months after the last dose.

- Advise pregnant women that CERVIMAB can cause fetal harm.

**1. Goals of the REMS**

The REMS for CERVIMAB is designed to:

- Inform healthcare providers (HCPs) prescribing and administering
  CERVIMAB about the serious risks of immune-mediated adverse reactions
  and infusion-related reactions associated with CERVIMAB;

- Inform HCPs of evidence-based management recommendations for these
  adverse reactions, including corticosteroid dosing, withholding
  criteria, and permanent discontinuation criteria;

- Ensure that patients are aware of the serious risks associated with
  CERVIMAB and know when to seek medical attention;

- Mitigate the risks of immune-mediated adverse reactions through
  monitoring, early identification, and prompt treatment;

- Evaluate the effectiveness of the REMS program through assessments at
  specified intervals.

**2. REMS Program Components**

The CERVIMAB REMS consists of the following elements to assure safe use
(ETASU):

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **2.1 Healthcare Provider Communication and Education**

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

**2.1.1 HCP Education and Training Program**

All healthcare providers who prescribe or administer CERVIMAB must
complete an HCP Education and Training Program prior to prescribing or
administering CERVIMAB. This program shall include:

- Online training module (estimated 30--45 minutes, completion
  confirmation required)

- Review of the CERVIMAB USPI and REMS materials

- Attestation that they have read and understand the REMS requirements

The HCP Training Module shall cover:

- The mechanism of action and rationale for immune-mediated adverse
  reactions with PD-1 inhibition

- The full spectrum of immune-mediated adverse reactions, including
  immune-mediated pneumonitis, colitis, hepatitis, endocrinopathies
  (including adrenal insufficiency, hypophysitis, hypothyroidism,
  hyperthyroidism, Type 1 diabetes mellitus), nephritis, dermatologic
  reactions, myocarditis, and neurological toxicities

- Evidence-based clinical management algorithms for each organ system,
  including grading per CTCAE v5.0

- Corticosteroid dosing guidance (prednisone 1--2 mg/kg/day or
  equivalent) and tapering protocols

- Criteria for withholding vs. permanently discontinuing CERVIMAB

- Infusion-related reaction recognition and management (including
  epinephrine use)

- Patient monitoring schedule requirements

- Required patient counseling prior to initiation and during treatment
